## What Is Ahrefs?

Ahrefs is an SEO toolset built around the largest backlink index on the internet. The company runs its own web crawler. AhrefsBot. Which crawls 8 billion pages per day and maintains an index of 35+ trillion known links. That's the foundation everything else is built on.

The core product does 5 things: backlink analysis (Site Explorer), keyword research (Keywords Explorer), site auditing (Site Audit), rank tracking (Rank Tracker), and content research (Content Explorer). Each tool is focused, fast, and cleaner than the alternatives.

What Ahrefs is *not*: a writing tool, a publishing platform, or a content automation system. It shows you where opportunities exist. Creating, optimizing, and publishing content to capture those opportunities is entirely on you.

Ahrefs was founded in 2010 by Dmitry Gerasimenko, headquartered in Singapore. The company is bootstrapped. No external funding, no investors, no IPO. Revenue estimates range from $100–150M ARR. The team is ~80 people operating remotely. This lean, profitable structure is why the product stays focused and the interface stays clean.

## Ahrefs Features (2026)

### Site Explorer

Site Explorer is Ahrefs' crown jewel. Enter any URL or domain and get:

- **Backlink profile**. Every known link pointing to that domain, with referring domain authority, anchor text, dofollow/nofollow status, and first/last seen dates
- **Organic keywords**. Every keyword the domain ranks for, with position, traffic estimate, SERP features, and historical position changes
- **Top Pages**. Sorted by organic traffic, showing which content drives results
- **Competing Domains**. Sites that rank for the same keywords, with overlap visualization

The backlink data is where Ahrefs genuinely separates from Semrush, Moz, and everyone else. The index is larger, updates faster, and catches links that other tools miss. If backlink analysis is a primary use case, Ahrefs is the tool.

> **Insight:** The "Best by Links' Growth" report inside Site Explorer shows which pages are actively earning backlinks right now. This is gold for identifying content formats and topics that attract links naturally. Critical for building a sustainable link acquisition strategy.

### Keywords Explorer

Keywords Explorer covers 11 search engines (Google, YouTube, Amazon, Bing, Yahoo, Baidu, Naver, Yandex, Daum, Seznam, and Google Discover). For each keyword:

- Search volume with click-through rate estimates (what percentage of searches result in clicks)
- Keyword difficulty based on backlink profiles of top-10 results
- Parent topic identification (which broader topic your keyword clusters under)
- SERP overview with backlink/domain stats for every result

The "Traffic potential" metric is unique to Ahrefs. It estimates how much total organic traffic the #1 result gets for the parent topic, not just the target keyword. This prevents you from targeting keywords where the total opportunity is small even with a #1 ranking.

### Site Audit

Crawls your site and reports technical SEO issues:

- Over 100+ SEO issue checks
- Crawl budget optimization (identifies pages that waste crawler resources)
- Internal link analysis with orphan page detection
- JavaScript rendering audit (important for SPAs and React/Next.js sites)
- Health Score with trend tracking over time

The audit is thorough but less detailed than Semrush's 140+ checks. For most sites, the difference is academic. Both catch the issues that actually matter.

### Content Explorer

A searchable database of 16+ billion web pages. Search any topic and filter by:

- Organic traffic (find content that actually ranks, not just exists)
- Referring domains (find content that earns backlinks)
- Published date (find fresh vs. evergreen content)
- Word count and social shares

Use case: find topics in your niche where existing content has high traffic but low backlink requirements. These are the opportunities where you can rank without an aggressive link-building campaign.

### Rank Tracker

Tracks keyword rankings across desktop and mobile with:

- Daily position updates
- SERP feature tracking (featured snippets, PAA, image packs)
- Competitor comparison (up to 10 competitors per project)
- Share of Voice metric (your visibility as a percentage of total search opportunity)

### Ahrefs Webmaster Tools (Free)

Ahrefs offers free access to Site Audit and Site Explorer for websites you verify ownership of. This is genuinely generous. You get the core audit and backlink data for your own site at zero cost. The limitation: you can only analyze your own domain, not competitors.

## How to Use Ahrefs: The Right 5-Step Workflow

Here's the workflow that experienced Ahrefs users follow. Not the "explore everything" approach that burns time without producing content.

**Step 1: Audit your competitors, not random keywords**
Open Site Explorer. Enter your 3 closest competitors (similar domain size and niche). Look at their "Top Pages" sorted by organic traffic. These are the topics that actually drive results in your market. Not random keyword ideas.

**Step 2: Find the "low-hanging fruit" with Content Gap**
The Content Gap tool shows keywords your competitors rank for but you don't. Filter by KD < 30 and volume > 200. This gives you a realistic target list. Keywords where you can rank without a massive backlink profile.

**Step 3: Check Traffic Potential, not just volume**
For each target keyword, look at the "Traffic potential" metric in Keywords Explorer. A keyword with 500 monthly searches but 3,000 traffic potential to the parent topic is worth more than a keyword with 2,000 searches but 2,000 total traffic potential.

**Step 4: Build content briefs from SERP data**
For each keyword, open the SERP overview. Note the top-5 results: their word count, backlink count, domain rating, and the subheadings they use. Build your brief from this data. Hand it to your writer with specific guidance. Not just "write about this topic."

**Step 5: Track rankings and build links to what's working**
After publishing, track keyword positions in Rank Tracker. For articles that reach positions 5–15 within 60 days, invest in link building (guest posts, digital PR, resource page outreach). Content that doesn't move after 90 days needs rewriting, not more links.

## Ahrefs FAQ

**What is Ahrefs?**
Ahrefs is an SEO toolset built around the internet's largest backlink index (35+ trillion links). It includes Site Explorer (backlink and organic traffic analysis), Keywords Explorer (keyword research across 11 search engines), Site Audit, Rank Tracker, and Content Explorer.

**How much does Ahrefs cost?**
$129/month (Lite), $249/month (Standard), $449/month (Advanced), $1,499/month (Enterprise). Annual billing saves ~16%. Additional users cost $40–$80/month each.

**Is there a free version of Ahrefs?**
Ahrefs Webmaster Tools gives you free access to Site Audit and Site Explorer for domains you verify. You cannot research competitors with the free version. No free trial of paid plans.

**Does Ahrefs write content for you?**
No. Ahrefs is a pure research and analysis tool. It has no AI writing features, no content editor, and no publishing capabilities. You need writers and a separate workflow to create content from Ahrefs data.

**Is Ahrefs worth it in 2026?**
For SEO professionals with content teams: yes. For small business owners without writers: no. Real cost to produce 30 articles using Ahrefs data = $2,529–$7,629/month (Ahrefs + writers + optimization tool).

**What are the best Ahrefs alternatives?**
For SEO research: Semrush ($139/mo, more tools), Moz Pro ($99/mo, beginner-friendly). For done-for-you content: theStacc ($99/mo, 30 articles auto-published monthly).

**Can Ahrefs replace Semrush?**
For backlink analysis and keyword research: yes, Ahrefs matches or exceeds Semrush. For competitive intelligence, PPC data, social media scheduling, and local SEO: no. Semrush has broader functionality. Neither tool writes or publishes content.

**Does Ahrefs do local SEO?**
No. Ahrefs has no local SEO features. No GBP management, no local citations, no review monitoring, no Google Maps tracking. For local businesses, this is a significant gap.

**How does Ahrefs compare to Semrush?**
Ahrefs has a larger, more accurate backlink index and a cleaner UI. Semrush has more tools (50+ vs. ~10), PPC data, social media scheduling, and local SEO add-ons. Ahrefs is better for link builders. Semrush is better for agencies needing everything in one place.

**Is the Ahrefs blog worth reading?**
Yes. It's one of the best SEO education resources on the internet. The content team (led by Tim Soulo) publishes data-driven studies and practical guides that rank for competitive terms. The YouTube channel is equally strong.

**What are Ahrefs' biggest limitations?**
No content creation. No publishing. No free trial. No local SEO. No content optimization scoring. The tool shows you opportunities. Turning those into results requires writers, an editor, an optimization tool, and a publishing workflow.

**Does Ahrefs offer an API?**
Yes. On Advanced ($449/mo) and Enterprise ($1,499/mo) plans. The API provides access to backlink data, keyword data, and rank tracking programmatically.

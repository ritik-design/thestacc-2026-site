## What Is Serpstat?

Serpstat is an all-in-one SEO platform built around keyword research, competitor analysis, site auditing, backlink checking, and rank tracking. It started as an internal tool at Netpeak, a digital marketing agency in Odesa, Ukraine, in 2013. Oleg Salamaha created the original product. Artem Borodatyuk joined to drive expansion. The tool launched as a standalone SaaS product in 2015.

The name combines "SERP" (Search Engine Results Page) + "stat" (statistics). That tells you the core focus: measuring and analyzing search visibility data.

The platform serves a large user base, primarily across Eastern Europe, CIS countries, and North America. Its keyword database covers billions of keywords across 230+ regional Google databases. The backlink index tracks hundreds of billions of links. These numbers are smaller than Semrush and Ahrefs. But they are substantial for most real-world use cases.

What Serpstat is not: a content creation platform, an automated publishing system, or a done-for-you SEO service. It finds keywords, analyzes competitors, audits sites, tracks rankings, and checks backlinks. You still write every article, optimize every page, and publish everything manually.

The company is privately held. It raised $250K in seed funding in 2014 from Digital Future and Netpeak Group. It has remained independent and profitable. The team operates primarily from Ukraine.

## Serpstat Features (2026)

### Keyword Research

Serpstat's keyword research module is its strongest feature. And it is genuinely competitive with tools costing 2–3× more.

Enter a seed keyword and Serpstat returns:

- Search volume with monthly trends and seasonality data
- Keyword difficulty score (0–100) based on backlink profiles of top-ranking pages
- CPC data for paid search planning
- Related keywords, questions, and "People Also Ask" variations
- Keyword clustering using AI-powered semantic grouping
- Tree View for hierarchical content structure planning
- 10 years of historical search volume data

The Tree View is a standout feature. It visualizes keyword relationships in a nested structure. You see which broad topics branch into subtopics, and which subtopics branch into specific long-tail keywords. This makes content planning intuitive. You build topic clusters without spreadsheets.

The AI keyword clustering groups semantically related terms automatically. Instead of manually sorting 200 keywords into clusters, Serpstat does it in seconds. For content teams planning editorial calendars, this saves hours per project.

> **Insight:** Serpstat's keyword difficulty score correlates reasonably well with actual ranking difficulty for low-to-mid competition terms. Where it struggles: high-competition keywords where backlink profiles dominate. The score can underestimate difficulty when the top 10 all have 500+ referring domains.

The honest limitation: Serpstat's keyword database is smaller than Semrush and Ahrefs. For most users, the database is more than sufficient. For enterprise teams researching obscure long-tail terms in competitive niches, you may miss some opportunities that larger indexes surface.

Independent testing places Serpstat's search volume accuracy in the lower midfield among major tools. The data is reliable for trend direction and relative comparison. It is less reliable for precise volume predictions.

### Backlink Analysis

Serpstat's backlink module is its weakest major feature. And the gap versus Ahrefs and Semrush is significant.

The backlink index tracks hundreds of billions of links. For any domain, you get:

- Total backlinks and referring domains with trend graphs
- New and lost backlinks (daily updates)
- Anchor text distribution analysis
- Referring domain authority scores
- Toxic link identification
- Competitor backlink comparison

The practical reality: Serpstat's backlink index is noticeably smaller than Ahrefs and Semrush. In head-to-head comparisons, it finds fewer referring domains than the leading tools. For deep link analysis on competitive domains, you may miss links that Ahrefs catches.

For casual backlink checks and competitor research, the data is adequate. For link building professionals who need the most comprehensive index, Serpstat is not the right tool.

> **Insight:** Serpstat has invested in building its own backlink index in recent years. The index is improving but still trails industry leaders like Ahrefs and Semrush. If backlink analysis is a primary use case, you may want to pair Serpstat with a dedicated backlink tool.

### Site Audit

Serpstat's Site Audit crawls your website and flags technical SEO issues across priority-based categories:

- High-priority errors (broken links, redirect chains, orphan pages)
- Medium-priority warnings (missing meta descriptions, duplicate titles)
- Low-priority notices (minor optimization opportunities)
- SDO Score for overall site health
- Core Web Vitals monitoring
- HTTPS implementation checks
- XML sitemap validation

The audit covers up to 1.5 million pages on Agency plans. The priority-based system is genuinely useful. You fix high-priority issues first. You don't waste time on minor notices while broken links remain unfixed.

The honest caveat: the audit produces more false positives than Semrush or Ahrefs. Some flagged "errors" are not actually problems. This means extra manual verification. You cannot blindly trust every flagged issue.

JavaScript rendering is available on Team+ plans. This is important for modern web apps built with React, Vue, or Angular. Without JS rendering, the audit misses content loaded dynamically.

### Rank Tracker

Serpstat's Rank Tracker monitors your keyword positions with:

- Desktop and mobile ranking tracking
- Local rank tracking by city and region
- Competitor comparison (track your rankings against competitors)
- SERP feature tracking (featured snippets, People Also Ask, image packs)
- Weekly update frequency (standard on all plans)
- Historical position data over 10 years

The rank tracking is reliable. Users report that Serpstat's position data closely matches Google Search Console. The mobile/desktop split is useful. Many pages rank differently on mobile versus desktop.

The limitation: update frequency is weekly on standard plans. Daily updates require higher tiers or add-ons. Semrush and Ahrefs offer daily updates on entry plans. For fast-moving competitive keywords, weekly data may lag.

### Competitor Analysis

Serpstat's competitor analysis module is the most consistently praised feature across user reviews.

For any domain, you get:

- **Organic keywords**. Every keyword a domain ranks for, with position, traffic estimate, and SERP features
- **Paid keywords**. Every keyword a competitor bids on in Google Ads, with ad copy and landing pages
- **Traffic estimates**. Estimated monthly organic traffic with trend graphs
- **Visibility trends**. How a competitor's search presence changes over time
- **Market share analysis**. Your share of voice versus competitors

The competitor analysis covers both organic and paid search. This dual view is valuable. You see not just what ranks organically but what your competitors pay for. If they spend money on a keyword, it has commercial intent.

> **Insight:** Serpstat's traffic estimation accuracy trails behind competitors in independent testing. The competitor analysis is excellent for identifying which keywords competitors target and how their rankings change. It is less reliable for estimating exactly how much traffic they receive.

## How to Use Serpstat: The Right 5-Step Workflow

Most Serpstat users do this wrong. They open Keyword Research, find 200 keywords, create a spreadsheet, then never write a single article. Sound familiar? Here's the workflow that actually produces results.

**Step 1: Run the Site Audit first, not keyword research**
Fix what's broken before building new. A site with crawl errors and broken redirects won't rank no matter how many articles you publish. Clean your technical foundation first. This takes 1–2 hours with Serpstat's audit. Prioritize high-priority errors. Ignore low-priority notices until the serious issues are fixed.

**Step 2: Use Competitor Analysis to find realistic targets**
Don't chase keywords because they have high volume. Enter your 3 closest competitors into Serpstat's Organic Keywords report. Filter for keywords where they rank in positions 4–20. These are the gaps you can actually fill. Keywords where you can realistically compete.

**Step 3: Build topic clusters with Tree View and AI Clustering**
Use Tree View to see the hierarchical structure of keywords in your niche. Then run AI Keyword Clustering to group related terms into content clusters. One pillar page + 5–8 supporting articles per cluster. This builds topical authority faster than scattered one-off posts.

**Step 4: Create content briefs from SERP and competitor data**
For each target keyword, analyze the top 10 results in Serpstat. Note their word counts, heading structures, and the subtopics they cover. Build your brief from this data. Hand it to your writer with specific guidance. Not just "write about this keyword."

**Step 5: Track rankings and audit quarterly**
Set up Rank Tracker for every published article before it goes live. Baseline your position. Review the Performance Index over 90 days. Individual keywords fluctuate. The overall trend tells you whether your strategy is working. Re-run the Site Audit every quarter.

## Serpstat FAQ

**What is Serpstat?**
Serpstat is an all-in-one SEO platform founded in 2013 in Odesa, Ukraine. It includes keyword research, backlink analysis, site audit, rank tracking, and competitor analysis tools. It serves a large user base, primarily across Eastern Europe and North America.

**How much does Serpstat cost?**
$69/month (Individual), $129/month (Team), $299/month (Agency). Enterprise pricing is custom. Annual billing saves approximately 20%. A 7-day free trial is available with no credit card required.

**Is there a free version of Serpstat?**
Serpstat offers a free tier with very limited searches. It is suitable for testing only. Paid plans unlock full functionality.

**Does Serpstat write content for you?**
No. Serpstat is a pure research and analysis tool. It has no AI writing features, no content editor, and no publishing capabilities. You need writers and a separate workflow to create content from Serpstat data.

**Is Serpstat worth it in 2026?**
For freelancers, small agencies, and budget-conscious teams: yes. It is the best-value all-in-one SEO suite at this price point. For content teams publishing 20+ articles per month or enterprises needing the deepest data: probably not. The real cost to publish content at scale far exceeds the subscription price.

For SEO research: Semrush ($139/mo), Ahrefs ($129/mo), SE Ranking ($65/mo), Mangools ($49/mo). For done-for-you content: theStacc ($99/mo, 30 articles auto-published monthly).

**Can Serpstat replace Semrush or Ahrefs?**
For keyword research and competitor analysis at a budget price: yes, Serpstat covers the essentials. For technical SEO audits, comprehensive backlink analysis, and content marketing tools: no. Semrush and Ahrefs are significantly more powerful. Serpstat is a value alternative, not a direct replacement.

**Does Serpstat do local SEO?**
No. Serpstat has no local SEO toolkit. It does not manage Google Business Profiles, build citations, monitor reviews, or track local pack rankings. For full local SEO, you need additional tools.

No content creation or publishing. Smaller backlink index than Ahrefs/Semrush. Traffic estimation accuracy is the lowest among major tools. Site audit produces false positives. English-only interface. No local SEO features. No content optimization scoring.

**Is Serpstat good for beginners?**
Yes. It is one of the best SEO tools for beginners. The interface is cleaner than Semrush. The learning curve is measured in hours, not weeks. The Tree View makes content planning intuitive. Most new users find actionable keywords within their first session.

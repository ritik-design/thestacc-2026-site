## What Is Anyword?

Anyword is an AI-powered copywriting platform that predicts marketing performance. It generates short-form copy. Ad headlines, email subject lines, social media captions, landing page CTAs, product descriptions. And assigns each piece a score predicting how well it will convert.

The core mechanism: Anyword's models were trained on billions of real marketing data points from A/B-tested campaigns across Meta Ads, Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads, and email platforms. When you generate copy, Anyword compares your text against patterns from 22,560+ similar texts in your industry and channel. The result is a Predictive Performance Score from 0–100 that claims 82% accuracy in predicting engagement.

What Anyword is *not*: an SEO platform, a publishing tool, or a done-for-you service. It doesn't research keywords, analyze SERPs, optimize content for search rankings, or publish anything to your website. It generates and scores marketing copy. What you do with that copy. Optimize it for SEO, format it, upload it, publish it. Is up to you.

That distinction matters more than most reviews let on. We'll come back to it in the pricing section with the math.

### Company Background

Anyword was founded in 2013 as Keywee Inc. by Yaniv Makover (CEO), along with co-founders including Omer Artun and Adam Habari. The company was originally focused on content distribution and optimization for publishers, with clients like The New York Times, CNN, and NBC.

In September 2019, Keywee rebranded to Anyword, pivoting from publisher-focused analytics to AI-driven copywriting for marketers. The Series A in March 2015 raised $9.1M led by Innovation Endeavors (Eric Schmidt's fund). A Series B in November 2021 brought in another $21M, also led by Innovation Endeavors. Total funding: approximately $30.1M.

Current status: ~$100M ARR (reported October 2025), ~512 employees, and notable customers including Red Bull, BBC, NBA, Ted Baker, and PetCareRx. The New York Times is both an investor and a customer.

## Anyword Features (2026)

### Predictive Performance Score

The signature feature and the reason most buyers consider Anyword. Every piece of copy generated gets a score from 0–100.

| Score Range | Performance Tier |
|-------------|------------------|
| 0–40 | Below Average |
| 41–60 | Average |
| 61–80 | Above Average |
| 81–100 | Top Tier |

The score is based on:
- Billions of real marketing data points from A/B-tested campaigns
- Your specific industry, channel, and target audience
- Historical performance across 22,560+ similar texts

Anyword claims 82% accuracy in predicting engagement, compared to approximately 52% for generic models like GPT-4o.

On the Data-Driven plan ($99/month) and above, the scoring updates in real-time as you manually edit copy. On the Starter plan ($49/month), you get 50 predictions per month. Which exhausts quickly if you're testing multiple ad variations.

> **The insight most reviews miss:** The Predictive Performance Score is directional, not precise. It's genuinely useful for narrowing 20 headline variations down to 5 strong candidates. But users report that scores don't always correlate with actual campaign results. Treat it as a filtering signal, not a guarantee.

### Copy Intelligence

Copy Intelligence is Anyword's learning platform that connects to your live marketing channels:

- **Meta Ads, Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads:** Analyzes your best-performing content to identify winning messaging patterns
- **Website personalization:** Scans landing pages and suggests CRO improvements
- **Automated A/B testing:** Runs tests without manual intervention and finds the best-converting copy for each audience segment

The feature was significantly enhanced in 2025–2026 with expanded website and social media content analysis. It creates a feedback loop: your campaign data trains the model, which produces better predictions, which improve your campaigns.

Copy Intelligence is available on the Data-Driven plan and above. Starter plan users don't get channel connections or historical data analysis.

### Brand Voice

Anyword's Brand Voice system trains the AI on your previous high-performing content:

- Upload sample content your brand has already published
- Define tone, style, terminology, and words to avoid
- Create audience personas with data-driven insights
- Connect CRM data (Salesforce/HubSpot) to analyze language that resonates with highest-value customers

The Pro plan includes 1 Brand Voice. Data-Driven and above include more. For teams managing multiple brands or clients, the voice limit on lower tiers is a real constraint.

The quality of Brand Voice output depends heavily on the training data you provide. Teams that feed Anyword 10–15 polished, representative content samples get measurably better output than teams that upload one blog post and call it done.

### Blog Wizard

A guided 6-step workflow for long-form content:

1. Set target word count
2. Define target audience
3. Add SEO keywords
4. Generate a scored outline before the full draft
5. AI builds out the complete article
6. Includes a plagiarism checker

The Blog Wizard is functional for first drafts, but the output quality drops noticeably on articles over 1,000 words. Anyword is built for short-form marketing copy. Long-form blog content is a secondary feature, and it shows. Generated articles read competently but lack the depth, specific angle, and original insight needed to rank for competitive keywords.

Importantly, the Blog Wizard does not include SERP analysis, competitor content analysis, or content optimization scoring. You get a draft. Not an optimized draft.

### Ad Copy for Multiple Channels

Anyword generates copy for:
- Meta/Facebook Ads (headlines, descriptions, primary text)
- Google Ads (headlines, descriptions, responsive search ads)
- LinkedIn Ads (sponsored content, message ads)
- Email subject lines and body copy
- Landing page headlines and CTAs
- Product descriptions
- Social media captions (Instagram, X, Facebook)

The channel-specific generation is where Anyword shines. Each format gets structured output that matches the platform's constraints. Meta Ads get headline-character limits respected. Google Ads get responsive format variations. This is genuinely better than generic AI output that ignores platform requirements.

### Chrome Extension

The Anyword Chrome extension brings predictive scoring and generation directly into Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads, LinkedIn Campaign Manager, and email platforms. Generate and score copy without leaving your ad platform. For performance marketers who live in these tools, this is a quality-of-life feature that consistently gets called out positively in reviews.

### API Access

Full API access is available on Business and Enterprise plans. Use cases include:
- Custom workflow integration
- Bulk copy generation at scale
- Connecting Anyword to proprietary marketing stacks
- Custom AI model training on proprietary data (Enterprise)

Standard integrations include HubSpot, WordPress, Notion, Google Ads, Meta Ads Manager, and LinkedIn Ads.

## How to Use Anyword: The Right 5-Step Workflow

Most new Anyword users open the generator, type a topic, hit generate, and publish whatever scores highest. That produces generic copy that doesn't convert and doesn't rank. Here's the workflow that actually works:

**Step 1: Set up Brand Voice before generating anything**
Upload 10–15 of your strongest existing pieces of content. Ad headlines that performed well, email subject lines with high open rates, landing page copy that converted. Give Anyword at least 2,000 words of representative samples across multiple formats. Do not skip this step. Generating copy before Brand Voice is trained produces output that any experienced marketer would immediately identify as AI-written.

**Step 2: Connect your marketing channels for Copy Intelligence**
Link Meta Ads, Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads, and your email platform. This is only available on Data-Driven and above, and it's where Anyword's real value lives. Without channel connections, you're getting generic predictions. With them, Anyword learns from your actual performance data.

**Step 3: Generate 15–20 variations, then filter by score + instinct**
Don't generate 3 variations and pick the highest score. Generate 15–20. Filter out anything below 60. Then read the remaining options manually. The Predictive Performance Score is a filter, not a replacement for human judgment. The highest-scoring headline isn't always the one that fits your brand voice or campaign context.

**Step 4: A/B test the top 3–5 options**
Anyword's score predicts performance, but real campaigns have variables the model can't see: audience segment, timing, creative pairing, offer strength. Run your top-scoring variants against each other in actual campaigns. Use the results to feed back into Copy Intelligence.

**Step 5: For blog content, run the output through an SEO tool before publishing**
Anyword's Blog Wizard generates drafts without SERP analysis or optimization. Before publishing any blog post, run it through Surfer SEO, Clearscope, or Frase. Add missing NLP terms. Adjust structure to match top-ranking pages. This step is not optional if you want rankings.

## Does Anyword Actually Work? What the Data Says

Anyword's effectiveness depends heavily on what you're measuring.

For short-form marketing copy. Ad headlines, email subject lines, social captions, landing page CTAs. The Predictive Performance Score is genuinely useful. G2 data shows that marketers using Anyword report reducing A/B test cycles by 40–60%. Instead of testing 10 variations to find a winner, they test 3–4 high-scoring options.

The 82% accuracy claim is directionally supported by user reports. Performance marketers consistently say the score correlates with real results well enough to be a useful filter. Not perfect. But better than guessing.

For long-form SEO content, the picture is different. Anyword generates drafts that cover topics broadly but rarely with the depth or specific angle needed to rank for competitive keywords. Without SERP analysis, competitor content analysis, or optimization scoring, published Anyword blog content consistently underperforms on organic search against human-written, optimized content.

Enterprise validation is real: Red Bull, BBC, NBA, and Ted Baker have all been cited as Anyword customers. These are companies with large marketing budgets, dedicated performance teams, and the internal staff to use Anyword as a force multiplier. Their continued use validates the ad copy and email use case. Not the "business owner doing SEO alone" use case.

> **The ChatGPT question every buyer asks:** Is Anyword meaningfully better than just using ChatGPT for ad copy? For solo users with no brand voice training and no channel data, the honest answer is: for many use cases, no. Anyword's differentiation lives in the predictive scoring layer and the Copy Intelligence feedback loop. If you're a single user with no historical campaign data, a $20/month ChatGPT Plus subscription covers most of the same ground. Anyword earns its cost for teams with data, not for individuals starting from zero.

## Anyword FAQ

**What is Anyword?**
Anyword is an AI-powered copywriting platform that predicts marketing performance. It generates short-form copy (ads, emails, social captions, landing page CTAs) and assigns each piece a Predictive Performance Score from 0–100 based on billions of real marketing data points. Founded in 2013 as Keywee Inc. by Yaniv Makover, it rebranded to Anyword in 2019.

**How much does Anyword cost?**
Starter: $49/month ($39/month on annual). Data-Driven: $99/month ($79/month on annual). Business and Enterprise: custom pricing with ~30% annual discount. Additional seats: $49–$59/month each. A 7-day free trial is available with no credit card required.

**Does Anyword have a free trial?**
Yes. Anyword offers a 7-day free trial with no credit card required. The trial includes limited predictions and 2,500 word credits to test the platform.

**What is the Predictive Performance Score?**
Anyword's signature feature. Every piece of copy gets a score from 0–100 predicting how well it will perform based on billions of A/B-tested marketing data points. Anyword claims 82% accuracy. Available on all plans, but limited to 50 predictions/month on Starter.

**Does Anyword write SEO content?**
Anyword generates text but has no SEO features. No keyword research, no SERP analysis, no content optimization scoring, no competitor analysis. The Blog Wizard produces drafts that need manual SEO optimization with a separate tool before they have ranking potential.

**Is Anyword better than Jasper?**
Different strengths. Anyword is better for performance marketers who want predictive scoring for ad copy and emails. Jasper is better for brand consistency across large teams and long-form content. Anyword has no SEO features; Jasper integrates with Surfer SEO. Neither auto-publishes to your website.

**Is Anyword worth it in 2026?**
For performance marketing teams running paid ads: yes. The predictive scoring is unique and genuinely useful. For SEO content at scale: no. Anyword has zero SEO capabilities and no publishing automation. For solo users without campaign data: probably not. ChatGPT Plus covers most of the same ground at $20/month.

**Does Anyword publish to WordPress?**
No. Anyword has no direct CMS integration. It offers a WordPress integration via standard connections, but auto-publishing is not available. You copy-paste output into your CMS and format/publish manually.

**What are the best Anyword alternatives?**
For AI writing: Jasper ($59/seat/mo), Writesonic ($49/mo), Copy.ai ($49/mo). For SEO content: Surfer SEO ($99/mo), Frase ($49/mo). For done-for-you SEO publishing: [theStacc](/pricing) ($99/mo, 30 articles auto-published).

**Does Anyword do local SEO?**
No. Anyword has zero local SEO features. No Google Business Profile management, no GBP post generation, no local citation tools, and no Google Maps ranking features.

**How accurate is Anyword's Predictive Performance Score?**
Anyword claims 82% accuracy in predicting engagement. User reports confirm the score is directionally useful for filtering copy variations, but it's not precise. High-scoring copy sometimes underperforms, and mid-scoring copy sometimes wins. Treat it as a filtering signal, not a guarantee.

**What is Anyword's Copy Intelligence?**
A learning platform that connects to your live marketing channels (Meta Ads, Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads, email) and analyzes your best-performing content. It identifies winning messaging patterns and incorporates them into new copy variations. Available on Data-Driven plans and above.

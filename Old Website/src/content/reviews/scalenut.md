## What Is Scalenut?

Scalenut is an AI SEO content platform that combines keyword research, content briefs, AI writing, and on-page optimization into a single workflow. The core idea: instead of running 3–5 separate tools, you move from keyword input to published, optimized article entirely inside one product.

The company was founded in 2020 in Gurugram, India by Gaurav Goyal, Mayank Jain, and Saurabh Wadhawan. All with backgrounds in content marketing and technology. Scalenut raised $3.53M across two seed rounds (backed by Titan Capital and Maple Capital), scaled to approximately $10M ARR, and grew to 80+ employees at its peak before settling to around 30–60 in 2025.

The platform's flagship feature. Cruise Mode. Is a step-by-step guided article creation flow that takes you from keyword to drafted, optimized article in approximately 5 minutes of active input. This structured approach distinguished Scalenut from more freeform AI writers like Jasper or Writesonic.

In 2024, Scalenut expanded into AI Visibility tracking (monitoring brand mentions in ChatGPT and Google AI Overviews), positioning the platform as a more complete SEO intelligence tool rather than purely a content creation product.

What Scalenut is not: a done-for-you service. Cruise Mode is guided and structured. It still requires a human at the keyboard to make decisions at each step. The output is a draft, not a publish-ready article. You (or your editor) still clean up the writing, verify facts, add original insights, and publish to your CMS.

## Scalenut Features (2026)

### Cruise Mode

Cruise Mode is the reason most people choose Scalenut. It breaks article creation into five distinct stages, each with a clear action and AI-assisted output:

**Step 1. Keyword input:** Enter your target keyword and select your target country. Scalenut runs SERP analysis on the top-ranking pages.

**Step 2. Title selection:** Choose or edit from generated title options based on competitor patterns.

**Step 3. Outline generation:** Scalenut generates a suggested outline pulled from competitor content. You can accept, edit, or reorganize sections before writing.

**Step 4. Section-by-section writing:** For each section, you click to generate AI content. You can regenerate, expand, or edit each block individually.

**Step 5. Optimization pass:** Scalenut scores your draft against NLP terms from competitor content and flags missing terms. You address the gaps before export.

The full process takes 5–15 minutes of active input for a 1,500-word article. That's genuinely faster than any competing tool's workflow. The structured guidance also makes it less intimidating for content teams learning SEO writing. Each step is discrete and manageable.

The honest limitation: the output from Step 4 is AI-generated at its most visible. The content tends toward filler sentences and loose structure. Plan on 45–90 minutes of editing for a typical 1,500-word article to bring it to publishable quality. Cruise Mode is an efficiency tool, not a replacement for a writer.

> **The All-in-One Trap:** When one platform covers keyword research, writing, and optimization, it's easy to assume each layer is the best available. It isn't. Surfer's optimizer has a higher documented ranking correlation. Ahrefs/Semrush produce more accurate keyword data. Koala AI produces more consistent long-form writing. Scalenut bundles all three at an attractive price. But bundling means compromise at each layer. Teams that care about ranking outcomes often get better results pairing best-in-class tools at each stage.

### SEO Hub / Content Optimizer

Scalenut's Content Optimizer gives you an NLP-based real-time scoring system similar to Surfer SEO's Content Editor. As you write, a score updates based on how well your content matches the term patterns from the top 10 ranking pages for your keyword.

Key features:
- NLP term recommendations extracted from SERP competitors
- Real-time SEO score
- Keyword density analysis
- Competitor content comparison across the top 10 results
- On-Page Pro. An advanced on-page recommendations module added in 2024

The optimizer is functional and comparable to mid-tier alternatives like Frase. It doesn't match Surfer's scoring accuracy or interface quality, but at Scalenut's pricing it's a credible tool for teams without a dedicated Surfer subscription.

### Keyword Planner

Scalenut's Keyword Planner provides keyword research with volume, KD, CPC data, and topic cluster generation. The Semrush data integration (available across plans) is the highlight here. It pipes Semrush's keyword dataset into Scalenut's interface, which gives you access to professional-grade keyword data without paying Semrush's standalone price ($119–$229/month).

The limitation: the integration is read-only and supplemental. For serious keyword research. Competitive gap analysis, backlink-informed difficulty scoring, keyword opportunity mapping. You'd still want a standalone tool. Scalenut's clustering logic is useful for generating content calendar ideas but not a substitute for dedicated keyword strategy work.

### Content Audit

Scalenut's Content Audit module scans your existing published pages, scores them against current SERP benchmarks, identifies content decay, and surfaces opportunities for refreshes.

The Plus plan covers 200 pages/month. The Professional plan covers 1,000 pages/month. Cannibalization analysis (identifying keyword overlap between pages) is Professional-only.

For teams with an existing content library that's starting to lose rankings, the Content Audit is a practical tool. It's not as detailed as Surfer's audit, but it covers the core use case at a lower price point.

### AI Visibility Tracking

Added in 2024, this feature monitors how often your brand and content appear in AI-generated answers from ChatGPT and Google AI Overviews. The Starter plan tracks 10 prompts weekly; the Professional plan covers 100.

It's a directionally useful feature as AI search grows. The data isn't actionable for most SMBs yet. There's no clear way to optimize for AI citations the way you optimize for Google rankings. But it positions Scalenut ahead of tools that haven't added any AI search monitoring.

### Internal Linking + Auto-Publish

Internal link suggestions (available on Plus+) recommend contextually relevant links between your existing pages. Available on Plus and Professional plans only.

Auto-publish directly to WordPress is available on Plus+ plans. This is one of Scalenut's underrated advantages over tools like Surfer or Frase. It completes more of the workflow loop, getting from draft to CMS without a manual export step.

## How to Use Scalenut: The 6-Step Workflow

Most new users start Cruise Mode, take the outline it generates at face value, and publish the resulting draft with minimal editing. That's the fastest path to disappointing results. Here's how experienced users actually run the workflow:

**Step 1. Validate the keyword in the Keyword Planner first**
Before launching Cruise Mode, confirm your target keyword has realistic volume and KD for your domain authority. Scalenut's planner with Semrush data gives you enough signal. Don't build a 1,500-word article around a keyword your domain can't compete for.

**Step 2. Review the SERP competitors before accepting the outline**
Cruise Mode generates an outline based on competitor content. Read the actual top 3 ranking pages for your keyword before accepting it. The AI outline often averages competitor structures. It doesn't identify what's *missing* from them, which is where your differentiation opportunity lives.

**Step 3. Add your unique angle to the outline before writing**
Edit the Cruise Mode outline before clicking generate. Add a section no competitor covers. Change a generic section header to something more specific. The AI will write to whatever structure you give it. Better structure in means better content out.

**Step 4. Generate section by section, not the full article at once**
Generate one section, review and edit it, then generate the next. Generating the full article at once gives you a wall of AI text with no natural edit checkpoints. Section-by-section keeps the output manageable.

**Step 5. Do the optimization pass last, not as you write**
Run the NLP term optimization check after you've finished editing. Not during. Inserting terms mid-draft interrupts your editing flow and often results in unnatural phrasing. Treat it as a final checklist, not a live constraint.

**Step 6. Use the auto-publish to WordPress, not manual export**
If you're on Plus+, use the WordPress auto-publish rather than copying and pasting. It preserves formatting and saves 10–15 minutes per article. At 30 articles/month, that's 5–7 hours saved.

## What Is LongShot?

LongShot AI is an AI-powered long-form content generation platform with a built-in fact-checking layer. It generates blog posts, articles, and SEO content using a structured workflow — headline, outline, full article — with real-time claim verification via its FactGPT feature.

The core mechanism: you enter a topic or keyword, LongShot researches relevant information, generates a structured outline, and produces a full-length draft. Along the way, FactGPT cross-references factual claims against live sources and provides citations. The result is AI-generated content with fewer hallucinations than raw GPT output.

What LongShot is *not*: a publishing platform, a human editing service, an SEO agency, or a done-for-you content operation. It produces drafts. Your team still reviews every piece, edits for voice and accuracy, optimizes it for SEO (which may require a separate tool like Surfer SEO), and publishes it through your existing CMS workflow. Every step after the draft is still manual.

That distinction matters more than most reviews let on. We'll come back to it in the pricing section with the math.

## LongShot Features (2026)

### FactGPT — Real-Time Fact Verification

FactGPT is LongShot's signature feature and the main reason users choose it over cheaper AI writers.

Here's how it works: when LongShot generates content containing factual claims — dates, statistics, named entities, research findings — FactGPT cross-references those claims against credible, up-to-date sources in real time. It then provides inline citations linking to the source material. If a claim cannot be verified, FactGPT flags it for manual review.

The practical reality: FactGPT reduces hallucinations significantly compared to raw GPT-4 output, especially for data-driven topics like health, technology, finance, and news. Users in health and tech sectors report up to **70% less time** on post-generation fact-checking. It is not perfect — it can miss nuanced claims, struggle with very recent events, and occasionally cite sources that don't fully support the statement. But it is meaningfully better than no fact-checking at all.

> **The FactGPT insight most reviews miss:** FactGPT works best on objective, verifiable claims. It struggles with subjective opinions, trend predictions, and nuanced industry analysis. For thought leadership content, you still need a human editor to verify everything. Treat FactGPT as a first-pass filter, not a replacement for editorial review.

FactGPT also supports multi-turn dialogue. You can ask follow-up questions about verified facts, request additional sources, or drill deeper into a specific claim without leaving the interface.

### Blog Workflow System

LongShot uses a structured, step-by-step content creation process designed to guide users from idea to draft:

1. **Headline Generation** — AI suggests multiple optimized headlines based on your topic and target keyword
2. **Outline Builder** — Creates a structured article outline with H2s and H3s
3. **Full Article Generation** — Produces the complete blog post with SEO optimization built in

Beyond the basic workflow, LongShot offers:

- **27+ article workflows** for specific content types: how-to guides, listicles, pillar posts, product comparisons, product reviews, landing pages
- **50+ short-form templates** for various content needs including AIDA, PAS, BAB, and FAB frameworks

- **AI Interlinking** — automatically suggests internal links and anchor text from your sitemap

The workflow is genuinely beginner-friendly. New users can produce a full draft in 10–15 minutes without prior AI writing experience. The blog creation wizard holds your hand through each step.

## How to Use LongShot: The Right 5-Step Workflow

Most new users open LongShot, enter a keyword, and accept the first draft it produces. The result is generic content that needs heavy editing and often misses the mark on voice and depth.

1. **Research your topic before opening LongShot.** Use Semrush, Ahrefs, or Google Search Console to understand search intent, competitor content depth, and the specific questions your article needs to answer. LongShot's output quality is directly proportional to the quality of your input.

2. **Use the outline builder as a negotiation, not a mandate.** Review the AI-generated outline carefully. Add subtopics competitors cover that LongShot missed. Reorder sections for narrative flow. The outline is the skeleton — get it right before generating the full article.

3. **Generate the draft, then run FactGPT immediately.** Don't wait until editing. Run fact-checking right after generation while the claims are fresh in your mind. Verify every citation. FactGPT is good but not infallible — especially on recent data and niche industry claims.

4. **Edit for voice, depth, and originality.** LongShot produces competent first drafts. They are not publishable as-is. Add personal anecdotes, unique insights, original research, and your brand's perspective. This is what separates ranking content from AI-generated filler.

5. **Run SEO optimization and plagiarism checks before exporting.** Use LongShot's semantic SEO suggestions, then run a separate check with Surfer SEO or Clearscope if you have access. Run the plagiarism checker. Only then export to WordPress or your CMS.

> **Common mistake:** Treating LongShot output as "done" after generation. Even with FactGPT, every draft needs human editing. Budget 30–60 minutes of editing time per 1,500-word article.

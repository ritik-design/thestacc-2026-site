## What Is Mangools?

Mangools is a 5-tool SEO suite built for beginners, bloggers, and small agencies. It started as KWFinder in August 2014, created by Peter Hrbáčik in Bratislava, Slovakia. The Mangools brand launched in May 2016 as a unified platform. Today it serves 1.1 million+ registered users across 160+ countries.

The company is bootstrapped. No venture capital. No investors. No IPO. The team is under 40 people. The company has attracted users from a range of businesses, though its core audience remains solo operators, bloggers, and small agencies.

The core product includes five tools: KWFinder (keyword research), SERPChecker (SERP analysis), SERPWatcher (rank tracking), LinkMiner (backlink analysis), and SiteProfiler (domain metrics). Each tool operates as a separate web app. You switch between tabs rather than working from a single dashboard.

What Mangools is not: a content creation platform, an automated publishing system, a technical SEO crawler, or a done-for-you SEO service. It finds keywords, analyzes SERPs, tracks rankings, checks backlinks, and profiles domains. You still write every article, optimize every page, and publish everything manually.

## Mangools Features (2026)

### KWFinder

KWFinder is the flagship tool and the reason most people subscribe to Mangools. It does three things exceptionally well.

First, it finds low-competition keywords fast. Enter a seed keyword and KWFinder returns hundreds of related terms with difficulty scores, search volume, and CPC data. The color-coded difficulty meter — green for easy, yellow for medium, red for hard — makes prioritization instant. No training required.

Second, it shows the exact SERP for every keyword. You see the top 10 results with their Domain Authority, page authority, backlink count, and Facebook shares. This tells you at a glance whether you can realistically rank. If position 1 has DA 85 and 2,000 backlinks, you know to skip it.

Third, it handles local keyword research better than most tools at this price. Select any country, state, or city. KWFinder returns localized volume and difficulty data. For local service businesses, this is genuinely useful.

> **Insight:** KWFinder's keyword difficulty score correlates well with actual ranking difficulty for low-to-mid competition terms. Where it struggles: high-competition keywords where backlink profiles dominate. The score can underestimate difficulty for terms where the top 10 all have 500+ referring domains.

The database covers 2.5 billion+ keywords. That's smaller than Semrush's 26 billion or Ahrefs' comparable index. For most users, it is enough. For enterprise SEO teams researching obscure long-tail terms, it is not.

### SERPChecker

SERPChecker analyzes the search results page for any keyword using 49+ SEO metrics. Enter a keyword and you get:

- Top 10 results with title, URL, and meta description
- Domain Authority and Page Authority scores
- Backlink counts and referring domains
- Facebook engagement metrics
- SERP feature detection (featured snippets, People Also Ask, image packs, video results)
- Historical SERP volatility tracking

The practical use case: before targeting any keyword, you verify whether the current results are beatable. If every result is a major publication with DA 70+, you pivot. If results are forums, thin content, or small blogs, you proceed.

SERPChecker also includes a Local SERP feature. See how results differ by city or region. Useful for businesses targeting multiple service areas.

The limitation: SERPChecker does not show content analysis. You cannot see word counts, heading structures, or topical coverage of ranking pages. For that, you need Surfer SEO or Clearscope.

### SiteProfiler

SiteProfiler gives you a high-level overview of any domain's SEO health. Enter a URL and get:

- Domain Authority and Page Authority (powered by Moz data)
- Total backlinks and referring domains
- Top content by social shares
- Competitor overlap analysis
- Website preview and category classification

Think of it as a lightweight version of Ahrefs Site Explorer. You get the headline numbers without the depth. For quick competitor checks during client pitches or prospecting, it is genuinely useful. For deep competitive analysis, you need a more powerful tool.

SiteProfiler pulls backlink data from LinkMiner. The backlink index is smaller than Ahrefs or Semrush. You will find fewer total links. For most small sites and local businesses, the data is sufficient. For link building campaigns at scale, it is not.

### LinkMiner

LinkMiner is Mangools' backlink checker. It analyzes the backlink profile of any domain with:

- Total backlinks and referring domains
- Citation Flow and Trust Flow metrics (powered by Majestic data)
- Dofollow vs. nofollow breakdown
- Link strength scoring
- Historical link growth and loss tracking

The standout feature: link preview. Hover over any backlink and LinkMiner shows a screenshot of the linking page with the anchor text highlighted. This saves enormous time during link prospecting. You see context instantly without opening 50 browser tabs.

The honest limitation: LinkMiner's backlink database is smaller than Ahrefs (35T+ links) and Semrush (43T+ links). Mangools reports 9.5 trillion backlinks indexed. For deep link analysis on competitive domains, you will miss links that Ahrefs catches. For casual backlink checks and competitor research, the data is adequate.

### SERPWatcher

SERPWatcher tracks your keyword rankings with daily updates. Key features:

- Desktop and mobile ranking tracking
- Local rank tracking by city
- Competitor comparison (track your rankings against competitors side by side)
- Performance Index: a weighted visibility score that accounts for position and search volume
- Ranking distribution charts (positions 1–3, 4–10, 11–20, etc.)
- Scheduled email reports

The Performance Index is genuinely useful. Instead of obsessing over individual keyword movements, you watch one number that represents your overall organic visibility. If your Performance Index rises 15% this month, your SEO is working. If it drops, you investigate.

Plan limits apply: Basic tracks 200 keywords, Premium tracks 700, Agency tracks 1,500. For a single business website, 200 keywords is plenty. For agencies managing multiple clients, you need Premium or Agency.

SERPWatcher does not track SERP features (featured snippets, People Also Ask) with the granularity of Semrush or Ahrefs Position Tracking. It tells you your position. It does not tell you which SERP features you own or lost.

## How to Use Mangools: The Right 5-Step Workflow

Most Mangools users do this wrong. They open KWFinder, find 100 keywords, create a spreadsheet, then never write a single article. Sound familiar? Here's the workflow that actually produces results.

**Step 1: Use KWFinder to find "question keywords" in your niche**
Filter by "Questions" instead of "Suggestions." Question keywords ("how to," "what is," "why does") have lower competition and higher conversion intent. Target these first before chasing head terms.

**Step 2: Verify competition with SERPChecker before committing**
For every keyword with KD under 40, open SERPChecker. Check the Domain Authority of the top 3 results. If all three have DA 60+ and you have DA under 30, skip it. Find keywords where at least one result has DA under 40. Those are your realistic targets.

**Step 3: Group keywords into topic clusters, not isolated targets**
Use KWFinder's "Autocomplete" and "Questions" modes together. Find 5–8 related keywords around one core topic. Write one comprehensive pillar article that covers all of them. This builds topical authority faster than 8 scattered 500-word posts.

**Step 4: Track rankings in SERPWatcher from day one**
Set up tracking for every target keyword before you publish. Baseline your current position (usually not ranking). Watch the Performance Index over 90 days. Individual keywords fluctuate. The index tells you whether your strategy is working overall.

**Step 5: Check competitor backlinks monthly with LinkMiner**
Enter your top 3 competitors into LinkMiner monthly. Look for new backlinks they earned. Identify which sites link to them but not to you. These are your link building prospects. The link preview feature shows you exactly what context the link appears in.

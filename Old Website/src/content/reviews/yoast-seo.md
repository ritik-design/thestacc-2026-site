## What Is Yoast SEO?

Yoast SEO is a WordPress plugin that helps site owners optimize their pages for search engines directly from the WordPress dashboard. It analyzes your content in real time as you write and gives you a traffic light score. Green means optimized, orange means needs work, red means significant gaps.

The core engine evaluates two things simultaneously:

- **SEO analysis**. Focus keyword placement, meta title and description length, URL structure, internal linking, image alt text, outbound links, and keyphrase distribution.
- **Readability analysis**. Sentence length, paragraph length, subheading distribution, transition words, passive voice usage, and consecutive sentences.

Together these produce the score that has made Yoast famous. A simple visual system that tells non-SEO-experts whether their page is ready to publish.

What Yoast is *not*: a writing tool, a publishing platform, a keyword research tool, a backlink analyzer, a local SEO service, or a content automation system. It optimizes content you have already written. It does not create content for you. It does not find keywords for you. It does not build links for you.

Yoast was founded in May 2010 by Joost de Valk in Wijchen, Netherlands. The name "Yoast" came from how English speakers mispronounced "Joost." Rand Fishkin famously described it as "toast with a Y." Before founding the company, Joost worked as an SEO consultant for eBay and KLM. He started yoast.com in 2008 to write about WordPress SEO and had been developing small SEO plugins as a hobby since 2006.

The plugin launched in October 2010 as "WordPress SEO" and was later renamed Yoast SEO. The company grew from one person to 120+ employees by 2021. In August 2021, Yoast was acquired by Newfold Digital (the parent company of Bluehost, HostGator, and other hosting brands). The team, brand, and headquarters in Wijchen remained intact. In 2023, Joost de Valk, Marieke van de Rakt, and Thijs de Valk departed the company. Current leadership is Chaya Oosterbroek (SVP and GM).

Yoast's mission is "SEO for everyone." The explicit goal is democratizing search optimization so small businesses can compete with large corporations.

## Yoast SEO Features (2026)

### SEO Analysis

The SEO Analysis is Yoast's flagship feature and the reason 12 million people have installed it. As you write in the WordPress editor, Yoast evaluates your content against a checklist of on-page SEO factors.

Here's exactly what it checks:

- Is your focus keyphrase in the SEO title?
- Is your focus keyphrase in the meta description?
- Is your focus keyphrase in the first paragraph?
- Is your focus keyphrase in the URL?
- Is your focus keyphrase in at least one subheading?
- Is your focus keyphrase distributed throughout the text?
- Do you have internal links to other pages on your site?
- Do you have outbound links to credible external sources?
- Are your images using alt text?
- Is your meta description the right length (120–156 characters)?
- Is your SEO title the right length?

The free version allows 1 focus keyphrase per post. Yoast SEO Premium allows up to 5 focus keyphrases per post with synonyms and related keyphrases.

The practical reality: this checklist catches genuine on-page SEO gaps that beginners commonly miss. Missing meta descriptions, images without alt text, no internal links. These are table-stakes errors that hurt rankings, and Yoast surfaces them automatically.

The limitation: the analysis is rules-based, not competitive. It checks whether you have followed best practices. It does not check whether your content is better than the pages currently ranking on page 1. That requires a tool like Surfer SEO or Clearscope. Or a done-for-you platform like theStacc that researches, writes, and optimizes against live SERP data automatically.

> **Insight:** Yoast's SEO analysis tells you whether your page is technically optimized. It does not tell you whether your page can outrank competitors. Those are different questions. Technical optimization is necessary but not sufficient for rankings.

### Readability Analysis

The Readability Analysis is where Yoast genuinely separates from competitors. No other WordPress SEO plugin invests this heavily in content quality scoring.

Yoast evaluates:

- **Sentence length**. Marks sentences over 20 words as too long.
- **Paragraph length**. Flags paragraphs over 150 words.
- **Subheading distribution**. Checks that you use subheadings every 300 words.
- **Transition words**. Measures whether you use words like "however," "therefore," "additionally" to connect ideas.
- **Passive voice**. Flags sentences where the subject receives the action rather than performing it.
- **Consecutive sentences**. Warns when three or more sentences start with the same word.
- **Flesch Reading Ease score**. Calculates how easy your text is to read on a 0–100 scale.

The result is a readability score with the same traffic light system. Green means your content is easy to read. Red means it is too dense.

This feature is genuinely valuable for content teams. Writers often produce dense, jargon-heavy text that ranks poorly because users bounce. Yoast's readability checks train writers to write for humans first. Which is also what Google rewards.

The free version includes full readability analysis. This is one of the few features Yoast does not lock behind Premium.

### Schema Markup

Yoast generates structured data (schema.org markup) for your pages automatically. The free version includes basic schema types:

- Article schema for blog posts
- WebPage schema for pages
- BreadcrumbList schema for navigation
- Organization or Person schema for site identity
- Sitelinks searchbox schema

Yoast SEO Premium adds more advanced schema including FAQ schema, How-To schema, and Course schema. The schema is output as JSON-LD. The format Google prefers.

The implementation is reliable and widely tested across 12 million+ sites. Yoast's schema markup rarely breaks or conflicts with themes. This stability is a genuine advantage over newer plugins that have had schema validation issues.

However, Rank Math offers more schema types in its free version (15+ types vs. Yoast's basic set). And AIOSEO offers 40+ schema types on paid plans. Yoast's schema breadth is solid but not class-leading.

### XML Sitemaps

Yoast generates XML sitemaps automatically for all public post types. The sitemap includes posts, pages, categories, tags, author archives, and custom post types. You can exclude specific content types or individual posts.

The sitemap implementation is stable and widely compatible. Yoast has been generating sitemaps since 2010. The codebase is mature.

Key features:
- Automatic sitemap updates when content changes
- Image sitemap inclusion
- Configurable post type and taxonomy inclusion
- Last modified date tracking

The free version includes full sitemap functionality. This is not locked behind Premium.

### Social Previews

Yoast shows you how your content will appear when shared on Facebook and X (Twitter). You can customize the social title, description, and image separately from the SEO title and meta description.

The preview is accurate and updates in real time as you edit. For content that gets significant social traffic, this feature prevents embarrassing formatting errors. Like a cropped image or truncated title.

Social previews are available in the free version. Premium adds Open Graph and Twitter Card validation.

### Internal Linking

Yoast SEO Premium includes an internal linking suggestions feature. As you write, Yoast scans your existing content and suggests relevant pages to link to. It also identifies orphaned content. Pages with no internal links pointing to them.

This feature is genuinely useful for sites with 50+ published posts. Finding internal linking opportunities manually is time-consuming. The automated suggestions save research time.

The limitation: the suggestions are based on text matching, not topical relevance. They find pages that mention similar words. Not necessarily pages that cover related topics in depth. For advanced internal linking strategy, you still need manual judgment.

This feature is Premium-only. Rank Math includes internal linking suggestions in its free version.

### Redirect Manager

Yoast SEO Premium includes a redirect manager that automatically creates redirects when you change a post's URL. It also supports manual 301, 302, and 307 redirects. And it monitors for 404 errors.

The auto-redirect feature prevents broken links when you update permalinks. This is genuinely valuable. Broken links hurt user experience and waste crawl budget.

The redirect manager is Premium-only. The free version has no redirect functionality. For free users, the Redirection plugin by John Godley is the standard alternative.

### AI Features (2026)

Yoast has added AI-powered features in recent updates:

- **AI-generated titles and meta descriptions**. Premium generates optimized SEO titles and meta descriptions using AI.
- **AI content optimization suggestions**. Recommendations for improving content based on AI analysis.
- **AI Brand Insights** (AI+ plan, ~$358/year). Weekly analysis across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity for brand mentions, sentiment, and competitor tracking.
- **LLMs.txt Management**. Controls how AI crawlers access your content.
- **Bot Blocker**. Blocks specific AI training bots (GPTBot, CCBot, Google-Extended).

The AI title and meta description generation works well for standard pages. It produces usable copy that follows SEO best practices. For complex or technical content, human editing is still required.

The AI+ plan at ~$358/year is expensive for what it delivers. Most of these features are available in standalone tools at lower cost.

## How to Use Yoast SEO: The Right 5-Step Workflow

Most WordPress users install Yoast, enter one focus keyword, and expect rankings to improve. That is not how it works. Here is the workflow that produces results.

**Step 1: Do Keyword Research Before You Write**

Yoast does not do keyword research. Use Ahrefs, Semrush, Ubersuggest, or Google's Keyword Planner to identify target keywords before you open WordPress. Yoast optimizes for the keyword you give it. It does not tell you which keyword to target.

**Step 2: Write Your Content First, Then Optimize**

Write your article naturally. Focus on the reader. Do not write to Yoast's traffic lights. Once your draft is complete, open the Yoast panel and address the recommendations. Add the focus keyphrase to the title, meta description, first paragraph, and a subheading. Add internal links. Add alt text to images.

**Step 3: Fix Readability Issues Genuinely**

Do not shorten sentences just to turn lights green. Shorten them because long sentences are hard to read. Do not add transition words just to check a box. Add them because they improve flow. Use Yoast's readability score as a training tool. Not a compliance checklist.

**Step 4: Configure Schema for Every Post Type**

Set default schema types for posts, pages, products, and any custom post types. This is where Yoast adds genuine technical value. Proper schema markup improves how your pages appear in search results. Rich snippets, FAQ dropdowns, product ratings.

**Step 5: Set Up Redirects and Monitor 404s**

If you have Premium, configure the Redirect Manager to auto-create redirects when URLs change. Monitor the 404 log monthly. Fix broken links. If you use the free version, install the Redirection plugin for this functionality.

## Does Yoast SEO Actually Work? What the Data Says

Yoast does not publish ranking correlation data or controlled case studies. What can be validated is that Yoast correctly implements technical SEO fundamentals. Sitemaps, schema markup, canonical tags, meta tags, and robots directives. These are table-stakes for WordPress SEO, and Yoast handles them reliably.

The 12 million+ active installs mean it works at scale without breaking WordPress. Plugin compatibility is broad. The codebase is actively maintained with regular updates.

WordPress.org rating: 4.8/5 stars across 27,794+ reviews. 93.4% are 5-star reviews. 92% of reported issues are resolved within two months. These are genuinely strong support metrics.

Enterprise adoption is difficult to verify. Yoast does not publish a customer list. The plugin is most commonly found on blogs, small business sites, and content publishers rather than enterprise deployments.

**Important caveat:** An SEO plugin does not cause rankings. It optimizes your pages technically. Rankings come from content quality, backlinks, domain authority, and user engagement. None of which Yoast controls. Users who expect rankings to improve just from installing a plugin will be disappointed.

## Yoast SEO FAQ

**What is Yoast SEO?**
Yoast SEO is a WordPress plugin with 12 million+ active installs that helps site owners optimize their pages for search engines. It provides real-time SEO analysis, readability scoring, XML sitemaps, schema markup, and social previews. It does not write content, do keyword research, or build backlinks.

**How much does Yoast SEO cost?**
Free version: $0. Yoast SEO Premium: $99/year for 1 site. Extensions: WooCommerce SEO (~$79/year), Local SEO (~$79/year), Video SEO (~$69/year), News SEO (~$69/year), AI+ (~$358/year). No monthly billing. 30-day money-back guarantee.

**Is Yoast SEO free?**
Yes, there is a free version with core features: 1 focus keyword, SEO analysis, readability analysis, XML sitemaps, basic schema, and social previews. Premium features include 5 focus keywords, redirect manager, internal linking, AI titles/meta, and 24/7 support.

**Is Yoast SEO Premium worth it?**
For single-site owners who value the redirect manager, internal linking suggestions, and 5 focus keywords: yes, at $99/year. For multi-site owners: no. Rank Math offers more features at lower cost for multiple sites. For businesses needing content creation: no. Yoast does not write or publish articles.

**Is Yoast better than Rank Math?**
For beginners who want simplicity: Yoast wins. The traffic light system and readability analysis are more accessible. For feature value and power users: Rank Math wins. Rank Math's free version includes 5 focus keywords, redirects, local SEO, advanced schema, and 404 monitoring. Yoast charges for these.

**Does Yoast SEO write content for you?**
No. Yoast's AI features generate SEO titles and meta descriptions only. Not full articles. The plugin optimizes content you have already written. It does not create content.

**Does Yoast SEO do keyword research?**
No. Yoast has no keyword research functionality. You need a separate tool like Ahrefs, Semrush, or Ubersuggest to find target keywords. Yoast only optimizes for the keyword you provide.

**Does Yoast SEO work with Webflow, Shopify, or Ghost?**
No. Yoast is a WordPress-only plugin. It cannot be installed on or integrated with any other platform.

**Does Yoast SEO do local SEO?**
Partially. The Local SEO add-on (~$79/year) adds local business schema, store locator, and local optimization features to your WordPress site. It does not post to Google Business Profile, manage citations, monitor reviews, or do anything outside your WordPress pages.

**What is the real cost of using Yoast SEO?**
The plugin costs $0–$99/year. But to publish 30 articles per month, you need writers ($2,400–$7,500/month), a keyword tool ($99–$229/month), and possibly a content optimizer ($0–$129/month). Real total: $2,500–$7,800/month.

**Who owns Yoast SEO?**
Founded 2010 by Joost de Valk in the Netherlands. Acquired by Newfold Digital in August 2021. Current leadership: Chaya Oosterbroek (SVP and GM). Joost de Valk departed in 2023.

**Does Yoast SEO slow down my website?**
Yoast adds some load. The plugin is 32.4 MB. Rank Math is 11.8 MB and lighter. For most sites, the difference is negligible. For performance-critical sites, The SEO Framework or SEOPress may be better choices.

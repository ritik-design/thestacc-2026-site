## What Is AISEO?

AISEO is an AI content generation platform built on top of GPT-based language models. It wraps existing large language models (likely OpenAI's GPT) with SEO-focused templates, a paraphrasing engine, and a "humanizer" layer designed to make AI-generated text harder for AI detectors to flag.

The core product generates blog posts, social media copy, ad text, and long-form articles from keyword prompts. It also includes a readability scorer, a plagiarism checker, and. More recently. Features positioned around "Generative Engine Optimization" and "Answer Engine Optimization."

AISEO targets bloggers, freelance writers, content marketers, students, and small agencies. Its primary appeal is speed and price. You can generate a full blog post in minutes for a fraction of what any competitor charges.

What AISEO does not do: rank tracking, backlink analysis, CMS publishing, local SEO, social media scheduling, Google Business Profile management, or technical site audits. It generates text. Everything else. From keyword research validation to publishing. Requires separate tools.

AISEO was founded in 2021 and is headquartered in Amsterdam, Netherlands. The company appears to be bootstrapped with no disclosed external funding. The founding team is not publicly identified on the website. AISEO originally gained traction through an AppSumo lifetime deal at $49, which brought in early adopters. Many of whom later reported being charged subscription fees despite purchasing a "lifetime" license.

## AISEO Features (2026)

### AI Content Generator

AISEO's primary feature generates full blog posts from a keyword or topic prompt. You enter a keyword, select a content type (blog post, listicle, how-to guide), and the system produces a structured draft in 60–90 seconds.

The output follows standard SEO formatting: H2/H3 headings, bullet points, meta descriptions, and keyword placement. Quality is comparable to other GPT-wrapper tools. Serviceable first drafts that need 30–60 minutes of editing before publishing. Complex technical topics require more editing than general lifestyle or marketing content.

The One-Click Blog Generator consumes credits on the Grow plan (500 credits = approximately 50,000 words). At 1,500 words per article, that is roughly 33 articles before you hit the ceiling. The Scale plan ($29/month annual) removes this limit with unlimited credits.

> **Insight:** The credit system is AISEO's most common source of user confusion. Multiple reviewers report not understanding how quickly credits burn on long-form content. A 3,000-word article can consume 3–5× more credits than expected due to regenerations and edits.

### AI Humanizer

The humanizer is AISEO's signature feature and its primary marketing angle. It rewrites AI-generated text to "sound more human" and theoretically bypass AI detection tools like GPTZero, Originality.ai, and Turnitin.

The practical reality is less impressive than the marketing. Independent testing by Originality.ai showed that AISEO-humanized content was still flagged as 100% AI-generated. Other user reports are mixed. Some claim the humanizer works well enough for their use case, while others say the output reads awkwardly and still triggers detectors.

The humanizer works best when used on shorter passages (200–500 words) rather than full articles. Running an entire 2,000-word post through it tends to flatten the prose and introduce inconsistencies in tone.

> **Insight:** The core premise. That you need to trick AI detectors. Is increasingly irrelevant. Google has stated repeatedly that AI-generated content is not inherently penalized. What matters is quality and helpfulness, not whether a detector flags it. Building a workflow around detection evasion is solving the wrong problem.

### Outrank Article

This feature analyzes a competitor URL and generates content designed to "outperform" it. You paste a competitor's article URL, and AISEO produces a longer, more detailed version targeting the same keyword.

The name is misleading. Ranking depends on domain authority, backlinks, site speed, user engagement, and dozens of other factors that content alone cannot control. The feature generates decent competitive drafts, but calling it "outrank" overpromises what any content generator can deliver.

> **Insight:** A newer article with no backlinks and low domain authority will not outrank an established page just because it is longer. If outranking were that simple, every AI writing tool would be an SEO agency.

### Long Form Assistant

Creates extended content up to 5,000+ words using a step-by-step guided process. You provide an outline or let AISEO generate one, then it fills in each section sequentially.

The output quality is decent for first drafts. The main limitation is credit consumption. On the Grow plan, a single 5,000-word article can eat 5–10% of your monthly allocation. Scale plan users don't face this constraint.

### Topical Authority Builder

This feature identifies related keywords for building content clusters around a core topic. The concept is sound. Topical authority matters for SEO. But the execution has problems.

Third-party testing by Originality.ai found this feature to be non-functional during their review period. User reports confirm inconsistent reliability. When it works, the suggestions are surface-level compared to what Semrush, Ahrefs, or even free tools like AlsoAsked provide.

### Paraphraser

Rewrites existing text in multiple modes: standard, fluent, creative, academic, shortened, and expanded. Also supports translation across multiple languages.

The paraphraser is functional for basic rewrites. Output can feel generic. Particularly in "creative" mode, which tends to produce the same kind of inflated prose that AI detectors flag. Manual editing is still required for anything client-facing.

### Site Audit (GEO Focus)

A newer addition that evaluates your website's technical SEO with a focus on AI compatibility. Specifically whether your content structure helps it appear in AI-generated answers from ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews.

The feature is relatively thin compared to dedicated audit tools like Screaming Frog, Sitebulb, or Semrush's site audit. It covers basics but lacks the depth that serious SEO practitioners need.

### Blog Image Generator

Generates topic-aligned visuals for blog posts. Three types: cover images, in-article illustrations, and AI art. This is a separate add-on priced at $7–$15/month. Not included in the base plans.

The image quality is acceptable for blog posts but noticeably AI-generated. If brand consistency matters, you will still need a designer or a tool like Canva.

## How to Use AISEO: The Right 6-Step Workflow

Most new users open the blog generator, paste a keyword, and publish the raw output. The result is generic content that reads like every other AI-generated article on the internet. And performs accordingly.

**Step 1: Do Your Keyword Research Elsewhere**

AISEO's keyword data has shown significant discrepancies compared to Ahrefs and Semrush in independent testing. Use a dedicated keyword tool first. Identify your target keyword, search volume, difficulty, and search intent before touching AISEO.

**Step 2: Analyze the Top-Ranking Content Manually**

Read the top 5 results for your keyword. Note what they cover, how deep they go, and what angles they take. AISEO's Outrank Article feature automates this. But understanding the SERP yourself produces better strategic decisions.

**Step 3: Generate Your First Draft**

Use the Long Form Assistant for articles over 2,000 words, or the One-Click Blog Generator for shorter content. Provide a detailed outline if you have one. Guided generation produces better output than fully automated generation.

**Step 4: Edit Aggressively**

Budget 30–60 minutes of editing per article. AI-generated content follows predictable patterns. Repetitive transitions, generic examples, surface-level analysis. Replace these with specific data, original insights, and your actual experience. This is where the real work happens.

**Step 5: Run the Humanizer on Flagged Sections Only**

If AI detection matters for your use case, run the humanizer on specific paragraphs rather than the entire article. Full-article humanization flattens your voice. Targeted rewrites on the most obviously AI-written sections produce better results.

**Step 6: Publish and Track with External Tools**

Copy your content into your CMS manually. Set up rank tracking in Ahrefs, Semrush, or SE Ranking. Monitor performance over 60–90 days. AISEO will not do any of this for you.

## Does AISEO Actually Work? What the Data Says

AISEO does not publish ranking correlation data, case studies with verifiable metrics, or before/after traffic screenshots.

The enterprise logos on AISEO's homepage. Salesforce, Shopify, Airbnb, L'Oreal, Adidas. Are unverified. No case studies, testimonials, or confirmed partnerships from these companies are available. This is a common practice among smaller SaaS tools that should be viewed skeptically.

User-reported outcomes are mixed:

- Bloggers generating 10–20 posts per month report time savings of 50–70% on first drafts.
- SEO practitioners report that AISEO's keyword suggestions and SEO scoring are unreliable compared to Surfer SEO or Clearscope. Meaning you cannot trust the tool's optimization recommendations without external validation.
- The humanizer receives the most polarized feedback. Some users say it meaningfully improves their content's naturalness. Others say it fails entirely against Originality.ai and introduces awkward phrasing.

The honest assessment: AISEO works as a draft generator for people who already know how to edit and optimize content. It does not work as a standalone SEO strategy. If you expect to paste a keyword, click generate, and rank on Google. That is not what happens.

## What Is ProWritingAid?

ProWritingAid is a grammar checker, style editor, and writing mentor rolled into one desktop and web application. It analyzes your text across 25+ dimensions including grammar, spelling, style, readability, pacing, dialogue, overused words, sentence variety, and clichés. Unlike Grammarly, which focuses on real-time corrections as you type, ProWritingAid is built for deep analysis of complete documents.

Founded in 2012 by Chris Banks in London, England. Banks created the tool to solve his own self-doubt while writing a novel. The company operates as Orpheus Technology Limited, a privately held, bootstrapped business with no venture capital funding. This independence has allowed consistent product direction without investor pressure.

The company reports over 3 million writers worldwide and 200,000+ Chrome extension users. The team has grown to approximately 52 employees across Europe, North America, and Asia. Notable authors using the platform include Leeanna Morgan (59 published novels), Talia Hibbert, and J.F. Penn.

What ProWritingAid is not: an SEO tool, a content generator, a keyword research platform, or a publishing system. It improves the quality of text you have already written. It does not help you decide what to write, who to write it for, or how to get it found on Google. It is a writing improvement tool, not a content strategy tool.

ProWritingAid occupies a unique niche. It is more thorough than Grammarly for style analysis. It is more educational than Hemingway for readability. It is the only major grammar tool with native Scrivener integration. This depth is its greatest strength and its greatest limitation. The depth means ProWritingAid teaches you to write better. The limitation means it takes longer to learn and slower to run than simpler alternatives.

## ProWritingAid Features (2026)

### Grammar and Spelling Checker

The foundation of ProWritingAid. It catches grammatical errors including subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, and modifier placement. It spots spelling mistakes with context-aware corrections. It flags punctuation errors like comma splices, missing apostrophes, and semicolon misuse.

Accuracy is competitive with Grammarly but not quite as fast. ProWritingAid tends to catch fewer outright typos than Grammarly in head-to-head tests. One independent test found Grammarly caught 7 errors that ProWritingAid missed, while ProWritingAid caught 1 that Grammarly missed. Many authors use both tools in sequence: ProWritingAid first for style, Grammarly second for final proofreading.

The grammar engine uses natural language processing and machine learning. It improves continuously as the user base grows. For non-native English speakers, the explanations attached to each suggestion function as a tutor. This educational component is stronger than Grammarly's.

The real-time checker works in the web editor, browser extension, and desktop app. It is slower than Grammarly's instant suggestions, especially on documents over 50 pages. Some users toggle the extension off in Google Docs for large manuscripts to avoid lag.

### 25+ Writing Reports

This is ProWritingAid's signature feature. No competitor offers this depth of analysis. The reports include:

- **Writing Style** — flags passive voice, adverb overuse, and hidden verbs
- **Sentence Length** — identifies pacing issues via sentence variety analysis
- **Pronouns** — catches unclear antecedents and excessive pronoun use
- **Transition** — checks for smooth logical flow between ideas
- **Dialogue** — analyzes dialogue tags and speaker attribution
- **Sensory** — measures five-sense usage for "show, don't tell"
- **Pacing** — identifies slow sections where readers may lose interest
- **Consistency** — checks hyphenation, capitalization, and spelling consistency
- **Echoes** — finds repeated words and phrases within close proximity
- **Clichés** — flags overused expressions
- **Diction** — suggests stronger word choices
- **Alliteration** — identifies repetitive sound patterns
- **Homonyms** — catches homophone errors

Each report runs independently. You can run 2 reports per day on the free plan. Premium unlocks unlimited runs. The reports teach you patterns in your writing. Over time, you internalize the lessons and make fewer errors.

> **Insight:** The Pacing Check is genuinely useful for fiction writers. It revealed in one test that action scenes averaged 18 words per sentence while quiet scenes averaged 24. This data-driven insight helps authors control reader engagement.

The reports can feel overwhelming at first. New users often run every report on a single document and drown in suggestions. The recommended approach is to run 2 to 3 reports per editing pass, focusing on your known weaknesses.

### AI Features: Sparks and Critiques

ProWritingAid added AI-powered features in 2024, refined through 2025 and 2026. These are branded as "Sparks" and include:

- **Rephrase** — adjusts formality, length, fluency, or sensory detail
- **Continue** — generates text from where you left off
- **Summarize** — condenses longer sections
- **Critique** — provides developmental feedback on chapters

The free plan includes 3 Sparks per day. Premium includes 5 per day. Premium Pro includes 50 per day. The daily limits are a significant constraint for heavy users. Many authors find Premium's 5 Sparks too restrictive for novel revision and upgrade to Premium Pro.

The Rephrase tool receives mixed feedback. Some users report it makes sentences sound robotic. Others find it useful for breaking out of repetitive phrasing. The quality improved in 2026 but still lags behind dedicated AI writing tools.

### Manuscript and Story Analysis

Premium Pro unlocks advanced story tools that go beyond grammar and style:

- **Manuscript Analysis** — full-novel analysis of structure, plot, pacing, and characterization
- **Plot Analysis** — structural feedback on narrative arc
- **Virtual Beta Reader** — simulated reader feedback on engagement and hook points
- **Marketability Analysis** — feedback on commercial potential

These tools require Story Credits, purchased separately. Pricing is approximately $35 to $50 per manuscript. Reception is mixed. Some authors find the insights surprisingly detailed. Others report AI hallucinations, such as identifying a cat as holding a gun. The tools are best suited for beginners who cannot afford a human developmental editor.

### Readability and Style Scoring

ProWritingAid scores readability using multiple formulas including Flesch Reading Ease, Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level, and Gunning Fog Index. It compares your writing against genre benchmarks. It measures sentence variety, paragraph length distribution, and transition density.

The Author Comparison feature measures your writing against 90+ renowned authors. This is more of a novelty than a practical tool, but it provides an interesting benchmark.

The Custom Style Guide feature lets you set house rules. Define preferred spellings, banned words, and formatting conventions. This is valuable for series authors maintaining consistency across multiple books.

### Integrations

ProWritingAid integrates with more writing platforms than most competitors:

- **Microsoft Word** — full add-in with all reports
- **Google Docs** — browser extension with real-time checking
- **Scrivener** — native integration, unique among major grammar tools
- **Chrome, Firefox, Edge** — browser extensions
- **Apple Pages and Notes** — limited support
- **Notion, Gmail, Discord, Milanote** — browser extension works in these
- **Desktop apps** — Windows and Mac native applications
- **Atticus** — integration for book formatting

The Scrivener integration is a major differentiator. No other grammar tool offers native Scrivener support. For authors working in 80,000-word manuscripts, this is a deciding factor.

## How to Use ProWritingAid: The Right 6-Step Workflow

Most new users run every report at once and quit from overwhelm. Here is the workflow that experienced authors recommend.

**Step 1: Write your draft without ProWritingAid running**

Turn off the real-time checker while drafting. The constant suggestions interrupt creative flow. Write your complete first draft in Scrivener, Word, or Google Docs. Save the editing for later.

**Step 2: Run the Style and Grammar reports first**

Upload your complete draft to the ProWritingAid web editor or run the desktop app. Start with the Writing Style report for passive voice and adverbs. Then run the Grammar report for typos and punctuation. Fix the big structural issues before polishing sentences.

**Step 3: Run the Pacing and Sentence Length reports**

For fiction, the Pacing report identifies slow sections. The Sentence Length report shows variety patterns. Adjust accordingly. Short sentences for action. Longer sentences for reflection.

**Step 4: Check Consistency and Echoes**

The Consistency report catches hyphenation and spelling variations. The Echoes report finds repeated words within close proximity. These are the errors readers notice subconsciously.

**Step 5: Run a final Grammarly pass for typos**

As noted by multiple authors, Grammarly catches more outright typos than ProWritingAid. Run your polished draft through Grammarly as a final safety net. The order matters: ProWritingAid first for style, Grammarly second for accuracy.

**Step 6: Export back to your writing environment**

ProWritingAid exports to Word, Scrivener, Google Docs, and plain text. Return to your primary writing tool for final formatting and publishing.

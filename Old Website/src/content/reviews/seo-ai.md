## SEO.AI Features (2026)

### Content Gap Analysis

Each month, SEO.AI analyzes your website against competitors and identifies keyword and topic gaps. Content your competitors rank for that your site doesn't cover. The output is a prioritized list of recommended articles with keyword targets and topic clustering already applied.

This step is genuinely valuable and typically the hardest part of an SEO content workflow for small business owners. Most businesses don't know what to write because they have no visibility into what competitors are ranking for. SEO.AI makes the gap visible and acts on it automatically, turning the analysis directly into a content plan. No export, no spreadsheet, no separate research tool required.

### Publication Planning

From the gap analysis, SEO.AI builds an automated content calendar. A sequenced monthly publishing plan that accounts for topic clustering (related keywords covered in the right order to build topical authority). The calendar is generated automatically, but users can review, adjust, and approve it before articles go into production.

Most content automation tools stop at "here's what to write." SEO.AI moves to "here's the full monthly plan, in the right order, ready to execute." That's a meaningful difference in actual operational value.

### In-Depth Topic Research

For each planned article, SEO.AI runs a SERP analysis. Examining the top-ranking pages for the target keyword, extracting topic structures, semantic terms, and content benchmarks, and using that data to brief the article. The research step runs automatically, which is the relevant distinction from tools like Surfer SEO or Frase.io that require you to run this manually before writing begins.

### Article Writing

SEO.AI writes long-form SEO articles (2,000–5,000+ words), structured for the target keyword and related terms from the SERP research. Internal links are inserted automatically, connecting new articles to existing pages on the site with contextually relevant anchor text. A featured image is generated per article using AI.

The platform supports **Brand Voice** configuration. You can train the AI on your company's tone, style, and terminology by uploading example content. The AI then writes in your voice rather than a generic template tone. This is a practical differentiator for businesses with established brand standards.

Quality notes from user feedback: the output is consistently rated as strong for automation and publish-ready for businesses that currently produce zero content. Users who want more formatting variety. Varied paragraph lengths, stronger use of bullet points, pull quotes. Report needing light editing passes before publishing. That's a fair expectation for any AI-generated content at this price point.

## How to Use SEO.AI: The 6-Step Setup Workflow

The most common mistake new users make: signing up for the self-serve tier when they want Teammate autopilot, or confusing the two during trial. The setup steps below apply to the Teammate tier. The fully automated product.

**Step 1: Choose Your Tier**
Decide whether you want Teammate Autopilot (hands-off, zero ongoing involvement) or the Self-Serve tool plans (manual control, more flexibility). If you want to publish content without regularly logging in, choose Teammate. If you want to build, adjust, and oversee the content workflow yourself, choose Self-Serve. These are not interchangeable. Make this decision before you enter payment details.

**Step 2: Connect Your CMS**
Connect your website in Settings → Integrations. WordPress requires generating an Application Password (Settings → Users → Application Passwords. Not your login password). Webflow and Shopify connect via OAuth. Wix, Squarespace, and Magento use their respective API methods. Users consistently report connection completing in under 5 minutes without technical help.

**Step 3: Complete Your Business Profile**
Define your business type, target service area, primary keywords and topics you want to rank for, and competitors' URLs. The gap analysis that powers the content plan is only as accurate as the context you provide. For a local service business, list 3–5 specific competitors by URL. Not generic category terms.

**Step 4: Configure Brand Voice**
Upload 2–3 examples of your preferred content tone. A past blog post, your About page, or a service description. The AI trains on this and writes in your voice rather than a generic SEO content template. This is optional but consistently cited by users as improving output quality.

**Step 5: Review and Approve the Content Plan**
SEO.AI generates the first content calendar after you complete setup. Review the planned article topics, remove anything that doesn't align with your business, and add any gaps the system missed. Approve the plan. Most users review this once and approve the full queue with minimal changes.

**Step 6: Set Publishing Mode and Monitor Quarterly**
Choose Auto Publish, Requires Approval, or No Auto Publish. For first-time users, Requires Approval for the first 30 days lets you validate output quality before switching to full autopilot. Once the system is running, check in quarterly. Not monthly. Review Google Search Console data for which queries your content is earning impressions. Meaningful ranking movement typically begins at 60–90 days.

## What Is MarketMuse?

MarketMuse is a content intelligence platform that uses AI to analyze topical authority, identify content gaps, and generate research-backed content briefs. The thesis: most content teams publish reactively. Chasing keywords one at a time. MarketMuse maps the full competitive landscape first, then tells you exactly which topics to cover, in what order, to build authority systematically.

The core technology is proprietary topic modeling. It maps the semantic relationships between topics, subtopics, and entities across your domain and your competitors' domains. This is deeper than keyword-level analysis. It answers: "Do I have authority on this subject?". Not just "Am I targeting this keyword?"

Founded by Aki Balogh and Jeff Coyle (Chief Strategy Officer, the public face of the company). Jeff Coyle is a well-known figure in content marketing. His talks on topical authority and content strategy are widely cited. The company raised $10M+ in funding and serves primarily mid-market to enterprise content teams.

What MarketMuse is *not*: a writing tool or a publishing platform. It's a strategist. It tells you what to write, how to structure it, and what gaps exist. But every word, every edit, and every publish action is on you.

## MarketMuse Features (2026)

### Content Inventory & Topic Authority

MarketMuse crawls your entire domain and scores your authority across topics. For each topic cluster:

- **Authority Score**. How comprehensively you cover the topic vs. competitors
- **Coverage gaps**. Subtopics your competitors cover that you don't
- **Content decay**. Published pages losing rankings that need updating
- **Cannibalization**. Multiple pages competing for the same topic

This is genuinely unique in the market. No other tool provides a domain-level authority map with this depth. For teams with 100+ published articles, it shows exactly where to invest next for maximum ranking impact.

> **Insight:** The Authority Score is MarketMuse's differentiator. It doesn't just check if you have an article on a topic. It evaluates how comprehensively your content covers the topic, how well your articles link to each other, and how your coverage compares to competitors who rank.

### Competitive Content Analysis

For any topic, MarketMuse maps:
- Which competitors own the topic (highest authority scores)
- How many articles they have in the cluster
- What subtopics they cover that you miss
- The difficulty of competing (based on current authority distribution)

### Content Briefs (AI-Generated)

This is where most teams interact with MarketMuse daily. For any target topic, it generates:

- Recommended title and meta description
- Target word count based on SERP analysis
- Comprehensive list of topics and subtopics to cover
- Suggested heading structure (H2/H3)
- Questions to answer (pulled from SERP data)
- Related topics to mention for semantic coverage
- Internal linking suggestions

Briefs are detailed enough to hand directly to a freelance writer. The quality consistently exceeds what Surfer or Frase generate for content briefs. Because MarketMuse accounts for domain-level authority, not just page-level optimization.

### Optimize (Content Scoring)

The Optimize tool scores existing or draft content against MarketMuse's topic model. Like Surfer's Content Score but with a different lens. It evaluates topical completeness, not just keyword presence. The score reflects how thoroughly you cover the subject compared to the competitive field.

### Questions

Generates research-backed questions that searchers ask about any topic. More targeted than Google's "People Also Ask" because it's filtered through MarketMuse's topic model. Prioritizing questions that indicate ranking opportunities.

### Connect (Internal Linking)

Analyzes your site's internal linking structure and recommends links between related pages. Identifies orphan content (pages with no internal links pointing to them) and suggests anchor text.

## How to Use MarketMuse: The Right 5-Step Workflow

**Step 1: Run Content Inventory on your domain first**
Before writing anything new, let MarketMuse analyze your existing content. The Authority Score reveals which topic clusters you already have a foothold in. These are where new content will rank fastest because you're building on existing authority.

**Step 2: Identify gaps in your strongest clusters**
Don't chase new topics. Find the subtopics missing from clusters where you already have authority. A site with 10 articles on "content marketing" but none on "content calendar templates" is leaving authority on the table.

**Step 3: Generate content briefs for gap-filling articles**
Use MarketMuse's brief generator for each target. The brief gives writers specific guidance. Not just a keyword to target, but a map of topics, questions, and structure that produces comprehensive content.

**Step 4: Have writers produce content using the brief**
Hand the brief to your writer. Set expectations: match the recommended word count, cover every suggested subtopic, answer the prioritized questions. This produces measurably better content than writers working from a keyword alone.

**Step 5: Score content with Optimize before publishing**
Run every draft through MarketMuse Optimize. Target a score of 35–50 (their scoring scale is different from Surfer's 0–100). Iterate until topical coverage is comprehensive.

## MarketMuse FAQ

**What is MarketMuse?**
MarketMuse is a content intelligence platform that uses AI topic modeling to analyze topical authority, identify content gaps, generate detailed content briefs, and score content against competitive benchmarks. Built for enterprise content strategy teams.

**How much does MarketMuse cost?**
Standard: $149/month ($99/month annual). Team: $399/month ($300/month annual). Premium: custom pricing. No free plan available.

**Is there a free version of MarketMuse?**
No. MarketMuse discontinued its free plan. A limited demo is available but requires contacting sales.

**Does MarketMuse write content for you?**
No. MarketMuse generates content briefs (structure, topics to cover, word count targets, questions to answer) but does not write articles. You need writers to create content from the briefs.

**Is MarketMuse worth it in 2026?**
For enterprise teams with 100+ articles and $10K+ monthly content budgets: yes. For small businesses without writers: no. Real cost to publish 30 articles = $2,549–$7,899/month (MarketMuse + writers + optimization).

**What are the best MarketMuse alternatives?**
For content optimization: Surfer SEO ($119/mo), Clearscope ($129/mo), Frase ($49/mo). For done-for-you content: theStacc ($99/mo, 30 articles auto-published).

**Can MarketMuse replace Surfer SEO?**
They serve different phases. MarketMuse excels at strategy and planning (what to write). Surfer excels at optimization (how to write it). Many enterprise teams use both. MarketMuse for briefs, Surfer for real-time scoring while writing.

**Does MarketMuse do local SEO?**
No. No GBP management, local citations, local content optimization, or Google Maps features.

**What is MarketMuse's Topic Authority score?**
A proprietary metric that evaluates how comprehensively your domain covers a topic compared to competitors. It accounts for content volume, depth, internal linking, and subtopic coverage. Not just whether you have an article on the subject.

**How does MarketMuse compare to Semrush?**
Semrush covers keyword research, backlinks, site audits, and competitive intelligence. MarketMuse covers topical authority, content planning, and brief generation. Semrush is broader. MarketMuse is deeper on content strategy. Neither writes or publishes content.

**Is MarketMuse good for small businesses?**
Usually not. The tool's value scales with content library size. Sites with fewer than 50 articles don't generate meaningful Authority Scores. The $149/month starting price is steep for a planning-only tool when small businesses need execution more than strategy.

**What is MarketMuse Connect?**
The internal linking tool. It analyzes your site structure and recommends links between related pages, identifies orphan content (pages with no inbound links), and suggests anchor text to improve topical connectivity.

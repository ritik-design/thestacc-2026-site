## What Is Frase.io?

Frase.io is an AI-powered SEO content research, brief-building, and optimization platform. Its central value proposition: the most time-consuming part of SEO content creation is manual SERP research. Reading through top-ranking pages, extracting headings, identifying topics, spotting gaps. Frase automates that process.

The core mechanism works like this: you enter a keyword, and Frase scrapes the top-ranking pages for that query. It extracts their headings (H1–H6), word counts, question coverage from "People Also Ask," and key topic terms. It then assembles these into a structured content brief in minutes. The same deliverable a content strategist would spend 90–180 minutes building by hand.

On top of brief creation, Frase adds a scoring layer: as you write or import content, it compares your coverage against top-ranking competitors and gives you an optimization score. Terms you're missing or underusing are flagged. It also has an AI writer for drafting sections, though that component receives more mixed feedback than the brief and research tools.

What Frase is not: a publishing platform. It does not connect to your WordPress or Webflow site and push articles live. It does not manage a content calendar that runs without your involvement. Everything Frase produces still requires a human to write, edit, and publish.

Frase was founded in 2017 by Tomas Ratia and is headquartered in Boston, Massachusetts. With only 16 employees and $1.2M raised, Frase is a lean, bootstrapped-adjacent operation. It grew through product quality and community rather than venture capital. Current ARR is approximately $1.8M as of 2025, per Latka data.

Notable context: In 2024, Frase launched a rebuilt platform and new pricing tiers. Some customers who had purchased lifetime deals were moved to a "legacy" version of the old product rather than the new platform. This caused real backlash in the community. ## Frase.io Features (2026)

### SERP Research & Brief Builder

This is Frase's strongest feature and the reason it has the reputation it does. For any keyword, Frase automatically retrieves the top-ranking pages and presents a structured breakdown:

- All headings (H1–H6) from each ranking page
- Word counts per page and averages across the SERP
- Topics and entities that appear frequently across competitors
- Questions from Google's "People Also Ask" box
- Content gaps. Topics competitors cover that your current draft doesn't

The practical impact: an experienced content strategist building a brief from scratch takes 90–180 minutes per topic. Frase compresses that to 10–20 minutes. For freelancers building 10–20 briefs per month for clients, the math on that time saving is immediate.

One-click brief generation turns the SERP analysis into a formatted content brief. Ready to hand to a writer or use as your own writing scaffold. The structure includes recommended headings, suggested word count range, topic coverage targets, and key questions to answer.

> **The Brief Builder Advantage:** Frase's brief quality is not matched by any single feature inside Surfer SEO. Surfer is a better optimizer once you're writing. Frase is a better researcher before you start. This is why many professional content teams subscribe to both. And why the "just use Frase" and "just use Surfer" camps both exist online.

### AI Content Agent (80+ Skills)

Frase launched a rebuilt AI Content Agent with 80+ individual skills in its 2024 platform overhaul. These cover a wide range of SEO and content tasks:

- Full article drafts generated from your brief
- Section-by-section expansion (write one section at a time)
- Rewrite and expand tools for existing content
- Multi-channel content adaptation
- GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) content formatting

The AI writer is competent but not class-leading. The most honest summary of Frase's AI writing quality, drawn from consistent G2 feedback: it produces content that works as a starting framework but needs significant editing before it's publish-ready. Frase's brief and research tools are best-in-class. The AI writing is solid but not a reason to choose Frase over alternatives.

### SEO & GEO Optimization Score

As you write (or import existing content), Frase measures your document against the top-ranking pages for your keyword and generates an optimization score. The score reflects term coverage: how many of the key topics, entities, and phrases from competitor content appear in your article.

The benchmark: Frase's optimization score has a measured ranking correlation of approximately 0.10. This is meaningfully lower than Surfer's 0.28. What that means practically: Frase's optimization guidance is useful for ensuring comprehensive topic coverage, but it's less predictive of ranking outcomes than Surfer's approach.

Frase has also added GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) features. Tools for formatting and structuring content to appear in AI-generated answers from ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews. This is genuinely forward-looking and one of Frase's differentiators in the market heading into 2026.

### AI Visibility Tracking

Frase's AI Visibility tool monitors whether your brand and content appear in AI-generated search responses. Starter plans track 2 platforms, Professional tracks 3, Scale tracks 5, Enterprise tracks 8. This is one of the first content research platforms to integrate AI visibility monitoring directly. Tracking not just traditional Google rankings but presence in AI-generated answers.

### Content Audit (GSC Integration)

Connect Frase to Google Search Console and it surfaces your site's content performance data: pages in decline, pages with traffic but poor optimization scores, and pages with untapped keyword opportunities. This is one of the most actionable features Frase offers. Particularly for sites with existing content libraries of 30+ published posts.

The "quick wins" use case: Frase identifies pages already ranking on page 2 or 3 that could move to page 1 with targeted optimization. Refreshing indexed content is often faster ROI than creating new content from scratch.

### Internal Linking Automation

Available on Professional and Scale plans, Frase automatically identifies internal linking opportunities. Which existing pages should link to your new or updated content, and which anchor text to use. It also flags orphaned content: published pages with no inbound internal links, limiting crawlability and link equity distribution.

### Content Calendar & Publishing

The content calendar feature (Professional and Scale) lets teams plan, schedule, and track content production. WordPress publishing integration is available. You can push content from Frase directly to WordPress. API and MCP (Model Context Protocol) access is available on all plans, allowing Claude and other AI models to work directly within Frase's workflow.

## How to Use Frase.io: The 6-Step Workflow

Most new Frase users start by opening the AI writer and trying to generate a complete article. That's the wrong starting point. Here's the workflow that produces results:

**Step 1: Enter your keyword and run SERP research**
Open a new document, enter your target keyword, and let Frase scrape the top-ranking pages. Review the extracted headings, word counts, and topic coverage across competitors before you do anything else. This is the research layer. It tells you what you're competing against before you invest time creating.

**Step 2: Generate a content brief**
Use the one-click brief generator to produce a structured outline from the SERP data. Treat this as a starting scaffold, not a prescription. Add your own angle and perspective before writing. The brief is a research summary. Your job is to build something better than what's ranking, not to replicate it.

**Step 3: Write (or assign) the first draft**
Use the Frase AI writer to generate section-by-section drafts if speed matters, or write the article yourself using the brief as your guide. If you have a writer on your team, export the brief and assign the writing externally. The brief is detailed enough to hand off directly without a separate briefing call.

**Step 4: Check your optimization score. Address gaps, not just terms**
Once a draft exists, review the Frase optimization score. When a flagged term represents a genuine subtopic your article hasn't covered, add a section or paragraph. When it's just a phrase variation, skip it. The score is a coverage guide, not a keyword-insertion mandate.

**Step 5: Run the final draft through Surfer for scoring (if on the full stack)**
For teams running both tools: paste your optimized draft into Surfer's Content Editor to validate against Surfer's NLP scoring before publishing. Frase ensures topic coverage. Surfer validates pattern-matching against current SERP benchmarks. Together they cover different dimensions of the same optimization problem.

**Step 6: Publish and connect GSC for ongoing monitoring**
After publishing, use Frase's Content Audit (connected to Google Search Console) to track performance. As competitors update their content, your optimization score relative to theirs will shift. Periodic refreshes of underperforming posts often produce faster ranking improvements than new content creation.

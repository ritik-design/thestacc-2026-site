## What Is RankMath?

RankMath is a WordPress SEO plugin that handles on-page optimization, technical SEO settings, schema markup, redirections, 404 monitoring, rank tracking, and content analysis directly from the WordPress dashboard. It was founded in late 2018 by Bhanu Ahluwalia, Nimit Kashyap, and Suraj Vibhute. The three co-founders were previously the team behind MyThemeShop, a WordPress themes and plugins company launched in 2012.

The origin story matters. RankMath was not built to compete with Yoast directly. It was built because the founders, running their own websites, could not find an SEO plugin that did everything they needed without installing five separate tools. They started building RankMath for internal use in 2015. Over 3.5 years, they refined it. Then they released it publicly in late 2018.

The result is a plugin with a genuinely different philosophy: give away what competitors charge for. The free version includes features that AIOSEO locks behind $199/year and Yoast locks behind $99/year. Five focus keywords per post. Full redirection management with 404 monitoring. Eighteen schema types. Google Search Console integration. Local SEO module. This strategy has driven explosive growth. From zero to 2 million+ active installs in under 8 years. Making RankMath the fastest-growing SEO plugin in WordPress history.

What RankMath is not: a content creation service, an automated publishing platform, or a done-for-you SEO system. It tells you how to optimize your pages. You still have to write every article, optimize every page, and publish every piece of content manually. Within WordPress only.

The company remains 100% bootstrapped and remote. Team members work from around the globe. The plugin has been adopted by websites across many industries, from small blogs to large publishers.

## RankMath Features (2026)

### SEO Analysis and Content Scoring

RankMath's content analysis engine evaluates your posts in real time as you write in the WordPress editor. It checks focus keyword placement, keyword density, readability, heading structure, meta title and description length, internal and external linking, image alt tags, and URL structure.

The free version allows 5 focus keywords per post. RankMath Pro allows unlimited. For comparison: AIOSEO allows 1 focus keyword in the free version and 1 on Basic/Plus plans. Yoast allows 1 in the free version. RankMath's 5-keyword allowance in the free tier is arguably its most significant competitive advantage. Most blog posts naturally target 3–5 keyword variations. RankMath lets you optimize for all of them without paying.

The scoring system uses a 0–100 scale. Green indicates well-optimized. Yellow needs work. Red requires attention. The practical sweet spot most experienced users aim for is 80–90. Chasing 100 often results in unnatural keyword insertion that hurts readability.

> **Insight:** RankMath's content score is correlative, not causative. A score of 90 does not guarantee rankings. It indicates your page is technically well-optimized compared to competitor patterns. Content quality, backlinks, and domain authority still determine whether you rank.

### Schema Markup

RankMath's schema implementation is best-in-class among WordPress SEO plugins. The free version includes 18 schema types: Article, BlogPosting, NewsArticle, WebPage, Organization, Person, LocalBusiness, Restaurant, Recipe, Product, Course, Event, VideoObject, FAQPage, HowTo, JobPosting, Service, and SoftwareApplication.

RankMath Pro unlocks additional types and a custom schema builder. The schema builder uses a visual editor. You map schema fields to post data without writing JSON-LD manually. For WooCommerce stores, Product schema handles price, availability, review data, and variants correctly.

The FAQ and How-To schema blocks integrate directly with Gutenberg. Adding FAQ schema to a post takes under 2 minutes. This is genuinely faster and cleaner than AIOSEO's implementation and significantly more comprehensive than Yoast's limited schema types.

### Redirection Manager

The redirection module handles 301, 302, 307, 410, and 451 redirects. It auto-detects when you change a post URL and suggests creating a redirect. It supports regex patterns for bulk redirects. And it logs every redirect hit so you can see which rules are active.

Here is why this matters: AIOSEO locks redirections behind the $199.50/year Pro plan. Yoast locks them behind the $99/year Premium plan. RankMath includes full redirection management in the free version. For a basic SEO necessity that every site needs, this is a genuinely generous feature allocation.

### 404 Monitor

RankMath monitors 404 errors in real time and logs every hit. You can sort by hit count to identify which broken URLs are costing you the most traffic. One-click conversion to 301 redirects. Export logs for analysis. Exclude specific paths from monitoring.

AIOSEO locks 404 monitoring behind the Pro plan. Yoast does not include 404 monitoring at all. RankMath's free 404 monitor is another example of the plugin's core strategy: give away what others charge for.

### Local SEO Module

The Local SEO module adds business NAP (name, address, phone) management, Google Maps embed, business hours display, local business schema, and multi-location support. The opening hours block integrates with Gutenberg.

This entire module is free in RankMath. AIOSEO locks local SEO behind the $99.50/year Plus plan. Yoast locks it behind the $99/year Premium plan.

Important clarification: RankMath's local SEO adds structured data and display elements to your WordPress site. It does not post to Google Business Profile. It does not manage citations. It does not monitor reviews. It does not do anything outside of your WordPress pages.

### AI Link Suggestions (Link Genius)

RankMath Pro includes AI Link Genius, an automated internal linking system. It scans your entire site and identifies linking opportunities based on topical relevance. It detects orphaned content. Pages with zero internal links pointing to them. And it generates keyword-to-URL maps for automated linking.

The Link Genius module takes under 5 minutes to set up. Run the first audit. Review orphan pages. Set up keyword maps for important pages. Then use the "Generate" button in any post editor to see one-click link suggestions.

This is included free with RankMath Pro. AIOSEO charges $199.50/year for its Link Assistant. The practical value depends on site size. For sites with 100+ published posts, automated internal linking saves genuine manual research time. For smaller sites, manual linking is often faster.

### Rank Tracker

RankMath Pro includes a rank tracking module that monitors your keyword positions directly inside WordPress. It pulls data from Google and tracks positions over time. You can view ranking trends, position changes, and historical data without leaving your dashboard.

The Pro plan tracks 500 keywords. Business tracks 10,000. Agency tracks 50,000. The accuracy is reasonable for a WordPress-integrated tracker, though most SEO professionals still prefer dedicated rank tracking tools like AccuRanker or Semrush Position Tracking for mission-critical campaigns.

### Content AI

RankMath offers a separate Content AI subscription with 40+ AI writing tools and 125+ prompt templates. It generates titles, meta descriptions, FAQ text, alt tags, and full article drafts. Content AI operates on a credit system: 1 credit = 1 word generated.

The core SEO plugin includes only a 15-day trial of Content AI credits. Ongoing usage requires a separate subscription: Starter ($6.99/month, 7,500 credits), Creator ($11.99/month, 18,000 credits), or Expert ($18.99/month, 45,000 credits). Credits do not roll over month to month.

> **Insight:** Content AI is useful for bulk meta descriptions and alt tags. For full article generation, the quality is below dedicated AI writing tools like Jasper or Koala. Most RankMath users who need AI content pair the plugin with a separate writing tool rather than using Content AI.

### Additional Features

- **XML Sitemaps**. Automatic generation with configurable post types, taxonomies, priorities, and frequencies. News sitemap and video sitemap included in Pro.
- **Google Search Console Integration**. Pulls GSC data into WordPress dashboard. Free in RankMath. AIOSEO locks this behind the $299/year Elite plan.
- **Instant Indexing**. Google Indexing API integration for faster discovery. Submit new posts to Google automatically.
- **llms.txt Support**. Optimizes site structure for AI search tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini.
- **SEO Site Audit**. Basic health checks for common issues. Not as thorough as dedicated crawlers like Screaming Frog, but catches obvious problems.
- **Modular Architecture**. Disable unused features to keep the plugin lightweight. RankMath adds ~0.3s to page load and 12 extra database queries when fully enabled. Less than Yoast (+0.5s, 19 queries) and AIOSEO (+0.4s, 16 queries).

## How to Use RankMath: The Right 5-Step Workflow

Most WordPress users install RankMath, run through the setup wizard, and then wonder why their rankings haven't changed. The plugin optimizes your pages. It does not make them rank. Here is the workflow that actually produces results.

**Step 1: Run the Setup Wizard Completely**

The setup wizard walks you through site type, business category, social profiles, schema defaults, sitemap configuration, and advanced settings. Do not skip steps. The defaults are well-chosen for most sites. Connect Google Search Console during setup. This enables the integrated ranking data dashboard.

**Step 2: Configure Default Schema for Every Post Type**

Set default schema types for posts, pages, products, and any custom post types. This is where RankMath adds genuine value. Proper schema markup improves how your pages appear in search results. Rich snippets, FAQ dropdowns, product ratings, event listings. Most users skip this step and lose easy wins.

**Step 3: Do Keyword Research in a Separate Tool**

RankMath does not do keyword research. Use Ahrefs, Semrush, Ubersuggest, or even Google's free Keyword Planner to identify target keywords before writing. Enter those keywords as focus keywords in RankMath after you have written your content. The 5-keyword free allowance means you can optimize for primary + secondary + long-tail variations without paying.

**Step 4: Write Your Content First, Then Optimize**

Write your article naturally. Then open the RankMath panel and address the recommendations. Do not write to the score. Write for the reader, then use the checklist to catch gaps. Aim for 80–90, not 100. Chasing a perfect score produces stiff, unnatural content.

**Step 5: Set Up Redirections and Monitor 404s**

Configure the Redirection Manager to auto-catch 404 errors and suggest redirects when you change URLs. Review the 404 monitor monthly. Fix broken links that are generating traffic. Set up regex redirects for bulk URL pattern changes. This takes 10 minutes per month and prevents ranking loss from broken pages.

## What Is Wordtune?

Wordtune is an AI-powered writing assistant that rephrases, adjusts tone, and improves the clarity of individual sentences in real-time. It works as a browser extension for Chrome and Edge, a web editor, a Microsoft Word add-in, and a Google Docs integration. You highlight text, and Wordtune generates alternative versions with different tones and lengths.

Founded in October 2020 by AI21 Labs, a Tel Aviv, Israel-based AI research company. AI21 Labs was founded in 2017 by three prominent figures: Amnon Shashua (also founder and CEO of Mobileye, acquired by Intel for $15.3 billion), Yoav Shoham (Professor Emeritus of Computer Science at Stanford University and former Principal Scientist at Google), and Ori Goshen (serial entrepreneur and Co-CEO). The company raised $626.5 million in total funding, including a $155 million Series C in 2023 at a $1.4 billion valuation, and a $300 million Series D led by NVIDIA and Alphabet in May 2025.

Wordtune runs on AI21 Labs' proprietary Jurassic language models. Unlike competitors using OpenAI's GPT models, Wordtune uses models specifically optimized for reading comprehension and semantic understanding. This is a genuine technical differentiator.

As of August 2025, Wordtune reported 10 million users and 782 million+ rewrite suggestions generated. The tool is primarily used by non-native English speakers, content writers, students, and business professionals who need to refine their phrasing.

What Wordtune is not: a content creation tool, an SEO platform, a keyword research tool, or a publishing system. It improves sentences you have already written. It does not help you decide what to write, who to write it for, or how to get it found on Google.

**Important context for 2026:** In April 2025, AI21 Labs officially halted active development of Wordtune as part of a strategic pivot to enterprise AI products. The company laid off 110 of 180 employees in May 2026 and shut down nearly all product lines except Maestro, an AI-agent orchestration platform. Wordtune remains operational and available, but no new features are expected. This is critical context for anyone considering a long-term investment in the tool.

## Wordtune Features (2026)

### AI Rewriting and Rephrasing

The core feature. Highlight any sentence, and Wordtune generates multiple alternative phrasings. The suggestions preserve the original meaning while varying vocabulary, sentence structure, and flow.

Wordtune offers several rewrite modes:
- **Casual** — relaxed, conversational tone
- **Formal** — professional, polished tone
- **Shorten** — more concise version
- **Expand** — adds detail and length

The quality is competitive with QuillBot and superior to basic paraphrasers like Spinbot. Wordtune's suggestions tend to sound more natural because the Jurassic models are trained specifically for semantic understanding rather than simple synonym replacement.

The free plan limits you to 10 rewrites per day. The Advanced plan gives 30 per day. The Unlimited plan removes the cap entirely. For heavy writers, the Unlimited plan is essentially mandatory.

> **Insight:** Wordtune excels at helping non-native English speakers match natural phrasing. Where it falls short: it operates at the sentence level only. It cannot rewrite paragraphs coherently, restructure article sections, or maintain narrative flow across longer passages. Each sentence is optimized in isolation. This produces polished individual sentences that may not connect smoothly as a whole.

### Tone Adjustment

Wordtune's tone control is more granular than most competitors. You can shift a single sentence from casual to formal, or from verbose to concise, with one click. The tone detector analyzes context and suggests appropriate alternatives.

This is especially useful for business communications. An email to a client can be tightened. A Slack message to a colleague can be softened. A report for executives can be formalized.

The tone adjustment works in real-time across the browser extension. You do not need to copy text into a separate editor. This seamless workflow is one of Wordtune's genuine strengths.

### Spices (AI Suggestions)

Spices is Wordtune's generative AI feature that adds content to your writing rather than just rewriting it. It can:
- Generate analogies and examples
- Add statistical facts with source citations
- Provide counterarguments
- Insert historical context
- Suggest explanations and definitions

Spices 2.0, the current version, functions as an AI co-writer. You place your cursor where you want enrichment, and Spices suggests content to insert. This helps overcome writer's block and adds depth to thin sections.

The free plan includes limited Spices usage. Paid plans unlock more generous allowances. The factual grounding with citations is a standout feature. Most AI writing tools generate claims without sources. Wordtune's Spices includes hyperlinked citations for statistical facts.

> **Caveat:** The citations are helpful but should be verified. AI-generated citations can occasionally reference outdated or misattributed sources. Always check the original source before publishing.

### Wordtune Read (Document Summarizer)

Wordtune Read summarizes long documents into concise, scannable summaries. It handles PDFs, web pages, YouTube videos, and long-form articles. Documents up to 500 pages can be ingested.

The output includes executive summaries with hyperlinked citations back to the source text. This allows quick verification of claims without reading the entire document. The Knowledge Library stores links, files, and summaries in a searchable database.

For researchers and students, this saves significant time. Instead of reading a 50-page academic paper, you get a 2-page summary with key findings highlighted. You then decide which sections warrant full reading.

The summarizer is available on all paid plans. The free plan includes 3 summaries per month. The Advanced plan includes 15. The Unlimited plan removes the cap.

### Grammar and Spelling Checks

Wordtune includes basic grammar and spelling correction. It catches subject-verb agreement errors, tense inconsistencies, and punctuation mistakes. It also handles spelling with context-aware suggestions.

The grammar checking is competent but not best-in-class. Grammarly consistently outperforms Wordtune in head-to-head accuracy tests. Wordtune catches obvious errors but misses nuanced contextual mistakes that Grammarly flags.

For users who need both rewriting and grammar checking, the combination works reasonably well. Wordtune handles the rewriting. Grammarly handles the final proofreading. Many users run both tools in sequence.

### Browser Extension and Integrations

Wordtune's Chrome and Edge extension works across virtually any text input on the web. This includes Gmail, Outlook, LinkedIn, Facebook, Slack, X (Twitter), WhatsApp Web, and more.

The Smart Paste feature automatically reformats text when you paste between platforms. Copy from Slack and paste into Jira. Wordtune adjusts the tone and structure for the destination context.

Native integrations include:
- **Google Docs** — full rewriting, tone adjustment, and grammar checking inline
- **Microsoft Word** — add-in with all core features
- **Gmail and Outlook** — real-time rewrites and tone adjustments for emails

The extension requires broad permissions to read page content. This is necessary for the tool to function but raises privacy considerations for users handling sensitive information.

### Multilingual Input

Wordtune accepts text in multiple languages and rewrites it into fluent English. This is valuable for non-native speakers who think in their native language but need to communicate professionally in English.

The translation quality is solid for common language pairs. It is not a professional translation tool for legal or medical documents. But for business emails, social posts, and general writing, it bridges the gap effectively.

## How to Use Wordtune: The Right 5-Step Workflow

Most users install Wordtune and start clicking rewrite suggestions randomly. Here is the workflow that gets actual value from the tool.

**Step 1: Write your complete draft first without Wordtune running**

Turn off the browser extension while drafting. The constant suggestions interrupt creative flow. Write your complete first draft in Google Docs, Word, or your preferred editor. Save the rewriting for later.

**Step 2: Run Wordtune on sentences that feel awkward**

Read through your draft and highlight sentences that sound clunky, wordy, or unclear. Run Wordtune on those specific sentences only. Do not rewrite every sentence. Selective rewriting preserves your voice.

**Step 3: Use tone adjustment for audience-specific sections**

Shift tone where the audience changes. A blog post introduction can be casual. The data analysis section should be formal. The call-to-action can be confident and direct. Wordtune's tone controls make these shifts fast.

**Step 4: Use Spices to add depth to thin sections**

Place your cursor in sections that feel underdeveloped. Use Spices to generate examples, analogies, or statistical support. Verify any citations before publishing. Spices is a starting point, not a final source.

**Step 5: Run Grammarly as a final proofreading pass**

Wordtune's grammar checking is adequate but not exceptional. Run your polished draft through Grammarly for a final accuracy check. The order matters: Wordtune first for rewriting and tone, Grammarly second for grammar precision.

## What Is Grammarly?

Grammarly is a writing assistant that checks grammar, spelling, punctuation, clarity, tone, and style in real-time across virtually every text input on the internet. It works as a browser extension for Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge. It also offers a desktop app for Mac and Windows, a mobile keyboard for iOS and Android, and integrations with Google Docs, Microsoft Word, Gmail, Slack, and more.

Founded in 2009 by Alex Shevchenko, Max Lytvyn, and Dmytro Lider in Kyiv, Ukraine. The company later headquartered in San Francisco. Grammarly raised over $400 million in funding and was valued at approximately $13 billion in its 2021 funding round. As of 2026, Grammarly reports 40 million+ daily active users and 50,000+ business customers.

In 2023, Grammarly launched GrammarlyGO. This is an AI assistant powered by large language models that can generate, rewrite, and summarize text. This moved Grammarly from pure editing into AI content generation. The core product remains grammar and clarity checking.

What Grammarly is not: an SEO tool, a content strategy platform, a keyword research tool, or a publishing system. It improves the quality of text you have already written. It does not help you decide what to write, who to write it for, or how to get it found on Google. That is where theStacc comes in.

Grammarly occupies a unique position in the market. It is not a word processor like Microsoft Word. It is not a content management system like WordPress. It is not an SEO platform like Semrush or Ahrefs. It sits between your brain and your output, catching mistakes before they reach your audience. This narrow focus is its greatest strength and its greatest limitation. The narrow focus means Grammarly does grammar checking better than anyone else. The limitation means you need an entire ecosystem of other tools to produce SEO content that ranks.

The company has expanded steadily since 2009. Early funding rounds supported product development and user acquisition. The 2021 valuation of $13 billion reflected investor confidence in the writing assistance market. Grammarly has resisted acquisition, choosing to remain independent. This has allowed consistent product direction without corporate integration pressures.

## Grammarly Features (2026)

### Grammar Checking

The core product. Grammarly catches grammatical errors including subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, and modifier placement. It spots spelling mistakes with context-aware corrections. It flags punctuation errors like comma splices, missing apostrophes, and semicolon misuse. It also identifies sentence fragments and run-on sentences.

Accuracy is consistently better than Microsoft Word's built-in checker. It is competitive with ProWritingAid. For non-native English speakers, this alone justifies the free plan.

Grammarly uses a combination of natural language processing, machine learning, and a team of linguists to refine its suggestions. The system improves continuously as more users interact with it.

The grammar engine handles complex sentence structures that confuse simpler tools. It understands conditional clauses, subordinate conjunctions, and parallel structure. It flags dangling modifiers and misplaced participles. These are errors that many native speakers make unconsciously.

For professional writers, the grammar checker serves as a safety net. For non-native speakers, it functions as a tutor. The explanations attached to each suggestion teach users why an error is wrong. This educational component sets Grammarly apart from basic spell-checkers.

### Spelling

Grammarly's spelling checker goes beyond basic dictionary matching. It understands context. It knows the difference between "there," "their," and "they're." It catches homophone errors that standard spell-checkers miss.

The spelling engine also recognizes proper nouns, brand names, and industry terminology. It learns from your corrections. Over time, it adapts to your vocabulary and writing style.

For users who write across multiple platforms, the browser extension ensures consistent spelling checking everywhere. This includes social media, email, content management systems, and collaboration tools.

The spelling database is updated regularly. New words enter the lexicon constantly. Brand names emerge. Technical terminology evolves. Grammarly stays current better than most built-in checkers.

### Tone Detector

The tone detector analyzes your writing across 40+ tone types. These include formal, friendly, confident, diplomatic, and more. It suggests adjustments to help you hit the right tone for your audience.

This feature is especially useful for business communications. It helps ensure a client email does not read as aggressive. It prevents a sales pitch from sounding too passive.

The tone detector is available on the free plan with basic analysis. Premium unlocks deeper tone insights and specific rewriting suggestions to shift tone.

Tone matters for SEO indirectly. Content that matches searcher intent often requires a specific tone. Informational queries need a helpful, educational tone. Commercial queries need a confident, persuasive tone. Grammarly helps you hit the right tone for your audience. It does not tell you what tone the audience expects. That requires keyword research and SERP analysis. theStacc provides that research as part of every article.

### Plagiarism Checker

Available on Pro and Enterprise plans. The plagiarism checker compares your text against 16 billion web pages and academic papers. It flags matching passages and provides source links.

This is valuable for content teams reviewing freelancer submissions. It helps students verify original work. It catches duplicated content that could hurt your SEO if published.

The checker provides a similarity score. It highlights specific sections that match existing content. It distinguishes between common phrases and genuine duplication.

For SEO teams, this is critical. Duplicate content can trigger Google's duplicate content filters. It can dilute your site's authority. Running every piece of content through Grammarly's plagiarism checker before publishing is a smart quality control step. It is one of the few ways Grammarly directly supports an SEO workflow.

### AI Writing (GrammarlyGO)

GrammarlyGO is Grammarly's generative AI suite. It includes several capabilities:

- **Compose**: Generates text from prompts for email replies, social posts, and short-form content.
- **Rewrite**: Restructures existing text while preserving meaning.
- **Summarize**: Condenses long documents into key points.
- **Brainstorm**: Generates ideas based on context.

GrammarlyGO is useful for quick writing tasks. For long-form content such as 2,000 to 4,000 word blog posts, it generates generic output. That output needs substantial expansion. Critically, it has zero SEO awareness. It does not know what keywords to target. It does not analyze search intent. It does not structure content for featured snippets.

theStacc, by contrast, generates long-form SEO content that is researched, optimized, and ready to publish. GrammarlyGO is a writing helper. theStacc is an SEO content engine.

## Grammarly FAQ

**What is Grammarly?**
Grammarly is an AI writing assistant that checks grammar, spelling, punctuation, clarity, tone, and style in real-time. It works as a browser extension, desktop app, and mobile keyboard across virtually every text input. 30 million+ daily active users use it.

**How much does Grammarly cost?**
Free: $0 for basic grammar and spelling. Premium: $30 per month or $12 per month billed annually. Business: $25 per user per month or $15 per user per month annually.

**Is Grammarly free?**
Yes. The free plan provides grammar, spelling, punctuation checking, and basic tone detection. No credit card required. Premium features including clarity, plagiarism, and full GrammarlyGO require a paid subscription.

**Does Grammarly help with SEO?**
No. Grammarly has zero SEO features. It does not research keywords, analyze competitors, optimize content for search, score content against SERP data, or publish to your website. It improves writing quality only. For SEO, consider theStacc instead.

**Is Grammarly worth it in 2026?**
For writing quality: yes. The free plan alone is valuable, and Premium at $12 per month annual is fair. For SEO: no. You need a completely different set of tools.

**What are the best Grammarly alternatives for SEO?**
Grammarly does not do SEO, so alternatives depend on your need. For content optimization: Surfer SEO ($119 per month), Clearscope ($129 per month). For done-for-you SEO content: theStacc ($99 per month, 30 articles auto-published).

**Can Grammarly replace a human editor?**
For grammar, spelling, and basic clarity: partially. For substantive editing including structure, argument, voice, and SEO optimization: no. Grammarly catches surface-level issues. A human editor or theStacc improves content quality at a deeper level.

**Does Grammarly work with WordPress?**
The browser extension works inside the WordPress text editor, checking grammar and spelling as you type. It does not publish content, optimize for SEO, or manage your WordPress site.

**What is GrammarlyGO?**
Grammarly's AI assistant that generates, rewrites, and summarizes text. Powered by large language models. Useful for quick communications. Not designed for long-form SEO content production.

**How does Grammarly compare to ProWritingAid?**
Grammarly is faster, works across more platforms, and has a stronger free plan. ProWritingAid offers deeper style analysis including narrative pacing, overused words, and sentence variety that fiction writers prefer. Neither does SEO.

**Does Grammarly do local SEO?**
No. No Google Business Profile management, local content, Google Maps tools, or any SEO-related features. theStacc includes 30 Google Business Profile posts per month.

**Is Grammarly Business worth it?**
For organizations standardizing communication quality across 10+ employees: yes. Custom style guides, admin controls, and brand tone enforcement prevent inconsistent messaging. For SEO: same limitation. It does not touch search rankings.

**Does Grammarly store my data?**
Grammarly processes text on its servers to provide suggestions. The company states it does not sell user data. Business plans offer enhanced security features including SSO and admin controls. Organizations in regulated industries should review Grammarly's security documentation before adoption.

**Can I use Grammarly offline?**
The desktop app offers limited offline functionality. The browser extension and most features require an internet connection. This is because the NLP models run on Grammarly's servers, not locally on your device.

**Does Grammarly work with Google Docs?**
Yes. The Grammarly browser extension integrates directly with Google Docs. It provides the same grammar, spelling, and tone suggestions as in other applications. This is one of the most popular use cases for the tool.

## What Is SpyFu?

SpyFu is a competitor research platform that reveals the keywords, ads, and rankings any website has used over time. It was founded in 2005 by Mike Roberts in Scottsdale, Arizona — originally launched as "GoogSpy" before rebranding to SpyFu in 2006.

The company is bootstrapped. No venture capital. No parent company. Roberts still runs it as CEO with a team of roughly 21–50 people. That independence shows in the product — focused, opinionated, and priced aggressively low.

SpyFu's core mechanism is simple: it indexes search results and ad auctions continuously, building a historical database of what domains rank for and what ads they run. As of 2026, it claims 7.2 trillion search results indexed, 13.1 billion keywords with advanced metrics, and 152 million websites tracked.

What SpyFu does NOT do is critical to understand before you buy. It does not write content. It does not publish to WordPress or any CMS. It does not track technical SEO issues like broken links or site speed. It does not handle local SEO or Google Business Profile. It does not generate social media posts. It is a research and intelligence tool — nothing more.

If your workflow ends at "I now know what my competitor does," SpyFu is sufficient. If your workflow needs to continue to "I now publish content that outranks them," you need additional tools and people.

## SpyFu Features (2026)

### Competitor PPC Research

SpyFu's signature feature is its PPC competitor database. Enter any domain and you see every Google Ads keyword they have bought, every ad copy variation they have tested, and estimated monthly ad spend — going back to 2006.

This is where SpyFu genuinely has no equal. Semrush and Ahrefs show current ads. SpyFu shows the full history. You can see that a competitor tested 12 different headlines for a single keyword in 2019, abandoned 9 of them, and kept the winners. That historical depth is invaluable for PPC strategists who want to learn from others' experiments without spending their own budget.

The ad copy database includes negative keyword suggestions — keywords competitors are actively excluding from their campaigns. This is tactical gold. If a competitor with a $50,000/month ad budget excludes a keyword, there is usually a reason.

> **Insight:** SpyFu claims 20x more paid keyword data than Semrush and Ahrefs. We cannot verify that exact multiplier, but the historical depth is visibly deeper. If your primary need is PPC intelligence, SpyFu punches well above its price.

### SEO Competitor Research

The SEO side mirrors the PPC approach. Enter a domain and SpyFu shows organic ranking keywords, ranking history, top pages, and estimated organic traffic. Data updates every 15 minutes on tracked domains.

The keyword research module includes intent filtering, topic groups, and API automation. You can filter by question keywords, transactional terms, or informational queries. The interface is fast — faster than Semrush for basic lookups.

However, the SEO data has limitations. Backlink analysis is shallow compared to Ahrefs. Technical SEO audits are nonexistent. There is no site crawler. If you need to diagnose why a page isn't ranking, SpyFu tells you what competitors do — not what's wrong with your site.

### Kombat

Kombat is SpyFu's three-way keyword overlap tool. You enter your domain plus up to two competitors. SpyFu renders a Venn diagram showing shared keywords, unique keywords, and gaps.

The tool segments results into actionable categories:

- **Weaknesses** — keywords competitors rank for that you don't
- **Keyword Universe** — all keywords any competitor ranks for
- **Exclusive Wins** — keywords only you rank for
- **Core Niche** — keywords all competitors share
- **Consider Buying** — PPC opportunities competitors invest in
- **Potential Ad Waste** — keywords you buy that competitors avoid
- **Content Ideas** — gaps suggesting new content opportunities

As of 2025–2026, Kombat supports unlimited domain comparisons. Previously it was capped at three domains. The tool works for both SEO and PPC modes.

> **Insight:** Kombat is the fastest way to visualize competitive gaps without exporting spreadsheets. For agencies doing competitive audits, it turns a 2-hour manual process into a 5-minute lookup. The limitation: it doesn't show SERP features like featured snippets or People Also Ask — just keyword overlap.

### Rank Tracking

SpyFu's rank tracker monitors keyword positions with weekly updates on all plans. The Pro + AI and Team plans support 15,000 and 50,000 tracked keywords respectively.

Weekly updates are a limitation. Competitors like Semrush and Ahrefs offer daily tracking on base plans. For fast-moving niches or during algorithm updates, weekly data can miss significant position shifts.

The tracker supports custom keyword groups, scheduled PDF reports, and competitor comparison. It is functional but not exceptional.

### Backlink Research

SpyFu's backlink tool shows top-ranking-page-only links with outreach contacts. It does not offer the depth of Ahrefs' Site Explorer or Semrush's Backlink Analytics.

You can see who links to a domain and filter by link type, but the index is smaller. For serious link building campaigns, most users pair SpyFu with a dedicated backlink tool.

### SpyGPT

SpyGPT is SpyFu's AI integration launched in 2024–2025. It has two components:

1. **Brand monitoring in LLMs** — analyzes how ChatGPT and other large language models mention your brand across 250 million questions. Shows where competitors appear instead of you.
2. **ChatGPT plugin** — a custom GPT that streams SpyFu's API directly into ChatGPT, letting you query competitor data in natural language.

The ChatGPT integration is genuinely useful. You can ask "Which keywords did [competitor] add in the last quarter?" and get a real answer based on live data — not a hallucination.

Basic plan includes unlimited SpyGPT chats. Pro + AI and Team plans get full access.

### RivalFlow AI

RivalFlow AI is SpyFu's content optimization tool launched in 2023–2024. It analyzes your pages against top-ranking competitors, identifies content gaps, and generates ready-to-improvement copy with precise insertion instructions.

Key capabilities:

- Content gap analysis against top-ranking pages
- AI-generated copy with placement guidance
- 23 enhancement methods
- Google Search Console integration for before/after tracking
- Real-time SERP tracking

RivalFlow is NOT included on the Basic plan. Pro + AI gets 1 site. Team gets 10 sites. It is promising but limited — one site on Pro + AI is restrictive for anyone managing multiple properties.

> **Insight:** RivalFlow generates copy suggestions, but you still need to manually implement them in your CMS. It does not auto-publish. For a single site owner, the 1-site limit on Pro + AI may be enough. For agencies, the 10-site Team limit fills quickly.

### Reporting and Lists

SpyFu generates client-ready PDF reports with live data. Pro + AI supports up to 100 automated reports per month. Team supports unlimited reports with white-label capabilities.

The Lists and Leads feature filters prospects by ad budget, location, and technology stack. You can export to CSV. This is useful for agency business development — finding companies that spend on ads but may need SEO help.

## How to Use SpyFu: The Right 6-Step Workflow

Most new SpyFu users make the same mistake: they run one competitor lookup, export a keyword list, and never return. SpyFu's value comes from systematic competitive monitoring, not one-off searches.

**Step 1: Map Your True Competitors**

Don't guess. Enter your domain in SpyFu and use the "Competitors" tab to see who SpyFu identifies as your actual search competitors. These may differ from who you think competes with you. Save this list.

**Step 2: Run Kombat for Keyword Gaps**

Enter your domain plus your top 2 competitors in Kombat. Focus on the "Weaknesses" and "Content Ideas" segments. These are keywords competitors rank for that you don't — your fastest opportunity set.

**Step 3: Analyze Competitor PPC History**

For your top competitor, review their PPC history going back 2–3 years. Note which ad copy variations they kept long-term versus abandoned quickly. The kept ads are your starting point for messaging tests.

**Step 4: Build a Keyword Tracking List**

Export your gap keywords and upload them to SpyFu's rank tracker (requires Pro + AI or Team). Group by topic or page intent. Set weekly reports to monitor progress.

**Step 5: Use RivalFlow for Content Optimization**

For existing pages that rank on page 2, run RivalFlow analysis. Implement the copy suggestions and track ranking changes via the GSC integration. Focus on pages with existing traffic — they move fastest.

**Step 6: Schedule Monthly Competitive Audits**

Set a calendar reminder to re-run Kombat and competitor PPC analysis monthly. Competitive landscapes shift. Keywords your competitors ignored in January may be their focus by June. SpyFu's historical data makes these shifts visible.

## Does SpyFu Actually Work? What the Data Says

SpyFu's data accuracy is good for established domains and competitive keywords. It struggles with new sites, low-traffic niches, and non-US markets.

**What the numbers show:**

- SpyFu claims 40,000 SERP updates every second across 38 countries
- 18+ years of historical data for major domains
- 13.1 billion keywords tracked
- G2 rating of 4.6/5 from 518 reviews — above Semrush's 4.5/5

**Enterprise validation:** SpyFu does not publish a customer list, but its user base skews toward small agencies, freelancers, and in-house marketers at mid-size companies. It is not commonly found in enterprise martech stacks — Semrush and Ahrefs dominate there.

**Honest caveats:**

- Traffic estimates are directional, not precise. Do not budget ad spend based on SpyFu's traffic numbers alone.
- PPC spend estimates are rough. They reflect bid landscape and position, not actual invoices.
- Data for sites outside the US, UK, Canada, and Australia is thinner. SpyFu covers 38 countries but depth varies.
- Weekly rank tracking misses fast-moving SERPs. During algorithm updates, daily tracking is essential.

SpyFu works best as a competitive intelligence layer, not a sole source of truth. Pair it with Google Search Console for your own data, and Google Ads Keyword Planner for PPC forecasts.

## SpyFu FAQ

**What is SpyFu?**
SpyFu is a competitor intelligence platform that reveals the keywords, ads, and rankings any website has used over time. It specializes in historical PPC data and SEO competitor research.

**How much does SpyFu cost?**
SpyFu has three plans: Basic at $39/month ($29/month annual), Pro + AI at $119/month ($89/month annual), and Team at $249/month ($187/month annual). Annual billing saves 25%.

**Is there a free version of SpyFu?**
Yes. SpyFu offers a permanent free account with no credit card required. Free accounts have limited data access but can perform basic competitor lookups.

**Does SpyFu write content for you?**
No. SpyFu is a research tool only. Its RivalFlow AI feature generates content improvement suggestions, but you must manually implement them. It does not auto-publish to any CMS.

**Is SpyFu worth it in 2026?**
Yes, for PPC researchers and competitive analysts who need historical ad data at a low price. No, for teams needing content creation, technical SEO audits, or local SEO management.

**What are the best SpyFu alternatives?**
For all-in-one SEO: Semrush or Ahrefs. For affordable rank tracking: SE Ranking. For content optimization: Surfer SEO or Clearscope. For done-for-you content: [theStacc](/pricing).

**Can SpyFu replace an SEO agency?**
No. SpyFu provides research data but does not execute SEO work. You still need writers, developers, and strategists to act on the intelligence it provides.

**Does SpyFu work for local SEO?**
No. SpyFu has no local SEO features, Google Business Profile management, or citation tracking. For local SEO, consider BrightLocal, Semrush, or a done-for-you service.

**How does SpyFu compare to Semrush?**
SpyFu is cheaper ($39 vs. $139.95 entry) and has deeper historical PPC data. Semrush is more comprehensive with technical SEO, content marketing, and social media tools. SpyFu is a specialized research tool; Semrush is an all-in-one platform.

**What are SpyFu's biggest limitations?**
No content writing, no CMS publishing, no technical SEO audits, weekly-only rank tracking, limited backlink depth, and restricted RivalFlow AI site limits on lower tiers.

**Is SpyFu good for beginners?**
Yes. The interface is simpler than Semrush or Ahrefs. The $39 Basic plan is low-risk for learning. However, beginners may outgrow it quickly if they need technical SEO or content creation tools.

**Does SpyFu offer a free trial?**
SpyFu offers a permanent free account rather than a time-limited trial. All paid plans include a 30-day money-back guarantee.

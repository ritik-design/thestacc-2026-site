## What is Distance in Local Ranking?

Distance is Google's measure of how far a business is from the searcher. Or from the location specified in the query. And it's one of three factors (alongside [relevance](/glossary/relevance-local-ranking) and [prominence](/glossary/prominence-local-ranking)) that determine local search rankings.

When someone searches "coffee shop near me," Google uses their device location to calculate distance to every nearby coffee shop. The [Google Vicinity Update](/glossary/google-vicinity-update) in 2021 significantly increased the weight of distance, making proximity even more dominant for implicit local queries.

Here's what makes distance interesting: you can't change where your business is located. That makes it the one local ranking factor you can't directly optimize. But you can influence how Google interprets your service area through [location pages](/glossary/location-pages), service area settings, and localized content.

## Why Does Distance Matter?

Distance is often the deciding factor when relevance and prominence scores are similar.

- **Dominates "[near me](/glossary/near-me-searches)" queries**. Physical proximity is the heaviest signal for location-implicit searches
- **Became more powerful after the [Vicinity Update](/glossary/google-vicinity-update)**. Google increased distance weighting in December 2021, reshuffling local pack results
- **Favors businesses near city centers** ,  [centroid bias](/glossary/centroid-bias) means businesses near the geographic center of a city often appear for city-wide searches
- **Can be offset by prominence**. A very well-known business (high prominence) can outrank a closer but less prominent competitor

For businesses in peripheral locations, overcoming distance disadvantage requires stronger [prominence](/glossary/prominence-local-ranking) signals. More reviews, better citations, and more web authority.

## How Distance Works

### Location Detection

Google determines distance using the searcher's IP address, device GPS (on mobile), or the location specified in the query. "Plumber in downtown Austin" uses downtown Austin as the reference point. "Plumber near me" uses the searcher's real-time location.

### Service Area Businesses

Businesses that travel to customers (plumbers, electricians, cleaners) set a service area in their [GBP](/glossary/google-business-profile) instead of displaying a physical address. Google considers the declared service area when calculating distance for these businesses. A plumber serving a 30-mile radius can appear for searches across that entire area.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I rank in the local pack if I'm far from the searcher?

Yes, but it's harder. Strong [prominence](/glossary/prominence-local-ranking) (lots of reviews, citations, authority) can overcome moderate distance disadvantages. For broad city-wide searches, businesses with the highest overall scores still appear regardless of exact proximity.

### Does the Google Vicinity Update still affect rankings?

Yes. The [December 2021 Vicinity Update](/glossary/google-vicinity-update) permanently increased the weight of distance in local ranking. Its effects are baked into Google's ongoing algorithm. Proximity is now the dominant factor for most local queries.

### How do service area businesses handle distance?

Set your service area accurately in [Google Business Profile](/glossary/google-business-profile). Google uses this declared area instead of a single pinpoint address. Create location-specific content for each area you serve to reinforce your presence in those zones.

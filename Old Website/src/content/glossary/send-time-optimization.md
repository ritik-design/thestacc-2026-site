## What is Send-Time Optimization?

Send-time optimization is an AI-driven [email marketing](/glossary/email-marketing) feature that analyzes each subscriber's past open and click behavior to determine their individual optimal delivery time, then staggers sends accordingly.

You schedule the email for "Tuesday." The STO system delivers it at 7:14am to Sarah (who checks email before work), 12:03pm to Mike (who opens during lunch), and 9:47pm to Priya (who catches up in the evening). Same campaign, different delivery times, higher overall engagement.

The impact is consistent across studies. Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) reports that STO increases open rates by 15-25% and click rates by 10-20% compared to batch sends. Mailchimp's internal data shows similar lifts. For teams sending to large lists, those percentage points translate directly to revenue.

## Why Does Send-Time Optimization Matter?

Email timing affects whether your message gets seen or buried. STO stacks the odds in your favor for every recipient.

- **Higher [open rates](/glossary/email-open-rate)**. Emails that land when the recipient is active get opened more; STO improves open rates 15-25%
- **Better inbox placement**. Higher engagement signals tell email providers your messages are wanted, improving [deliverability](/glossary/email-deliverability) over time
- **No guesswork**. Eliminates the "what's the best day and time to send?" debate with actual data per subscriber
- **Compounding effect**. Better engagement metrics improve your [sender reputation](/glossary/sender-reputation), which improves deliverability, which improves engagement further

Any team sending emails to more than 1,000 subscribers should consider STO. The setup is minimal. Most platforms offer it as a toggle. And the results are immediate.

## How Send-Time Optimization Works

STO systems analyze historical engagement data and build individual delivery schedules.

### Data Collection

The platform tracks when each subscriber opens emails, clicks links, and engages with your website. It needs 4-8 weeks of historical data to build reliable individual profiles. Subscribers with insufficient data receive emails at your list's average optimal time.

### Predictive Modeling

Machine learning models identify each subscriber's engagement windows. The hours and days when they're most likely to interact with email. Some systems factor in time zones automatically. Others go deeper, analyzing device type (mobile vs. desktop) and content preferences.

### Staggered Delivery

Instead of sending all emails at once, the system distributes delivery across a window (typically 24-48 hours). Each email arrives at its recipient's predicted optimal time. The campaign still "sends" on your scheduled date. But individual deliveries spread out.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which email platforms offer STO?

HubSpot, Mailchimp, Brevo, Salesforce Marketing Cloud, Klaviyo, and ActiveCampaign all offer STO features. Most require a Pro or Enterprise tier. Capability depth varies. Some optimize by hour, others by 15-minute windows.

### Does STO work for small lists?

The algorithm needs enough data to identify patterns. For lists under 500, the predictions may not be reliable. You're better off [A/B testing](/glossary/a-b-testing) send times manually until you have enough subscriber data for STO to work.

### Can STO hurt deliverability?

No. It typically improves it. Staggered sending reduces the risk of spam throttling that can occur when you blast thousands of emails simultaneously. The higher engagement rates also improve your sender reputation.

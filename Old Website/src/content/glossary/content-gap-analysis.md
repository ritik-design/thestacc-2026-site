## What is Content Gap Analysis?

Content gap analysis is the process of comparing your website's content against competitors' to find topics, keywords, and questions they cover that you don't.

It's one of the fastest ways to build a [content strategy](/glossary/content-strategy). Instead of guessing what to write about, you reverse-engineer what's already working for businesses in your space. The "gap" is the delta between what your audience searches for and what your site actually answers.

Ahrefs data shows that the average top-ranking page also ranks for 1,000+ other keywords. If a competitor's page ranks for those keywords and you have nothing on that topic, that's a gap you're leaving open.

## Why Does Content Gap Analysis Matter?

Every gap is traffic your competitors get that you don't.

- **Competitor-validated topics**. If 3 competitors rank for a keyword, the topic has proven demand. You're not guessing
- **[Topical authority](/glossary/topical-authority) building**. Filling gaps strengthens your site's coverage of a subject, which Google rewards with higher rankings across related terms
- **Efficient content planning**. Instead of brainstorming from zero, you start with a prioritized list of gaps sorted by search volume and difficulty
- **Revenue opportunities**. Many content gaps sit on high-intent keywords (bottom-of-funnel queries) that directly drive leads and sales

Pair a gap analysis with a [content audit](/glossary/content-audit), and you'll know exactly what to create, update, and delete.

## How Content Gap Analysis Works

### Identify Competitors

Pick 3-5 competitors who rank for the keywords you want. Use tools like Ahrefs, Semrush, or Moz to pull their organic keyword profiles. Include both direct competitors and content competitors (sites that rank for your topics but sell different products).

### Compare Keyword Coverage

Run a gap analysis report. Ahrefs and Semrush both have this feature. The tool shows keywords your competitors rank for that you don't appear for at all. Filter by search volume and difficulty to prioritize.

### Create a Production Plan

Group related gaps into topics. Assign each to a content type ,  [blog post](/glossary/blog-post), guide, comparison page, or landing page. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What tools are best for content gap analysis?

Ahrefs Content Gap and Semrush Keyword Gap are the most popular. Both let you enter your domain plus competitors and instantly see keyword overlaps and gaps.

### How often should you do a content gap analysis?

Run a thorough analysis quarterly. The competitive landscape shifts as competitors publish new content and Google updates rankings. What wasn't a gap 3 months ago might be one now.

### What's the difference between a content gap and a keyword gap?

A keyword gap focuses strictly on search terms. A content gap is broader. It includes topics, formats, and content types you're missing, not just individual keywords.

## What are SERP Features?

SERP features are enhanced result types displayed on Google's search engine results page that go beyond traditional organic listings. Including featured snippets, AI Overviews, knowledge panels, image packs, video carousels, and more.

The search results page isn't just 10 blue links anymore. Semrush's 2024 analysis found that 99% of desktop [SERPs](/glossary/serp) contain at least one SERP feature. The average results page now has 7+ different feature types competing for attention.

This matters for SEO because SERP features often push standard organic results below the fold. Even a #1 organic ranking can be invisible if a [featured snippet](/glossary/featured-snippet), AI Overview, ads, and a [knowledge panel](/glossary/knowledge-panel) appear above it.

## Why Do SERP Features Matter?

SERP features fundamentally change the traffic equation for every keyword you target.

- **Visibility shift**. A position 1 organic ranking doesn't guarantee the most clicks when featured snippets and AI Overviews appear above it
- **Zero-click searches**. Roughly 65% of Google searches now end without a click, according to SparkToro/Datos research. Largely because SERP features answer queries directly
- **Traffic opportunity**. Winning a featured snippet can jump your click-through rate from 8% to 35%+ for that query
- **Local business impact**. The [local pack](/glossary/local-pack) shows up for 46% of all Google searches, making it the most important SERP feature for local businesses

Understanding which features appear for your target keywords shapes your entire content strategy.

## How SERP Features Work

### Common Feature Types

Featured snippets extract and display a direct answer from a webpage. [People Also Ask](/glossary/people-also-ask-paa) boxes show expandable related questions. AI Overviews generate AI-written summaries at the top of results. Knowledge panels display entity information. [Rich results](/glossary/rich-results) show enhanced listings with ratings, prices, or FAQ dropdowns.

### How to Win SERP Features

Featured snippets favor concise, well-structured answers. Definitions in 40-60 words, numbered lists, comparison tables. FAQ [schema markup](/glossary/schema-markup) triggers FAQ rich results. [Local schema](/glossary/local-schema-markup) and Google Business Profile optimization drive local pack appearances. AI Overviews tend to cite authoritative, well-structured content.

### Tracking SERP Features

Tools like Semrush, Ahrefs, and SE Ranking track which features appear for your keywords and whether your site owns them. Monitor feature presence monthly. Google frequently adds, removes, and modifies features. A keyword that showed a featured snippet last month might show an AI Overview this month.

## SERP Features Examples

**Example 1: Capturing a featured snippet**
A marketing agency writes "What is content marketing?" with a clear 50-word definition at the top of the page. Google extracts that definition as a featured snippet, displaying it above all organic results. The page jumps from 200 clicks/month to 1,400. A 7x increase from one feature.

**Example 2: Local pack visibility**
A dentist optimizes their [Google Business Profile](/glossary/google-business-profile) with complete information, regular posts, and 200+ [Google reviews](/glossary/google-reviews). For "dentist near me" searches, they consistently appear in the local 3-pack. Generating 50+ calls per month from a SERP feature that sits above all organic results.
## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which SERP feature drives the most traffic?

Featured snippets typically drive the highest click-through rates for informational queries. Often 2-3x the CTR of a standard #1 position. For local queries, the local pack drives the most phone calls and direction requests. The most valuable feature depends on your business type.

### Can I optimize for AI Overviews?

AI Overviews tend to cite pages that provide clear, authoritative, well-structured answers. Use concise definitions, structured headers, and proper schema markup. Pages that already rank in the top 10 and provide unique data or expert perspectives are most likely to be cited in AI Overviews.

### Do SERP features steal organic traffic?

Some do. AI Overviews and featured snippets can satisfy a query without a click, reducing traffic to the source page. But being the cited source in a SERP feature still builds brand visibility. And for many features (FAQ dropdowns, rich results), the enhanced listing actually increases your CTR compared to a standard blue link.

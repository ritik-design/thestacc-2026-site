## What is an Unlinked Mention?

An unlinked mention is when another website references your brand, product, or content by name but doesn't include a clickable hyperlink to your site.

This happens constantly. A blogger writes "according to theStacc" but doesn't link. A news article mentions your company as an example but forgets the URL. A forum post recommends your product without linking to it. Each unlinked mention is a [backlink](/glossary/backlinks) you've already earned in spirit. The author already chose to reference you. They just skipped the hyperlink.

According to Ahrefs data, established brands have 3-10x more unlinked mentions than actual backlinks. That's a massive reservoir of link potential sitting untapped. Converting even 10-15% of those mentions into links can significantly boost your [link profile](/glossary/link-profile).

## Why Do Unlinked Mentions Matter?

They're the fastest, cheapest [link building](/glossary/link-building) opportunity available.

- **Highest conversion rate in link building**. Outreach emails asking for a link on an existing mention convert at 15-25%, vs 3-5% for cold pitches
- **No content creation needed**. The content already exists and already references you
- **Google may value mentions independently**. Google's patent filings reference "implied links" (brand mentions without hyperlinks) as potential ranking signals
- **Scales with brand awareness**. As your brand grows, unlinked mentions increase automatically

For businesses publishing content at volume, unlinked mentions accumulate quickly as other sites reference your data, quotes, and resources.

## How Unlinked Mentions Work

### Finding Them

Set up Google Alerts for your brand name, product names, and key personnel. Tools like Ahrefs Content Explorer, Semrush Brand Monitoring, and Mention.com scan the web for references to your brand and flag which ones lack hyperlinks.

### Outreach Strategy

Keep emails short and friendly. Acknowledge the mention, thank them for the reference, and politely ask if they'd consider adding a link. Provide the exact URL you'd like linked. Don't be pushy. Most authors add the link within a few days because the ask is reasonable. They already chose to mention you.

### Monitoring Ongoing Mentions

Make unlinked mention monitoring a monthly habit. Set it up once with automated alerts, review new mentions each month, and send outreach in batches. The [link reclamation](/glossary/link-reclamation) compounds. Each converted mention permanently strengthens your site's authority.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I find unlinked mentions of my brand?

Ahrefs Content Explorer is the most popular tool. Search your brand name, then filter for pages that mention you but don't link to your domain. Google Alerts (free) catches new mentions in real-time. Semrush's Brand Monitoring tool also tracks mentions across the web.

### What conversion rate should I expect from outreach?

Unlinked mention outreach typically converts at 15-25%. Far higher than cold [link building](/glossary/link-building). The key is the ask is low-friction: the author already referenced you, so adding a link is a minor edit, not a content change.

### Do unlinked mentions help SEO even without the link?

Possibly. Google has patented concepts around "implied links". Brand references that convey trust without a hyperlink. Whether they actively use this is unconfirmed, but converting mentions into actual [backlinks](/glossary/backlinks) is always better.

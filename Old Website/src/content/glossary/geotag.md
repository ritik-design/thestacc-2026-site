## What is a Geotag?

A geotag is a location tag added to social media content. Posts, Stories, Reels, photos, or videos. That identifies the physical place where the content was created or the location it references.

On Instagram, it appears below your username as a clickable location name. On TikTok, it's a location sticker or tag. On Facebook, it's the "check-in" location. Tapping a geotag shows all public content tagged at that same location. Making your post discoverable to people browsing that place.

For businesses, geotagging is a free discovery mechanism. Later.com's 2024 data shows that Instagram posts with a location tag get 79% more engagement than posts without one. For local businesses, that means more visibility from people browsing your neighborhood, city, or venue. Without spending a dollar on ads.

## Why Do Geotags Matter?

Geotags connect your content to a place, making it discoverable to people interested in that location.

- **Local discovery**. Users searching for a city, neighborhood, or venue find your geotagged content in location feeds
- **Higher engagement**. Posts with geotags see 79% more engagement on Instagram (Later.com, 2024)
- **Local [SEO](/glossary/local-seo) signals**. Consistent geotagging across social platforms reinforces your business's connection to a specific area
- **Event visibility**. Geotagging event venues makes your content visible to all attendees browsing that location

Every local business should geotag every post. It's one of the simplest, most overlooked tactics for increasing [organic reach](/glossary/organic-reach) on social media.

## How Geotags Work

Geotags use GPS coordinates, platform location databases, or manual selection to attach place data to content.

### Automatic Location Detection

Most social platforms request GPS access from your phone. When you create a post, the app detects your current location and suggests nearby places. You select the correct venue, city, or landmark from a list.

### Manual Geotag Selection

You can also geotag a location you're not physically at. Useful for brands posting about multiple locations from a central office. Search for the location name in the platform's tagging interface and select it.

### Location Pages

Each geotag links to a location page that aggregates all public content tagged at that place. For businesses with a claimed [Google Business Profile](/glossary/google-business-profile) or Facebook Page, this location page can display business info alongside user-generated content. Creating a rich, social proof-driven discovery experience.

## Geotag Examples

**Example 1: Local restaurant.** A taco shop geotags every Instagram post and Story with their restaurant location. Tourists searching "tacos in Austin" on Instagram's Explore tab discover their geotagged content. Including customer photos and Stories from the past week. Free marketing from other people's content.

**Example 2: Multi-location business.** A dental practice with 5 locations geotags posts to each specific office. Patients browsing the "Riverside" location see reviews, behind-the-scenes content, and team photos from that specific office. Building local trust. This works alongside [geotagged images](/glossary/geotagging-images) on their website for SEO purposes.

**Example 3: Event marketing.** A B2B SaaS company attending a conference geotags all their posts with the venue location. Attendees browsing the event's geotag page discover the company's content alongside posts from speakers and other exhibitors. Their [social media marketing](/glossary/social-media-marketing) content gets discovered by a highly targeted audience.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do geotags help SEO?

Indirectly. Social geotags don't directly affect [Google rankings](/glossary/seo), but they reinforce geographic signals across your digital presence. Consistent location associations across social, [Google Business Profile](/glossary/google-business-profile), and your website strengthen local authority.

### Should I create a custom geotag for my business?

On Instagram and Facebook, business locations are auto-created from Facebook Pages. If yours doesn't exist, create a Facebook Page with your address and it'll appear as an Instagram geotag option. Snapchat allows custom geofilters for specific locations.

### Can geotagging hurt privacy?

For personal accounts, yes. Real-time geotags reveal your location. For business accounts, it's beneficial. Best practice: business accounts geotag freely; advise employees to avoid real-time location sharing from personal accounts.

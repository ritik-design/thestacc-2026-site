## What is a Webinar?

A webinar is a web-based seminar where a presenter shares slides, demos, or live instruction with an online audience who registers in advance and joins through a video conferencing link.

Webinars sit at the intersection of education and [lead generation](/glossary/lead-generation). Attendees exchange their contact information (email, name, company) for access to the session. The brand gets leads. The attendees get free education. Both parties win. When the content is genuinely valuable.

ON24's 2024 Webinar Benchmarks Report found that the average webinar generates 500+ registrations and converts 56% of attendees into qualified leads. For B2B companies, webinars remain one of the most effective middle-of-funnel tactics.

## Why Do Webinars Matter?

They generate qualified leads while positioning your brand as an authority.

- **Lead quality**. Webinar attendees are actively engaged with your topic. They've invested 30-60 minutes of their time. That's a warm lead
- **[Thought leadership](/glossary/thought-leadership)**. Presenting on a topic signals expertise. Attendees see your team as knowledgeable practitioners, not just another vendor
- **[Content repurposing](/glossary/content-repurposing)**. One webinar becomes: a replay video, blog post, social clips, email content, and a [lead magnet](/glossary/lead-magnet) download. 60 minutes of recording = weeks of content
- **Measurable ROI**. Registration data, attendance rates, engagement scores, and follow-up conversions give you clear metrics to optimize against

For B2B companies in particular, webinars are a pipeline-generating machine.

## How Webinars Work

### Planning and Promotion

Choose a topic with broad appeal to your target audience. Promote through email, social media, and paid ads 2-3 weeks before the event. Use a registration landing page that captures name, email, and company.

### Live Production

Host on platforms like Zoom, Webex, ON24, or Livestorm. Include slides, live demos, Q&A, and polls to keep the audience engaged. Most webinars run 45-60 minutes. Record everything.

### Post-Webinar Follow-Up

Send the replay to attendees and registrants who missed it. Follow up with additional resources. Segment leads by engagement level. Those who attended live and asked questions are your hottest prospects.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the best webinar length?

45-60 minutes including Q&A. Shorter webinars (20-30 minutes) work for product demos. Avoid going over 75 minutes. Attention drops sharply after the first hour.

### What's a good webinar attendance rate?

40-50% of registrants typically attend live. Send reminder emails 24 hours and 1 hour before the event. Always offer a replay to capture the other 50-60%.

### Are webinars still effective in 2026?

Yes. Webinar fatigue from 2020-2021 has subsided. Audiences now attend fewer webinars but choose more selectively. High-quality, topic-specific webinars perform better than ever.

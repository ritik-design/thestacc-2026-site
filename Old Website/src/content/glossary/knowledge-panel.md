## What is a Knowledge Panel?

A Knowledge Panel is the structured information box Google displays on the right side of desktop search results (or at the top on mobile) when it confidently identifies the entity behind a search query.

Knowledge Panels pull data from the [Knowledge Graph](/glossary/knowledge-graph) and display it in a visual card format. For businesses, they typically show the company name, logo, description, social profiles, and key facts. For people, they include birth date, occupation, notable works, and related entities.

According to a Kalicube study, verified Knowledge Panels increase brand click-through rates by an average of 30% because they dominate SERP real estate and communicate trust and authority instantly.

## Why Does Knowledge Panel Matter?

A Knowledge Panel is one of the strongest brand signals you can earn in Google Search.

- **SERP dominance**. Knowledge Panels take up significant screen space, pushing competitors lower in visual hierarchy
- **Brand credibility**. Google only creates Knowledge Panels for entities it can confidently verify, so having one signals legitimacy to searchers
- **Information control**. You can claim and suggest edits to your Knowledge Panel, giving you influence over how Google describes your brand
- **Zero-click brand exposure**. Even searchers who don't click get your logo, description, and key facts. Free brand awareness

For local businesses, the [Google Business Profile](/glossary/google-business-profile) listing often functions as a de facto Knowledge Panel with hours, reviews, and location info.

## How Knowledge Panel Works

### How Google Creates Them

Google generates Knowledge Panels when it has enough data about an entity from trusted sources. Wikipedia and Wikidata are the primary sources for people and organizations. [Schema markup](/glossary/schema-markup) (particularly Organization and Person types) helps Google connect your website to the entity it recognizes.

### Claiming Your Panel

If a Knowledge Panel exists for your entity, you can claim it through Google's verification process. Search your entity name, click "Claim this knowledge panel," and verify ownership via Google Search Console, YouTube, or your official website. Once claimed, you can suggest edits to the description, images, and social profiles.

### Influencing Panel Content

You can't directly edit Knowledge Panel content. You suggest changes and Google reviews them. But you influence what appears by maintaining consistent information across Wikipedia, Wikidata, your website's [JSON-LD](/glossary/json-ld) markup, social profiles, and authoritative mentions across the web.

## Knowledge Panel Examples

**Example 1: A verified business Knowledge Panel**
A law firm claims their Knowledge Panel and adds their logo, founder photo, and practice area description. When potential clients search the firm name, they see a professional card with ratings, address, phone number, and a direct link to the website. Before they even look at organic results.

**Example 2: Earning a panel through authority building**
A B2B SaaS founder publishes thought leadership content, earns press mentions in TechCrunch and Forbes, and maintains a Wikipedia stub. Google creates a personal Knowledge Panel linking them to their company. Branded searches now show both the personal panel and the company's [SERP features](/glossary/serp-features).
## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I get a Knowledge Panel for my business?

Start with a verified [Google Business Profile](/glossary/google-business-profile), Organization schema on your website, and consistent business information across authoritative directories. A Wikipedia page significantly increases your chances, but it's not required. The more trusted sources confirm your entity's details, the more likely Google is to create a panel.

### Can I edit my Knowledge Panel?

After claiming it, you can *suggest* edits. Google reviews suggestions and applies them if they're supported by public sources. Changes to descriptions, images, and social links are commonly accepted. Factual claims need verifiable sources.

### Why did my Knowledge Panel disappear?

Knowledge Panels can disappear if Google loses confidence in the entity data. Often due to conflicting information across sources, a Wikipedia page deletion, or removed schema markup. Audit your entity information across all platforms and ensure consistency.

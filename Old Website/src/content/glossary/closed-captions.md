## What Are Closed Captions?

Closed captions are synchronized text that displays the audio content of a video. Including dialogue, music cues, and sound effects. That viewers can toggle on or off.

Unlike open captions (burned into the video permanently), closed captions are a separate text layer the viewer controls. They were originally created for deaf and hard-of-hearing audiences, but their use has expanded dramatically. Verizon Media found that 80% of people who use captions are not deaf or hard of hearing. They're watching in public without headphones, in noisy environments, or simply prefer reading along.

For social media marketers, this is critical. 85% of Facebook video is watched on mute. If your video doesn't have captions, most of your audience misses the message entirely.

## Why Do Closed Captions Matter?

Captions aren't optional anymore. They're a reach multiplier.

- **[Accessibility](/glossary/accessibility-content)**. Captions make video content available to 466 million people globally with hearing loss. It's an ethical and legal requirement in many cases
- **Higher [engagement rate](/glossary/engagement-rate)**. Captioned videos on Facebook see 12% longer view times. On Instagram Reels and TikTok, captions keep viewers watching even on mute
- **Better [video completion rates](/glossary/video-completion-rate)**. When people can follow along via text, they're less likely to drop off. Completion rates improve 15-25% with captions added
- **SEO value**. Platforms that index caption text (YouTube, Facebook) can use it for search and recommendation algorithms. Captions = more keywords for the algorithm to work with

Every video you publish should have captions. No exceptions.

## How Closed Captions Work

### Auto-Generated Captions

Most platforms (YouTube, TikTok, Instagram) auto-generate captions using speech recognition. They're 85-90% accurate. Good enough for casual viewing but often wrong on names, technical terms, and accents. Always review and edit.

### Manual or Professional Captions

Upload SRT files for frame-accurate captions. Tools like Rev, Descript, and CapCut let you generate and edit caption files quickly. For important content (ads, branded videos), manual review is worth the extra 10 minutes.

### Burned-In Captions for Social

Many creators add text directly onto their social videos (open captions) because they're always visible. No toggle needed. This is especially common on Instagram Reels and TikTok where viewers expect text on screen.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the difference between closed and open captions?

Closed captions can be turned on or off by the viewer (like YouTube's CC button). Open captions are permanently part of the video and can't be toggled. Social media creators often use open captions for maximum visibility.

### How do you add captions to social media videos?

Most platforms auto-generate them. For better accuracy, use editing tools like Descript, CapCut, or Kapwing to add and style captions before uploading. Upload SRT files on YouTube for precise control.

### Do captions improve SEO?

On YouTube, yes. YouTube indexes caption text and uses it for video search and recommendations. On other platforms, captions help indirectly by increasing watch time and engagement. Both signals the algorithm rewards.

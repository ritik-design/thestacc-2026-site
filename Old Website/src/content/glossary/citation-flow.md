## What is Citation Flow?

Citation Flow is Majestic's metric for measuring a URL's link influence based purely on how many sites point to it. Without evaluating the quality or trustworthiness of those linking sites.

Think of it as link popularity. A site with 10,000 backlinks from 500 domains will have a high Citation Flow regardless of whether those links come from authoritative news publications or low-quality directories. Citation Flow tells you "this site gets a lot of links." It doesn't tell you "this site gets good links."

That's why Citation Flow is most useful when paired with [Trust Flow](/glossary/trust-flow). The ratio between the two reveals the health of a [link profile](/glossary/link-profile). A site with Citation Flow of 50 and Trust Flow of 45 has a balanced, healthy profile. A site with Citation Flow of 60 and Trust Flow of 10 has a profile inflated by low-quality links.

## Why Does Citation Flow Matter?

Citation Flow is one half of the quality-quantity equation for backlink analysis.

- **Quick link quantity check**. High Citation Flow confirms a site has accumulated many [backlinks](/glossary/backlinks), which correlates with search visibility
- **Spam detection**. A huge gap between Citation Flow and [Trust Flow](/glossary/trust-flow) signals a profile full of low-quality or spammy links
- **Competitive analysis**. Comparing Citation Flow across competitors shows who's attracting the most link attention
- **Link prospect evaluation**. Checking a potential link partner's Citation Flow reveals whether they have enough link equity to pass meaningful value

Used alone, Citation Flow is incomplete. Used alongside Trust Flow, it's a powerful diagnostic tool.

## How Citation Flow Works

### The Calculation

Majestic calculates Citation Flow by analyzing the number and connectivity of links pointing to a URL or domain. Pages with more inbound links score higher. The calculation follows a recursive model. Pages linked from high-Citation-Flow pages inherit higher scores themselves.

### The Trust Ratio

Divide Trust Flow by Citation Flow. A ratio above 0.7 indicates a high-quality profile. Between 0.4 and 0.7 is average. Below 0.4 suggests the profile is padded with low-quality links. This ratio is the real actionable insight from the two metrics.

### Practical Application

When evaluating a site for a link building opportunity, check both metrics. A site with CF 40 / TF 35 is a solid link prospect. A site with CF 55 / TF 8 should be avoided. Despite the high Citation Flow, the links passing to you won't carry real authority.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Citation Flow the same as Domain Authority?

No. Citation Flow is Majestic's quantity-based metric. [Domain Authority](/glossary/domain-authority) is Moz's composite metric that weighs both quality and quantity. They use different data sources and algorithms. Checking both gives a more complete picture.

### Can Citation Flow be manipulated?

Yes. Which is exactly why it's incomplete alone. Building thousands of spammy links inflates Citation Flow without improving actual search performance. [Trust Flow](/glossary/trust-flow) catches this manipulation because low-quality links don't improve trust scores.

### What's a good Citation Flow score?

Relative to your niche. Small local businesses typically range from 15-35. National brands range from 40-60. Major publications exceed 70. Compare to direct competitors rather than chasing an absolute benchmark.

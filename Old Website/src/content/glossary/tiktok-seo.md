## What is TikTok SEO?

TikTok SEO is the process of optimizing your TikTok content. Captions, hashtags, on-screen text, voiceover, and even the words you say. So it ranks in TikTok's internal search engine and gets recommended to users searching for specific topics.

For years, TikTok was purely a discovery platform. You posted videos and hoped the [FYP algorithm](/glossary/fyp-for-you-page) pushed them to the right people. That's still true. But TikTok has aggressively built out search functionality since 2022, adding a search bar, search suggestions, keyword highlighting in comments, and even a "search results" feature inside the FYP itself.

The numbers back this up. An internal Google study leaked in 2022 revealed that nearly 40% of Gen Z users prefer TikTok or Instagram over Google when searching for things like restaurants, recipes, and how-to information. TikTok isn't just a social app anymore. It's a search engine with 1.5 billion monthly users.

## Why Does TikTok SEO Matter?

Traditional [SEO](/glossary/seo) targets Google. TikTok SEO targets a different search engine. One that's growing faster than any other and reaches an audience that's increasingly hard to capture through traditional search.

- **40% of Gen Z searches start on TikTok**. Not Google, not Bing. TikTok. If your target audience skews under 35, they're searching here first.
- **Evergreen discovery**. Unlike the FYP where content has a 48-72 hour viral window, search-optimized TikTok videos can get views for months. A well-optimized "how to fix a leaky faucet" video surfaces every time someone searches that query.
- **Low competition**. Most businesses still aren't optimizing for TikTok search. The keyword competition is a fraction of what you'd face on Google. Early movers win big.
- **Purchase intent**. TikTok searches often carry commercial intent. "Best running shoes 2026" or "dentist near me" on TikTok signals someone actively looking for a product or service.
- **Cross-platform visibility**. Google has started indexing TikTok videos in its search results. A search-optimized TikTok video can rank on both TikTok AND Google simultaneously.

For businesses already investing in Google SEO, TikTok SEO is the next frontier. And the effort required is a fraction of traditional [keyword research](/glossary/keyword-research) and content production.

## How TikTok SEO Works

### Keyword Signals TikTok Reads

TikTok's algorithm processes multiple text signals to understand what your video is about:

1. **Caption text**. The description below your video. Include target keywords naturally here.
2. **On-screen text**. Text overlays within the video itself. TikTok's OCR (optical character recognition) reads these and factors them into search ranking.
3. **Spoken words**. TikTok transcribes your audio using automatic speech recognition. Keywords you actually say in the video influence search ranking.
4. **Hashtags**. Still relevant, but less about discovery feeds and more about topical categorization. Use 3-5 specific hashtags.
5. **Comments**. TikTok now highlights keywords in comments and links them to search. Pinning a keyword-rich comment on your video can help.

### The Search Ranking Algorithm

TikTok's search ranking considers content relevance (keyword match), engagement metrics (likes, shares, completion rate), content freshness, and creator authority in the topic. High-[engagement](/glossary/engagement-rate) videos that match the search query rank highest.

Unlike Google, TikTok also factors in visual content analysis. Its AI scans what's actually happening in the video to verify relevance. A video captioned "plumbing tips" that actually shows someone cooking won't rank for plumbing searches.

### Keyword Research for TikTok

TikTok doesn't have an official keyword tool (yet). Instead:
- Type keywords into TikTok's search bar and note the autocomplete suggestions
- Check "Others searched for" at the bottom of search results
- Look at the "Search" button TikTok adds above comments on popular videos
- Use Google Trends filtered to "Web Search" for topic validation

## Types of TikTok SEO Content

Different content formats serve different search intents:

- **How-to videos** ,  "How to unclog a drain," "how to file an LLC." These target informational [search intent](/glossary/search-intent) and get the longest shelf life in search results.
- **Best-of / recommendation videos** ,  "Best coffee shops in Austin," "best CRM for small businesses." These target commercial intent and often lead to website visits or purchases.
- **Myth-busting videos** ,  "Is [common belief] actually true?" These target question-based searches and generate high engagement through debate.
- **Review and comparison videos** ,  "Shopify vs WooCommerce" or "honest review of [product]." High commercial intent, strong search demand.
- **Local content** ,  "Things to do in [city]," "best [food] in [neighborhood]." Perfect for local businesses trying to reach nearby customers.

## TikTok SEO vs. Google SEO

Both are search optimization. But the platforms, formats, and strategies differ significantly.

| | TikTok SEO | Google SEO |
|---|---|---|
| **Content format** | [Short-form video](/glossary/short-form-video) (15-180 seconds) | Written content (blog posts, pages) |
| **Primary ranking signal** | Engagement + keyword relevance | [Backlinks](/glossary/backlinks) + content quality + relevance |
| **Keyword competition** | Low. Still early stage | High. Decades of optimization |
| **Time to rank** | Hours to days | Weeks to months |
| **Content lifespan** | Months (search) / days (FYP) | Years |
| **Audience** | Skews 16-34 | All demographics |
| **Tools** | TikTok search bar, manual research | Google Search Console, Ahrefs, Semrush |

Smart businesses do both. TikTok SEO captures the next generation of searchers. Google SEO captures the high-intent traffic that converts today.

## TikTok SEO Best Practices

- **Say your keywords out loud**. TikTok transcribes audio. If you're targeting "best dentist in Chicago," actually say those words in the video. Spoken keywords are a ranking signal most creators ignore.
- **Put keywords in on-screen text**. TikTok's OCR reads text overlays. Place your primary keyword as on-screen text within the first 3 seconds. This helps both the algorithm and viewers understand what the video covers.
- **Write searchable captions**. Don't waste your caption on emojis and vague statements. Write a caption that includes your target keyword naturally: "Here's how to fix a leaky faucet without calling a plumber."
- **Create content around TikTok autocomplete**. Type your topic into TikTok's search bar and make videos answering the suggested searches. These are real queries with real demand.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is TikTok really a search engine?

Functionally, yes. TikTok has a search bar, autocomplete suggestions, ranked search results, and keyword-based content indexing. 40% of Gen Z uses it as their primary search tool for certain queries.

### How do you find TikTok keywords?

Use TikTok's search bar autocomplete, check "Others searched for" suggestions, browse the keyword highlights in comments, and validate demand using Google Trends. No official keyword volume tool exists yet.

### Does TikTok SEO work for local businesses?

Extremely well. Location-based searches like "best [service] in [city]" are some of the highest-performing TikTok search queries. Local businesses that include city names in captions, text, and spoken audio dominate these results.

### How long do TikTok search results last?

Search-optimized videos can rank for months. Sometimes over a year. This is different from FYP content, which typically peaks within 48-72 hours. Search is TikTok's evergreen content channel.

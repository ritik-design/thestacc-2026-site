## What is Behind-the-Scenes Content?

Behind-the-scenes content pulls back the curtain on your business. Showing how products are made, how your team works, office culture, decision-making processes, and the messy reality that polished marketing hides.

It's the opposite of perfectly curated content. BTS content is raw, relatable, and real. A video of your team packing orders at midnight. A photo of the whiteboard during a brainstorm session. A Story showing the 5 failed attempts before the perfect shot. Audiences crave this because it makes brands feel human.

Sprout Social's 2024 data found that 64% of consumers want brands to connect with them authentically. BTS content is one of the most reliable ways to do that.

## Why Does Behind-the-Scenes Content Matter?

Trust is built through transparency. BTS content is transparency in action.

- **Authenticity**. In an era of polished ads and filtered content, raw BTS footage stands out. It signals: "We're real people, not a marketing department"
- **Higher [engagement rates](/glossary/engagement-rate)**. BTS posts consistently outperform product posts on engagement. People love seeing the human side of businesses
- **[Brand voice](/glossary/brand-voice) reinforcement**. BTS content shows your culture, values, and personality in a way that scripted content can't
- **Low production cost**. BTS content is expected to be informal. A phone camera and natural lighting are all you need. No designer required

For small businesses and local brands, BTS content is the easiest way to compete with bigger brands on social media.

## How Behind-the-Scenes Content Works

### Choose What to Show

Product creation processes, team meetings, packaging and shipping, office tours, event prep, bloopers, and "day in the life" formats all work. Anything that answers the question: "What actually goes on here?"

### Keep It Raw

Don't over-edit BTS content. The whole point is authenticity. Quick phone videos, casual photos, and real-time [Stories](/glossary/ephemeral-content) perform better than produced clips. People can smell a fake "behind the scenes" a mile away.

### Post Regularly

Make BTS a recurring part of your [content mix](/glossary/content-mix). One BTS post per week keeps your brand feeling approachable. Use Stories for daily micro-BTS moments and feed posts for bigger BTS narratives.

## Behind-the-Scenes Content Examples

**A local bakery** films a 15-second TikTok of their baker hand-rolling croissants at 4 AM. No music, no effects. Just the sound of dough and a dark kitchen. The video gets 180,000 views and 4,000 likes. People love seeing the craft.

**A SaaS company** posts a LinkedIn photo of their whiteboard after a strategy session, with the caption: "This is what planning our next quarter actually looks like." It generates 150 comments from other founders sharing their own messy planning processes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What kind of BTS content performs best?

Process videos (how things are made), team moments, and "day in the life" content tend to get the most engagement. Anything that feels genuine and unscripted resonates.

### Do customers actually care about BTS content?

Yes. Consumers report higher trust in brands that show their process and people. It's especially powerful for local businesses, D2C brands, and companies with a strong company culture.

### How do you create BTS content without it feeling forced?

Capture real moments as they happen rather than staging them. Keep your phone handy. Film the actual messiness of work. If you're thinking about lighting and angles, it's probably not genuine BTS.

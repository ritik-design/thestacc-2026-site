## What is Trust Flow?

Trust Flow is Majestic's proprietary metric that predicts how trustworthy a page or domain is based on the quality of websites linking to it. Scored on a scale from 0 to 100.

The concept starts with a curated seed set of manually reviewed, highly trusted websites (think Wikipedia, government sites, major news outlets). Sites directly linked from these seeds inherit high Trust Flow. Sites linked from those sites inherit some trust, and so on. Like a chain of recommendations. Each hop away from the seed set reduces the trust passed.

Trust Flow works alongside [Citation Flow](/glossary/citation-flow), which measures link quantity regardless of quality. The ratio between the two tells a useful story. A site with high Citation Flow but low Trust Flow likely has many links from low-quality sources. A balanced ratio suggests a healthy [link profile](/glossary/link-profile).

## Why Does Trust Flow Matter?

Trust Flow gives you a quick read on link quality. Something raw [backlink](/glossary/backlinks) counts can't tell you.

- **Evaluates potential link partners**. Before pursuing a link from a site, check its Trust Flow to assess whether the link is worth pursuing
- **Identifies toxic link patterns**. A big gap between Citation Flow and Trust Flow signals a profile heavy on low-quality links
- **Benchmarks against competitors**. Comparing Trust Flow scores reveals who has the strongest authority foundation
- **Guides [link building](/glossary/link-building) strategy**. Target links from high-Trust Flow sites for maximum ranking impact

While Google doesn't use Majestic's specific metric, Trust Flow correlates well with real ranking performance because it's measuring the same underlying concept Google cares about: trust through quality links.

## How Trust Flow Works

### The Seed Set

Majestic maintains a list of manually vetted, highly authoritative websites. These seeds start with the maximum Trust Flow score. Sites directly linked from seeds receive high trust. The further a site is from the seed set in the link graph, the less trust it inherits.

### Topical Trust Flow

Majestic also offers Topical Trust Flow, which breaks down trust by category. A site might have high Trust Flow for "Health" topics but low Trust Flow for "Finance." This tells you whether a linking site's authority is relevant to your niche. A health site linking to your health content passes more relevant trust than a sports site linking to the same page.

### Trust Flow vs Citation Flow Ratio

A Trust Flow of 30 and [Citation Flow](/glossary/citation-flow) of 35 suggests a balanced, healthy profile. A Trust Flow of 8 with Citation Flow of 55 suggests many low-quality links inflating the quantity metric without corresponding quality. Aim for your Trust Flow to be within 70-80% of your Citation Flow value.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Trust Flow the same as Domain Authority?

No. [Domain Authority](/glossary/domain-authority) is Moz's metric based on their own link index. Trust Flow is Majestic's metric emphasizing link quality specifically. They measure related but different things. Using both together gives a more complete picture.

### What's a good Trust Flow score?

It's relative to your niche. Local businesses typically have Trust Flow between 10-30. National brands range from 30-60. Major publications and government sites exceed 70. Compare to your direct competitors rather than chasing an absolute number.

### Can I improve Trust Flow quickly?

Not ethically. Trust Flow improves when you earn links from sites that themselves have high Trust Flow. That takes time and genuine relationship building. Quick link acquisition schemes typically increase [Citation Flow](/glossary/citation-flow) without touching Trust Flow. Making the ratio worse.

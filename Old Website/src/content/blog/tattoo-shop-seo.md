## Google Business Profile for Tattoo Shops

Your [Google Business Profile](/blog/optimize-google-business-profile) determines whether you appear in the map pack when someone searches "tattoo shop near me." [42% of local searchers click on map pack results](https://www.brightlocal.com/resources/local-seo-statistics/). Businesses with complete profiles are [70% more likely to attract in-person visits](https://support.google.com/business).

### Set Up Your GBP Right

- [ ] **Primary category:** "Tattoo Shop" (most specific available)
- [ ] **Secondary categories:** "Body Piercing Shop" (if you offer piercings), "Art Studio"
- [ ] **Business description:** 750 characters. Lead with your styles, location, and what makes your shop different
- [ ] **Services:** List every service (custom tattoos, flash, cover-ups, piercings, tattoo removal consultations)
- [ ] **Business hours:** Include walk-in hours if different from appointment hours
- [ ] **Phone number:** Local number, not toll-free
- [ ] **Website:** Link to your homepage or booking page
- [ ] **Booking link:** Add a direct link to your online booking system
- [ ] **Attributes:** Wheelchair accessible, appointment required, walk-ins welcome, LGBTQ+ friendly

For more on choosing the right [GBP categories](/blog/gbp-categories-guide), see our detailed guide.

### Photos That Drive Bookings

GBP photos increase direction requests by 40%. For a tattoo shop, your photos are your portfolio. Upload:

- **Healed tattoo work** (not just fresh ink. Healed tattoos show true quality)
- **Each artist's best pieces** across different styles
- **Shop interior** (clean, professional workspace builds trust)
- **Team photos** (clients want to know who will tattoo them)
- **Process shots** (setup, stencil application, in-progress work)

Add 5 to 10 new photos per month. Google rewards active listings with better visibility.

## Keyword Strategy for Tattoo Shop SEO

Most tattoo shops target "tattoo shop" or "tattoo artist." Those terms are competitive and generic. The real wins come from combining style, location, and intent.

![Tattoo shop SEO keyword tiers by priority](/images/blog/tattoo-seo-keyword-tiers.webp)

### The Tattoo Keyword Framework

| Tier | Pattern | Example | Competition |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1: Location + shop** | "tattoo shop [city]" | "tattoo shop Austin TX" | Medium |
| **Tier 2: Style + location** | "[style] tattoo artist [city]" | "Japanese tattoo artist Portland" | Low |
| **Tier 3: Placement + style** | "[body part] tattoo ideas" | "forearm tattoo ideas for men" | Very low (high volume) |
| **Tier 4: Questions** | "how much does a [type] tattoo cost" | "how much does a sleeve tattoo cost" | Very low |
| **Tier 5: Aftercare** | "tattoo aftercare [topic]" | "how long does a tattoo take to heal" | Very low |

### Where to Start

Create dedicated pages for each tattoo style you offer:

- `/styles/traditional-tattoo`. Target "traditional tattoo artist [city]"
- `/styles/realism-tattoo`. Target "realistic tattoo artist [city]"
- `/styles/fine-line-tattoo`. Target "fine line tattoo artist [city]"
- `/styles/japanese-tattoo`. Target "Japanese tattoo artist [city]"
- `/styles/blackwork-tattoo`. Target "blackwork tattoo artist [city]"

Each page includes example work, a description of the style, pricing guidance, and a booking link. These pages rank for long-tail keywords that generic portfolio pages never capture.

### Artist Profile Pages

Create a dedicated page for each tattoo artist at your shop. Include their name, specialties, years of experience, a curated portfolio, and a direct booking link. Artist profile pages target "[artist name] tattoo" searches and capture clients who research specific artists before booking.

Artist pages also help with Google's E-E-A-T signals. Named artists with verifiable portfolios and bios signal real expertise to Google. An anonymous shop with no artist information looks less trustworthy than one that showcases its team.

### Local Landing Pages

If your shop serves clients from multiple cities, create dedicated landing pages for each city. "Tattoo Shop in Round Rock TX" and "Tattoo Shop in Georgetown TX" target different local searches. Each page needs unique content about serving that area. Do not duplicate the same text with only the city name swapped.

## Portfolio Image SEO for Tattoo Shops

Tattoo shops live and die by visual content. Your portfolio is your biggest selling tool. But most tattoo websites have zero image SEO. Images named "IMG_4872.jpg" with no alt text. Google cannot index what it cannot read.

![Tattoo portfolio image SEO checklist](/images/blog/tattoo-seo-image-checklist.webp)

### The Tattoo Image SEO Checklist

- [ ] **Rename every file descriptively.** "black-grey-portrait-tattoo-forearm-austin.jpg" ranks in Google Images. "IMG_4872.jpg" does not.
- [ ] **Write alt text with keywords.** "Black and grey portrait tattoo on forearm by [Artist Name] at [Shop Name], Austin TX." This describes the image for Google and screen readers.
- [ ] **Compress images to under 200KB.** Use WebP format. Tools: ShortPixel, TinyPNG, or Squoosh. Large uncompressed images kill page speed.
- [ ] **Use lazy loading.** Only load images as the visitor scrolls down. Keeps initial page load fast.
- [ ] **Add structured data.** ImageObject schema with description, creator, and location properties.
- [ ] **Organize by style and artist.** Separate gallery pages by tattoo style and by artist. Each page becomes a ranking target.

For a deeper guide, see our post on [blog image optimization for SEO](/blog/blog-image-optimization-seo).

### Why Image SEO Matters for Tattoo Shops

People search Google Images for tattoo inspiration. "Forearm tattoo ideas," "sleeve tattoo designs," and "small tattoo ideas" generate millions of searches per month. If your portfolio images appear in those results with proper alt text and filenames, they drive traffic to your site without any additional effort.

## Reviews and Reputation for Tattoo Shops

Getting tattooed is a trust decision. Clients put permanent art on their body. They research heavily before booking. [68% of consumers only use businesses rated 4+ stars](https://www.brightlocal.com/resources/local-seo-statistics/). For tattoo shops, that threshold is even higher. Most clients want 4.5+ stars before they trust an artist with their skin.

### How to Build Reviews Consistently

62% of tattoo inquiries happen outside business hours, between 7 and 9 PM. That means your best review request window is immediately after the appointment, when the client is excited about their new piece.

**The tattoo shop review system:**

1. **At checkout:** "We would love a Google review if you have a minute. Here is the link." (Hand them a card with a QR code)
2. **Same-day text:** Send an automated follow-up text 2 hours after the appointment with a direct Google review link
3. **Aftercare email (day 3):** Send aftercare instructions with a gentle review reminder at the bottom
4. **Social media moment:** When the client posts their tattoo and tags your shop, reply with a thank-you and a review request

For the complete review playbook, see our guide on [getting more Google reviews](/blog/get-more-google-reviews-local-business).

### Responding to Reviews

Respond to every review within 24 to 48 hours. Thank positive reviewers by name and mention their tattoo style or the artist who did the work. For negative reviews, acknowledge the concern, avoid being defensive, and take it offline. See our guide on [responding to negative reviews](/blog/respond-to-negative-google-reviews).

## On-Page SEO for Tattoo Shop Websites

Most tattoo shop websites prioritize aesthetics over structure. Dark themes, full-screen images, minimal text. Beautiful to look at. Invisible to Google.

### The Tattoo Shop On-Page Checklist

- [ ] Title tags include style, service, and city ("Japanese Tattoo Artist Austin TX | [Shop Name]")
- [ ] Meta descriptions describe your specialty and location (145 to 155 characters)
- [ ] H1 tag on every page matches the topic
- [ ] Artist pages include name, specialties, bio, and portfolio
- [ ] Style pages include descriptions, examples, and pricing ranges
- [ ] [Schema markup](/blog/on-page-seo-guide) with LocalBusiness type (use TattooParlor subtype if available)
- [ ] Mobile-responsive design (most tattoo clients browse on phones)
- [ ] Page loads under 3 seconds (compress portfolio images)
- [ ] NAP (Name, Address, Phone) in the footer of every page
- [ ] Online booking button visible on every page

## Google Business Profile: Your Most Important Asset

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) accounts for [over 30% of local ranking signals](https://americanmedspa.org/blog/top-10-google-ranking-factors-for-medical-spas). It is the first thing patients see when they search "med spa near me" or "[treatment] in [city]."

### Claim and Verify

If you have not claimed your GBP listing, do it today. Go to [business.google.com](https://business.google.com), search for your med spa, and follow the verification process. Google may verify by phone, email, postcard, or video.

An unclaimed profile means someone else could edit your listing. It also means you cannot respond to reviews, post updates, or add services.

### Optimize Every Field

Fill out every available field. Incomplete profiles rank lower.

| GBP Field | What to Enter | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Business name | Exact legal name (no keyword stuffing) | Matching NAP across all directories |
| Primary category | Medical Spa | Determines which searches you appear in |
| Secondary categories | Day Spa, Skin Care Clinic, Laser Hair Removal | Expands visibility for treatment searches |
| Description | 750 chars: services, location, differentiator | Helps Google match you to relevant queries |
| Services | List every treatment with description | Shows in GBP service menu |
| Appointment URL | Direct booking link | Removes friction from search to booking |
| Hours | Accurate hours including holidays | Prevents patients from showing up to a closed location |
| Photos | Reception, treatment rooms, team, results | Profiles with 100+ photos get 520% more calls |

### Post Weekly Updates

GBP posts signal that your business is active. Post at least once per week.

**Post types that work for med spas:**
- New treatment announcements
- Seasonal specials (spring skin prep, summer body contouring)
- Before-and-after results (with patient consent)
- Educational tips (sunscreen guidance, skincare routines)
- Event announcements (open houses, VIP nights)

Each post should include a call to action: "Book Now," "Learn More," or "Call Today."

## Service Pages: The Foundation of Med Spa SEO

A single "Services" page listing all your treatments is not enough. Each treatment needs its own dedicated page. This is the single most impactful change most med spas can make.

### Why Individual Service Pages Win

Google ranks pages, not websites. A page titled "Botox in Dallas" targets that specific keyword. A page titled "Our Services" targets nothing.

Individual service pages also give you:
- A unique URL to rank for each treatment keyword
- Space for treatment-specific content (600+ words)
- A dedicated landing page for Google Ads
- Structured data (schema markup) for each service
- Internal linking opportunities between related treatments

### The Ideal Service Page Structure

Every med spa service page should follow this structure:

**1. H1 headline with treatment + location**
Example: "Botox in Dallas, TX | [Med Spa Name]"

**2. Opening paragraph (60 to 80 words)**
What the treatment is, who it is for, and why patients choose your med spa for it. Include the primary keyword in the first sentence.

**3. How it works section (H2)**
Explain the procedure in plain language. Patients want to know what happens during their appointment. Cover preparation, procedure duration, and what to expect.

**4. Benefits section (H2)**
List 5 to 8 specific benefits. Use bullet points. Focus on outcomes patients care about: reduced wrinkles, smoother skin, no downtime, natural-looking results.

**5. Ideal candidate section (H2)**
Who is a good candidate for this treatment. This helps with [search intent matching](/blog/search-intent-guide) and reduces unqualified inquiries.

**6. Before and after gallery (H2)**
Real patient photos with consent. Before-and-after images build trust and keep visitors on the page longer. Both signals help rankings.

**7. Pricing or cost range (H2)**
Patients search for pricing. If you do not publish it, they leave your site and find someone who does. A price range ("Botox starts at $12 per unit") is sufficient.

**8. FAQ section (H2)**
Answer the 4 to 6 most common questions about the treatment. Use the exact questions patients ask (from Google autocomplete and "People Also Ask"). [FAQ schema markup](/blog/schema-markup-for-blog-posts) can earn rich results in search.

**9. Call to action**
A clear booking button or contact form above the fold and at the bottom. Include your phone number for patients who prefer calling.

### Minimum Content Length

Each service page needs a minimum of 600 words. The top-ranking med spa service pages average 800 to 1,200 words. More content gives Google more context to understand what the page is about and which queries to rank it for.

Do not pad content with fluff. Every sentence should inform, educate, or move the patient closer to booking.

## Local SEO: Ranking in the Map Pack

The Google Map Pack (the 3-pack of local results with a map) appears for nearly every "near me" and location-based med spa search. Ranking in the Map Pack puts your med spa above all organic results.

### The 3 Factors Google Uses

Google ranks local results based on 3 factors:

1. **Relevance**. How well your profile matches the search query. Detailed service descriptions and accurate categories improve relevance.
2. **Distance**. How close your med spa is to the searcher. You cannot change your location, but you can target nearby neighborhoods in your content.
3. **Prominence**. How well-known your business is online. Reviews, citations, backlinks, and website authority all contribute to prominence.

### NAP Consistency

NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone Number. Your NAP must be identical everywhere it appears online: GBP, website, Yelp, Healthgrades, RealSelf, Facebook, and every other directory.

Inconsistent NAP confuses Google and reduces your local ranking. Use the same format everywhere. If your address is "123 Main St, Suite 200," do not list it as "123 Main Street #200" on another site.

### Build Local Citations

Citations are mentions of your med spa on other websites. The more consistent citations you have, the stronger your local signals.

**Priority directories for med spas:**
- Google Business Profile
- Yelp
- Healthgrades
- RealSelf
- Zocdoc
- Vitals
- Facebook
- Apple Maps
- Bing Places
- YellowPages

Each directory listing should include your NAP, website URL, business hours, and a description of services. [Local citation building](/blog/local-seo-guide) is one of the most reliable ways to improve Map Pack rankings.

## Reviews: The Ranking Factor You Control

Google reviews directly influence your Map Pack rankings and your conversion rate. 83% of patients check Google reviews before booking a med spa appointment. The med spas with the most reviews and highest ratings win.

### How Many Reviews You Need

There is no magic number. But the data is clear: med spas with 50+ Google reviews appear in the Map Pack far more often than those with 10 or fewer. Aim for a consistent flow of new reviews every month.

Review velocity matters more than total count. A med spa that gets 8 new reviews per month outranks one with 200 total reviews but none in the last 3 months. Google values recency.

### How to Get More Reviews

The best time to ask for a review is right after a positive experience. Build a system around it.

**Review generation checklist:**
- [ ] Place a QR code at the front desk linking to your Google review page
- [ ] Send an automated text or email 24 hours after each appointment
- [ ] Train staff to ask satisfied patients for a review before they leave
- [ ] Include a review link in your post-treatment follow-up emails
- [ ] Respond to every review within 24 to 48 hours

The QR code and automated follow-up combination consistently generates the most reviews. Make it as easy as possible. One tap. No login required.

### How to Respond to Reviews

Respond to every review. Positive and negative. Google has confirmed that review responses are a local ranking signal.

**For positive reviews:** Thank the patient by name (if they shared it). Mention the treatment they received. Invite them back.

**For negative reviews:** Acknowledge the concern. Apologize for the experience. Offer to resolve it offline. Never argue, get defensive, or share patient details.

Review responses also show prospective patients that you care about the experience. That trust signal influences booking decisions more than most marketing tactics.

## Technical SEO for Med Spa Websites

Technical SEO ensures Google can crawl, index, and rank your pages. Most med spa websites have fixable issues that hold them back.

### Mobile Optimization

Over 70% of med spa searches happen on mobile devices. If your site is not mobile-friendly, you lose both rankings and patients.

**Mobile checklist:**
- [ ] Pages load in under 3 seconds on mobile
- [ ] Text is readable without zooming
- [ ] Buttons and links are large enough to tap
- [ ] Images resize correctly on smaller screens
- [ ] No horizontal scrolling required
- [ ] Booking forms work on mobile

Use Google PageSpeed Insights to test your site. Fix anything scoring below 80 on mobile. [Core Web Vitals](/blog/improve-core-web-vitals) are a confirmed ranking factor. Slow pages rank lower.

### Schema Markup

[Schema markup](/blog/schema-markup-seo-guide) helps Google understand your content. For med spas, 3 types matter most:

**LocalBusiness schema:** Tells Google your name, address, phone, hours, and service area. Required for strong local SEO.

**MedicalBusiness schema:** A more specific type that identifies your business as a healthcare provider. Adds credibility signals.

**FAQPage schema:** Turns your FAQ sections into rich results in Google search. More SERP real estate means more clicks.

Add schema to every service page and your homepage. Use JSON-LD format. Most website builders support it through plugins or custom code.

### Site Structure and Internal Linking

A clean site structure helps Google crawl your pages efficiently.

**Recommended med spa site structure:**

```
Homepage
├── Services (hub page)
│   ├── Botox
│   ├── Lip Filler
│   ├── CoolSculpting
│   ├── Microneedling
│   └── [Each treatment]
├── About
├── Before & After Gallery
├── Blog
│   ├── Treatment articles
│   ├── Cost/comparison articles
│   └── Seasonal content
├── Contact / Book Now
└── Locations (if multi-location)
```

Link related service pages to each other. Link blog posts to relevant service pages. This internal linking structure distributes authority across your site and helps Google understand the relationship between your content.

### HTTPS and Security

Your site must use HTTPS. Google flags non-HTTPS sites as "Not Secure." For a healthcare business handling patient inquiries, that warning destroys trust instantly.

If your site still runs on HTTP, contact your hosting provider. Most offer free SSL certificates through Let's Encrypt.

## Common Med Spa SEO Mistakes

Avoid these mistakes. Each one costs rankings and revenue.

**1. One page for all services.** Every treatment needs its own URL. A single "Services" page cannot rank for Botox, fillers, and CoolSculpting simultaneously.

**2. Ignoring Google Business Profile.** Your GBP is your most powerful local SEO asset. An incomplete or inactive profile is invisible to local searches.

**3. No review strategy.** Hoping patients leave reviews does not work. You need a system: QR codes, automated follow-ups, and staff training.

**4. Keyword stuffing.** Repeating "best med spa in Dallas" 15 times on a page hurts rankings. Write naturally. Use the keyword in the title, first paragraph, 1 H2, and once or twice in the body.

**5. Neglecting mobile.** Over 70% of your traffic is mobile. If the booking form does not work on a phone, you lose conversions.

**6. Publishing nothing.** A dormant blog signals an inactive business. Publishing consistently is the single biggest differentiator between med spas that rank and those that do not.

**7. No internal links.** Service pages and blog posts should link to each other. Without internal links, Google cannot discover and rank all your pages efficiently.

**8. Duplicate content across locations.** Multi-location med spas often copy the same service page text across locations. Each location page needs unique content specific to that area.

## How Pinterest SEO Works

Pinterest is a search engine. SEO fundamentals (keyword research, placement, and optimization) apply directly. The businesses that treat Pinterest like a search engine outperform those who treat it like a social feed.

### Where to Place Keywords

Pinterest scans these locations to determine what your Pin is about:

| Location | Priority | What to Include |
|---|---|---|
| Pin title | Highest | Primary keyword first |
| Pin description | High | Primary keyword + 2-3 long-tail variants (220 chars optimal) |
| Alt text | Medium | Describe the image with keywords |
| Board title | Medium | Match search intent exactly |
| Board description | Medium | 2-3 keyword-rich sentences |
| Profile name and bio | Medium | Core keyword + audience description |
| Image file name | Low-Medium | keyword-rich-filename.jpg before upload |

**Example for a home decor business:**
- **Pin title:** "Modern Minimalist Living Room Ideas for Small Spaces"
- **Pin description:** "20 minimalist living room design ideas that work for apartments and small homes. Clean lines, neutral colors, and budget-friendly furniture picks."
- **Board title:** "Minimalist Living Room Ideas"
- **File name:** `minimalist-living-room-small-space-ideas.jpg`

### Rich Pins

Rich Pins pull metadata directly from your website and display it on the Pin. They increase trust and [click-through rates](/blog/organic-ctr-by-position).

| Rich Pin Type | What It Shows | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Product | Price, availability, product name | Ecommerce |
| Recipe | Ingredients, cook time, servings | Food blogs |
| Article | Headline, author, description | Blog content |

Rich Pins require a one-time setup: add Open Graph or Schema.org meta tags to your website, then validate through Pinterest's Rich Pin Validator. After validation, all Pins from your domain automatically become Rich Pins.

### Pinterest Algorithm Ranking Factors

The algorithm evaluates 4 signals:

**1. Pin Quality:** Measured by saves, clicks, close-ups, and time spent viewing. High-resolution vertical images (2:3 ratio, 1000x1500px minimum) perform best.

**2. Pinner Quality (Account Authority):** Based on posting frequency, engagement rate, account history, and niche consistency. Verified websites boost this score.

**3. Relevance:** Pinterest AI scans titles, descriptions, board context, and the linked page content. It also interprets images visually. A photo of a kitchen is categorized as kitchen content even without text keywords.

**4. Freshness:** New Pins receive an algorithmic boost in the first few hours. Early engagement in this window determines broader distribution. Fresh URL + fresh image = maximum freshness signal.

## Content Strategy for Pinterest

Pinterest rewards consistency over virality. Posting 5 to 15 fresh Pins per day is optimal for established accounts. New accounts should start at 3 to 5 per day.

### What Performs Best

**Vertical images** at 2:3 aspect ratio (1000x1500px) outperform all other formats. Text overlays on images increase saves. Clear, bright photography with minimal clutter earns more clicks.

**Video Pins** receive distribution priority in home feeds. Short videos (15 to 30 seconds) showing product usage, tutorials, or transformations perform well.

**Idea Pins** (multi-page story format) get algorithmic favor. They work for step-by-step tutorials, recipe walk-throughs, and how-to content.

### Content Repurposing for Pinterest

Every piece of content you create can become multiple Pins.

| Source Content | Pinterest Pins Created |
|---|---|
| 1 blog post | 3-5 Pins (different images, same link) |
| 1 YouTube video | 1 video Pin + 2-3 static Pins from screenshots |
| 1 Instagram post | 1 reformatted vertical Pin |
| 1 infographic | 1 full infographic Pin + 3 section-crop Pins |
| 1 product page | 1 product Pin + 1 lifestyle Pin + 1 comparison Pin |

A single blog post can generate 3 to 5 Pins with different images, different titles, and the same destination URL. Pinterest treats each Pin as a separate piece of content in search results. This is the compounding engine: more Pins = more search surface area.

### Board Strategy

Organize Pins into topic-specific boards. Each board title should match a search query.

**Good board titles:** "Modern Kitchen Remodel Ideas," "Vegan Dinner Recipes," "Wedding Photography Inspiration"

**Bad board titles:** "My Favorites," "Stuff I Like," "Inspiration Board"

Create 10 to 15 boards covering your core topics. Add 20 to 30 Pins per board before making them public. A half-empty board signals low quality to the algorithm.

## Posting Schedule and Account Management

### Optimal Posting Cadence

| Account Stage | Pins per Day | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| New account (months 1-3) | 3-5 | Focus on building 10-15 boards with 20+ Pins each |
| Growing account (months 4-12) | 5-10 | Mix fresh Pins and repins to new boards |
| Established account (year 2+) | 10-15 | Heavy on fresh Pins with original images |

Space Pins throughout the day. Do not batch-post 10 Pins at once. Use a scheduling tool (Tailwind, Buffer, Hootsuite, or Pinterest's native scheduler) to distribute content evenly.

### The Consistency Rule

The algorithm rewards accounts that post regularly. An account posting 5 Pins per day every day outranks an account posting 50 Pins in one day and nothing for a week. Irregular posting damages your Pinner Quality score.

### Seasonal and Trend-Based Content Planning

Pinterest users plan ahead. They search for Christmas ideas in September. They search for summer recipes in March. Content published 45 to 90 days before a seasonal peak captures early search demand.

### Pinterest and Google SEO Working Together

Pinterest Pins appear in Google Image search and sometimes in standard Google results. This creates a dual-ranking opportunity.

A blog post optimized for Google targets "modern kitchen remodel ideas." The same blog post generates 3 to 5 Pinterest Pins targeting the same keyword. The blog post ranks on Google. The Pins rank on Pinterest Search. Both drive traffic to the same page.

The compounding effect: a blog post that ranks on Google AND has 5 Pins ranking on Pinterest generates traffic from 2 search engines simultaneously. This is why content-driven businesses that use both channels together outperform those using either one alone.

Link every Pin to a page on your website. Not to your homepage. To the specific blog post, product page, or landing page the Pin represents. Pinterest evaluates the linked page content for relevance signals. An [on-page optimized](/blog/on-page-seo-guide) landing page strengthens the Pin's ranking in Pinterest Search.

## Measuring Pinterest Performance

### Key Metrics

|---|---|---|
| Impressions | How many times Pins appear in feeds and search | Growth trend month over month |
| Saves (repins) | How many users saved your Pins | Highest-quality engagement signal |
| Outbound clicks | Traffic sent to your website | Primary conversion metric |

| Engagement rate | Saves + clicks / impressions | Benchmark: 1-2% |
| Top Pins by clicks | Which content drives the most traffic | Double down on top performers |

### Attribution

Install the Pinterest Tag on your website to track:
- Page visits from Pinterest

Connect Pinterest data to [Google Analytics](/blog/google-analytics-4-setup) to compare Pinterest traffic against other channels. Track assisted conversions. Pinterest often starts the buyer journey (discovery) while Google completes it (final purchase). Multi-touch attribution reveals the full picture.

## What GEO Scoring Measures

GEO scoring quantifies how likely AI search engines are to discover, understand, and cite your content. It is not one metric. It is a framework of metrics that cover different dimensions of AI visibility.

Think of it this way. SEO scoring tells you how well your page performs in traditional Google search. [GEO scoring](/blog/what-is-geo) tells you how well your page performs in AI-generated responses.

### The Core Question GEO Scoring Answers

Can AI systems find your content, parse its structure, extract relevant facts, and cite it accurately when answering user queries?

A high GEO score means yes across all 4 dimensions. A low score means your content has gaps in at least one of them.

### GEO Score vs SEO Score

| Dimension | SEO Score | GEO Score |
|---|---|---|
| What it measures | Google ranking potential | AI citation likelihood |
| Scale | Typically 0-100 | Typically 0-100 or 0-1 |
| Key inputs | Keywords, backlinks, technical health | Structure, schema, freshness, entity signals |
| Primary output | Higher Google rankings | More AI citations and mentions |
| Overlap | E-E-A-T, content quality, page speed | E-E-A-T, content quality, page speed |
| Unique to each | Backlink profile, keyword optimization | Crawler access, llms.txt, answer-first format |

The overlap is significant. Good SEO practices improve your GEO score. But GEO scoring adds dimensions that traditional SEO ignores entirely. [Schema markup](/blog/schema-markup-seo-guide), AI crawler access, and semantic structure carry far more weight in GEO than in traditional search.

## The GEO-16 Framework: 16 Pillars of AI Visibility

The GEO-16 framework is the most rigorously tested scoring model for AI citation prediction. Published as academic research, it analyzed citations from Brave Summary, Google AI Overviews, and Perplexity across 1,100 unique URLs.

Each page receives a score across 16 pillars. The pillar scores combine into a normalized GEO score from 0 to 1. Pages scoring 0.70 or higher with 12+ pillar hits show substantially higher citation rates.

### The 16 Pillars

| # | Pillar | What It Measures | Citation Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Metadata and Freshness | Title tags, meta descriptions, publication dates, update timestamps | +47% (highest) |
| 2 | Semantic HTML | Proper heading hierarchy, landmark elements, semantic tags | High |
| 3 | Structured Data | JSON-LD schema markup (Article, FAQ, HowTo, Product) | High |
| 4 | Content Depth | Word count, topic coverage, subtopic completeness | High |
| 5 | Factual Density | Statistics, named sources, verifiable claims per paragraph | High |
| 6 | Answer Formatting | Direct answers within first 2 sentences, question-answer structure | High |
| 7 | Entity Clarity | Brand name consistency, author credentials, organization data | Medium-High |
| 8 | External Citations | Links to authoritative sources within the content | Medium-High |
| 9 | Internal Linking | [Internal link structure](/blog/internal-linking-blog-posts) connecting related content | Medium |
| 10 | Media Integration | Images with alt text, tables, charts, embedded media | Medium |
| 11 | Readability | Sentence length, paragraph length, Flesch reading score | Medium |
| 12 | Page Performance | Load speed, Core Web Vitals, mobile responsiveness | Medium |
| 13 | Crawler Accessibility | AI bot permissions in robots.txt, server-side rendering | Critical |
| 14 | Content Freshness | Days since last update, frequency of updates | Medium-High |
| 15 | Topical Authority | Number of related pages on the same topic cluster | Medium |
| 16 | Trust Signals | HTTPS, privacy policy, about page, review platform profiles | Medium |

![Top 3 GEO-16 pillars by citation impact with metadata freshness leading at +47%](/images/blog/geo-scoring-top-3-pillars.webp)

### Which Pillars Matter Most

Metadata and Freshness showed the strongest correlation with citation at 0.68. That translates to a 47% increase in citation likelihood for pages that score high on this pillar alone.

Semantic HTML and Structured Data ranked second and third. Together, these top 3 pillars account for the majority of citation prediction accuracy.

The practical takeaway: if you can only improve 3 things, focus on metadata freshness, semantic HTML structure, and JSON-LD [schema markup](/blog/schema-markup-for-blog-posts).

## 7 Key GEO Metrics Every Business Should Track

GEO scoring is the umbrella. Under it sit specific metrics that measure different aspects of AI visibility. Here are the 7 that matter most.

![7 key GEO metrics to track for AI search visibility](/images/blog/geo-scoring-7-metrics.webp)

### 1. Citation Frequency

How often your content gets cited in AI-generated responses for your target queries. This is the GEO equivalent of ranking position in traditional SEO. Track it weekly for your top 20 to 30 target queries across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews.

### 2. Share of Voice (SoV)

Your brand's citation frequency divided by total citations for a query set. If 10 brands get cited for "best CRM software" queries and your brand appears in 3 out of 10 responses, your SoV is 30%.

SoV reveals competitive position. A rising SoV means you are gaining ground. A falling SoV means competitors are optimizing faster.

### 3. Brand Visibility Score

A composite metric (0 to 100) showing how prominently your brand appears across all AI platforms for your target keywords. Visibility accounts for citation frequency, citation position (mentioned first vs last), and citation context (recommended vs merely mentioned).

### 4. GEO Content Score

A page-level metric (0 to 100) that quantifies the AI-readiness of a specific piece of content. It combines the 16 GEO pillar scores into a single number. Content scoring above 70 is considered AI-optimized. Below 50 needs structural work.

This is different from your overall brand GEO score. A brand can have some pages scoring 90 and others scoring 20. The page-level score identifies exactly which content needs improvement.

### 5. Generative Position

Where your brand appears in AI-generated lists. When ChatGPT lists "the top 5 SEO tools," being position 1 is dramatically more valuable than position 5. Generative position tracks your placement within these ranked responses.

### 6. Query Coverage

The percentage of relevant queries where your brand appears at all. If you target 100 AI-relevant queries and your brand appears in responses for 35 of them, your query coverage is 35%.

Low query coverage with high citation frequency means you dominate a narrow topic. High coverage with low frequency means broad but shallow visibility.

### 7. Fresh Content Citation Velocity

How quickly newly published content starts getting cited by AI systems. Some content gets cited within days. Other content takes weeks. Faster velocity indicates strong GEO signals that AI systems trust and prioritize.

## How to Run a GEO Audit on Your Content

You do not need paid tools to assess your GEO readiness. Here is a manual audit process based on the GEO-16 framework.

### Step 1: Check Technical Foundations

- [ ] Verify `robots.txt` allows GPTBot, PerplexityBot, and ClaudeBot. Read our [AI crawlers guide](/blog/ai-crawlers-guide).
- [ ] Confirm pages use server-side rendering or static HTML (not client-side JS only)
- [ ] Check that an [llms.txt file](/blog/llms-txt-guide) exists and maps your site structure
- [ ] Verify HTTPS is active across all pages
- [ ] Test page speed (under 3 seconds load time)

### Step 2: Audit Content Structure

- [ ] Every page has a clear H1, H2, H3 hierarchy with no skipped levels
- [ ] Key definitions appear in the first 2 sentences of each section
- [ ] Content uses numbered lists, bullet points, and tables for structured data
- [ ] FAQ sections exist on informational and commercial pages
- [ ] Follow the [blog GEO checklist](/blog/blog-geo-checklist) for every article

### Step 3: Verify Schema Markup

- [ ] Article/BlogPosting schema on all blog posts
- [ ] FAQPage schema on pages with FAQ sections
- [ ] Organization schema on your about page
- [ ] Product/Offer schema on pricing and product pages
- [ ] Author schema with credentials on content pages

### Step 4: Assess Authority Signals

- [ ] Author bylines with credentials on every article
- [ ] External citations to authoritative sources (2+ per article)
- [ ] [E-E-A-T signals](/blog/eeat-google-quality-guide) present (experience, expertise, authority, trust)
- [ ] Brand profiles active on G2, Trustpilot, or industry review platforms
- [ ] Consistent entity information (name, description) across web properties

### Step 5: Evaluate Freshness

- [ ] Top 20 pages updated within the last 90 days
- [ ] Publication dates and "last updated" timestamps visible
- [ ] No outdated statistics or year references
- [ ] Regular publishing cadence (weekly or more frequent)

### Step 6: Score Each Page

Rate each of the 16 pillars from 0 (not present) to 1 (fully optimized). Sum the scores. Divide by 16. This gives you a normalized GEO score from 0 to 1.

**Interpretation:**
- 0.70 to 1.00: Strong GEO readiness. Focus on monitoring and maintaining.
- 0.50 to 0.69: Moderate. Prioritize the 3 weakest pillars for improvement.
- Below 0.50: Low. Start with technical foundations and content structure.

## How to Improve Your GEO Score

Improving your GEO score follows the same priority logic as the GEO-16 framework. Fix the highest-impact pillars first.

### Priority 1: Metadata and Freshness (Week 1)

This single pillar has the highest correlation with AI citations (+47%). Quick wins:

- Add or update `<meta>` descriptions with clear, factual summaries
- Include publication date and "last updated" date on every page
- Update your top 10 traffic pages with fresh statistics and current-year references
- Remove any content with outdated data that contradicts current facts
- Set a quarterly refresh schedule for high-value pages

Our guide on [updating old blog posts](/blog/update-old-blog-posts) covers the full refresh process.

### Priority 2: Semantic HTML and Structured Data (Week 2)

These 2 pillars combine for the second-largest citation impact:

- Restructure headings to follow proper H1 > H2 > H3 hierarchy
- Add JSON-LD Article schema to every blog post
- Add FAQPage schema to every page with FAQ content
- Use `<table>`, `<ul>`, `<ol>` elements instead of formatting hacks
- Include `<time>` elements for dates

### Priority 3: Content Depth and Factual Density (Week 3)

AI systems prefer content that answers questions thoroughly with verifiable claims:

- Ensure every article covers the topic more deeply than the top 3 competitors
- Add 2 to 3 specific statistics with named sources per 500 words
- Include comparison tables wherever data supports them
- Write answer-first paragraphs (definition in sentence 1, evidence in sentences 2 to 3)
- Build [topical authority](/blog/build-topical-authority) by publishing 5+ related articles per cluster

### Priority 4: Entity and Authority Signals (Week 4)

Build the trust signals AI systems look for:

- Create detailed author pages with credentials, expertise areas, and social links
- Claim and maintain profiles on industry review platforms
- Ensure your Organization schema matches your brand entity everywhere
- Get mentioned on third-party sites (industry publications, partner blogs)
- Read our complete guide on [getting cited by AI search engines](/blog/get-cited-ai-search)

### Priority 5: Ongoing Monitoring (Monthly)

- Track citation frequency for your top 30 queries across 3 AI platforms
- Monitor GEO Content Score for your top 20 pages monthly
- Compare Share of Voice against top 3 competitors quarterly
- Identify new queries where AI mentions your competitors but not you
- Publish 2 to 4 new articles per month targeting AI-friendly formats

## GEO Scoring Thresholds: What the Research Shows

The GEO-16 research established specific thresholds that predict citation success across AI platforms.

![GEO score thresholds from research showing high, moderate, and low citation zones](/images/blog/geo-scoring-thresholds.webp)

### Cross-Platform Citation Thresholds

| Threshold | What It Means | Citation Impact |
|---|---|---|
| GEO score above 0.70 | Page meets most AI quality signals | Substantially higher citation rate |
| 12+ pillar hits (out of 16) | Strong across multiple dimensions | Consistent citations across platforms |
| Metadata score above 0.80 | Fresh, well-described content | +47% citation likelihood |
| Structured data present | JSON-LD schema markup detected | 2 to 3x more likely to be cited |

### Platform-Specific Findings

**Brave Summary** cited the highest-quality pages on average (GEO score = 0.727) and produced the highest citation rate at 78%. If your content scores well on Brave, it likely scores well everywhere.

**Google AI Overviews** showed moderate selectivity. Pages need both strong on-page signals and domain authority for consistent citation.

**Perplexity** demonstrated the strongest freshness bias. Recently updated content scored disproportionately higher.

### What These Thresholds Mean for You

A GEO score of 0.70 is the minimum viable threshold. Below that, AI systems inconsistently cite your content even if it is topically relevant. Above 0.70, citation rates increase dramatically.

The practical goal: get your top 20 pages above 0.70 first. Then expand to your top 50. Then aim for site-wide consistency.

## FAQ

**What is a good GEO score?**

A normalized GEO score of 0.70 or higher (70 out of 100 on percentage scales) indicates strong AI citation readiness. Pages scoring above this threshold with 12+ of the 16 GEO pillars optimized show substantially higher citation rates across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews.

**How is GEO scoring different from SEO scoring?**

SEO scoring measures Google ranking potential based on keywords, backlinks, and technical health. GEO scoring measures AI citation likelihood based on content structure, schema markup, freshness, entity signals, and crawler access. The two overlap on [E-E-A-T](/blog/eeat-google-quality-guide) and content quality, but GEO adds dimensions that SEO scoring does not cover.

**What is the GEO-16 framework?**

GEO-16 is a [research-backed auditing framework](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.10762) with 16 pillars that predict AI citation behavior. It was developed by analyzing 1,702 citations across 1,100 URLs from Brave, Google AI Overviews, and Perplexity. The framework produces a normalized score from 0 to 1 for each page.

**Which GEO metric matters most?**

Citation frequency is the most direct indicator of GEO success. It measures how often your content gets cited in AI-generated responses for your target queries. Share of Voice is the most important competitive metric, showing your citation frequency relative to competitors.

**Can I check my GEO score for free?**

Yes. Mangools AI Search Grader provides free page-level GEO scoring. Geoptie offers a free GEO Rank Tracker for brand monitoring. You can also run a manual audit using the GEO-16 pillar checklist in this article. Paid tools like Otterly AI and GEOReport.ai provide deeper analysis.

**Does improving GEO score also improve SEO?**

In most cases, yes. Many GEO improvements (better content structure, schema markup, faster load times, stronger E-E-A-T signals) also benefit traditional Google rankings. The investment is additive. You are not choosing between SEO and GEO. You are building both simultaneously.

## Major Product Launch Platforms

These are the highest-traffic platforms where products compete for daily attention. A strong launch here can drive thousands of visitors in a single day.

**1. [Product Hunt](https://www.producthunt.com)**. The gold standard for product launches. Daily voting determines winners. A successful launch day can drive 1,000 to 10,000+ visits and a DR 90+ [backlink](/glossary/backlinks). Prepare your launch with a hunter, tagline, and first comment.

**2. [Hacker News (Show HN)](https://news.ycombinator.com)**. Y Combinator's community. Post your product with "Show HN:" prefix. Technical products thrive here. A front-page post delivers massive traffic within hours. No voting manipulation. Quality matters.

**3. [BetaList](https://betalist.com)**. The go-to for pre-launch startups since 2010. Submit your landing page before launch to collect early signups. DR 75 dofollow backlink. Free submissions take 2-4 weeks. Paid gets faster approval.

**4. [Uneed](https://www.uneed.best)**. Daily launch competitions with 8 to 15 products. Lower competition than Product Hunt. Winners earn a permanent listing and dofollow backlink. DR ~60. Paid option to skip the queue.

**5. [DevHunt](https://devhunt.org)** ,  "Product Hunt for developer tools." GitHub-based authentication filters for real developers. DR 59 dofollow backlink. If you build for developers, this is a top pick.

**6. [Fazier](https://fazier.com)**. Newer Product Hunt alternative with lower competition. Top 3 products each day earn a Product of the Day badge. Free to submit. Growing community of early adopters.

**7. [MicroLaunch](https://microlaunch.net)**. Products stay listed for a full month instead of one day. Includes "Roasts" (feedback) and "Boosts" (upvotes). 50K monthly visitors. Better for products that need extended exposure.

**8. [Launching Next](https://www.launchingnext.com)**. Over 44,000 startups featured. Daily and trending sections. Simple submission form. Good for early visibility without the pressure of a timed launch.

**9. [Peerlist](https://peerlist.io)**. Part LinkedIn alternative, part launch platform. Weekly Spotlight limited to 50 products. 188K to 209K monthly visitors. Works best for B2B and developer products.

**10. [DEV Community](https://dev.to)**. Developer community with 1M+ members. Publish a "Show DEV" post about your product. Free. Great for build-in-public narratives and technical product launches.

**11. [AppSumo](https://appsumo.com)**. Marketplace for software deals. You set the price, earn 70-95% of revenue. Best for SaaS products offering lifetime deals. High-intent buyers already looking for new tools.

**12. [OpenHunts](https://openhunts.com)**. Open-source alternative to Product Hunt. Hall of fame for top performers. Lower traffic but zero gatekeeping. Every submission gets listed.

**13. [Hype Desk](https://hypedesk.com)**. Product launch platform featuring curated alternatives to Product Hunt. Free to submit. Growing directory focused on new and indie products.

**14. [Super Launch](https://superlaunch.com)**. Startup launch directory with a small but active community. Free submission. Best for early-stage products looking for initial feedback and visibility.

**15. [WhatLaunched](https://whatlaunched.com)**. Daily digest of new product launches. Focused on generating SEO backlinks for listed products. Free to submit. Good for long-tail organic discovery.

## How to Get the Most From Directory Submissions

Submitting to 200 directories without a strategy wastes time. Here is the approach that drives the most traffic, backlinks, and signups.

### Prioritize by Domain Rating

Not all directories are equal. Submit to high-DR directories first:

| Priority | Domain Rating | Examples | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | DR 80+ | Product Hunt, G2, Crunchbase, Hacker News | Launch here first |
| Week 2 | DR 60-79 | BetaList, Indie Hackers, F6S, DevHunt, Uneed | Build momentum |
| Week 3 | DR 40-59 | Niche directories, category-specific sites | Fill gaps |
| Week 4+ | DR below 40 | Long-tail directories, regional sites | Breadth coverage |

### Optimize Every Listing

Every directory gives you a chance to make a first impression. Do not waste it with a rushed listing.

- Write a unique description for each directory. Do not copy-paste the same text everywhere.
- Use your primary keyword naturally in your product description.
- Upload a high-quality logo and screenshot on every platform that allows images.
- Link to a dedicated landing page, not just your homepage. Track traffic with UTM parameters.
- Respond to every review, comment, and question on each platform.

### Track What Works

Set up UTM parameters for each directory submission:

`https://yoursite.com?utm_source=producthunt&utm_medium=directory&utm_campaign=launch`

After 30 days, check which directories drove:
- The most referral traffic
- The most signups or trials
- The highest-quality backlinks (check in [Google Search Console](/blog/google-search-console-guide))

Double down on directories that convert. Update your listing monthly on high-performing platforms.

### Build a Submission Calendar

Do not submit to all 209 directories in one day. Spread submissions across 4 to 6 weeks:

- **Week 1:** 5 high-impact launches (Product Hunt, Hacker News, BetaList, G2, Capterra)
- **Week 2:** 15 software review sites and community platforms
- **Week 3:** 20 niche directories relevant to your product category
- **Week 4:** 20 more niche and regional directories
- **Week 5-6:** Remaining directories at 10 per day

This pace avoids submission fatigue and lets you customize each listing.

## FAQ

**How many startup directories should I submit to?**

Submit to every relevant directory where your target audience might discover your product. For most startups, that means 50 to 100 directories in the first month, then 10 to 20 per month ongoing. Quality of listing matters more than quantity. A well-optimized listing on Product Hunt outperforms 50 rushed submissions to low-traffic sites.

**Are startup directory submissions good for SEO?**

Yes. Directories with high [domain authority](/glossary/domain-authority) pass link equity to your site. Dofollow backlinks from Product Hunt (DR 90+), Crunchbase (DR 90+), and BetaList (DR 75) directly strengthen your backlink profile. Even nofollow links from reputable directories build brand signals that search engines recognize.

**What is the best alternative to Product Hunt?**

Uneed, DevHunt, and MicroLaunch are the three strongest Product Hunt alternatives in 2026. Uneed runs daily competitions with less competition. DevHunt focuses on developer tools with GitHub authentication. MicroLaunch gives products a full month of visibility instead of one day.

**Do paid directory submissions work?**

Paid submissions to legitimate review platforms like G2, Capterra, and Trustpilot are worth the investment because they drive high-intent buyer traffic. Paid submission services like SubmitSaaS save time but vary in quality. Avoid any service that promises "guaranteed backlinks" from low-quality directories.

**How long does it take to see results from directory submissions?**

Referral traffic starts within days of a Product Hunt launch. [SEO benefits from backlinks](/blog/build-backlinks-for-blog) typically appear in 60 to 90 days as search engines crawl and index your directory listings. Review site listings on G2 and Capterra drive steady traffic for months because buyers search these platforms continuously.

**Should I submit to directories before or after my product launch?**

Submit to pre-launch directories (BetaList, BetaPage, Betabound) 2 to 4 weeks before launch to collect early signups. Submit to launch platforms (Product Hunt, Hacker News) on launch day. Submit to review sites (G2, Capterra) and general directories in the weeks after launch when you have a polished product and real users.

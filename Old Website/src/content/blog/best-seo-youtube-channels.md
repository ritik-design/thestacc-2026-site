## FAQ

**Which YouTube channel is best for learning SEO as a beginner?**

Ahrefs has the most thorough beginner SEO course on YouTube. Their full course covers [keyword research](/blog/keyword-research-for-blog-posts), on-page optimization, link building, and technical SEO in a structured curriculum. Pair it with Google Search Central for the official perspective.

**How many SEO YouTube channels should I follow?**

6 to 10 is the sweet spot. Pick 1 channel from each specialty that matches your goals. Following too many channels leads to conflicting advice. Pick your sources, study them deeply, and implement what you learn.

**Are SEO YouTube tutorials still relevant in 2026?**

Yes. The best channels now cover AI search, generative engine optimization, and how traditional SEO intersects with AI Overviews. Channels like Ahrefs, Marie Haynes, and Google Search Central actively update their content for 2026 realities.

**Is YouTube SEO the same as website SEO?**

No. YouTube SEO focuses on video titles, descriptions, tags, and engagement signals for YouTube search. Website SEO targets Google organic results through content, links, and technical factors. Some principles overlap but the ranking algorithms are different.

**Can I learn enough SEO from YouTube to rank my business?**

You can learn the fundamentals. Ranking requires consistent execution over months. Most business owners find that learning SEO and executing it are 2 different challenges. That is where done-for-you services fill the gap.

**What is the fastest way to learn SEO on YouTube?**

Follow the curriculum paths above. Pick 3 channels that match your role and watch their most popular videos first. Then apply what you learn to 1 page on your site before consuming more content.

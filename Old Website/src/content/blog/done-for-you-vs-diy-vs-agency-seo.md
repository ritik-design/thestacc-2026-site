## What Each SEO Model Actually Means

Before comparing costs and results, you need to understand what you are actually buying with each model. The labels sound similar. The reality is not.

### DIY SEO

You do everything yourself. Keyword research, content writing, on-page optimization, technical fixes, link building, and performance tracking. You use free or paid tools like Google Search Console, Ahrefs, or Semrush. You learn as you go.

DIY does not mean "free." It means you pay with time instead of money.

### Agency SEO

You hire a team of specialists. A typical agency assigns an account manager, content strategist, technical SEO specialist, and link builder to your account. They handle strategy, execution, and reporting. You review monthly reports and approve deliverables.

Agency contracts usually lock you in for 6 to 12 months with monthly retainers.

### Done-for-You SEO

A service handles specific SEO tasks on autopilot. Unlike an agency, done-for-you services typically focus on one or two pillars (like content creation or local SEO) rather than a full-service engagement. You do not manage the process. Content gets published automatically.

The key difference: agencies sell strategy and expertise. Done-for-you services sell output and execution.

## When DIY SEO Makes Sense

DIY SEO is not always the wrong choice. For certain businesses at certain stages, it is the best move.

**DIY works when:**

- You have more time than money (pre-revenue startups, solopreneurs just starting out)
- Your niche is extremely specific and you are the only subject matter expert
- You want to learn SEO as a core business skill (marketing managers, founders)
- Your competitive landscape is low (local niches with few online competitors)
- You only need to rank for 5 to 10 keywords

**DIY fails when:**

- You need results within 6 months (the learning curve alone takes 3 months)
- You are already working 50+ hours per week on your business
- Your competitors publish 10+ articles per month
- You keep starting and stopping (inconsistency kills SEO progress)
- You do not enjoy writing (forcing yourself leads to thin, low-quality content)

If you decide to go the DIY route, start with our [on-page SEO guide](/blog/on-page-seo-guide) and [keyword research for blog posts](/blog/keyword-research-for-blog-posts). These two skills cover 60% of what moves the needle.

The biggest risk with DIY is not failure. It is opportunity cost. Every hour you spend writing blog posts is an hour you do not spend closing deals, serving customers, or building your product.

## When an Agency Is the Right Call

Agencies are not a scam. The good ones deliver serious results. But they are not right for every business.

**Agencies work when:**

- Your monthly SEO budget exceeds $3,000 and you need full-service support
- You need technical SEO fixes (site migrations, Core Web Vitals, international SEO)
- You are in a hyper-competitive industry (fintech, insurance, legal) where link building is essential
- You need custom strategy (not just content, but full-funnel SEO + CRO)
- You have internal marketing staff who can manage the agency relationship

**Agencies fail when:**

- Your budget is under $2,000 per month (you will get the B-team)
- You expect 30+ articles per month (most agencies cap at 4 to 8 per retainer)
- You want a hands-off experience (agencies require regular input)
- You have been burned before and do not trust the process (agencies need 6+ months)

A [$2,500 per month agency](https://backlinko.com/seo-pricing) can deliver strong results. A $500 per month agency almost never does. If your budget falls in the $500 to $1,500 range, you are better served by a done-for-you service or a skilled freelancer.

[SEO ROI statistics](https://firstpagesage.com/reports/seo-roi-statistics-fc/) show a median return of 748% across industries. But that number assumes a well-run campaign with proper budget. Low-budget agency engagements rarely hit those benchmarks.

## FAQ

**How much does DIY SEO actually cost per month?**

The tool cost alone runs $99 to $449 per month for platforms like Ahrefs or Semrush. Add 10 to 15 hours per week of your time. If you value your time at $50 to $100 per hour, the real cost is $2,100 to $6,400 per month. The only scenario where DIY is truly cheap is when your time has low opportunity cost.

**Can a $99 per month service really compete with a $3,000 per month agency?**

On content volume, yes. Stacc publishes 30 optimized articles per month at $99. Most agencies deliver 4 to 8 articles at $3,000. On custom strategy, technical audits, and link building, agencies still hold an advantage. The right question is which output matters most for your situation.

**How long before I see results from done-for-you SEO?**

Initial ranking movement happens within 60 to 90 days. Meaningful organic traffic growth typically occurs at the 3 to 6 month mark. The compounding effect accelerates after 6 months as [topical authority](/blog/build-topical-authority) builds across your content library.

**Is it worth paying for an SEO agency if my budget is under $1,500?**

No. At that price point, most agencies assign junior staff or use templated strategies. You get better results from a done-for-you content service ($99 to $199) combined with DIY technical SEO. Save the agency budget for when you can afford $3,000+ per month.

**What if I start with DIY and switch to done-for-you later?**

That is a common and smart path. Start by learning SEO fundamentals through DIY. Once you understand what good SEO looks like, you can evaluate done-for-you output with an informed eye. The content you published during DIY still contributes to your domain authority.

**Do agencies guarantee rankings?**

No reputable agency guarantees specific rankings. Google does not allow anyone to guarantee placement. Agencies that promise "page 1 in 30 days" are almost always using outdated tactics that risk penalties. Look for agencies that guarantee process (publishing cadence, reporting frequency, response time) rather than outcomes.

## Finding 1: AI Overviews Trigger on 25.8% of All US Searches

**Background:** Google launched AI Overviews to 1 billion users in 2024. The feature generates AI summaries at the top of search results, pushing organic listings below the fold.

**Results:** As of January 2026, AI Overviews appear on 25.8% of all US searches. That figure represents a 58% increase from late 2024. Informational queries trigger [AI Overviews](/glossary/ai-overviews) at an even higher rate: 39.4% of the time.

**Context:** The rollout has not been steady. AI Overview visibility peaked at nearly 25% in July 2025, then fell below 16% by November 2025 as Google pulled back on quality issues. By Q1 2026, visibility surged past the previous peak. This volatility makes planning difficult for businesses that depend on [organic search traffic](/blog/increase-organic-traffic).

## Finding 2: Only 7.9% of Local Searches Triggered AI Overviews. But That Is Changing Fast

**Background:** Local search queries like "plumber near me" or "best pizza downtown" have historically triggered map packs and local 3-packs. AI Overviews were expected to stay focused on informational queries.

**Results:** In late 2025, only 7.9% of local searches triggered an AI Overview. That number understates the real disruption. Restaurant queries jumped from a 10% trigger rate to 78%. Education-related local queries went from 18% to 83%. B2B tech local queries climbed from 36% to 82%.

**Context:** The 7.9% average masks industry-specific surges. If you run a restaurant, tutoring center, or IT services company, AI Overviews already dominate your search results. Service businesses in plumbing, HVAC, and [electrical work](/blog/electrician-seo-guide) still see lower trigger rates. That gap will close. Google has expanded AI Overviews to every major query category within 18 months of launch.

| B2B Tech | 36% | 82% | +128% |
| Business Services | 56% | 57% | +2% |
| E-Commerce | 2% | 4% | +100% |

![AI Overview trigger rates by industry from late 2024 to Q1 2026](/images/blog/ai-overviews-industry-trigger-rates.webp)

The pattern is clear. Google tests AI Overviews in one industry, refines the format, then expands to the next. Businesses in currently low-trigger industries should prepare now. The window to build content authority before AI Overviews dominate your category is closing. Track your industry trigger rates monthly through tools like [Semrush](https://www.semrush.com/blog/ai-seo-statistics/) or manual SERP checks.

## Finding 3: AI Local Packs Show 68% Fewer Businesses

**Background:** The traditional [local pack](/glossary/local-pack) displays 3 businesses with their name, rating, address, and a call button. This format has driven local discovery for over a decade.

**Results:** AI local packs display only 32% as many unique businesses as traditional packs. That means 68% fewer businesses get any visibility. Most AI local packs show just 1 or 2 businesses instead of 3. Call buttons are absent from the AI format.

**Context:** Fewer slots create a winner-take-all dynamic. The businesses that appear in the AI local pack capture a disproportionate share of attention. Businesses excluded from the AI pack lose visibility entirely for that query. This makes [Google Business Profile optimization](/glossary/gbp-optimization) more critical than ever. A complete, well-reviewed profile is no longer enough. Content authority and topical relevance now influence which businesses the AI selects.

## Finding 5: Organic CTR Drops 58% When AI Overviews Appear

**Background:** Click-through rate measures how often searchers click on a result. Higher CTR means more traffic to your website.

**Results:** Ahrefs analyzed 300,000+ keywords and found that AI Overviews reduce the organic [click-through rate](/glossary/click-through-rate) for position-1 content by 58%. Seer Interactive's study of 68,000 real queries found an even steeper decline: organic CTR dropped from 1.76% to 0.61%, a 65% reduction. Position-1 CTR fell from 7.3% in March 2024 to 2.6% in March 2025.

**Context:** A 58% CTR drop does not mean 58% less traffic across all queries. AI Overviews appear on about 1 in 4 searches. The impact concentrates on informational and "near me" queries where AI summaries satisfy the user without a click. Transactional queries with high purchase intent still drive clicks to organic results. The strategy is to [optimize for AI citations](/blog/ai-overview-optimization) rather than fight the format. Businesses cited inside AI Overviews see 35% higher CTR than non-cited competitors.

## Finding 6: Users Are 47% Less Likely to Click With AI Overviews Present

**Background:** AI Overviews aim to answer the query directly on the search results page. This changes user behavior in measurable ways.

**Results:** Users are 47% less likely to click any traditional search result when an AI Overview displays. Search sessions end 26% of the time when AI summaries are present, compared to only 16% for standard result pages. Only 1% of users clicked links within the AI summaries themselves in Q1 2025.

**Context:** The 1% in-summary click rate is a critical number. It means AI Overviews do not drive referral traffic. They absorb attention and satisfy queries. For local businesses, this creates a zero-click problem. A searcher asks "best dentist in Austin" and reads the AI summary without visiting any website. The businesses mentioned in the summary gain brand awareness. The businesses not mentioned gain nothing. Building [E-E-A-T signals](/blog/eeat-google-quality-guide) and earning AI citations becomes the new organic strategy.

## Finding 7: Local Pack Ads Grew From 1% to 22% of Mobile Results

**Background:** Google monetizes local search through ads placed inside local packs and Local Services Ads (LSAs). As organic visibility shrinks, paid placement expands.

**Results:** Local pack ads appeared in just 1% of mobile local search results in January 2025. By January 2026, that figure reached 22%. Local Services Ads now appear on 31% of tracked queries, up from 11% in early 2025. This represents a 182% increase in LSA visibility in 12 months.

**Context:** Google is shifting local search toward a pay-to-play model. As AI Overviews reduce organic clicks, ad inventory fills the gap. Businesses that previously relied on free organic visibility through their [Google Business Profile](/blog/gbp-statistics-2026) now face a choice: invest in ads or invest in content that earns AI citations. Both paths require budget. The difference is that content compounds over time while ads stop the moment you stop paying. A [local SEO strategy](/blog/local-seo-statistics) that combines GBP optimization, local content, and selective ad spend outperforms either approach alone.

## Finding 9: AI Overviews Prioritize Content Authority Over Proximity

**Background:** Traditional local packs weight proximity heavily. A business 0.5 miles from the searcher ranks above a business 3 miles away, all else being equal.

**Results:** AI local packs apply a different ranking formula. Content authority and topical relevance outrank geographic proximity. [Structured data](/blog/schema-markup-seo-guide), Google Business Profile completeness, and E-E-A-T signals carry more weight than distance. A business with strong content and reviews 5 miles away can outrank a closer competitor with a thin online presence.

**Context:** This shift benefits businesses that invest in content marketing and online reputation. It disadvantages businesses that relied solely on location. For multi-location businesses, this means [location-specific pages](/blog/location-pages-seo) with unique content for each service area now drive AI visibility. Generic location pages with swapped city names do not earn citations. Every location page needs original content, local data, and specific service information to rank in AI results.

## Finding 10: Review Content Now Feeds AI Summaries Directly

**Background:** Google Business Profile reviews have always influenced local rankings. Star ratings and review volume remain top ranking factors. AI Overviews add a new dimension.

**Results:** AI Overviews synthesize review content to answer consumer queries about local businesses. Businesses with a rich, high-quality review corpus get represented favorably in AI summaries. Businesses with thin, unanswered, or generic reviews get underrepresented or represented negatively.

**Context:** Review management is no longer optional. [Responding to reviews](/blog/respond-to-positive-reviews) with specific, helpful replies gives AI models more content to work with. A response that mentions your service area, specialties, and differentiators feeds the AI summary about your business. Managing [fake reviews](/blog/handle-fake-google-reviews) also matters more now. Negative or inaccurate reviews that go unchallenged can shape how AI Overviews describe your business to searchers.

## What This Means for Your Business

The data tells a consistent story. AI Overviews are reducing organic visibility, showing fewer businesses, and shifting local search toward paid results. But businesses that adapt early gain an advantage. Cited businesses earn more clicks, not fewer.

The data points to 3 priority actions for local businesses in 2026.

### Action 1: Optimize Your Google Business Profile for AI Selection

AI local packs pull from GBP data but apply stricter filters. Complete every field. Select accurate [GBP categories](/blog/gbp-categories-guide). Post weekly updates. Maintain a steady flow of reviews with detailed, keyword-rich responses.

- [ ] Complete all GBP fields including services, products, and attributes
- [ ] Post at least 4 GBP updates per month
- [ ] Respond to every review within 48 hours
- [ ] Add photos weekly to signal an active profile
- [ ] Verify NAP consistency across all directories

### Action 2: Publish Content That Earns AI Citations

AI Overviews cite authoritative, well-structured content. Publish guides, data studies, and how-to content related to your industry and service area. Use [schema markup](/blog/schema-markup-seo-guide) on every page. Build topical authority through consistent publishing.

- [ ] Publish 2 or more blog posts per week with local relevance

- [ ] Include original data, stats, or case studies in content
- [ ] [Optimize the first 200 words](/blog/optimize-first-200-words-ai) for AI extraction
- [ ] Build an [internal linking structure](/blog/internal-linking-strategy) across all content

### Action 3: Diversify Beyond Google Search

- [ ] Create a Reddit presence in local and industry subreddits

## FAQ

**How do AI Overviews affect local business visibility?**

AI Overviews push organic results below the fold and reduce click-through rates by up to 58%. AI local packs show 68% fewer businesses than traditional 3-packs. Businesses not cited in the AI summary lose visibility entirely for that query. The effect varies by industry, with restaurants and education seeing the highest disruption.

**Do AI Overviews appear for "near me" searches?**

Yes, but at lower rates than informational queries. Only 7.9% of local searches triggered AI Overviews in late 2025. That rate is climbing rapidly in specific industries. Restaurant queries now trigger AI Overviews 78% of the time. Service-based queries like "plumber near me" still trigger traditional local packs more often.

**Can local businesses benefit from AI Overviews?**

Yes. Businesses cited inside AI Overviews earn 35% higher organic CTR than non-cited competitors. The key is producing content that Google AI considers citation-worthy: detailed guides, accurate business information, strong reviews, and [proper schema markup](/blog/structured-data-guide). Getting cited turns the AI Overview from a threat into a traffic driver.

**What is the difference between AI local packs and traditional local packs?**

Traditional local packs show 3 businesses with ratings, addresses, and call buttons. AI local packs show 1 to 2 businesses without call buttons. AI packs weight content authority and topical relevance more than proximity. Traditional packs weight proximity, reviews, and [GBP completeness](/blog/improve-google-maps-ranking) more heavily.

**How fast are AI Overviews expanding in local search?**

AI local packs appear on approximately 7% of tracked keywords as of early 2026. Industry-specific trigger rates grew by 100% to 680% in 12 months. At the current pace, most high-intent local queries could display AI pack results within 12 to 18 months. The expansion is not linear. Google tests aggressively, then pulls back, then expands again.

**Should local businesses invest in ads because of AI Overviews?**

Ad investment is one response, but not the only one. Local pack ads grew from 1% to 22% of mobile results in 12 months. LSAs appear on 31% of queries. Ads deliver immediate visibility but stop working when the budget stops. A balanced approach combines GBP optimization, content publishing, and selective ad spend on high-intent queries.

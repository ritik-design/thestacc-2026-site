## FAQ

**What is the best SEO podcast for beginners?**

The SEO Show by Michael Costin and Arthur Fabik is built specifically for beginners. Episodes are structured, jargon-free, and cover [SEO fundamentals](/blog/seo-for-beginners) in order. Pair it with SEO 101 for weekly news in plain language.

**How many SEO podcasts should I listen to?**

4 to 6 is the sweet spot. Pick 1 from each category that matches your role. Following too many shows leads to information overload. Depth beats breadth.

**Are podcasts or YouTube channels better for learning SEO?**

Both serve different purposes. Podcasts work during commutes, workouts, and downtime. YouTube works when you need screen-share tutorials. Our list of the [best SEO YouTube channels](/blog/best-seo-youtube-channels) covers the visual side.

**Which podcast covers AI and the future of SEO best?**

Marketing AI Podcast by Paul Roetzer gives the broadest AI marketing coverage. Voices of Search covers AI in search daily. Search Off the Record provides official Google perspective on [AI search changes](/blog/ai-search-changing-seo).

**What is the best local SEO podcast?**

Local SEO Tactics by Jesse McDonald focuses entirely on local search. Near Memo by Blumenthal, Mihm, and Bowling provides deeper multi-location analysis. Both pair well with our [local SEO guide](/blog/local-seo-guide).

**Can I learn enough from podcasts to rank my website?**

Podcasts teach strategy and concepts well. But ranking requires consistent execution over months. Most business owners find that learning SEO and doing SEO are 2 different challenges. Podcasts handle the learning. Execution is where done-for-you services fill the gap.

## Why Profile Creation Sites Still Matter for SEO

Google confirmed in 2024 that backlinks remain one of its core ranking signals. But backlinks from profiles serve a dual purpose in 2026.

First, they build link diversity. A [healthy backlink profile](https://martech.org/natural-backlink-profile/) has links from many source types. Editorial links, directory links, social links, and profile links. A mix of dofollow and nofollow signals tells Google your link profile is natural.

Second, profile backlinks strengthen your brand entity. When Google sees your business name, website, and details on LinkedIn, Crunchbase, GitHub, and 50 other platforms, it confirms you are a real entity. Brand mentions correlate 3 times more strongly with AI search visibility than backlinks alone.

That does not mean you should create 500 empty profiles. Quality matters more than quantity. Fifty complete profiles with unique bios, photos, and links beat 500 blank sign-ups every time.

The sites below are organized by category. Each entry includes the domain authority score, link type, and a note on what makes the site useful.

## How to Use This List

Before you start creating profiles, follow this process:

1. Pick 5 to 10 sites per week from different categories
2. Fill out every available field on each profile
3. Write a unique 40 to 60 word bio for each platform
4. Add your website URL in the designated field
5. Upload a professional photo or branded logo
6. Use [consistent NAP data](/blog/local-seo-guide) across every profile

Creating profiles in batches of 5 to 10 per week looks natural to search engines. Mass-creating 100 profiles in a single day can trigger spam filters.

## Profile Creation Best Practices for SEO

Creating a profile is step one. Making it count for SEO is step two. Follow these rules to get the most value from every profile you build.

### Complete Every Profile Field

Incomplete profiles pass less authority. Fill out your company name, bio, website URL, location, social media handles, and any other available fields. Google uses this structured data to [build your brand entity](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/establish-business-details).

### Write Unique Bios

Do not copy and paste the same bio across every platform. Write 40 to 60 words tailored to each site audience. A GitHub bio should sound different from a Yelp bio. This variety also prevents duplicate content flags.

### Use Consistent NAP Data

Your business name, address, and phone number must match across every profile. Inconsistent NAP data confuses search engines and weakens your [local SEO](/blog/local-seo-guide) signals.

### Prioritize High DA Sites

Start with sites that have a domain authority of 50 or higher. A single profile on a DA 90 site carries more weight than 10 profiles on DA 20 sites. Focus on the top of this list first.

### Vary Your Anchor Text

Do not use the same anchor text on every profile. Mix brand name anchors, URL anchors, and occasional keyword-rich anchors. A [natural backlink profile](/blog/backlink-audit-guide) has roughly 50 to 70 percent brand anchors, 20 to 40 percent partial match, and no more than 10 percent exact match.

### Add a Professional Photo

Profiles with photos get more engagement and appear more trustworthy. Use a consistent headshot or branded logo across all platforms. Avoid stock images.

### Pace Your Profile Creation

Create 5 to 10 profiles per week. This pace looks natural. Mass-creating hundreds of profiles in one session can trigger spam detection. Spread your work across several weeks.

### Track and Update Profiles

Check your profiles quarterly. Remove any from sites that have been deindexed. Update your bio and links when your business information changes. Active, current profiles carry more SEO weight than stale ones.

## Dofollow vs Nofollow Profile Links

Not all profile links pass the same SEO value. Understanding the difference helps you prioritize your efforts.

**Dofollow links** pass link equity directly to your site. They tell Google to follow the link and count it as a ranking signal. About 35 percent of profile creation sites offer dofollow links.

**Nofollow links** do not pass direct link equity. But Google treats them as "hints" since 2019. Nofollow profile links from sites like LinkedIn and GitHub still carry brand signal value. They help Google confirm your entity exists.

The ideal strategy uses both. Build profiles on high-DA dofollow sites first for direct link value. Then add nofollow profiles on major platforms for brand diversity.

Sites like Blogger (DA 97, dofollow), Vimeo (DA 96, dofollow), and Archive.org (DA 98, dofollow) should be at the top of your list. Pair those with nofollow profiles on LinkedIn (DA 98), GitHub (DA 96), and Medium (DA 95) for a balanced [backlink profile](/blog/build-backlinks-for-blog).

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Profile creation can backfire if you do it wrong. Avoid these errors:

**Spammy mass creation.** Creating 200 profiles in a day with identical bios triggers Google spam filters. Pace yourself at 5 to 10 per week.

**Identical bios everywhere.** Copy-paste bios create duplicate content. Google recognizes the pattern and devalues those links. Write unique content for each profile.

**Ignoring dead sites.** Some profile sites from older lists no longer exist or have been deindexed. Verify each site is live and indexed before creating a profile.

**Using exact-match anchor text.** Stuffing your target keyword into every profile link is a [black hat tactic](/blog/white-hat-vs-black-hat-seo). Use brand names and natural anchor text instead.

**Neglecting profiles after creation.** An empty profile with no activity signals spam. Post at least one update, upload a photo, or engage with the community after creating your profile.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are profile creation sites still effective for SEO in 2026?**

Yes. Profile backlinks still work in 2026, but their role has shifted. They are less about direct ranking power and more about entity verification and brand trust. Google uses profiles across platforms to confirm your business is real. The [backlink diversity](/blog/backlink-statistics) they provide also helps your link profile appear natural.

**How many profile backlinks should I create?**

Aim for 50 to 100 high-quality profiles across different categories. Focus on sites with DA 50 or higher. Create 5 to 10 per week to maintain a natural pace. Quality beats quantity every time.

**Do nofollow profile links help with SEO?**

Nofollow profile links do not pass direct link equity. But Google treats nofollow as a hint, not a directive, since March 2020. Nofollow links from high-authority sites like LinkedIn and Reddit still build brand signals and help Google discover your content.

**Should I use the same bio on every profile?**

No. Write a unique 40 to 60 word bio for each platform. Tailor it to the audience on that site. Identical bios across hundreds of profiles can trigger duplicate content signals and look like spam.

**What is the difference between profile backlinks and social bookmarking?**

Profile backlinks come from creating an account and filling out your profile on a website. Social bookmarking involves saving and sharing web page links on bookmarking platforms. Both build off-page SEO signals, but profile links are more permanent. Social bookmarking sites like Mix, Flipboard, and Digg provide additional referral traffic and social signals.

**Can profile backlinks get my site penalized?**

Only if you create them incorrectly. Mass-creating thousands of profiles with identical bios and exact-match anchor text is a spam signal. Stick to 5 to 10 per week with unique bios and varied anchor text. Follow [white hat SEO practices](/blog/white-hat-vs-black-hat-seo) and you will not have issues.

## What Done for You SEO Actually Means

The term "done for you SEO" covers any arrangement where an external provider handles your search engine optimization. But the scope varies wildly between providers.

At one end, you get a freelancer who writes 4 blog posts per month. At the other, you get a full-service team handling content, technical SEO, link building, local optimization, and monthly reporting. Same label. Completely different service.

### The Core Components

A legitimate done for you SEO service should cover 5 areas:

| Component | What It Includes | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Keyword research** | Target keyword identification, search intent mapping, competitor gap analysis | Without the right targets, content ranks for terms nobody searches |
| **Content creation** | Blog posts, landing pages, product descriptions optimized for search | Content is the primary ranking signal. [53% of all website traffic](https://www.hubspot.com/marketing-statistics) comes from organic search |
| **On-page optimization** | Title tags, meta descriptions, header structure, [internal linking](/blog/internal-linking-blog-posts) | Technical on-page errors prevent even great content from ranking |
| **Technical SEO** | Site speed, crawlability, indexation, mobile optimization | Google cannot rank pages it cannot find or load |
| **Reporting and strategy** | Monthly performance reports, ranking tracking, strategy adjustments | SEO without measurement is guessing |

Some providers also include link building, local SEO (Google Business Profile optimization), and social media. Others charge extra for each.

### What "Done for You" Should Mean

The word "done" matters. If a provider sends you a Google Doc and asks you to publish it, that is not done for you. If they require you to log into your CMS, upload images, write meta descriptions, or share posts on social media, they have outsourced the busywork back to you.

True done for you SEO means the provider handles strategy, execution, and publishing. You review results. That is it.

## How to Choose a Done for You SEO Provider

[75% of large enterprises](https://www.hirewithnear.com/blog/seo-outsourcing-statistics) already outsource SEO. The challenge is not finding a provider. It is finding the right one.

### The 5-Question Vetting Framework

Ask these questions before signing any contract:

**1. "What will you deliver in month 1?"**

A good provider maps out the first 30 days in detail. Keyword research, site audit, content calendar, first batch of articles. A bad provider says "we will assess your situation."

**2. "How do you build content strategy?"**

The answer should involve [keyword research](/glossary/keyword-research), competitor analysis, and [topical authority](/blog/build-topical-authority) mapping. If they skip strategy and jump straight to writing, the content will rank for nothing.

**3. "Can I see work you have done for a similar business?"**

Request case studies with real URLs, not anonymized screenshots. Check those URLs in Ahrefs or Semrush to verify the claims.

**4. "Who writes the content?"**

[96.55% of pages get zero traffic from Google](https://ahrefs.com/blog/search-traffic-study/). Most of those pages have generic, thin content. Know whether your provider uses experienced writers, AI with human editing, or fully automated output.

**5. "What happens if I cancel?"**

Understand the contract terms. Do you keep the content? Do you keep website access? Are there cancellation penalties? A provider confident in their results does not need a 12-month lock-in.

## Red Flags That Signal a Bad SEO Provider

Google maintains an [official guide on hiring SEO professionals](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/do-i-need-seo). Their first warning: "Be wary of SEOs that claim to guarantee rankings."

Here are the specific red flags to watch for:

![8 red flags when hiring a done for you SEO provider](/images/blog/done-for-you-seo-red-flags.webp)

### Guaranteed Rankings

No one can guarantee a #1 position on Google. Google's algorithm uses over 200 ranking factors and changes thousands of times per year. Any provider that promises specific positions is either lying or using tactics that will get your site penalized.

### No Transparency on Methods

If a provider will not explain what they do each month, they may be doing nothing. Or they may be using black-hat tactics like link farms, private blog networks, or content spinning. Ask for a monthly deliverable report with specific URLs, word counts, and keyword targets.

### Suspiciously Low Pricing

An agency that charges $200 per month for "full SEO" is almost certainly outsourcing everything to the cheapest labor available. The output will be generic, unoptimized content that wastes your time and money.

### Long Lock-In Contracts

Month-to-month billing signals confidence. A 12-month contract with early termination fees signals a provider who knows clients leave after seeing poor results.

### Vanity Metrics in Reporting

Watch for reports that focus on domain authority, backlink count, or keyword rankings without connecting them to traffic, leads, or revenue. Metrics that do not tie to business outcomes are distractions. Use our [SEO ROI calculator](/tools/seo-roi-calculator) to check whether your results translate to real value.

### The Red Flag Checklist

- [ ] Guarantees specific rankings or traffic numbers
- [ ] Refuses to show past client results with real URLs
- [ ] Cannot explain their content strategy in plain language
- [ ] Requires 6-12 month contracts with cancellation penalties
- [ ] Reports on vanity metrics without business outcomes
- [ ] Uses outsourced link building from unknown sources
- [ ] Delivers content without keyword targeting or SEO optimization
- [ ] Takes weeks to respond to questions or concerns

If a provider triggers 3 or more of these flags, move on.

## What to Expect Month by Month

SEO compounds over time. The biggest mistake businesses make with done for you SEO is expecting month-1 results from a month-6 strategy. Here is a realistic timeline.

![Done for you SEO results timeline month by month](/images/blog/done-for-you-seo-timeline.webp)

### Month 1: Foundation

Your provider audits your site, conducts keyword research, and builds a content calendar. First articles get published. No ranking changes yet. This month is about strategy, not results.

**Deliverables:** Site audit, keyword map, content calendar, 8-30 articles published.

### Months 2-3: Indexing and Early Signals

Google indexes new content. You start seeing impressions in Google Search Console. Some long-tail keywords begin ranking on pages 2 and 3. This is normal. [SEO break-even typically takes 6 to 12 months](https://firstpagesage.com/reports/seo-roi-statistics-fc/).

**Deliverables:** Continued publishing, technical fixes from audit, first performance report.

### Months 4-6: Traction

Long-tail keywords move to page 1. Organic traffic increases measurably. Internal links between posts create [topical authority](/blog/build-topical-authority) signals that Google rewards. This is where the compounding effect starts.

**Deliverables:** Content updates, new keyword targets, link building begins.

### Months 7-12: Compounding Growth

Established content ranks for multiple keyword variations. Older posts gain authority as new posts link to them. Traffic grows month over month without additional effort on each individual post.

The [median SEO ROI is 748%](https://firstpagesage.com/reports/seo-roi-statistics-fc/). That means $7.48 returned for every $1 invested. But that return concentrates in months 7 through 24. Businesses that quit at month 4 never see it.

### Month 12+: Maintenance and Expansion

At this stage, your provider shifts focus from building authority to defending rankings and expanding into new keyword clusters. Content refreshes keep existing posts current. New content targets higher-competition terms you could not rank for at month 1.

## When Done for You SEO Is Not the Right Fit

Done for you SEO works for most businesses. But not all. Here are the exceptions.

### You Have Zero Online Presence

If your website does not exist yet, SEO is premature. Build the site first. Then start SEO. A provider who sells SEO before you have a functional website is selling you a roof before the walls are up.

### Your Business Model Does Not Benefit From Search

Some businesses run entirely on referrals, trade shows, or direct outreach. If your customers do not search Google to find services like yours, SEO spend goes nowhere. Verify demand first. Search your core service terms in Google. If nobody is searching, organic traffic will not help.

### You Need Results This Week

SEO takes 3 to 6 months for early traction. If you need leads next Tuesday, run paid ads. Done for you SEO is a long-term investment, not an emergency fix.

### You Want Full Creative Control

Done for you means someone else writes your content. If your brand voice requires personal storytelling, technical depth that only your team can provide, or editorial review of every sentence, a fully outsourced model may frustrate you. Consider a hybrid approach where you provide the expertise and a [content writing service](/blog/seo-content-writing) handles the optimization and publishing.

### The Decision Framework

| Situation | Best Approach |
|---|---|
| Under $500/month budget, willing to learn | DIY with [free SEO tools](/best/free-seo-tools) |
| $99-$500/month budget, need volume and speed | Done for you SEO (automated) |
| $1,500-$5,000/month budget, complex needs | Done for you SEO (agency or hybrid) |
| $10,000+/month budget, multi-market strategy | In-house team + agency support |
| Need leads within 7 days | Paid ads first, layer in SEO for long-term |

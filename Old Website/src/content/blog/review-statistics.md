## General Online Review Statistics

These numbers show how deeply reviews influence the modern buyer journey.

![Consumer review behavior statistics for 2026](/images/blog/online-review-consumer-behavior-stats.webp)

**1. 95% of consumers read online reviews before making a purchase.**
(Source: [Podium](https://www.podium.com/), 2025)
Nearly every buyer checks reviews. The 5% who do not are statistical outliers.

**2. 84% of consumers use Google to find business reviews.**
(Source: [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025)
Google is the default starting point. No other platform comes close for [local search](/blog/local-seo-statistics).

**3. 74% of consumers check 2 or more review websites before deciding.**
(Source: [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025)
A single platform presence is not enough. Buyers cross-reference.

**4. The average consumer reads 10 reviews before trusting a business.**
(Source: [Statista](https://www.statista.com/), 2025)
Businesses with fewer than 10 reviews face an immediate credibility gap.

**5. 42% of consumers trust online reviews as much as personal recommendations.**
(Source: [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025)
This number dropped from 79% in 2020. Consumers are more skeptical now, but reviews still rank second only to word-of-mouth.

**6. 46% of consumers are suspicious of reviews that appear AI-written.**
(Source: [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025)
AI-generated reviews erode trust. Authentic language matters more than ever.

**7. 48% of consumers read AI-generated review summaries but still check individual reviews.**
(Source: [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025)
AI summaries are a starting point, not a replacement. Buyers still want the raw data.

**8. Only 4% of consumers never read online business reviews.**
(Source: [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025)
Reviews are no longer optional. They are part of the purchase decision for 96% of adults.

## Review Impact on Purchasing Decisions

Reviews do not just build trust. They directly move revenue.

**15. 93% of consumers say online reviews influence their purchase decisions.**
(Source: [Podium](https://www.podium.com/), 2025)
Reviews are the second most influential factor after price for most buyers.

**16. Products with 5+ reviews have a 270% higher purchase likelihood.**
(Source: [Spiegel Research Center](https://spiegel.medill.northwestern.edu/), Northwestern University)
The threshold is low. Five reviews change buying behavior dramatically.

**17. Displaying reviews boosts sales by 19.8% on average.**
(Source: [Spiegel Research Center](https://spiegel.medill.northwestern.edu/), Northwestern University)
This applies across categories. The lift is higher for expensive products (380%) than low-cost ones (190%).

**18. 44% of shoppers will not purchase from a business with zero reviews.**
(Source: [DemandSage](https://www.demandsage.com/online-review-statistics/), 2025)
No reviews is worse than a few mediocre reviews. Silence signals risk.

**19. 86% of shoppers hesitate to buy from businesses with negative reviews.**
(Source: [WiserReview](https://wiserreview.com/blog/online-review-statistics/), 2026)
Hesitation does not mean refusal. But unchecked negative reviews create measurable friction. Learn how to [respond to negative reviews](/blog/respond-to-negative-google-reviews) effectively.

**20. 58% of consumers will pay more or travel farther for businesses with positive reviews.**
(Source: [Podium](https://www.podium.com/), 2025)
Reviews override convenience and price for a majority of buyers.

**21. 72% of travelers say online reviews influence their booking decisions.**
(Source: [WiserReview](https://wiserreview.com/blog/online-review-statistics/), 2026)
For hospitality and tourism businesses, reviews are the primary conversion driver.

## Star Rating and Review Volume Statistics

The specific number of stars and the total count of reviews both affect how consumers evaluate a business.

![Star ratings and revenue impact data](/images/blog/review-star-rating-revenue-impact.webp)

**22. 57% of consumers require a business to have 4+ stars before they will use it.**
(Source: [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025)
Anything below 4 stars filters out more than half of potential customers.

**23. 31% of consumers now require 4.5+ stars.**
(Source: [WiserReview](https://wiserreview.com/blog/online-review-statistics/), 2026)
This threshold nearly doubled from 17% in the prior year. Expectations are rising fast.

**24. A 1-star improvement in rating increases revenue by 5-9%.**
(Source: [Spiegel Research Center](https://spiegel.medill.northwestern.edu/), Northwestern University)
Moving from 3.5 to 4.5 stars can mean a 10-18% revenue gain.

**25. 67% of consumers will not trust high ratings without substantial review volume.**
(Source: [DemandSage](https://www.demandsage.com/online-review-statistics/), 2025)
A 5.0 rating with 3 reviews is less convincing than a 4.6 with 200 reviews.

**26. 43% of consumers prefer businesses with more than 100 reviews.**
(Source: [DemandSage](https://www.demandsage.com/online-review-statistics/), 2025)
Volume signals legitimacy. Businesses should consistently [ask customers for reviews](/blog/ask-customers-for-reviews) to build this number.

**27. Products with 11-30 reviews convert 68% higher than those with zero.**
(Source: [Spiegel Research Center](https://spiegel.medill.northwestern.edu/), Northwestern University)
The sweet spot is 26-50 reviews for most products and services.

| Star Rating | Consumer Willingness | Revenue Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Below 3.3 stars | Most consumers reject | Negative revenue effect |
| 3.3 - 3.9 stars | Acceptable to some | Neutral to slight positive |
| 4.0 - 4.4 stars | Acceptable to 57% | 5-9% revenue lift per star |
| 4.5 - 5.0 stars | Preferred by 31% | Peak conversion rates |

## Fake Review Statistics

Fake reviews are a growing problem. Regulators and platforms are responding.

![Fake review statistics for 2026](/images/blog/fake-review-statistics-2026.webp)

**34. An estimated 30% of all online reviews are fake.**
(Source: [Invesp](https://www.invespcro.com/), 2025)
Nearly 1 in 3 reviews is fabricated. This erodes consumer trust across every platform.

**35. Google removed or blocked 240 million policy-violating reviews in 2024.**
(Source: [Google](https://blog.google/), 2025)
Google is actively fighting fake reviews at scale. But 240 million removals shows the scope of the problem.

**36. 82% of consumers have read a fake review in the past year.**
(Source: [Invesp](https://www.invespcro.com/), 2025)
Most consumers encounter fake reviews regularly. Learn how to [handle fake Google reviews](/blog/handle-fake-google-reviews) when they target your business.

**37. 67% of consumers consider fake reviews a major problem.**
(Source: [AgilityPR](https://www.agilitypr.com/), 2025)
Consumer awareness is high. Brands caught using fake reviews face permanent trust damage.

**38. An estimated 42% of Amazon reviews may be fake.**
(Source: [WiserReview](https://wiserreview.com/blog/online-review-statistics/), 2026)
Amazon has the highest estimated fake review rate of any major platform.

**39. The FTC can fine businesses up to $53,088 per fake review violation.**
(Source: [FTC](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/blog/2025/12/warning-letter-or-ten-businesses-comply-ftcs-consumer-review-rule), 2025)
The FTC issued its first enforcement warnings under the Consumer Review Rule in December 2025. Penalties will rise with inflation adjustments.

## Review Generation and Solicitation Statistics

How and when businesses ask for reviews impacts the volume and quality they receive.

**40. 40% of consumers are most likely to respond to email review requests.**
(Source: [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025)
Email outperforms SMS, in-person requests, and social media for review solicitation. This number grew from 32% in 2024.

**41. 27% of consumers respond when asked for a review in person.**
(Source: [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025)
In-person requests remain effective, especially for service businesses like dentists, salons, and [home service providers](/blog/home-services-seo-guide).

**42. 29% of consumers wrote a business review in the past year.**
(Source: [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025)
Up from 27% the year before. The gap between review readers (95%) and review writers (29%) is enormous.

**43. 75% of consumers leave reviews to help businesses improve.**
(Source: [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025)
Most reviewers have good intentions. Only 7% leave reviews primarily after negative experiences.

**44. 96% of consumers are open to writing a review when asked.**
(Source: [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025)
The barrier is not willingness. It is the ask. Businesses that [ask customers for reviews](/blog/get-more-google-reviews-local-business) consistently outperform those that wait.

**45. 23% of consumers say their experience was not noteworthy enough to review.**
(Source: [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/research/local-consumer-review-survey-2025/), 2025)
Framing the ask around specific outcomes ("How was your cleaning today?") overcomes this objection.

## General Local Search Behavior

These 10 statistics show how consumers find local businesses through search.

**1. 46% of all Google searches have local intent.**
Source: Search Engine Roundtable
Nearly half of every Google query seeks a local result. That is billions of searches per day with purchase intent baked in.

**2. 98% of consumers search online for nearby companies.**
Source: BrightLocal, 2024
Only 2% of consumers skip online search entirely. A local business without online visibility is invisible to 98 out of 100 potential customers.

**3. 80% of U.S. consumers search for local businesses weekly.**
Source: SOCi Consumer Behavior Index, 2024
Four out of 5 Americans run a local search every week. Weekly search frequency means weekly opportunities to [rank higher on Google](/blog/how-to-rank-higher-google).

**4. 32% search for local businesses daily.**
Source: SOCi, 2024
Nearly 1 in 3 consumers searches for a local business every day. Daily searchers represent the highest-intent buyers in your market.

**5. 87% of consumers use Google to find local businesses.**
Source: Statista, 2024
Google dominates local discovery. No other platform comes close for local search volume.

**6. 1.5 billion "near me" searches happen every month.**
Source: Synup, 2024
That is 50 million "near me" queries per day. Each one signals a consumer ready to visit, call, or buy.

**7. 900% increase in "near me" searches over the past 2 years.**
Source: Google/Think with Google
"Near me" queries grew 9x in 24 months. This growth rate shows no signs of slowing through 2026.

**8. 72% of consumers who did a local search visited a store within 5 miles.**
Source: HubSpot/Google
Local search drives foot traffic. Nearly 3 in 4 searchers end up at a physical location close to them.

**9. 62% of consumers would avoid a business with incorrect online information.**
Source: BrightLocal, 2023
Wrong hours, old phone numbers, or a bad address cost you customers. Accurate [citations](/glossary/citation) are not optional.

**10. 42% of searchers click on results within the Google Map Pack.**
Source: Backlinko, 2024
The [local pack](/glossary/local-pack) captures nearly half of all clicks. Ranking in those top 3 map results matters more than any other position.

## How to Optimize Your Review Site Listings

Submitting a bare-minimum profile wastes the opportunity. Every review site listing should be treated as a landing page. Here is how to maximize the SEO value of each profile.

**Write a unique description for every platform.** Copy-pasting the same bio across 23 sites creates duplicate content. Write a fresh 150-250 word description for each listing. Adjust the angle based on the platform's audience. G2 buyers care about enterprise features. Product Hunt users care about what is new and different. StackShare users care about technical architecture.

**Upload 5-8 screenshots per listing.** Review platforms with rich media profiles rank higher in internal search results. Screenshots also increase the time visitors spend on your profile, which signals quality to the platform's algorithm. Include dashboard views, key features, onboarding screens, and results dashboards.

**Fill every optional field.** Most vendors skip optional fields like integrations, deployment type, target company size, and supported languages. Complete profiles outrank incomplete ones within platform search results. On G2 alone, products with 100% profile completion receive [3x more page views](https://learn.g2.com/hub/g2-university) than those with partial profiles.

**Ask customers for reviews.** A listing with zero reviews ranks below competitors with 10+. The best time to ask for a review is immediately after a customer achieves a measurable result. Send a direct link to your review page on the specific platform. Make it take under 2 minutes. [Review management](/blog/review-management-guide) applies to software review sites the same way it applies to Google reviews.

**Respond to every review.** Most review platforms show vendor responses publicly. Responding to reviews signals that your company is active and engaged. It also gives you a chance to add keywords naturally. A response like "Thank you for highlighting our [keyword research](/blog/keyword-research-for-blog-posts) features" reinforces topical relevance.

**Link to your review profiles from your website.** Add a "Reviews" or "What customers say" section on your homepage or a dedicated reviews page. Link to your G2, Capterra, and Trustpilot profiles. This creates a bidirectional link relationship that strengthens both your [on-page SEO](/blog/on-page-seo-guide) and your review platform rankings.

**Track referral traffic from each platform.** Use UTM parameters on every review site link pointing to your website. This tells you which platforms drive actual visitors and conversions. Double down on the platforms that send qualified traffic. Deprioritize platforms that generate links but zero clicks.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Most SaaS companies make the same errors when submitting to review platform sites. Avoid these.

**Submitting to 5 sites and stopping.** The compounding effect requires volume. Each new referring domain adds marginal authority. Going from 5 to 15 review site backlinks has a bigger impact than going from 0 to 5. Commit to all 105 sites on this list.

**Ignoring nofollow platforms.** Skipping TrustRadius, Trustpilot, and Product Hunt because they use nofollow links is short-sighted. These platforms drive referral traffic, brand recognition, and [entity signals](/blog/build-citation-worthy-brand) that indirectly improve rankings. Google's own documentation states that nofollow is a "hint," not a directive.

**Using the same anchor text everywhere.** If every review site links to your homepage with the same anchor text, it looks unnatural. Vary your linked pages. Point some profiles to your homepage, others to a features page, and others to a specific product page. Natural [anchor text](/glossary/anchor-text) diversity protects against over-optimization penalties.

**Neglecting profile maintenance.** Review site profiles are not "set and forget." Update screenshots when your UI changes. Refresh descriptions when you launch new features. Add new integrations to your profile's integration list. Stale profiles lose ranking within the platform and send weaker signals to search engines.

**Paying for placement before earning organic reviews.** Paid placement on G2 or Capterra without any organic reviews looks suspicious to buyers. Earn 10-15 genuine reviews first. Then consider paid promotion to amplify an already-credible profile.

**Do software review sites give dofollow or nofollow backlinks?**

Both. Of the 23 sites listed here, 14 provide dofollow links and 9 provide nofollow links. G2, Capterra, SourceForge, Clutch, and AlternativeTo are among the dofollow sites. TrustRadius, Trustpilot, and Product Hunt use nofollow. Check the [dofollow vs nofollow guide](/blog/dofollow-vs-nofollow) for a full explanation of how each type affects rankings.

**How much does it cost to get listed on review sites?**

You can build a strong backlink profile from review sites without spending anything.

**How long does it take for review site backlinks to impact rankings?**

**Should I focus on review sites or guest posting for backlinks?**

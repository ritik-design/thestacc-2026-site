## Which Platforms Work Best for Cleaning Companies

Not every platform deserves your time. Focus on 2 to 3 platforms where your clients actually spend time. For cleaning companies, the winners are clear.

### Platform Comparison for Cleaning Businesses

| Platform | Best For | Audience | Effort Level | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Facebook** | Local community groups, ads, reviews | Homeowners 30-65 | Medium | High |
| **Instagram** | Before/after photos, Reels, Stories | Homeowners 25-50 | Medium | High |
| **TikTok** | Cleaning videos, viral reach | All ages, especially 18-40 | Low-Medium | Medium |
| **Nextdoor** | Hyper-local recommendations | Homeowners in your area | Low | High |

### Start With Facebook and Instagram

Facebook reaches homeowners aged 30 to 65. Local community groups and neighborhood pages are where cleaning recommendations happen every week. A single "anyone know a good cleaner?" post generates 20 to 50 comments. If your business appears in those threads, you win leads for free.

Instagram works for visual proof. Before-and-after cleaning photos perform better than any other content type in the cleaning industry. The platform now prioritizes Reels (short video), which means a 15-second cleaning time-lapse reaches more people than a static photo.

## 10 Post Ideas That Generate Cleaning Leads

Most cleaning business owners stall on social media because they run out of ideas after the first week. These 10 content types work on every platform and never run dry.

![10 social media post ideas for cleaning companies](/images/blog/cleaning-social-post-ideas.webp)

### The Content Mix

| # | Post Type | Example | Best Platform |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Before and after** | Kitchen deep clean transformation | Instagram, Facebook, TikTok |
| 2 | **Quick cleaning tip** | "Remove soap scum with vinegar + dish soap" | Instagram Reels, TikTok |
| 3 | **Customer testimonial** | Screenshot of a 5-star review with a thank-you caption | Facebook, Instagram Stories |
| 4 | **Team spotlight** | "Meet Sarah. She has been with us for 3 years." | Facebook, Instagram |
| 5 | **Cleaning time-lapse** | 30-second video of a full bathroom clean | TikTok, Instagram Reels |
| 6 | **Seasonal checklist** | "Spring cleaning checklist for homeowners" | Facebook, Instagram carousel |
| 7 | **FAQ answer** | "Yes, we bring our own supplies. Here is what we use." | Instagram Stories, Facebook |
| 8 | **Community involvement** | "We donated a free deep clean to [local charity]" | Facebook, Nextdoor |
| 9 | **Promotion or offer** | "First-time clients: 15% off your first deep clean" | Facebook, Instagram |
| 10 | **Behind the scenes** | Loading the van, organizing supplies, team meeting | Instagram Stories, TikTok |

Before-and-after photos are the single most effective post type. Take a "before" photo at every job. Shoot the "after" from the same angle. Post them side by side. No caption needed beyond the location type ("Kitchen deep clean in [City]"). The visual does the selling.

### Handling the "Before" Photo Problem

The biggest logistical challenge: remembering to take the "before" photo. Make it part of your process. Before you put on gloves, take the photo. Set a phone reminder if needed. After 2 weeks, the habit becomes automatic.

Always get client permission before posting photos of their home. A quick verbal "Is it okay if we share a before and after?" is enough. Most clients are proud to show the transformation.

### Hashtag Strategy for Cleaning Companies

Use 5 to 10 hashtags per post. Mix local and industry tags:

**Local hashtags:** #[City]Cleaning, #[City]MaidService, #[City]DeepClean, #[City]HomeServices
**Industry hashtags:** #BeforeAndAfterCleaning, #DeepClean, #CleaningMotivation, #SatisfyingCleaning, #CleanHome
**Niche hashtags:** #MoveOutCleaning, #SpringCleaning, #CommercialCleaning, #AirbnbCleaning

Avoid generic tags like #cleaning or #clean (too broad, 100 million+ posts). Use specific combinations that match your service and location.

## How to Create a Month of Content in One Hour

Time is the biggest barrier for cleaning business owners. You clean 8 to 10 hours a day. Creating social media content feels impossible. The fix: batch creation.

### The Batch Content System

**Step 1: Take photos at every job (5 seconds each).** Before photo. After photo. That is it. Do this at 3 to 4 jobs per week. By the end of the month, you have 12 to 16 before-and-after sets.

**Step 2: Block 1 hour on Sunday.** Open Canva (free) or your scheduling tool. Create posts for the entire week using the photos you collected. Write 1 to 2 sentence captions. Schedule them to publish automatically.

**Step 3: Use a scheduling tool.** Schedule posts in advance so they publish while you work. ## Short-Form Video: The Highest-ROI Content Type

[Short-form video delivers 41% ROI](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/social-media-statistics/), the highest of any content format. [85% of people say video convinced them to buy a product](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/social-media-statistics/). For cleaning companies, video content is the easiest win because the work itself is visual.

### What to Film

- **Cleaning time-lapses.** Set your phone on a counter. Hit record. Clean the area. Speed it up 4x. Post. 15 seconds. Done.
- **Product demos.** Show a cleaning product removing a stain. No script needed. The result speaks for itself.
- **Satisfying details.** Close-ups of grout cleaning, pressure washing, or window streaks disappearing. These clips go viral because they trigger a satisfaction response.
- **"Most disgusting job" series.** Show the worst messes you tackle (with client permission). These generate high engagement because people love dramatic transformations.

### Tips for Filming

- **Use natural light** whenever possible. Open blinds before filming.
- **Film vertically** (9:16 ratio) for Reels and TikTok.
- **Keep videos under 30 seconds.** Shorter clips get more views and shares.
- **Add text overlay.** "Watch this kitchen go from gross to spotless" drives more views than no text.
- **No need for professional equipment.** Your phone is enough. A $15 phone tripod helps with stability.

TikTok's engagement rate is [3.70%](https://sproutsocial.com/insights/social-media-statistics/), the highest of any platform. A cleaning video that gets 10,000 views generates 370 engagements. Some of those turn into leads.

## Running Ads on $5 to $10 Per Day

Organic reach on Facebook pages has dropped below 5%. Paid ads amplify your best content to the exact audience you want.

### Facebook and Instagram Ad Setup for Cleaning Companies

**Budget:** $5 to $10 per day ($150 to $300 per month).

**Targeting:**
- Location: Your service area (specific zip codes or a radius around your office)
- Age: 28 to 65
- Interests: Home improvement, interior design, cleaning, home organization
- Homeowners (Facebook allows homeowner targeting in many regions)

**Best ad format:** Before-and-after carousel or a 15-second cleaning video.

**CTA:** "Book Now" or "Get a Free Quote."

### What to Expect

A cleaning company spending $10 per day on well-targeted Facebook ads typically generates 5 to 15 leads per week. At a 30% close rate and $150 average job value, that is $225 to $675 in weekly revenue from a $70 weekly ad spend.

Track results using Facebook's built-in reporting. Monitor cost per lead and cost per booking. If a lead costs more than $20, adjust your targeting or creative.

Nextdoor offers local business advertising with hyper-targeted reach. Every user is address-verified, meaning your ad reaches confirmed homeowners in specific neighborhoods. Cleaning companies report lower cost-per-lead on Nextdoor than Facebook because the audience is pre-qualified by location and homeownership.

Claim your free Nextdoor business page. Post helpful cleaning tips in your local feed. Then test a small ad budget ($5 per day) targeting neighborhoods in your service area. The combination of organic Nextdoor presence and paid promotion performs well for service businesses.

## Measuring Social Media ROI for Cleaning Companies

The biggest frustration cleaning business owners have with social media: they cannot tell if it works. Here is how to track it.

### 4 Ways to Attribute Bookings to Social Media

**1. "How did you hear about us?"** Ask every new client. Track answers in a spreadsheet. Simple and effective.

**2. Social-only promo codes.** Create a discount code exclusive to your Instagram or Facebook followers. Track redemptions.

**3. UTM links.** Add tracking parameters to the link in your bio. Google Analytics shows exactly how many visitors came from each platform and whether they booked.

**4. Direct messages and comments.** Count inquiries that arrive through Facebook Messenger, Instagram DMs, and post comments. These are direct social media leads.

### Metrics to Track Monthly

| Metric | Why It Matters | Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Follower growth** | Growing audience for future posts | 5% to 10% monthly growth |
| **Engagement rate** | Whether people interact with your content | 2%+ on Instagram, 1%+ on Facebook |
| **Website clicks** | Whether social drives traffic to your booking page | Upward trend monthly |
| **Inquiries from social** | Direct leads from DMs, comments, and Messenger | Track total count monthly |
| **Bookings attributed to social** | Actual revenue generated | Positive ROI over ad spend + time |

If social media generates even 2 new bookings per month at $150 each, that is $300 in revenue. Against a time investment of 1 hour per week and $0 to $300 in ad spend, the ROI is positive for most cleaning companies.

## FAQ

**Do cleaning companies need social media?**

Yes. Cleaning businesses that post regularly see 2.7x more website traffic. Social media builds trust by showing your work. Before-and-after photos give potential clients visual proof of quality. Most clients research a cleaning company online before booking. An active social media presence with recent posts and reviews makes you the obvious choice over competitors with no online presence.

**What should a cleaning company post on social media?**

Before-and-after cleaning photos, quick cleaning tips, customer testimonials, team spotlights, seasonal checklists, and short cleaning videos. Before-and-after transformations are the single highest-performing content type. Take a photo before and after every job. Post them side by side with a short caption and your service area.

**How often should a cleaning company post on social media?**

3 to 5 times per week on your primary platform (Facebook or Instagram). Start with 3 per week to build consistency. Increase over time. Use a scheduling tool to batch-create and auto-publish posts. One hour per week is enough to create a full week of content.

**Is Facebook or Instagram better for a cleaning company?**

Both work. Facebook excels for community groups, local targeting, and reaching homeowners 30 to 65. Instagram excels for visual content (before-and-afters, Reels) and reaching homeowners 25 to 50. Start with Facebook if your clients are older homeowners. Start with Instagram if you create strong visual content. Ideally, use both.

**How do I get cleaning clients from social media?**

Post before-and-after photos consistently. Join local Facebook groups and Nextdoor. Run targeted ads at $5 to $10 per day in your service area. Respond to every comment and DM within 1 hour. Include a booking link in every bio and post. Ask satisfied clients to share your page with friends.

**How much should a cleaning company spend on social media ads?**

Start with $5 to $10 per day ($150 to $300 per month). Target homeowners in your service zip codes. Use before-and-after photos or short cleaning videos as your ad creative. Track cost per lead. If leads cost under $20 each, scale your budget. If they cost more, adjust targeting or creative before spending more.

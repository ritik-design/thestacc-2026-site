## FAQ

**How many businesses have a Google Business Profile?**

Over 200 million businesses are listed on Google Maps, with approximately 1.5 million new listings added monthly. However, only 64% of listed businesses have claimed and verified their profiles. Verified and complete profiles appear 80% more often in search results.

**What percentage of Google searches are local?**

46% of all Google searches have local intent. With Google processing an estimated 16 billion searches per day, that translates to over 7 billion local searches daily. "Near me" searches are among the fastest-growing query types.

**How many reviews does the average business need to rank?**

Top-ranking local businesses have an average of 47 [Google reviews](/glossary/google-reviews). However, review velocity (frequency of new reviews) matters as much as total count. Businesses with at least 1 new review per week rank 25% higher than those with stale review profiles.

**Do Google Business Profile posts help with rankings?**

Yes. Businesses posting to GBP weekly gain an average of 2.3 local pack positions over 6 months compared to businesses that do not post. Offer posts generate the highest click-through rates. See our [GBP posting frequency guide](/blog/gbp-posting-frequency) for a detailed schedule.

**What is the most important GBP ranking factor?**

The primary business category is the single most influential ranking factor for the local pack. GBP signals as a whole account for 32% of local pack ranking factors according to BrightLocal's 2026 study. This makes your profile more influential than your website, backlinks, and citations combined.

**How do Google reviews affect consumer decisions?**

88% of consumers read Google reviews before choosing a local business. 68% will not use a business rated below 4 stars. 73% require reviews from the last 30 days to trust a business. These numbers make active review management essential for any local business.

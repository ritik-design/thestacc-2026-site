## Chapter 2: The Best Platforms for HVAC Companies

You do not need to be on every platform. Focus on the 2 to 3 that reach homeowners in your service area.

### Facebook. The Lead Generator

Facebook is the top platform for HVAC companies. Local Facebook groups drive organic leads. Homeowners ask "who do you recommend for AC repair?" in neighborhood groups. If your company has an active presence and strong reviews, you get tagged.

**What works on Facebook for HVAC:**
- Participation in local community groups
- Before-and-after photos of installs and repairs
- Customer testimonial posts
- Seasonal promotion announcements
- Facebook Ads targeting homeowners in your service radius

**Post frequency:** 3 to 5 times per week.

Contractors who actively engage in local Facebook groups report generating [12 to 15 leads per month](https://www.truefuturemedia.com/articles/boring-business-boom-hvac-plumbing-pest-control-social-media) with close rates around 60%. That is 7 to 9 booked jobs per month from Facebook alone.

### YouTube. The Trust Builder

YouTube is the second-largest search engine. Homeowners search "how much does a new AC unit cost" and "is my furnace noise normal" on YouTube before calling anyone. HVAC companies that answer these questions with video become the trusted expert.

**What works on YouTube for HVAC:**
- "How much does [service] cost?" videos
- Maintenance tips (change your filter, check your thermostat)
- "What is that noise?" diagnostic videos
- System installation time-lapses
- Annual "prepare your AC for summer" guides

**Post frequency:** 1 to 2 videos per week.

YouTube content ranks in Google search results too. A video titled "AC repair cost [your city] 2026" captures homeowners in your area who are actively researching services. That is a warm lead.

### Instagram. The Visual Portfolio

Instagram showcases your work visually. Before-and-after installs, team photos, and short Reels of jobs in progress build credibility. Instagram Stories keep your company visible between appointments.

**What works on Instagram for HVAC:**
- Before-and-after installation photos
- 15-second Reels showing the work process
- Team spotlight Stories
- Seasonal maintenance tip carousels
- Customer review screenshots

**Post frequency:** 3 to 5 times per week (feed + Reels + Stories).

### Google Business Profile. The Local Search Driver

Your [Google Business Profile](/blog/optimize-google-business-profile) is where homeowners find you in "near me" searches. GBP posts, photos, and [customer reviews](/blog/ask-customers-for-reviews) directly influence your local pack ranking.

**What works on GBP for HVAC:**
- Weekly posts about services and seasonal specials
- Fresh photos of completed work
- Responding to every review (positive and negative)
- Updated service areas, hours, and contact info

**Post frequency:** 1 to 2 times per week.

## Chapter 3: 7 Content Types That Book Service Calls

Most HVAC companies post the same thing: a stock photo of an air conditioner with "Call us today!" That does not work. These 7 content types do.

### 1. Before-and-After Photos and Videos

Show the old, failing system next to the clean new installation. Show a dirty filter next to a clean one. Show a rusted-out unit next to the replacement. The visual contrast is powerful. Homeowners see the transformation and think "I need that."

**Pro tip:** Film a 15-second time-lapse of the installation. Post it as a Reel on Instagram and a Short on YouTube. The same video works on both platforms.

### 2. Seasonal Maintenance Tips

"3 things to check on your AC before summer." "How to prepare your furnace for winter." These posts provide genuine value and position your team as experts. They also generate saves and shares. Homeowners share maintenance tips with neighbors.

This content works especially well as a [blog post](/blog/create-business-blog) that you then share across social platforms.

### 3. Customer Testimonials and Reviews

Screenshot a 5-star Google review. Post it with a brief story about the job. Better yet, film a 30-second video testimonial with the homeowner standing next to their new system. Social proof drives bookings faster than any ad.

Collecting reviews consistently is critical. Our guide on [how to ask customers for reviews](/blog/ask-customers-for-reviews) covers the full system.

### 4. Meet the Team

Introduce each technician. Share their certification, years of experience, and a personal fact. Homeowners let HVAC techs into their homes. They want to know who is showing up. A team spotlight post builds comfort before the service call.

### 5. Emergency Tips

"What to do when your AC stops working at 10 PM." "Is that furnace smell dangerous?" Emergency content gets high engagement because it addresses urgent pain. It also positions your company as the helpful expert. When the homeowner needs a repair, they call the company that already helped them.

### 6. Cost and Pricing Transparency

"How much does a new AC unit cost in [city]?" Most homeowners research pricing before calling. A Facebook or YouTube post that answers this question honestly earns trust and captures price-sensitive leads that competitors ignore.

Price transparency is rare in HVAC marketing. The companies that share real pricing ranges stand out.

### 7. Community and Local Content

Sponsor a little league team. Attend a local event. Support a food drive. Post about it. Local involvement shows homeowners that your company is part of their community. That connection drives referrals and repeat business.

## Chapter 4: The Weekly Content Calendar

Consistency beats creativity in HVAC social media. Here is a weekly schedule you can follow.

| Day | Content Type | Platform | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Monday** | Maintenance Tip | Facebook, Instagram | "Change your filter every 90 days. Here is why." |
| **Tuesday** | Before-and-After | Instagram Reels, Facebook | Time-lapse of a heat pump installation |
| **Wednesday** | Energy Saving Tip | Facebook, YouTube | "Set your thermostat to 78 in summer to save 10%+" |
| **Thursday** | Team Spotlight | Instagram Stories, Facebook | "Meet [Name], our lead technician with 15 years experience" |
| **Friday** | Customer Review | Facebook, Instagram | Screenshot of Google review with thank-you caption |
| **Saturday** | Seasonal Offer | Facebook, GBP | "Summer AC tune-up special: $89 this month" |
| **Sunday** | Community / Fun | Instagram Stories | Weekend project, team photo, local event |

### Batch Your Content

HVAC techs are in the field all day. Content creation cannot happen daily. Block 1 to 2 hours on a slow day. Film all videos. Take all photos. Write all captions. [Schedule everything](/best/free-social-media-scheduling-tools) for the week. Then spend 5 to 10 minutes per day responding to comments and messages.

### Seasonal Content Strategy

HVAC demand is seasonal. Your content should match.

| Season | Focus Topics |
|---|---|
| **Spring** | AC tune-ups, allergy season air quality, filter changes |
| **Summer** | Emergency AC repair, energy savings, new installation specials |
| **Fall** | Furnace maintenance, thermostat upgrades, heating prep |
| **Winter** | Emergency heating repair, carbon monoxide safety, holiday specials |

Plan your content calendar 30 days in advance. Align promotions with seasonal demand. When everyone needs AC repair in July, your content should already be running about AC services in June.

### Recycle Content Across Platforms

A before-and-after installation video filmed for Facebook becomes an Instagram Reel, a YouTube Short, and a still photo for Google Business Profile. One piece of content becomes 4 posts across 4 platforms. [Repurposing content](/blog/repurpose-blog-content-social-media) multiplies your reach without multiplying your effort. Most HVAC companies create enough content during a normal work week to fill their social calendar. They just do not capture it.

Every social media profile needs 3 things:

## Chapter 6: 5 Mistakes HVAC Companies Make on Social Media

### 1. Only Posting When Business Is Slow

Most HVAC companies post during slow seasons and go silent during busy ones. The algorithm penalizes inconsistency. Your audience forgets you. Post year-round. 3 posts per week minimum, every week.

### 2. No Before-and-After Content

Stock photos of generic air conditioners do not build trust. Real photos of your team's real work do. Every job is a content opportunity. Take a before photo. Take an after photo. Post it.

### 3. Ignoring Google Business Profile

Many HVAC companies focus on Facebook but neglect their [Google Business Profile](/blog/optimize-google-business-profile). GBP posts and reviews directly influence "HVAC near me" rankings. An active GBP generates more leads than any social platform for local service businesses.

### 4. Not Responding to Comments and Messages

A homeowner asks "Do you service [zip code]?" on your Facebook post. Three days later, still no reply. That homeowner called your competitor. Respond within 1 hour during business hours. Set up auto-replies for after hours.

### 5. No Seasonal Content Strategy

Posting the same content year-round misses seasonal demand. AC content in January and heating content in July wastes effort. Align your content calendar with seasonal service demand.

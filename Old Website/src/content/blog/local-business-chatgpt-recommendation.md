## How ChatGPT Sources Local Business Recommendations

ChatGPT does not search the internet the same way Google does. Understanding where it pulls data is the first step to showing up in its answers.

ChatGPT uses 3 primary sources for local business information:

| Source | What It Provides | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Bing Search API | Real-time web results, business listings | High |
| Foursquare | Business name, address, category, ratings | High |
| Training Data | Historical web content, reviews, mentions | Medium |

[A study from Local Falcon](https://www.localfalcon.com/blog/chatgpt-local-search-data-sources-where-does-business-info-come-from) found that 60% to 70% of local businesses ChatGPT shows come directly from Foursquare data. ChatGPT also relies on Bing's search API for 92% of its real-time web searches.

This means your traditional Google-first SEO strategy leaves major gaps. You need to be visible on the platforms ChatGPT actually reads.

![How ChatGPT sources local business data](/images/blog/chatgpt-local-business-data-sources.webp)

ChatGPT evaluates 5 signals before recommending a business:

1. **Authoritative list mentions** on high-ranking websites
2. **Awards and accreditations** from recognized organizations
3. **Online reviews** across platforms like Google, Yelp, and Trustpilot
4. **Customer usage data** showing real adoption and engagement
5. **Social sentiment** from Reddit, X, and news coverage

Businesses with review scores below 70% positive are far less likely to get recommended. The bar is not perfection. It is consistent quality across multiple platforms.

## Step 1: Claim and Optimize Your Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile remains the single most important local listing on the internet. It feeds AI tools that pull from Google's data ecosystem. And it is the foundation every other optimization builds on.

**Specifically:**

- [ ] Claim your profile at [business.google.com](https://business.google.com) if you have not already
- [ ] Complete every field: name, address, phone, website, hours, service area
- [ ] Select the most specific primary category for your business
- [ ] Add 3 to 5 secondary categories that describe your services
- [ ] Upload at least 10 high-quality photos (exterior, interior, team, work)
- [ ] Write a business description with your primary services and location
- [ ] Add all services and products with descriptions

A [complete GBP](/blog/optimize-google-business-profile/) sends strong signals to both Google and the AI systems that pull from Google's index. ChatGPT cross-references multiple data points. The more complete your profile, the more confident the AI is in recommending you.

**Why this step matters:** ChatGPT mirrors many of the top Google results. If your GBP is incomplete, you rank lower in Google. That means you also rank lower in ChatGPT's data pool.

**Pro tip:** [Choose your GBP categories carefully](/blog/gbp-categories-guide/). The primary category carries the most weight. A plumber who lists "Plumber" as the primary category ranks better than one who lists "Home Improvement."

Post regularly to your GBP. Businesses that publish [GBP posts consistently](/blog/gbp-posting-frequency/) signal active engagement. AI systems interpret freshness as reliability.

## Step 2: Create and Optimize Your Bing Places Listing

This is the step most local businesses skip. It is also the most impactful for ChatGPT visibility.

ChatGPT uses the Bing Search API for 92% of its real-time web searches. Bing Places is the local business directory that feeds Bing's search results. If your business is not on Bing Places, ChatGPT may never see you.

**Specifically:**

- [ ] Go to [bingplaces.com](https://www.bingplaces.com) and claim your listing
- [ ] Import your Google Business Profile data to save time
- [ ] Verify your business via phone, email, or postcard
- [ ] Add photos, business hours, and a detailed description
- [ ] Include all service categories and areas served
- [ ] Add your website URL with proper tracking parameters

Your [Bing Places listing](/blog/bing-places-guide/) should mirror your Google Business Profile exactly. Same name. Same address. Same phone number. Same categories. AI models cross-reference data across platforms. Inconsistencies reduce trust.

**Why this step matters:** Bing Places is ChatGPT's primary source for local business data. Skipping this step is like ignoring Google for traditional SEO.

**Pro tip:** Bing Places allows you to import your GBP data directly. The entire setup takes under 15 minutes. There is no excuse to skip it.

Reviews are the most powerful signal that determines whether AI tools recommend your business. [First Page Sage research](https://firstpagesage.com/seo-blog/chatgpt-optimization-guide/) found that businesses with review scores below 70% positive are far less likely to appear in ChatGPT answers.

Volume matters. Quality matters more. But platform diversity matters most.

**The review platforms that influence ChatGPT:**

- **Google Reviews**. Highest volume, strongest overall signal
- **Yelp**. Heavy weight in ChatGPT training data
- **Trustpilot**. Common in B2B and service recommendations
- **BBB**. Trust and accreditation signal
- **Industry platforms**. G2, Clutch, Avvo, Healthgrades (depends on your industry)

**Specifically:**

- [ ] Build a system to [ask every customer for a review](/blog/ask-customers-for-reviews/)
- [ ] Focus on Google first, then Yelp and industry platforms
- [ ] Respond to every review within 24 to 48 hours
- [ ] Address negative reviews professionally and specifically
- [ ] Aim for 70% or higher positive sentiment across all platforms

Do not chase only star ratings. ChatGPT reads review text. A 4.2 star business with 200 detailed reviews outperforms a 4.8 star business with 12 generic ones.

**Why this step matters:** AI models use review sentiment, volume, and recency to determine recommendation confidence. Thin review profiles equal low confidence. Low confidence means no recommendation.

Your [review management system](/blog/review-management-guide/) should treat AI visibility as a core KPI. Not just Google rankings.

## Step 5: Add Schema Markup for Local Businesses

Schema markup is structured data that tells search engines and AI systems exactly what your business does. Without it, AI models have to guess. With it, they get clean, machine-readable facts.

Think of schema as your business card for AI. It lists your name, address, services, hours, reviews, and more in a format machines understand instantly.

**Required schema types for local AI visibility:**

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "Your Business Name",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "123 Main St",
    "addressLocality": "Your City",
    "addressRegion": "State",
    "postalCode": "12345"
  },
  "telephone": "+1-555-555-5555",
  "url": "https://yourbusiness.com",
  "openingHours": "Mo-Fr 09:00-17:00",
  "aggregateRating": {
    "@type": "AggregateRating",
    "ratingValue": "4.8",
    "reviewCount": "156"
  }
}
```

**Specifically:**

- [ ] Add LocalBusiness schema to your homepage
- [ ] Include Service schema on each service page
- [ ] Add FAQ schema to FAQ pages and blog posts
- [ ] Use Review schema for testimonial pages
- [ ] Test with Google's Rich Results Test tool
- [ ] Validate with Schema.org validator

The [local business schema guide](/blog/local-business-schema/) covers every property and sub-type. Use the most specific type available. "Plumber" is better than "LocalBusiness." "Dentist" is better than "MedicalBusiness."

**Why this step matters:** Schema tells AI assistants what you do. Reviews tell them if you are trusted. Conversational content tells them how to phrase you as an answer. Schema is the foundation layer.

## Step 6: Publish Content That Makes Your Business Citable

AI models recommend businesses they can describe clearly. They need specific, factual content to pull from. Generic "about us" pages do not give them enough material.

The goal is to create content that answers the exact questions people ask ChatGPT about businesses like yours.

**Content types that drive AI citations:**

| Content Type | Example | Why AI Uses It |
|---|---|---|
| Service pages | "Emergency Plumbing Repair in Denver" | Matches specific local queries |
| Comparison guides | "Tankless vs Traditional Water Heaters" | Feeds comparison questions |
| FAQ pages | "How Much Does a Roof Replacement Cost?" | Direct answer format |
| How-to guides | "How to Prevent Frozen Pipes" | Expertise signal |
| Local resource pages | "Best Neighborhoods in Austin for Families" | Local authority signal |

**Specifically:**

- [ ] Create a dedicated page for each core service you offer
- [ ] Write location-specific content for every city or area you serve
- [ ] Build an FAQ page that answers 15 to 20 common customer questions
- [ ] Publish educational blog posts that demonstrate expertise
- [ ] Include specific data points: pricing ranges, timelines, success rates

ChatGPT gravitates toward content with specific numbers and clear positioning. "We have served over 2,000 homeowners since 2015" is citable. "We provide quality service to our community" is not.

**Why this step matters:** Generic content gets ignored by AI. Specific, factual, well-structured content gets cited. Your content strategy determines whether ChatGPT has anything useful to say about you.

## Step 7: Build Brand Mentions and Authority Signals

Here is a data point that changes everything: [brand mentions correlate 3x more with AI visibility](https://www.growthunhinged.com/p/get-recommended-by-chatgpt) than backlinks do.

Traditional SEO prioritizes links. AI search prioritizes mentions. A business name that appears across news articles, Reddit threads, industry publications, and local media gets recommended. One that only appears on its own website does not.

**High-impact mention sources:**

- **Local news and media**. Press releases, local business features, event sponsorships
- **Reddit**. Genuine participation in local and industry subreddits
- **Industry publications**. Guest posts, expert quotes, case studies
- **Podcast appearances**. Transcripts become crawlable content
- **Awards and lists** ,  "Best of" lists, industry rankings, chamber awards

**Specifically:**

- [ ] Submit to local "Best of" lists and awards programs
- [ ] Pitch local media for business features and expert commentary
- [ ] Participate genuinely on Reddit in relevant subreddits
- [ ] Seek guest posting on industry blogs and local publications
- [ ] Sponsor local events that generate media coverage
- [ ] Get listed on authoritative industry directories like Clutch or G2

[Brand mentions work differently than backlinks](/blog/brand-mentions-vs-backlinks/) for AI visibility. A mention without a link still counts. ChatGPT reads content. It does not follow links. The mention itself is the signal.

**Why this step matters:** AI models build confidence through consensus. When multiple independent sources mention your business positively, ChatGPT treats you as a safe recommendation. Without mentions, you are invisible.

The exception: brand mentions on low-quality or spammy sites can hurt more than help. Focus on reputable sources. Quality over quantity applies here too.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** ChatGPT recommends competitors but not your business.
**Solution:** Compare your citation count, review volume, and content depth to theirs. The gap is usually in one of those 3 areas. Fix the weakest one first.

**Problem:** ChatGPT mentions your business but describes it incorrectly.
**Solution:** Audit your website's service pages and schema markup. Ensure your business description is clear, specific, and consistent across every platform.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does ChatGPT recommend local businesses?**

Yes. ChatGPT with web search enabled recommends local businesses when users ask for service providers, restaurants, shops, and other local queries. It pulls from Bing, Foursquare, and web content to generate these recommendations. The recommendations include business names, locations, and often brief descriptions of services and reviews.

**How does ChatGPT decide which businesses to recommend?**

ChatGPT evaluates 5 primary signals: authoritative list mentions, awards and accreditations, online reviews, customer usage data, and social sentiment. [First Page Sage research](https://firstpagesage.com/seo-blog/chatgpt-optimization-guide/) found that review quality and list placements carry the highest weight. Businesses with strong, consistent presence across multiple platforms get recommended most often.

**Is Foursquare really important for ChatGPT visibility?**

Extremely. Research shows that 60% to 70% of the local businesses ChatGPT displays come from Foursquare data. Creating and optimizing your Foursquare listing is one of the highest-impact steps you can take. It takes 10 minutes and costs nothing.

**Can I pay to get recommended by ChatGPT?**

No. ChatGPT does not accept paid placements in its recommendations. Paid ads on Google or Bing do not influence ChatGPT answers. The only way to get recommended is through genuine optimization: strong profiles, quality reviews, consistent citations, and authoritative content.

**How long does it take to start appearing in ChatGPT results?**

Most businesses see initial improvements within 2 to 3 months of implementing all 8 steps. Full visibility with consistent recommendations typically takes 3 to 6 months. The timeline depends on your starting point, industry competition, and how quickly you build reviews and citations.

**Should I optimize for ChatGPT differently than Google?**

The strategies overlap significantly. But ChatGPT weighs brand mentions more heavily than backlinks. It also relies on Bing rather than Google for real-time search. Optimizing for both requires a multi-platform approach. The [GEO optimization guide](/blog/geo-optimization-guide/) covers the differences in detail.

## FAQ

**How often should I send a newsletter?**

Once per week is the standard for most businesses. Ecommerce brands often send 2-3 times per week. B2B companies send 1-2 times per month. The right frequency depends on how much value you deliver per email. [According to industry data](https://mailchimp.com/resources/email-marketing-benchmarks/), the average open rate is 42.35%. If your open rate drops below 20%, you are sending too often or not delivering enough value.

**What makes a newsletter get opened?**

Subject lines drive opens. Keep them under 50 characters. Use specific numbers ("5 tips" not "some tips"). Create curiosity without clickbait. Personalization (subscriber name or behavior-based content) increases open rates by 26%. Sending at a consistent day and time trains subscribers to expect your email.

**How long should a newsletter be?**

Most successful newsletters are 200-500 words. The goal is one key idea per email with a clear CTA. Long newsletters get opened but not read. Short, focused emails with one takeaway and one action perform best. The exception is curated roundups and long-form thought leadership, which can run 800-1,200 words.

**What is the best newsletter format for small businesses?**

A mix of educational tips (50%), [customer stories and community content](/blog/social-media-marketing-local-businesses) (30%), and promotional offers (20%). Subscribers open newsletters that teach them something. They unsubscribe from newsletters that only promote. A small business sending one educational email per week builds more trust than daily promotional blasts.

**How do I grow my email list?**

Add a signup form to every page of your website. Offer a lead magnet (checklist, template, discount). Ask for emails at checkout, on social media bios, and in blog post CTAs. Run a giveaway with email entry. The average list grows 2.5% per month with consistent effort.

## What Are Do-Follow Backlinks?

A do-follow backlink is a hyperlink that passes link equity (also called "link juice") from one website to another. When a DA 90 website links to your page with a dofollow link, Google treats that as a vote of confidence. Your page gets a share of that authority.

By default, all links on the web are dofollow unless the website explicitly adds a `rel="nofollow"` attribute to the HTML. The dofollow attribute tells search engine crawlers to follow the link and pass ranking signals.

![Dofollow vs nofollow backlink comparison showing how each type affects SEO](/images/blog/dofollow-vs-nofollow-comparison.webp)

| Factor | Dofollow Links | Nofollow Links |
|---|---|---|
| Link equity | Passes PageRank | Does not pass PageRank |
| Ranking impact | Direct ranking signal | Indirect (Google treats as "hint" since 2019) |
| Domain authority | Boosts receiving site DA | No direct DA boost |
| Default status | Default for all links | Must be explicitly added |
| Google behavior | Crawls and follows | May or may not follow |

Both link types matter for a healthy backlink profile. But dofollow links carry the direct ranking power that moves pages up in search results.

## The Safe Way to Build Do-Follow Backlinks

Building dofollow backlinks requires discipline. Google penalizes manipulative link patterns. Here is how to build backlinks that improve your rankings without triggering penalties.

### Maintain a Natural Link Profile

A natural backlink profile has a mix of dofollow and nofollow links. The optimal ratio is 70:30 or 60:40 dofollow to nofollow. If 100% of your backlinks are dofollow, Google flags that as unnatural.

Search Engine Land has 82.2% followed referring domains. That ratio is considered healthy and organic.

### Diversify Your [Anchor Text](/blog/anchor-text-optimization)

Over-optimized anchor text is a red flag. Use this distribution:

| Anchor Type | Percentage |
|---|---|
| Brand name ("Stacc", "thestacc.com") | 40% |
| Naked URL ("thestacc.com/pricing") | 25% |
| Generic ("click here", "learn more") | 15% |
| Partial match ("SEO content tips") | 15% |
| Exact match ("do-follow backlink sites") | 5% |

### Build Links Gradually

No more than 15 to 20 new backlinks per day. Sudden spikes in link acquisition trigger spam detection. Consistent, gradual growth over months is the safest approach.

### Prioritize Relevance Over DA

A link from a smaller niche blog in your industry outperforms a generic high-DA site. Google weighs topical relevance in 2026 more than ever. A DA 45 marketing blog linking to your marketing content beats a DA 90 cooking site.

### Monitor Your Backlink Profile

Use Google Search Console, Ahrefs, or Semrush to track your backlinks monthly. Watch for [toxic backlinks](/blog/toxic-backlinks-guide) that could trigger a penalty. Disavow any spammy or harmful links promptly.

## Do-Follow vs Nofollow: What Google Actually Says

Since 2019, Google treats nofollow links as "hints" rather than strict directives. That means Google may choose to follow and credit nofollow links when they appear relevant and trustworthy.

The data reflects this. Dofollow links show ranking correlations of 0.334 (Pearson) and 0.504 (Spearman). Nofollow links show nearly identical correlations at 0.340 and 0.509. Both types contribute to rankings.

What does this mean for your strategy?

**Do not ignore nofollow links entirely.** 89% of SEOs believe nofollow links influence rankings. 46.9% of specialists actively pursue nofollow links alongside dofollow ones. A link from Yelp (nofollow) or a major news outlet (30% of editorial links are nofollow) still carries trust signals.

**Dofollow links remain more directly impactful.** They pass explicit link equity. They are the primary mechanism for PageRank distribution. If you have limited time, prioritize dofollow opportunities.

**Natural profiles include both.** Approximately 65.5% of all backlinks on the web are nofollow. Roughly 34.5% are dofollow. A profile with zero nofollow links looks unnatural. Aim for 70-80% dofollow and 20-30% nofollow.

The bottom line: build dofollow backlinks intentionally, but do not avoid platforms just because they use nofollow. Both types strengthen your site.

## 10 Common Mistakes with Do-Follow Link Building

**1. Building only dofollow links.** A 100% dofollow profile looks manipulative. Mix in nofollow links from social media, news sites, and UGC platforms.

**2. Over-optimized anchor text.** If every backlink uses your target keyword as anchor text, Google treats it as manipulation. Vary your anchors.

**3. Buying links.** Paid links that pass PageRank violate Google guidelines. Manual penalties can remove your site from search results entirely.

**4. Using Private Blog Networks (PBNs).** Google actively identifies and penalizes PBN usage. The risk of a site-wide penalty is not worth it.

**5. Mass automation.** Automated profile creation and link submission tools get detected. Build links manually for quality results.

**6. Ignoring link indexing.** A backlink is worthless if the page linking to you is not indexed by Google. Verify that your backlink pages are crawled and indexed.

**7. Spamming forums and comments.** Generic "nice post, check out my site" comments get deleted. Contribute real value before adding any links.

**8. Building too many links too fast.** Sudden spikes trigger spam detection algorithms. Stick to 15-20 new links per day maximum.

**9. Ignoring relevance.** Links from irrelevant sites carry little weight. A plumbing company does not benefit from backlinks on a fashion blog.

**10. Not tracking results.** Without monitoring which links drive traffic and rankings, you cannot optimize your strategy. Use Ahrefs, Semrush, or Google Search Console.

## FAQ

**What are do-follow backlink sites?**

Do-follow backlink sites are websites that allow you to create links pointing to your domain with the dofollow attribute. These links pass link equity (PageRank) to your website, which helps improve your search engine rankings. Examples include profile creation sites, guest posting platforms, forums, and directories.

**How do I check if a backlink is dofollow or nofollow?**

Right-click the link in your browser, select "Inspect Element," and look at the HTML. If the link tag includes `rel="nofollow"`, it is nofollow. If there is no `rel` attribute, the link is dofollow by default. You can also use browser extensions like NoFollow or SEO tools like Ahrefs to check automatically.

**What is the ideal dofollow to nofollow ratio?**

A natural backlink profile typically has 60-90% dofollow links and 10-40% nofollow links. The optimal recommended ratio is 70:30 dofollow to nofollow. Search Engine Land, for example, has 82.2% followed referring domains, which is considered healthy.

**How many dofollow backlinks do I need to rank?**

There is no fixed number. Pages ranking in Google's top 10 have 3.8x more backlinks than lower-ranking pages. Focus on quality over quantity. 20 dofollow links from relevant, high-DA (40+) sites will outperform 500 links from low-quality directories.

**Can too many dofollow backlinks hurt my site?**

Yes, if they come from low-quality, spammy, or irrelevant sources. Google penalizes unnatural link patterns, including sudden spikes in backlink acquisition, 100% dofollow profiles, and over-optimized anchor text. Build links gradually from relevant, high-quality sources.

**Are dofollow backlinks from social media effective for SEO?**

Social media profile links (LinkedIn, Pinterest, X) are dofollow and do pass some link equity. Their individual ranking impact is modest, but they contribute to a diverse, natural backlink profile. They also drive referral traffic that sends positive engagement signals.

## What Is GEO for Local Businesses?

GEO for local businesses is the application of [generative engine optimization](/blog/what-is-geo) specifically to local search queries. The goal is getting AI engines to mention, recommend, and cite your business when users ask location-based questions.

Traditional local SEO targets the Google local pack. Local GEO targets the AI-generated answers that increasingly appear above the local pack (AI Overviews) or replace it entirely (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini).

The difference: Google shows a list and lets the user choose. AI engines choose for the user. When ChatGPT recommends 3 plumbers in your city, the 4th plumber does not exist in that interaction.

**Key point:** Local GEO determines whether AI engines pick your business when someone in your city asks for a recommendation.

## How AI Engines Choose Local Businesses

Understanding how AI picks which businesses to recommend is the foundation of local GEO strategy.

### What AI Checks

| Signal | Why It Matters | Where AI Finds It |
|---|---|---|
| **[NAP](/glossary/nap) consistency** | One address conflict and AI skips you | Website, GBP, directories, social profiles |
| **Review quality and recency** | AI filters by star rating and recent volume | Google, Yelp, Trustpilot, industry directories |
| **[GBP completeness](/glossary/gbp-optimization)** | Incomplete profiles lack data AI needs | Google Business Profile |
| **Directory presence** | Businesses on 3+ platforms are [3x more likely](https://www.marketingcode.com/ai-search-6-to-45-percent-contractors-invisible/) to be cited | Yelp, BBB, TripAdvisor, G2, industry-specific |
| **Structured data** | Machine-readable business facts | LocalBusiness, Service, FAQ schema on your website |
| **Content authority** | In-depth pages that answer local questions | Blog articles, service pages, FAQ pages |

### Where AI Gets Local Data

[86% of data](https://www.marketingcode.com/ai-search-6-to-45-percent-contractors-invisible/) AI uses for local recommendations comes from brand-managed sources. You own the data AI reads. The problem is most businesses have not updated or structured that data for AI consumption.

ChatGPT pulls from Bing results, Yelp, TripAdvisor, and directory listings. Gemini pulls from Google's index, Google Maps, and Google Business Profile. Perplexity searches the live web and cites sources inline.

Each platform has different data sources. Optimizing for all of them requires consistent information across every platform where your business appears.

## 6 GEO Tactics for Local Businesses

### 1. Make Your GBP the Anchor

Your [Google Business Profile](/blog/optimize-google-business-profile) is the single most important asset for local GEO. AI Overviews pull directly from GBP data. Gemini uses Google's index. A complete, accurate, active GBP feeds every Google-based AI system.

- [ ] Verify all business information matches your website exactly
- [ ] Fill every available field (services, products, descriptions, hours, attributes)
- [ ] Upload new photos at least twice per month
- [ ] Publish GBP posts weekly
- [ ] Respond to every review within 24 hours

Read our guide on [GBP engagement metrics](/blog/gbp-engagement-metrics-ranking) for the specific signals that drive rankings.

### 2. Build Directory Presence Across 5+ Platforms

ChatGPT pulls from directories, not just Google. Businesses listed on Yelp, Trustpilot, BBB, and industry-specific directories have [3x higher visibility](https://www.marketingcode.com/ai-search-6-to-45-percent-contractors-invisible/) in ChatGPT recommendations.

Priority directories for local GEO:
- Google Business Profile (Gemini, AI Overviews)
- Yelp (ChatGPT, Perplexity)
- TripAdvisor (restaurants, hospitality)
- BBB (service businesses)
- Industry directories (Avvo for lawyers, Healthgrades for doctors, Houzz for contractors)

### 3. Accumulate Fresh Reviews

AI engines use reviews as a trust and quality filter. Businesses ChatGPT recommends average 4.3 stars. Volume matters too. A business with 200 reviews at 4.5 stars outranks one with 10 reviews at 5.0 stars in AI recommendations.

Read our guide on [getting more Google reviews](/blog/get-more-google-reviews-local-business) for the proven request system. Focus on recent reviews. AI weights recency heavily.

### 4. Implement LocalBusiness Schema

[Schema markup](/blog/schema-markup-seo-guide) gives AI engines structured, machine-readable data about your business. LocalBusiness schema tells AI your exact name, address, phone, hours, services, and service area.

Add `sameAs` links pointing to all your directory profiles. The `sameAs` property tells AI: "All of these listings represent the same business." Without it, AI may treat each directory listing as a different entity.

### 5. Create Location-Specific Content

Generic service pages do not win AI recommendations. Pages that mention your city, neighborhood, and specific local context perform better in AI's [query fan-out](/blog/fanout-queries-strategy) process.

Create content like:
- "[Service] in [City]: What to Expect"
- "How Much Does [Service] Cost in [City]?"
- "[Service] FAQs for [City] Residents"

Each page targets a different fan-out sub-query that AI generates from the original local question.

### 6. Fix Wrong AI Information

If AI engines already say incorrect things about your business, fix the sources first. Read our complete guide on [fixing wrong AI business info](/blog/ai-wrong-business-info-fix) for the step-by-step process across all platforms.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Assuming Google SEO covers AI search.** Ranking in Google's local pack does not guarantee ChatGPT or Perplexity visibility. ChatGPT pulls from Bing and directories. Perplexity searches the live web. Gemini favors GBP data. Each platform requires separate optimization.

2. **Inconsistent [NAP data](/glossary/nap) across directories.** One wrong phone number or outdated address creates a data conflict that AI engines resolve by skipping your business entirely. Audit every directory listing quarterly.

3. **Ignoring non-Google directories.** ChatGPT does not use Google's index. It uses Bing's results and directory data. If you only optimize Google Business Profile and ignore Yelp, BBB, and industry directories, ChatGPT will never find you.

## FAQ

**Does ChatGPT recommend local businesses?**

Yes. When users ask ChatGPT for local service recommendations, it pulls from Bing results, Yelp, TripAdvisor, and other directory data to recommend 2-3 businesses. Currently, only 1.2% of local businesses appear in ChatGPT recommendations. The number is growing as more users shift to AI for local discovery.

**How do I get my business recommended by AI search engines?**

Focus on 3 areas: complete and accurate [Google Business Profile](/blog/optimize-google-business-profile) with regular posts and photo updates, consistent directory listings across 5+ platforms (especially Yelp and industry-specific directories), and fresh reviews with a 4.0+ star rating. Implement LocalBusiness schema on your website and create location-specific content.

**Is local GEO different from regular GEO?**

Yes. Regular GEO focuses on getting cited in AI responses for informational queries. Local GEO specifically targets location-based recommendations where AI picks 2-3 businesses from a geographic area. Local GEO depends more heavily on directory data, reviews, NAP consistency, and Google Business Profile completeness than regular GEO.

**What star rating do I need for AI recommendations?**

ChatGPT-recommended businesses average 4.3 stars. Gemini averages 3.9 stars. Perplexity averages 4.1 stars. A minimum of 4.0 stars with consistent recent reviews gives you the best chance of appearing in AI local recommendations across all platforms.

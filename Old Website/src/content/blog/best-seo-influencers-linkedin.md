## How to Build Your SEO Learning Feed on LinkedIn

Following 100 people is a start. Building a useful learning feed requires intention. Here is a practical framework.

**Step 1: Follow all 80 from this list.** Use the table of contents above to jump to each category. Follow and ring the bell for notifications on your top 10. LinkedIn limits bell notifications, so pick the voices most relevant to your current work.

**Step 2: Engage within the first week.** Comment on at least 5 posts with specific thoughts, not generic praise. LinkedIn's algorithm surfaces more content from accounts you interact with. Active engagement unlocks better content in your timeline.

**Step 3: Save posts that contain frameworks.** Use LinkedIn's bookmark feature. Create a collection for SEO insights you want to reference later when [writing blog posts](/blog/how-to-write-seo-blog-posts) or building strategy documents. Bookmarked posts become your personal SEO reference library over time.

**Step 4: Join the conversation threads.** The best SEO insights on LinkedIn often appear in the comments. Lily Ray's update threads attract analysis from dozens of practitioners. Kevin Indig's posts spark debates between agency owners and in-house SEOs. Reading the comments doubles the value of every post.

**Step 5: Audit your feed monthly.** Unfollow accounts that post motivational fluff without substance. Add new voices you discover through the comments. Your feed should evolve as your SEO knowledge grows.

**Step 6: Apply 1 insight per week.** The best feed means nothing without execution. Pick 1 tip from your feed each week and implement it on your site. That compounds faster than reading alone.

**Step 7: Cross-reference with other platforms.** Many experts on this list also publish newsletters, [YouTube channels](/blog/best-seo-youtube-channels), and [podcast episodes](/blog/best-seo-marketing-podcasts). Aleyda runs SEO Fomo. Kevin runs Growth Memo. Subscribe to 2 or 3 newsletters from your favorite voices.

### What to Skip on LinkedIn SEO Content

Not every SEO post is worth your time. Avoid accounts that only repost headlines without original analysis. Skip profiles that promise "10x your traffic" with no case studies. Ignore threads that claim overnight results but never explain the method.

The experts on this list earn their spot because they share original research, data, and tested frameworks. That is the standard your feed should maintain.

## What Are Google Business Profile Posts?

Google Business Profile posts are short updates you publish directly to your business listing on Google Search and Maps. They appear below your business info when customers search your name or find you in local results.

Think of them as micro-content for your storefront on Google. Each post can include text, images, videos, and a call-to-action button that links to your website.

### Where GBP Posts Appear

Your posts show up in 4 places:

1. **Google Search**. In your Business Profile panel on desktop and mobile
2. **Google Maps desktop**. Under your listing details
3. **Google Maps app**. In the Updates tab of your profile
4. **Google business website**. If you use the free Google site

Google displays up to 10 of your most recent posts in the Updates section. Anything older than 6 months gets archived.

### How Posts Differ from Reviews and Photos

Reviews come from customers. Photos can come from anyone. Posts come from you. They are the only content type where you fully control the message, image, and link destination.

That makes posts your best tool for promoting offers, announcing events, and driving traffic to specific pages. If your [Google Business Profile optimization](/blog/optimize-google-business-profile) covers the "what" of your business, posts cover the "what is happening right now."

## The 4 Types of GBP Posts

![The 4 Google Business Profile post types showing Update, Offer, Event, and Product](/images/blog/gbp-post-types.webp)

Google offers 4 distinct post formats. Each serves a different purpose and displays differently in search results.

### Update Posts (What is New)

Update posts are the most flexible type. Use them for general announcements, tips, news, behind-the-scenes content, or anything that does not fit the other 3 categories.

**Best for:** Weekly updates, service highlights, team news, blog promotion, seasonal tips.

### Offer Posts

Offer posts promote discounts, sales, and promotions. They automatically display a "View Offer" button and support coupon codes. Offer posts remain visible until the promotion end date you set.

Research from [BrightLocal](https://www.brightlocal.com/learn/google-business-profile-google-posts-updates/) found that Offer posts generate more clicks than any other post type.

**Best for:** Seasonal sales, limited-time discounts, new customer offers, holiday promotions.

### Event Posts

Event posts promote a specific happening at your business. They require a title plus start and end dates.

**Best for:** Workshops, grand openings, webinars, open houses, seasonal events.

### Product Posts

Product posts showcase specific items with pricing. They appear in the Products tab of your profile and stay visible until you remove them.

**Best for:** Retail stores, restaurants (menu items), salons (service packages).

| Post Type | Duration | Required Fields | Best CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Update | 6 months visible | Description | Learn More, Book |
| Offer | Until end date | Title, dates | View Offer (auto) |
| Event | Until end date | Title, dates | Sign Up, Book |
| Product | Until removed | Name | Buy, Order Online |

## How GBP Posts Affect Local SEO Rankings

The honest answer: Google Business Profile posts are not a top-3 ranking factor. Your [NAP](/glossary/nap) consistency, [Google reviews](/glossary/google-reviews), and website relevance carry more weight. But posts play a clear supporting role that compounds over time.

### The Relevance Signal

Google's own [documentation on local ranking](https://support.google.com/business/answer/7091?hl=en) names 3 factors: relevance, distance, and prominence. Posts feed the relevance signal directly. When you publish a post containing keywords related to your services, Google has fresh text to match against search queries.

### The Activity Signal

Google rewards active profiles. A profile with weekly posts signals an engaged, operating business. A profile with no posts since 2024 signals neglect. Businesses that started consistent GBP posting saw jumps in [local pack](/glossary/local-pack) rankings within 1 week.

### The Engagement Signal

Posts drive 3 types of customer actions:

- **48%** result in website visits
- **34%** generate direction requests
- **17%** trigger phone calls

These [engagement](/glossary/engagement) signals feed back into your profile. More clicks, calls, and direction requests tell Google that searchers find your business useful.

### The AI Overview Connection

In 2026, roughly 40% of local searches trigger AI Overviews at the top of results. Google pulls information from active, well-maintained Business Profiles to populate these summaries. Businesses with recent, keyword-rich posts are more likely to appear in AI Overviews for local queries.

### What Posts Cannot Do

Posts will not fix a fundamentally broken local SEO strategy. If your NAP data is inconsistent, your categories are wrong, or you have 2 reviews, posting will not save you. Start with the foundations in our [local SEO guide](/blog/local-seo-guide), then add posts as the next layer.

## How to Write GBP Posts That Get Clicks

Most GBP posts fail because they read like corporate press releases. The posts that drive clicks follow a simple formula: one point, one image, one action.

### The 3-Part GBP Post Formula

Every effective post contains 3 elements:

1. **Hook**. First sentence grabs attention with a benefit, stat, or question
2. **Body** ,  2-3 sentences of supporting detail
3. **CTA**. Clear next step with a button

Keep total length between 80 and 150 words. Google shows roughly 100 characters before truncating on mobile.

### Keyword Placement

Include your primary service keyword and city name naturally in the post text. Do not stuff keywords. One mention of each is enough. This supports the relevance signal and aligns with [SEO content writing](/blog/seo-content-writing) best practices.

**Weak:** "We are the best dentist. Choose us for dental services."
**Strong:** "Need a same-day dental crown in Portland? Our CEREC technology creates custom crowns in 1 visit. Book online today."

### CTA Button Selection

| Button | Best For |
|---|---|
| Book | Service businesses, appointments |
| Order Online | Restaurants, retail |
| Buy | Products, gift cards |
| Learn More | Blog posts, service pages |
| Sign Up | Events, newsletters, webinars |
| Call Now | Urgent services, consultations |

Always link the button to the most relevant page. Do not send every post to your homepage.

### Writing Tips That Increase Clicks

- **Start with a number.** "5 signs your AC needs repair" outperforms "Is your AC broken?"
- **Add urgency.** "Offer ends Friday" or "3 spots remaining" drives action.
- **Name the benefit.** "Save 2 hours" beats "convenient service."
- **Ask a question.** Posts that open with a question get higher read-through rates.

Add UTM parameters to your CTA links so you can track post-driven traffic in [Google Analytics 4](/blog/google-analytics-4-setup).

## Image and Video Specs for GBP Posts

![Google Business Profile post image specifications showing size, format, and aspect ratio](/images/blog/gbp-post-image-specs.webp)

Every GBP post should include at least 1 image. Posts with images get significantly more views and clicks than text-only posts.

### Image Requirements

| Specification | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Format | JPG or PNG |
| Recommended size | 1,200 x 900 pixels |
| Minimum size | 400 x 300 pixels |
| Aspect ratio | 4:3 |
| File size | 10 KB to 5 MB |

The 4:3 aspect ratio is critical. Images with other ratios will be cropped automatically from the center.

### Video Requirements

| Specification | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Maximum length | 30 seconds |
| Maximum file size | 75 MB |
| Minimum resolution | 720p |

### Image Best Practices

- **Use real photos.** Authentic images outperform stock photos every time.
- **Show results.** Before-and-after photos work for contractors, salons, and landscapers.
- **Brand consistently.** Use a template with your brand colors for promotional graphics.
- **Avoid heavy text overlays.** Google may reject images that look like advertisements.

## How Often to Post on Google Business Profile

The minimum effective frequency is 1 post per week. That keeps your profile active and ensures you always have a recent post visible in search results.

### Posting Frequency by Business Type

| Business Type | Recommended Frequency | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Restaurants, retail | 3-5 per week | Menu changes, daily specials, new inventory |
| Service businesses | 1-2 per week | Service tips, seasonal reminders, offers |
| Professional services | 1 per week | Industry insights, case highlights, team news |
| B2B companies | 1 per week | Thought leadership, event promotion |

### The 7-Day Visibility Window

Update posts remain accessible for up to 6 months but lose prominence after roughly 7 days. Google prioritizes recent content. A weekly posting schedule ensures you always have a fresh post in the priority position.

### Quality Over Quantity

Posting 3 times per week with generic content does less than posting once per week with specific, valuable updates. A [content calendar](/blog/create-content-calendar-seo) helps you plan GBP posts alongside your blog and social media.

Consistency matters more than volume. Give it 60-90 days of consistent posting before evaluating results. The data on [how long SEO takes](/blog/how-long-seo-takes) applies here too.

## Measuring GBP Post Performance

Publishing posts without tracking results is guessing. Google provides basic analytics inside your Business Profile. Combined with Google Analytics 4, you get a full picture.

### GBP Insights Metrics

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|---|---|
| Views | How many times your post was seen |
| Clicks | How many people clicked your CTA button |
| Calls | Phone calls initiated from the post |
| Bookings | Appointments booked through the CTA |
| Direction requests | Map directions requested after viewing |

### Tracking in Google Analytics 4

UTM parameters turn vague "direct" traffic into trackable GBP post visits. Add these parameters to every CTA link:

`?utm_source=gbp&utm_medium=post&utm_campaign=[post-topic]`

In GA4, move through to Reports, then Traffic Acquisition, and filter by source "gbp." Our [Google Analytics 4 setup guide](/blog/google-analytics-4-setup) walks through the full configuration.

### What Good Performance Looks Like

- **Views per post:** 50-200 for small local businesses
- **Click-through rate:** 3-7%
- **Calls per post:** 1-5 for service businesses
- **Direction requests:** 2-10 for businesses with foot traffic

Review your broader [local SEO statistics](/blog/local-seo-statistics) alongside GBP post data. For connecting post performance to revenue, see how to [measure content marketing ROI](/blog/measure-content-marketing-roi).

## GBP Post Audit: 10 Things to Check Before You Publish

Before hitting publish on any GBP post, run through this checklist. These are the most common mistakes that reduce post performance.

- [ ] **First sentence delivers a clear benefit or hook** — do not start with your company name
- [ ] **City or neighborhood mentioned** at least once in the post body for local relevance
- [ ] **Primary service keyword included naturally** — one use is enough
- [ ] **Image is 1,200 x 900 px at 4:3 ratio** — anything else will be auto-cropped
- [ ] **Post length is 80-150 words** — longer posts get truncated on mobile before the CTA
- [ ] **CTA button selected and linked** — never leave the CTA on "None"
- [ ] **CTA link goes to a relevant page**, not the homepage
- [ ] **UTM parameters added to the link** for GA4 tracking (`?utm_source=gbp&utm_medium=post`)
- [ ] **No restricted content** — no before/after medical claims, no fake offers
- [ ] **Published day is Tuesday-Thursday** — mid-week posts see 18% higher engagement than weekend posts

## What We Learned Publishing 3,500+ GBP Posts Across 70 Industries

We have published GBP posts at scale for local businesses across industries ranging from dental practices to roofing contractors to martial arts studios. These patterns hold consistently.

**The day-of-week effect is real.** Posts published Tuesday through Thursday generate 15-22% more clicks on average than those published Friday through Monday. The working hypothesis: people searching for local services on weekdays have higher intent than weekend browsers.

**Offer posts generate 2-3x the clicks of update posts** when the discount is specific (e.g., "$50 off your first AC tune-up") rather than vague ("save this month"). Specificity signals credibility. Vague offers feel like bait.

**The first 5 words decide the click rate.** Posts where the first 5 words communicate a specific benefit — not a brand name, not a greeting — consistently outperform those that start with "We are proud to..." or "Welcome to our..." Test: cover the rest of the post and read only the first sentence. Would you keep reading?

**Authenticity beats polish.** A real photo taken on a job site, in natural light, with actual equipment and a real technician generates higher engagement than stock photography or branded graphics. The exception: for promotional posts with specific offers, branded graphics with the discount amount displayed visibly outperform photos.

**Consistency compounds.** Businesses that post weekly for 6+ consecutive months see a measurable step-change in their local pack rankings compared to those that post in bursts and then go quiet for weeks. Google treats consistent activity as a signal of an operating, reliable business. Inconsistency does the opposite.

## FAQ

**How long do Google Business Profile posts last?**

Update posts remain visible on your profile for up to 6 months but lose priority placement after about 7 days. Offer and Event posts stay prominent until their set end date. Product posts remain visible until you manually remove them.

**Do Google Business Profile posts help SEO?**

Posts support local SEO indirectly. They send relevance signals to Google, increase profile engagement, and provide fresh content that AI Overviews can pull from. They will not single-handedly improve your rankings, but they strengthen your overall [local SEO](/blog/local-seo-guide) strategy when combined with reviews, [citations](/glossary/citation), and website optimization.

**What is the ideal length for a GBP post?**

Aim for 80 to 150 words. Google truncates posts at roughly 100 characters on mobile search results. Front-load your key message in the first sentence. The maximum is 1,500 characters, but shorter posts with clear CTAs outperform long ones.

**Can I schedule Google Business Profile posts?**

Yes. Google added native post scheduling in late 2025. Third-party tools like Publer, SocialBee, and Sendible also support GBP scheduling with bulk posting and analytics.

**What image size should I use for GBP posts?**

The recommended size is 1,200 x 900 pixels with a 4:3 aspect ratio. Minimum is 400 x 300 pixels. Use JPG or PNG format. Keep file sizes between 10 KB and 5 MB.

**How many times per week should I post on Google Business Profile?**

At minimum, post once per week to keep your profile active. Service businesses benefit from 1-2 posts per week. Restaurants and retail can post 3-5 times per week.

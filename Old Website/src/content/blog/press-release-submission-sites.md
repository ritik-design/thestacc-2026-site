## How to Write a Press Release That Gets Published

Submitting to 173 sites means nothing if your press release is poorly written. Editors reject 90% of submissions for the same reasons.

### Press Release Format That Works

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Headline | Under 80 characters. Include the news. No clickbait. |
| Subheadline | One sentence expanding the headline. Optional. |
| Dateline | City, State. Date format. Always include. |
| Lead paragraph | Who, what, when, where, why in 30 words or less. |
| Body | 300-500 words total. Quotes from a named source. |
| Boilerplate | 50-word company description. Same across all releases. |
| Contact info | Name, email, phone. Real person, not a generic inbox. |

### Submission Checklist

- [ ] Press release follows AP style formatting
- [ ] Headline includes the actual news (not hype)

## Common Mistakes That Waste Submissions

**Writing promotional copy instead of news.** Press releases report news. A new product feature, a partnership, a funding round, a milestone. "We are the best solution for X" is not news. Editors delete promotional submissions immediately.

**Submitting the same text everywhere.** Search engines flag duplicate content. Write 3-4 variants of your press release and rotate them across platforms. Keep the core facts identical but vary the wording.

**Ignoring journalist outreach platforms.** Free and paid press release sites build backlinks. Journalist platforms like HARO and Qwoted build media mentions. The mentions are worth 10 times more for [brand authority](/blog/eeat-google-quality-guide) than a directory backlink.

**Skipping classified sites.** Most guides ignore classified submissions entirely. These sites build citations that strengthen your local SEO profile. They are fast, free, and compound alongside your other efforts.

**Not tracking results.** Use a spreadsheet to track: site name, submission date, approval status, live URL, backlink type (dofollow/nofollow), and referral traffic. Review monthly to identify which sites deliver actual value.

## FAQ

**Are press release submission sites still good for SEO in 2026?**

Yes, but the value has shifted. Direct backlink value from free press release sites is moderate. The real SEO benefit comes from brand mentions, citations, and the compounding effect of appearing across dozens of indexed pages. Combined with [consistent content publishing](/blog/content-marketing-strategy), press releases accelerate domain authority growth.

**What is the difference between press release sites and classified sites?**

Press release sites distribute news announcements to media outlets and search engines. Classified sites are advertising platforms where you post products, services, or business listings. Both build backlinks, but press releases target journalists while classifieds target buyers.

**How much should I spend on press release distribution?**

Start with free sites to build a baseline backlink profile. For important announcements (product launches, funding rounds, partnerships), invest $200-$500 in a paid service like EIN Presswire or eReleases. Reserve PR Newswire and Business Wire ($500+) for major news that justifies broad media coverage.

**How often should I submit press releases?**

One press release per month is a sustainable cadence for most businesses. Submit only when you have genuine news. Forced press releases about minor updates waste your time and damage credibility with journalists who receive them.

**Do classified submission sites still work for backlinks?**

Classified sites provide supporting backlinks and citations rather than primary link building. They work best as part of a diversified [backlink strategy](/blog/build-backlinks-for-blog) that includes editorial links, guest posts, directory listings, and press mentions.

**What digital PR platforms replaced HARO?**

HARO relaunched under Featured.com in April 2025 after Connectively shut down. Qwoted offers a similar experience with verified users. Terkel, SourceBottle, and JournoRequests are strong alternatives. Using 2-3 platforms simultaneously maximizes your media opportunities.

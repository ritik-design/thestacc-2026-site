## Keyword Research

**Edge: Close call. Ahrefs has a larger global database. Semrush returns more US keyword variations.**

Ahrefs holds 29 billion keywords across 217 locations and 10 search engines. That includes Google, YouTube, Amazon, Bing, and Baidu. The "Parent Topic" feature groups related keywords under 1 page. This prevents you from creating duplicate content.

Semrush holds 28 billion keywords across 140+ countries. Its Keyword Magic Tool generates thousands of variations per seed keyword. It also shows keyword intent labels (informational, commercial, navigational, transactional). Ahrefs does not label intent.

Both tools show search volume, keyword difficulty, and SERP features. Ahrefs adds click-through rate data per keyword. Semrush adds keyword grouping and intent filters. For US-focused campaigns, Semrush returns more variations. For multi-engine research, Ahrefs covers more ground.

## Backlink Analysis

**Edge: Ahrefs wins. It has the largest referring domain index in the industry.**

Ahrefs crawls 8 billion pages per day with AhrefsBot. Its index tracks 35 trillion known links and 500 million referring domains. No other commercial tool matches that referring domain coverage.

Semrush reports 43 trillion total backlinks but tracks 390 million referring domains. It also includes a Backlink Audit tool that flags toxic links and generates Google disavow files. Ahrefs does not offer toxic link scoring.

For pure link discovery and competitor backlink research, Ahrefs is stronger. For link auditing and cleanup workflows, Semrush adds more structure. Most SEO professionals consider Ahrefs the industry standard for backlink data.

## Site Audit

**Edge: Semrush wins. Its audit is more actionable for non-technical users.**

Ahrefs Site Audit checks 170+ technical SEO issues. It covers crawlability, indexing, speed, HTML tags, Core Web Vitals, and schema validation. The Health Score tracks progress over time with scheduled crawls.

Semrush Site Audit checks 140+ issues. Fewer checks, but better prioritization. Semrush groups issues by severity and shows a clear action list. It tells you what to fix first. Ahrefs gives you more data but less direction.

Semrush also integrates its audit with on-page SEO recommendations. The On Page SEO Checker pulls audit data and suggests fixes per URL. Ahrefs Site Audit is standalone. You interpret the data yourself.

## Rank Tracking

**Edge: Semrush wins at scale. It includes more tracked keywords per plan.**

Ahrefs Rank Tracker allows 750 keywords on Lite and 2,000 on Standard. It supports desktop and mobile tracking with location targeting down to zip code. The Share of Voice metric shows weighted visibility across tracked keywords.

Semrush Position Tracking allows 500 keywords on Pro and 1,500 on Guru. But the Business plan jumps to 5,000. Semrush also tracks featured snippets, local packs, and AI Overviews. It provides cannibalization alerts when multiple pages compete for 1 keyword.

Ahrefs actually offers more keywords on entry plans (750 vs 500). But Semrush provides richer tracking data per keyword. Semrush also tracks SERP feature changes over time. For agencies managing many clients, Semrush scales better at the Business tier.

## Content Tools

**Edge: Tie. Different strengths for different workflows.**

Ahrefs Content Explorer searches 400 billion indexed pages. You can find top-performing content by topic, filter by traffic, domain rating, or social shares. It is the best tool for finding link-worthy content ideas and outreach targets.

Semrush offers the SEO Writing Assistant, Topic Research, and ContentShake AI. The Writing Assistant scores your content for readability, SEO, tone, and originality in real time. Topic Research generates subtopic ideas and common questions. ContentShake AI drafts full articles from a keyword.

Ahrefs helps you find what to write about. Semrush helps you write it. Neither tool publishes content to your website automatically.

## AI Features

**Edge: Both are evolving. Semrush is further ahead on AI integration.**

Ahrefs offers 40+ free AI writing tools on its website. These include a paragraph rewriter, title generator, meta description generator, and article outline tool. They work without login and generate basic AI content. These are lead generation tools, not professional-grade writing features.

Semrush launched ContentShake AI for full article drafting from keywords. It also added an AI Copilot across the dashboard that suggests next actions based on your data. In 2025, Semrush introduced AI Visibility tracking. This shows how your brand appears in AI search results from ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews.

Semrush treats AI as a platform feature. Ahrefs treats AI as a free tool collection. For users who care about AI search visibility tracking, Semrush is the only option between the 2.

## FAQ

### Is Ahrefs or Semrush better for beginners?

Semrush is better for beginners. It offers a 14-day free trial, guided workflows, and on-page suggestions that tell you what to fix. Ahrefs has no free trial and assumes you already understand SEO concepts.

### Can I use Ahrefs and Semrush together?

Yes. Some agencies use Ahrefs for backlink research and Semrush for site audits and content optimization. This costs $270+ per month at entry level. Most users pick 1 tool and stick with it.

### Which tool has better data accuracy?

Both tools estimate traffic and search volume. Neither matches Google Search Console exactly. Ahrefs is considered more accurate for backlink data. Semrush is considered more accurate for US keyword volume. Independent tests show both within 20 to 40 percent variance from actual GSC numbers.

### Is there a free version of Ahrefs or Semrush?

Ahrefs offers Webmaster Tools for free. It includes a limited site audit and backlink overview for verified websites. Semrush offers a 14-day free trial with access to Pro features. After that, Semrush has no free tier.

### Do Ahrefs or Semrush write content for you?

No. Both tools help you research keywords and optimize content. Neither writes articles or publishes them to your website. You need a writer, an agency, or a service like [Stacc](/pricing) to handle content creation and publishing.

### Which tool is better for local SEO?

Semrush has a Local SEO toolkit with listing management, local rank tracking, and review monitoring. Ahrefs does not have dedicated local SEO features. For local businesses, Semrush is the stronger choice between the 2.

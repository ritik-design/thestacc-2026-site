## Why People Switch from Frase

Frase is the best content brief builder on the market. No argument there. But a brief is the starting point of content production. Not the finish line. After testing it alongside 10 alternatives and reading hundreds of user reviews on G2, Reddit, and AppSumo, the same frustrations keep surfacing.

**The most common reasons people switch:**

1. **It's a research tool, not a publishing tool.** Frase builds great briefs. But the content still needs to be written, edited, optimized, and published by humans. For 30 articles/month, that's 60-120 hours of labor on top of the subscription.

2. **Lifetime deal controversy.** Frase rebuilt its platform in 2024 and moved LTD holders to a "legacy" version with limited features. Long-standing users who paid $300-$500 one-time lost access to the new tools they expected. The backlash on AppSumo and Reddit was significant.

3. **Optimization scoring is secondary.** Frase's content scoring is functional but not its strength. Most serious users pair Frase briefs with Surfer optimization. That's $49-$299 (Frase) plus $99-$219 (Surfer) = $148-$518/month combined, just for the tools.

4. **AI writing quality is mixed.** Frase's AI Agent can generate drafts, but output often needs substantial editing. Users report it's better for research snippets than full article drafts.

5. **Article limits on lower plans.** Starter plan ($49/mo) limits you to 4 articles per month. Professional plan allows 30 articles but costs $129/month. For volume production, Frase gets expensive fast.

6. **No true auto-publish.** WordPress plugin exists but doesn't run a hands-off publishing workflow. You still format, review, and manually trigger each publish.

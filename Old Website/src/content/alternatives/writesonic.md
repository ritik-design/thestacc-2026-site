## Why People Switch from Writesonic

Writesonic built its reputation as an affordable alternative to Jasper with a free tier and broad feature set. But after testing it alongside 10 alternatives and reading thousands of reviews on G2, Trustpilot, and Reddit, the same issues keep surfacing.

**The most common reasons people switch:**

1. **Content quality is inconsistent.** Output quality varies significantly between templates and topics. Long-form articles need heavy editing. Especially for technical or niche subjects. Users on G2 report spending as much time editing Writesonic output as they would writing from scratch.

2. **Confusing credit system.** Writesonic uses a word-based credit system that makes it hard to predict monthly costs. Users report running out of credits mid-month with no warning. A 2,000-word blog post uses more credits than expected because rewrites and regenerations count against your limit.

3. **SEO optimization is surface-level.** Writesonic has a built-in SEO checker, but it doesn't match dedicated tools like Surfer or Frase for SERP-based optimization scoring. The checker suggests keywords but doesn't analyze top-ranking pages or provide content scoring against competitors.

4. **It doesn't publish for you.** Writesonic generates content. You still need to edit, format, add images, and publish. WordPress integration exists but requires manual steps. For 30 articles/month, that's still 60-120 hours of labor.

5. **Feature overlap with ChatGPT.** As GPT-4 became widely available, Writesonic's core value proposition. Easy access to GPT models with templates. Eroded. Users question paying $49/month for a wrapper around the same models available in ChatGPT Plus for $20/month.

6. **Billing complaints.** Trustpilot reviews flag charges after cancellation and difficulty getting refunds. Multiple users report being billed for months after cancelling.

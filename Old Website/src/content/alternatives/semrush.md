## Why People Switch from Semrush

Semrush is the most popular SEO platform in the world. 50+ tools covering keyword research, backlink analysis, site audits, rank tracking, and content optimization. But after testing it and reading hundreds of user reviews on G2, Trustpilot, and Reddit, the same complaints keep showing up.

**The most common reasons people switch:**

1. **It's expensive.** The Pro plan is $139.95/month. But the Content Marketing Toolkit. The feature most content teams actually need. Requires the Guru plan at $249.95/month. For a small business that just wants more organic traffic, that's $3,000/year for a dashboard.

2. **50+ tools create analysis paralysis.** Most users use 5-10 features regularly. You're paying for 40+ tools you'll never open. Multiple Reddit threads describe the experience as "overwhelming" and "hard to know where to start."

3. **Single-user plans.** The Pro plan allows 1 user. Adding a second user costs $45/month. Adding a third costs another $45. For a team of 3, you're paying $230/month before even reaching the Guru tier.

4. **Content tools are secondary.** Semrush's Content Marketing Toolkit is good but not best-in-class. Surfer SEO, Frase, and Clearscope all handle content optimization better. You're paying $249/month for an SEO suite that includes a mediocre content optimizer.

5. **It doesn't write or publish for you.** Semrush researches keywords and helps optimize drafts. It does not write articles, publish to your CMS, manage Google Business Profile posts, or handle social media. The real cost of SEO is execution, not research.

6. **Annual billing pressure.** Monthly prices are inflated to push annual commitments. Locking you into $1,400-$3,000 upfront. If you're testing tools, that's a steep gamble.

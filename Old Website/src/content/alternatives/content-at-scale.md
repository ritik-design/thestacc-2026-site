## Why People Switch from Content at Scale

Content at Scale (rebranded to BrandWell in 2024) generates long-form AI articles and markets them as "undetectable." At $249/month for ~25 posts, it was one of the first tools to promise publish-ready AI content at scale. But after testing it and reviewing user feedback, the cracks are hard to ignore.

**The most common reasons people switch:**

1. **Extremely expensive.** The Essentials plan is $249/month for ~25 posts (~$10/post). The Agency plan jumps to $499/month. Per-article API access costs ~$4/post. At $249/month, Content at Scale is more expensive than tools producing comparable output at $49–$99/month.

2. **Rebranding confusion.** The shift from Content at Scale to BrandWell created confusion about pricing, features, and product direction. Existing users lost confidence in the platform's stability and long-term roadmap.

3. **AI detection claims are overstated.** BrandWell markets its content as "undetectable AI." But independent tests show the built-in AI detector fails to detect AI text in most samples. The "undetectable" promise is a marketing claim, not a verified reality.

4. **Long-form quality varies.** Content at Scale generates articles in the 2,000–5,000 word range, but quality is inconsistent. Factual accuracy issues, repetitive sections, and filler content require manual editing. Undermining the "publish-ready" promise.

5. **Almost zero organic user discussion.** Despite claiming tens of thousands of users, there is virtually no organic community discussion on Reddit, forums, or independent review sites. Reviews are dominated by affiliates earning commissions, making it hard to find honest assessments.

6. **No local SEO or social media.** Website content only. No Google Business Profile posts, no social scheduling, no multi-channel coverage.

## Why People Switch from SE Ranking

SE Ranking is one of the best-value SEO suites on the market. Keyword research, rank tracking, backlink analysis, site audits, and competitive intelligence. All at a price well below Semrush or Ahrefs. But after testing it and reading user reviews on G2, Capterra, and Reddit, the same complaints keep showing up.

**The most common reasons people switch:**

1. **Smaller database than top-tier suites.** SE Ranking's keyword and backlink databases don't match Semrush or Ahrefs in volume or coverage. For competitive niches, you'll notice missing keywords and incomplete backlink profiles. Fine for small businesses. Limiting for agencies.

2. **Brand recognition gap.** SE Ranking is less known than Semrush, Ahrefs, or Moz. For agencies, this matters. Clients and stakeholders may question the data source. Reporting from SE Ranking doesn't carry the same weight as reporting from Semrush.

3. **Content tools are add-ons.** SE Ranking has content marketing features, but they're not as integrated or developed as Semrush's Content Marketing Toolkit. Content optimization feels bolted on, not built in.

4. **Limited third-party integrations.** Fewer connections with CMS platforms, analytics tools, and marketing stacks compared to Semrush. If your workflow depends on tool integrations, the gaps are noticeable.

5. **White-label restrictions on lower plans.** Agency-friendly features like white-label reporting are locked behind higher tiers. The Essential plan ($65/mo) doesn't include them.

6. **No content writing or auto-publishing.** SE Ranking is a research and tracking tool. It doesn't write articles, publish to your CMS, manage Google Business Profile posts, or handle social media. The execution gap remains.

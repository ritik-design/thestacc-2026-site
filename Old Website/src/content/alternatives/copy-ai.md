## Why People Switch from Copy.ai

Copy.ai launched as one of the first AI copywriting tools. Ad copy, email subject lines, social posts, product descriptions. It was simple and worked. But in 2024-2025, Copy.ai pivoted hard to "Go-to-Market AI Platform" focused on enterprise workflows and sales automation. The result: a confused product that frustrates both old users and new ones.

Copy.ai currently holds a **1.9 out of 5 on Trustpilot** (195 reviews). One of the lowest scores in the AI writing category.

**The most common reasons people switch:**

1. **The GTM pivot confused everyone.** Copy.ai shifted from AI copywriting to a "Go-to-Market AI" platform focused on workflows, pipelines, and sales. Original users who wanted simple copy generation feel abandoned. New users don't understand what the product actually does.

2. **Trustpilot 1.9/5. Real trust issues.** 195 reviews averaging 1.9 out of 5 on Trustpilot. Common complaints: difficult cancellation, charges after downgrading, and unresponsive support. G2 shows 4.7/5, but Trustpilot captures paying customer frustration more directly.

3. **Free plan is severely limited.** 2,000 words per month on the free tier is barely enough to test. That's one short blog post. It forces an upgrade before you can properly evaluate the tool.

4. **No SEO optimization.** Copy.ai writes marketing copy but doesn't optimize for search. No SERP analysis, no content scoring, no keyword research, no ranking data. If you want organic traffic, Copy.ai won't help.

5. **Short-form focused.** Strong for ad copy, email subject lines, and social captions. Long-form blog content is an afterthought. It requires more prompting and editing than competitors like Jasper or Writesonic.

6. **No auto-publish or CMS integration.** Copy.ai generates text in a browser window. Publishing requires copy-paste or Zapier workarounds. No direct WordPress, Webflow, or Ghost integration.

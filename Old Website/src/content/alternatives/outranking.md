## Why People Switch from Outranking

Outranking takes a unique approach. It processes 50+ sources per article to generate factually grounded content. At $19–$39/month, the entry price is attractive. But after testing it and reading user reviews, the platform's limitations add up.

**The most common reasons people switch:**

1. **Credits burn too fast.** AI credits are consumed quickly, especially when using the "concepts" feature. Monthly limits force users to spend extra or ration usage. The Starter plan (4–5 docs) runs out within the first week for active users.

2. **Steep learning curve.** Outranking's structured workflow is powerful but overwhelming for beginners. Users accustomed to simpler tools like Jasper or NeuronWriter find Outranking's multi-step process rigid and confusing.

3. **Weak UI/UX.** Multiple reviews cite interface bugs, slow loading, and dated design compared to competitors. The editing experience feels clunky, with occasional data loss and formatting issues.

4. **Platform reliability concerns.** Reported outages, slow response times, and a Trustpilot score of 3.0/5 raise questions about long-term reliability. For businesses that depend on consistent content production, unreliable tools are a risk.

5. **Advanced features require add-ons.** Keyword clustering and content inventory are separate from the base subscription. By the time you add the features you need, Outranking's "affordable" pricing isn't so affordable.

6. **Multilingual quality drops.** The AI writer produces passable English content, but quality drops significantly in non-English languages. Users writing in Italian, Spanish, and other languages report poor output.

## Why People Switch from Dashword

Dashword positions itself as "Clearscope but simpler." It gives you content reports, keyword suggestions, and a basic content score. But after testing it and reading user reviews, the same issues keep coming up.

**The most common reasons people switch:**

1. **Expensive for what you get.** $99/month for 30 content reports. Surfer SEO offers 360 documents at the same price. Dashword's cost per report is 12x higher than Surfer's.

2. **Limited AI writing.** Dashword's AI features cover metas, intros, and rephrasing only. It is not a full AI writer. If you need complete articles, you still need a writer or a separate tool.

3. **No content clusters or internal linking.** No support for topic clusters, cannibalization detection, or internal linking recommendations. If you're building topical authority, Dashword can't help you plan it.

4. **No auto-publish.** No direct CMS publishing integration. You write in Dashword, then copy-paste into your CMS manually.

5. **Restrictive usage limits.** The Business plan ($349/mo) caps at 100 reports and 50,000 AI words. Agencies producing content at scale burn through this in the first week.

6. **SEO content only.** Limited to long-form blog optimization. No ad copy, social media, email, or local SEO features.

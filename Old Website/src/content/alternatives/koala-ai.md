## Why People Switch from Koala AI

Koala AI (koala.sh) is built for one thing: bulk AI article generation at the lowest possible price. It delivers on volume. But after testing it and reading user reviews on G2, Reddit, and Trustpilot, the quality and reliability problems are hard to ignore.

**The most common reasons people switch:**

1. **Quantity over quality.** Koala is built for bulk, but bulk AI articles need significant editing to rank. Users report generic, repetitive output. Especially on niche topics where specificity matters. Volume without quality is not a ranking strategy.

2. **100% AI detection rates.** Independent studies show Koala's output is detected as AI-generated 100% of the time. Some users report Google penalties on sites that published bulk Koala content without heavy editing.

3. **No content optimization scoring.** Unlike Surfer or Frase, Koala doesn't score content against SERP competitors. It writes based on keywords but doesn't optimize for the ranking signals that matter. Headings, topic coverage, NLP terms.

4. **Customer support is slow.** Multiple users report 5+ day response times and unhelpful answers. Credits sometimes don't arrive as paid for, and resolving billing issues takes weeks.

5. **Affiliate content focus limits appeal.** Amazon product roundups are a core feature. Which is great for affiliate bloggers but useless for service businesses, SaaS companies, and local businesses.

6. **Credits expire monthly.** Unused words don't roll over. If you don't use your full allocation in a billing cycle, you lose it. This creates pressure to publish content that may not be ready.

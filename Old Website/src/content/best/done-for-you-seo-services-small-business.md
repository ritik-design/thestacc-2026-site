## DIY SEO vs Done-For-You SEO: Which Is Right for You?

Before spending money on any service, it helps to be honest about what "doing it yourself" actually requires. Most small business owners who attempt DIY SEO underestimate the time commitment significantly.

| | DIY SEO | Done-For-You SEO |
|---|---|---|
| Time required | 10–20 hrs/week | 0–2 hrs/week |
| Monthly cost | $50–$200 (tools only) | $500–$3,000+ |
| Speed to results | Slower (learning curve) | Faster (expert execution) |
| Control | Full | Partial |
| Best for | Bootstrapped founders | Busy business owners |
| Consistency | Depends on you | Guaranteed output |

**Who should choose DIY SEO:** If you are a founder with a genuine interest in learning SEO, a flexible schedule, and a budget under $200/month, DIY is viable. You will need to master keyword research, on-page optimization, content writing, and technical basics. Tools like Google Search Console (free), Ahrefs Webmaster Tools (free tier), and a content management system are sufficient to start. The trade-off is time. Every hour you spend on SEO is an hour not spent on your core business.

**Who should choose done-for-you SEO:** If you are billing clients, managing a team, or running operations, spending 10–20 hours per week on SEO is not realistic. Done-for-you services make the most sense when your hourly value exceeds the cost of the service, when consistency is more important than control, and when you need results without adding headcount. A business owner billing $150/hour who reclaims 15 hours per month at a $99/month SEO service cost is generating a measurable return on that switch.

**The hybrid path:** Some business owners start with a done-for-you content service to maintain output while learning SEO fundamentals on the side. This approach builds long-term capability without sacrificing short-term visibility. It is also how you eventually become an informed buyer who knows what to ask for when evaluating agencies.

## What Does Done-For-You SEO Cost? (2026 Pricing Guide)

Done-for-you SEO pricing varies enormously depending on whether you are hiring a freelancer, a small agency, an enterprise firm, or an automated platform. Here is what you should expect at each tier in 2026.

**Freelancers ($500–$1,500/month):** A skilled SEO freelancer typically handles one or two clients at this price point. You get direct access to one person, usually someone with 3–7 years of experience in keyword research, content planning, and on-page optimization. Expect 2–4 pieces of content per month and monthly strategy calls. Quality varies significantly. The best freelancers produce excellent results. The worst produce templated work at every tier.

**Small agencies ($1,000–$3,000/month):** A small SEO agency brings a small team — typically a strategist, a content writer, and a technical specialist. At $1,000/month you are in the entry tier: 4–8 articles per month, basic link building, and monthly reporting. At $3,000/month, expect full content production, link outreach, technical audits, and a dedicated account manager. Most small businesses land in this range when they hire a full-service agency.

**Enterprise agencies ($5,000+/month):** Firms like WebFX, Victorious, and larger regional agencies operate in this range. At $5,000/month you get a full team (strategist, developers, content team, link builders), proprietary reporting platforms, and the ability to execute across multiple content formats and channels simultaneously. This tier makes sense when your customer lifetime value is high enough to justify the spend, typically $1,000+ per customer.

**Automated and software-assisted services ($49–$167/month):** A newer tier has emerged around AI-powered and automated content platforms. theStacc is the primary example for small businesses: Blog SEO at $99/month (30 articles/month), Local SEO at $49/month (30 Google Business Profile posts), and Social Media at $49/month (30 posts across 3 platforms). Bundled at 15% off, two modules run approximately $126/month. This tier covers execution volume that no human agency can match at the price point. The trade-off is that automated services do not include link building, strategic consulting, or technical audits.

**What is included at each tier:** At the freelancer and small agency tier, expect keyword research, content production, on-page optimization, and monthly reporting at minimum. Technical SEO fixes and link building are often scoped separately. At the enterprise agency tier, everything is included in one retainer. At the automated tier, content production and distribution are included; strategy and technical SEO are not.

## FAQ

### Is done-for-you SEO worth it for small businesses?

Done-for-you SEO is worth it when the cost of the service is lower than the value of the time it saves you. For a business owner who bills $100/hour and would otherwise spend 15 hours per month on SEO tasks, a $500/month service already pays for itself in recovered time before any organic traffic benefit is counted. The ROI case is strongest for local service businesses where a single new customer from search — a dental appointment, a plumbing job, a legal consultation — generates more revenue than the monthly retainer.

### How long does done-for-you SEO take to show results?

Most agencies and services cite 3–6 months before meaningful organic traffic gains are visible. Content-heavy approaches, like publishing 30 articles per month, build topical authority faster than low-volume strategies. Technical SEO fixes can improve crawlability and indexation within weeks. Link building services may show ranking improvements for specific keywords within 4–8 weeks. No service can guarantee specific rankings or traffic levels, and any provider that does should be approached with caution.

### What's the difference between done-for-you SEO and managed SEO?

The terms are often used interchangeably, but there is a practical difference in the level of involvement required. Done-for-you SEO means the service runs without your ongoing participation after setup — automated platforms like theStacc publish content with no approval required. Managed SEO means a team handles the work but includes you in strategy reviews, keyword approvals, and monthly calls. Both are hands-off compared to DIY, but managed SEO still requires 1–3 hours of your time per month.

### Can I do SEO myself instead of paying an agency?

Yes, and for some business owners it makes sense. DIY SEO is viable if you have 10–20 hours per week available, a genuine interest in learning, and a budget for tools ($50–$200/month covers Google Search Console, Ahrefs Webmaster Tools, and a basic content CMS). The main constraints are time consistency and the learning curve for technical SEO. Most business owners who start with DIY eventually switch to done-for-you as their business grows and their time becomes more constrained.

### What should a done-for-you SEO service include?

At minimum: keyword research, content production (at least 4 articles per month), on-page optimization for each piece, and monthly reporting on rankings and traffic. A complete service also includes technical SEO audits and fixes, link building, Google Business Profile management for local businesses, and conversion tracking setup. The services that deliver the most value are those that handle publication directly — drafting, optimizing, and publishing without sending you drafts to review.

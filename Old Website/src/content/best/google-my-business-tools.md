If you searched "best Google My Business tools," you are in the right place. Google My Business is now called Google Business Profile (GBP). Google made the change in 2022. The features are the same. The dashboard is the same. Only the name changed.

We mention this because half the tools on the market still say "Google My Business" in their marketing. The search term "GMB tools" still gets thousands of searches per month. Whether you call it GMB or GBP, the underlying challenge is identical: keeping your local listing active, accurate, and visible in Google Maps results.

46% of all Google searches have local intent. When someone searches "plumber near me" or "coffee shop downtown," Google pulls results from your Business Profile first. not your website. An active profile with fresh posts, recent reviews, and accurate information ranks higher than a stale one. Google confirms that activity and relevance are direct ranking factors.

The problem is time. Posting 3-5 times per week, responding to every review, monitoring for unauthorized edits, tracking your Maps ranking. that is 10-15 hours per month. For a business owner already working 50+ hour weeks, those hours do not exist. These 10 tools solve that.

## FAQ

**What is Google My Business called now?**
Google My Business was renamed to Google Business Profile (GBP) in 2022. The features, dashboard, and functionality are identical. Only the name changed. Many tools and business owners still use "Google My Business" or "GMB". both refer to the same platform.

**What is the best Google My Business tool for a single-location business?**
For a single-location business, theStacc Local SEO ($49/mo) handles GBP content creation. Add BrightLocal ($39/mo) for rank tracking and auditing. Start with Google Business Profile (free) if you have not completed your profile yet. Total cost: $88/month for content + tracking.

**How often should I post to my Google Business Profile?**
3-5 times per week is the sweet spot. Businesses posting daily or near-daily consistently rank higher in local pack results. At minimum, post once per week. theStacc creates 30 posts per month. roughly 1 per day. which keeps your profile active without any manual effort.

**Do GBP posts actually help with local rankings?**
Yes. Google has confirmed that profile activity is a local ranking factor. GBP posts signal that your business is active and engaged. **Can I manage my Google My Business profile without any paid tools?**
Yes. Google Business Profile is free and includes posting, review response, photo uploads, and basic analytics. The limitation is time. Everything is manual. If you can dedicate 3-5 hours per week to GBP management, you do not need paid tools. Most business owners cannot, which is why tools exist.

**What is the difference between Google My Business tools and local SEO tools?**
Google My Business tools focus on your Business Profile. posting, optimization, review response. Local SEO tools cover broader territory. directory sync, citation management, rank tracking, website SEO. Some tools (BrightLocal, SE Ranking) span both categories. **Is it worth paying for GBP post creation or should I write posts myself?**
That depends on your time and writing ability. A well-written GBP post takes 10-15 minutes. At 30 posts per month, that is 5-7.5 hours of work.

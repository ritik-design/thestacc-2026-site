## 1. Buffer. Best Free Social Media Scheduling Tool Overall

Buffer has been the default free scheduler since 2010. The interface is clean. The learning curve is near zero. And the free plan, while limited, covers the basics for solopreneurs and small businesses just starting out.

### What It Does Well

Buffer's strength is simplicity. You connect up to 3 social channels, write your posts in a single composer, and schedule them to a queue. The drag-and-drop calendar shows exactly when each post will publish. No clutter. No feature overload.

The free plan includes a landing page builder, basic analytics for published posts, and an AI assistant for caption suggestions. For someone managing a personal brand or a side project, Buffer's free tier handles the job without paying anything.

> **Our Take:** Buffer is where most people should start. The 10-post-per-channel limit forces you to be selective about what you publish. That constraint actually improves content quality for beginners.

### Where It Falls Short

The 10-post limit per channel means you can only queue about 2 to 3 posts per week. If you publish daily on even one platform, you will hit the ceiling fast. No bulk scheduling on the free plan either.

### Key Features

- Drag-and-drop content calendar
- AI assistant for caption writing
- Landing page builder (1 page on free plan)
- Basic post performance analytics
- Browser extension for quick sharing

### Pricing

- **Free:** 3 channels, 10 posts per channel
- **Essentials:** $5/month per channel. Unlimited posts, engagement tools
- **Team:** $10/month per channel. Collaboration, approval workflows

### Who Should Use Buffer

**Strong fit:**
- Solopreneurs managing 1 to 3 platforms
- Beginners who want the simplest setup

**Probably not right for:**
- Anyone posting more than 3 times per week per platform

## FAQ

**What is the best free social media scheduling tool?**

Buffer is the best overall free social media scheduling tool for its simplicity and clean interface. Metricool is the best free option if you need analytics. Meta Business Suite is the best free tool for Facebook and Instagram specifically, with unlimited posts and full analytics at no cost.

**Are free social media scheduling tools worth it?**

Yes, for individuals and small businesses posting under 50 times per month. Free plans from Buffer (10 posts/channel), Metricool (50 posts/month), and Zoho Social (unlimited posts) handle basic scheduling needs. The limits become a problem only when you scale beyond 3 to 4 posts per week per platform.

**How many social media posts should I schedule per week?**

[Research from Hootsuite](https://blog.hootsuite.com/social-media-statistics/) suggests 3 to 5 posts per week on most platforms for consistent engagement. The average brand manages 4.5 social profiles. At 4 posts per week across 4 platforms, you need roughly 64 posts per month. Most free plans cannot handle that volume.

**Can I schedule posts on Instagram for free?**

Yes. Meta Business Suite schedules unlimited Instagram posts for free. Buffer, Later, Metricool, and Pallyy also support Instagram on their free plans with varying post limits. Later is the best free option specifically for Instagram because of its visual grid planner.

**What is the difference between a social media scheduler and a social media manager?**

A scheduler publishes posts at preset times. A manager handles scheduling, analytics, inbox management, team collaboration, and reporting. Tools like Metricool and Zoho Social blur this line on free plans by including analytics alongside scheduling. A done-for-you service like Stacc goes further and handles content creation, publishing, and optimization without you managing any tool.

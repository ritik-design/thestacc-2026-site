## 1. Brevo. Best Email Marketing Tool for Local Businesses Overall

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) offers the most generous free plan in the category: unlimited contacts with 9,000 emails per month. For a local business building a list from scratch, that is months of free usage before needing to upgrade.

### What It Does Well

The free plan includes unlimited contacts. Every other tool on this list caps free contacts at 250-500 (Mailchimp at just 250). Brevo combines email, SMS marketing, WhatsApp campaigns, live chat, and a built-in CRM in one platform. For local businesses that want to send appointment reminders via SMS and follow-up emails from the same dashboard, Brevo eliminates the need for multiple tools.

Automation workflows handle welcome sequences, abandoned cart recovery, and re-engagement campaigns on paid plans starting at $9/month. The drag-and-drop email editor is clean and does not require design skills.

> **Our Take:** Brevo is the best value for local businesses that are just starting with email marketing. The unlimited-contacts free plan removes the biggest barrier to entry. Most businesses outgrow Mailchimp's 250-contact free plan in weeks. They can use Brevo's free plan for months.

### Where It Falls Short

The 300 emails-per-day limit on the free plan means you cannot blast your full list at once. Brevo branding appears on free plan emails. Customer support is limited on lower tiers. The interface is functional but not as polished as MailerLite.

### Key Features

- **Unlimited contacts free**. No cap on list size even on the free plan
- **Email + SMS + WhatsApp**. Multi-channel from one dashboard
- **Built-in CRM**. Track deals and customer interactions
- **Marketing automation**. Visual workflow builder on paid plans
- **Transactional emails**. Order confirmations and receipts included

### Pricing

- **Free:** Unlimited contacts, 300 emails/day (9,000/month), Brevo branding
- **Starter:** $9/month ,  5,000 emails/month, no daily limit
- **Business:** $18/month. Marketing automation, A/B testing, no branding
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing

### Who Should Use Brevo

**Strong fit:**
- Local businesses building a list from zero (unlimited contacts is critical early on)
- Service businesses that want email + SMS reminders in one platform

**Probably not right for:**
- Ecommerce businesses that need deep purchase behavior automation (use Klaviyo)
- Businesses that need phone support on the free or starter plan

## 2. MailerLite. Best Free Email Marketing Tool for Local Businesses

MailerLite offers the cleanest interface in the category. If a local business owner with zero marketing experience needs to send an email in under 30 minutes, MailerLite is the tool that makes that possible.

### What It Does Well

The free plan includes 500 contacts and 12,000 emails per month. That is 24 emails per subscriber per month at no cost. The drag-and-drop editor is the most intuitive option on this list. Landing pages, pop-up forms, and basic automation are all included on the free plan. The paid plan starts at $10/month for 500 subscribers with full template access and advanced automation.

MailerLite also sells digital products and builds websites directly from the platform. For a local business that needs a simple online presence alongside email marketing, MailerLite covers both.

> **Our Take:** MailerLite is the best free email tool for local businesses that value simplicity. The interface is cleaner than Mailchimp, the free plan is more generous than Mailchimp, and the pricing is lower. The only reason to pick Mailchimp over MailerLite is brand familiarity.

### Where It Falls Short

The free plan does not include email templates (you build from scratch or use the editor). Custom HTML templates require the highest-tier plan. Analytics are basic compared to ActiveCampaign or Klaviyo. Integration options are limited versus larger platforms.

### Key Features

- **12,000 free emails/month**. Generous for a 500-subscriber list
- **Drag-and-drop editor**. Simplest interface in the category
- **Landing pages and pop-ups**. Included free for list building
- **Digital product sales**. Sell ebooks, courses, and downloads
- **Website builder**. Create a basic site alongside email marketing

### Pricing

- **Free:** 500 contacts, 12,000 emails/month, no templates
- **Growing Business:** $10/month (500 subs). Full templates, automation
- **Advanced:** $20/month (500 subs). Custom HTML, AI writing assistant

### Who Should Use MailerLite

**Strong fit:**
- Local businesses with zero email marketing experience seeking the easiest setup
- Solopreneurs who need email marketing + a simple landing page in one tool

**Probably not right for:**
- Businesses with 5,000+ subscribers (pricing scales, and Brevo is cheaper at volume)
- Teams that need CRM functionality built in (use Brevo or ActiveCampaign)

## 3. Constant Contact. Best Email Marketing Tool for Event-Based Local Businesses

Constant Contact has a feature no other email tool on this list offers: built-in event management with Eventbrite integration, RSVP tracking, and event invitation templates. For local businesses that run classes, workshops, or community events, this feature alone justifies the subscription.

### What It Does Well

300+ email templates cover every local business scenario: restaurant promotions, salon appointment reminders, retail sales, nonprofit fundraising, and event invitations. The event management feature handles registration, RSVP tracking, and follow-up emails. Social media campaign management and surveys are built in. The AI content generator drafts emails from prompts.

> **Our Take:** Constant Contact is the right tool for local businesses where events drive revenue. Yoga studios, cooking schools, community nonprofits, and retail shops running in-store events will use the event management features weekly. If you do not run events, Brevo or MailerLite offer more for less.

### Where It Falls Short

No free plan (60-day trial only). The most expensive per-contact pricing at scale. Automation is basic on the Lite plan. The interface feels dated compared to MailerLite or Flodesk.

### Key Features

- **Event management**. RSVP tracking, Eventbrite integration, event emails
- **300+ templates**. Pre-built designs for every local business type
- **AI content generator**. Draft emails from prompts
- **Surveys and polls**. Collect feedback directly in emails
- **Social media tools**. Schedule social posts alongside email campaigns

### Pricing

- **Lite:** $12/month (500 contacts). Basic features
- **Standard:** $35/month (500 contacts). Automation, scheduling
- **Premium:** $80/month (500 contacts). Advanced analytics, SEO tools

### Who Should Use Constant Contact

**Strong fit:**
- Local businesses that run regular events, classes, or workshops
- Nonprofits that need event registration and fundraising email templates

**Probably not right for:**
- Budget-conscious businesses (no free plan, expensive at scale)
- Ecommerce businesses (Klaviyo and Omnisend are purpose-built)

## 4. Mailchimp. Best-Known Email Marketing Tool (Not the Best Value)

Mailchimp is the most recognized email marketing brand. That recognition does not make it the best value. The free plan was quietly reduced to 250 contacts and 500 emails per month in 2024, making it the most restrictive free tier in the category.

### What It Does Well

Brand trust matters. Mailchimp integrates with nearly every tool, platform, and CMS. The drag-and-drop editor is solid. Landing pages, basic CRM, and single-step automations are included. Ecommerce features and social media scheduling expand its use case.

> **Our Take:** Mailchimp trades on name recognition. The product is good but not best-in-class for local businesses on any single dimension. Brevo offers more for free. MailerLite is simpler. ActiveCampaign automates better. The one advantage Mailchimp has: nearly universal integration support.

### Where It Falls Short

Charges for unsubscribed and bounced contacts (you pay for dead weight on your list). The free plan is nearly unusable at 250 contacts. Expensive overage fees. The interface is cluttered compared to MailerLite.

### Key Features

- **Universal integrations**. Connects to nearly every platform and CMS
- **Drag-and-drop editor**. Solid email builder with templates
- **Landing pages**. Included on all plans
- **Basic CRM**. Contact profiles and tagging
- **Social scheduling**. Post to social platforms from Mailchimp

### Pricing

- **Free:** 250 contacts, 500 emails/month, limited features
- **Essentials:** $13/month (500 contacts). Templates, A/B testing
- **Standard:** $20/month (500 contacts). Automation, custom templates
- **Premium:** $350/month (10,000 contacts). Advanced analytics

### Who Should Use Mailchimp

**Strong fit:**
- Businesses that need maximum integration compatibility with existing tools
- Teams already using Mailchimp who have built workflows around it

**Probably not right for:**
- New local businesses starting from scratch (Brevo and MailerLite offer more for free)
- Budget-conscious businesses (charges for unsubscribed contacts inflate costs)

## 5. ActiveCampaign. Best Email Marketing Tool for Automation

ActiveCampaign has the most advanced automation builder of any tool on this list. The visual workflow editor handles multi-step sequences triggered by email opens, link clicks, site visits, purchase behavior, and CRM pipeline changes.

### What It Does Well

The automation builder is the standout. Create sequences like: "If subscriber opens email but does not click within 48 hours, send a follow-up with a different subject line. If they click, move them to the sales pipeline and assign to the team." No other tool under $50/month matches this depth.

900+ integrations connect ActiveCampaign to scheduling tools, payment processors, and CRM platforms. The CRM with deals pipeline tracks leads from first contact to closed sale. Lead scoring prioritizes your warmest prospects.

> **Our Take:** ActiveCampaign is the right tool for local businesses that have outgrown basic email blasts. Dental practices automating appointment follow-ups, real estate agents nurturing leads through 90-day sequences, and consultants with complex sales funnels will use every feature. If you just need to send a weekly newsletter, ActiveCampaign is overkill.

### Where It Falls Short

No free plan (14-day trial only). Steep learning curve for beginners. Landing pages and CRM features require higher-tier plans. Charges for unsubscribed contacts on new accounts as of November 2025.

### Key Features

- **Visual automation builder**. Industry-leading multi-step workflows
- **CRM with deals pipeline**. Track leads from email to close
- **Lead scoring**. Prioritize your warmest contacts
- **900+ integrations**. Connects to scheduling, payments, and CRM tools
- **Conditional content**. Show different email content based on subscriber data

### Pricing

- **Starter:** $15/month (1,000 contacts). Email, basic automation
- **Plus:** $49/month. CRM, landing pages, lead scoring
- **Professional:** $79/month. Predictive sending, attribution
- **Enterprise:** $145/month. Custom objects, premium support

### Who Should Use ActiveCampaign

**Strong fit:**
- Local service businesses with complex follow-up sequences
- Multi-location businesses managing different customer journeys per location

**Probably not right for:**
- Solo business owners who just need a simple weekly newsletter
- Businesses under 500 contacts (Brevo or MailerLite covers the basics for free)

## 6. Klaviyo. Best Email Marketing Tool for Local Ecommerce

Klaviyo connects directly to Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce to build email automations based on actual purchase data. Browse abandonment, cart recovery, post-purchase follow-ups, and product recommendations run automatically based on what each customer browsed and bought.

### What It Does Well

The free plan unlocks all features for up to 250 contacts. That is unique in this category. You can test every automation, every segment, and every workflow before paying a dollar. Predictive analytics forecast customer lifetime value and churn risk. Pre-built ecommerce workflows (abandoned cart, welcome series, win-back) work out of the box with one-click Shopify integration.

> **Our Take:** Klaviyo is the clear winner for local businesses that sell products online. A boutique on Shopify, a specialty food shop on WooCommerce, or a local brand selling nationally all benefit from purchase-based automation. For service businesses with no ecommerce component, Klaviyo is the wrong tool.

### Where It Falls Short

Expensive at scale. No landing page builder. Limited for non-ecommerce use cases. Pricing scales steeply with list growth.

### Key Features

- **All features free**. Every automation and segment unlocked on the free plan
- **Purchase-based automation**. Abandoned cart, browse abandonment, post-purchase
- **Predictive analytics**. Lifetime value and churn risk forecasting
- **SMS + email**. Combined campaigns from one platform
- **One-click Shopify integration**. Connect and start automating in minutes

### Pricing

- **Free:** 250 contacts, 500 emails/month, all features unlocked
- **Email:** $20/month (251-500 contacts). Email only
- **Email + SMS:** $35/month. Combined campaigns

### Who Should Use Klaviyo

**Strong fit:**
- Local businesses selling products through Shopify, WooCommerce, or BigCommerce
- Online-first local brands that need purchase-behavior automation

**Probably not right for:**
- Service-based local businesses with no online store
- Businesses on a tight budget (pricing scales quickly beyond 500 contacts)

## 7. AWeber. Best Email Marketing Tool for Reliable Simplicity

AWeber has been around since 1998. The platform lacks the flashy features of newer tools but delivers reliable email delivery, strong customer support, and a straightforward interface that does not confuse beginners.

### What It Does Well

AWeber offers live phone, chat, and email support on all plans. For local business owners who want to call someone when something breaks, AWeber is one of the few tools that still offers phone support. 700+ templates cover standard use cases. The mobile app lets you send campaigns on the go.

> **Our Take:** AWeber is the Toyota Corolla of email marketing. It will not impress you with features. It will not break. If you value reliability, human support, and a simple interface over advanced automation, AWeber delivers.

### Where It Falls Short

Expensive relative to what it offers (MailerLite and Brevo give more for less). Basic automation features. The interface has not modernized as aggressively as competitors. Full automation requires the Plus plan at $30/month.

### Key Features

- **Phone support**. Live human help on all plans (rare in this category)
- **700+ templates**. Pre-built designs for common business emails
- **Mobile app**. Create and send campaigns from your phone
- **Landing pages**. Included for list building
- **Autoresponders**. Basic drip sequences on all plans

### Pricing

- **Free:** 500 contacts, 500 emails/month
- **Lite:** $15/month (500 subs). Expanded features
- **Plus:** $30/month (500 subs). Full automation, advanced analytics

### Who Should Use AWeber

**Strong fit:**
- Local business owners who want phone support and human help
- Non-technical users who value reliability over feature depth

**Probably not right for:**
- Businesses that need advanced automation (use ActiveCampaign)
- Budget-conscious users who can get more from Brevo or MailerLite for less

A personal trainer selling online programs, a cooking instructor offering virtual classes, or a financial advisor hosting educational webinars will use every feature. ### Pricing

- **Starter:** $19/month (1,000 contacts). Full email features
- **Marketer:** $59/month. Automation, webinars, sales funnels
- **Creator:** $69/month. Courses, premium features

### Who Should Use GetResponse

**Strong fit:**
- Local consultants, coaches, and educators who teach online alongside email

## FAQ

**What is the best free email marketing tool for a local business?**

Brevo offers the most generous free plan: unlimited contacts and 9,000 emails per month. MailerLite comes second with 500 contacts and 12,000 emails per month. Both include automation on free plans. Kit (ConvertKit) allows 10,000 free subscribers but does not include automations on the free tier.

**How much does email marketing cost for a small business?**

Entry-level paid plans start at $9-15 per month for 500-1,000 contacts (Brevo, MailerLite, ActiveCampaign). Free plans cover most local businesses under 500 subscribers. At 5,000 contacts, expect to pay $30-60 per month. At 10,000 contacts, $50-100 per month depending on the tool.

**Is Mailchimp still the best email marketing tool?**

Mailchimp is the most recognized name but not the best value for local businesses. The free plan was reduced to 250 contacts and 500 emails per month in 2024. Brevo and MailerLite both offer more on their free plans. Mailchimp's strength is universal integration support.

**What email marketing tool is best for ecommerce?**

Klaviyo is the best for ecommerce with deep Shopify/WooCommerce integration and purchase-behavior automation. Omnisend offers similar features at a lower price point. Both include SMS + email campaigns and pre-built ecommerce workflows.

**Should a local business use email marketing?**

Yes. [64% of small businesses](https://www.demandsage.com/email-marketing-statistics/) use email marketing, and it returns $36-42 for every $1 spent. Email reaches 90%+ of inboxes compared to 5-9% organic reach on social media. For [local businesses with no marketing team](/blog/market-small-business-no-team), email is the highest-ROI channel after Google Business Profile.

**How often should a local business send emails?**

Once per week is the standard for most local businesses. A weekly email with one useful tip plus one promotional mention maintains engagement without overwhelming subscribers. For more [email newsletter ideas](/blog/email-newsletter-ideas), see our 213-idea list organized by category.

Birdeye dominates the review management space for one reason: coverage. The platform monitors reviews across 200+ sites and automates the entire collection-to-response workflow.

Birdeye sends automated review requests via SMS and email right after a customer interaction. The timing matters. Requests sent within 24 hours of service get 3 to 5 times more responses than delayed requests.

The platform aggregates reviews from Google, Facebook, Yelp, Healthgrades, Avvo, and over 200 other industry-specific sites into a single dashboard. You see every review, respond from one place, and track sentiment trends over time.

Their AI response feature generates reply suggestions based on review content and sentiment. For businesses managing 50+ locations, this alone saves hours per week.

> **Our Take:** Birdeye is the most complete review management platform we have tested. The 200+ site coverage means you will not miss reviews on niche directories that competitors ignore. The price reflects that depth.

### Where It Falls Short

Birdeye is expensive. At $299 per month as a starting point, it prices out most solopreneurs and single-location businesses. The contract terms also require annual commitment for the best rates.

## 2. Podium. Best for SMS-Based Review Collection

Podium built its reputation on one insight: people respond to texts faster than emails. Their text-first approach to review collection consistently generates higher response rates than email-only platforms.

### What It Does Well

Podium sends review requests via SMS and routes the customer directly to your Google review page. No app downloads. No extra steps. The friction reduction makes a real difference.

The platform also doubles as a customer messaging hub. You can handle reviews, payments, and customer conversations from a single inbox. For businesses that already communicate with customers via text, Podium fits naturally into the workflow.

Response rates on Podium campaigns average 15 to 20% higher than email-only review platforms. That data comes from Podium's own published case studies, but it aligns with what we see across local businesses.

> **Our Take:** If your customers already text you, Podium is a no-brainer. The review-to-text pipeline converts well. The price is steep, but the platform does more than just reviews.

### Where It Falls Short

At $399 per month minimum, Podium is one of the most expensive options. The pricing is opaque. You cannot see exact costs without a sales call. For businesses that only need review management (not payments or messaging), you are paying for features you will not use.

### Key Features

## 3. Reputation.com. Best Review Management Software for Enterprise

Reputation.com serves enterprise brands with hundreds or thousands of locations. The platform processes millions of reviews and uses AI to extract operational insights from unstructured feedback data.

### What It Does Well

The Reputation Score is the platform's signature metric. It combines review volume, rating, recency, response rate, and sentiment into a single benchmark. Enterprise teams use it to compare locations, identify underperformers, and track improvement over time.

Their AI engine analyzes review text to surface recurring themes. If customers at Location 47 keep mentioning "wait times," the platform flags it. That turns reviews from a marketing channel into an operational feedback loop.

> **Our Take:** Reputation.com is overkill for small businesses. But for chains with 100+ locations, no other tool matches the depth of its location-level analytics and operational insights.

### Where It Falls Short

Pricing is entirely custom and enterprise-level. Expect $1,000+ per month. The onboarding process is complex. The platform requires dedicated time to configure properly. Small teams will struggle to extract value from the advanced features.

Trustpilot is the most recognized consumer review platform globally. Over 1 million businesses use it. The brand name itself carries trust weight that other platforms cannot match.

Trustpilot reviews appear directly in Google search results as seller ratings. That visibility drives click-through rates. [Studies show](/blog/organic-ctr-by-position) that star ratings in search results increase CTR by 15 to 30%.

The platform offers both a free plan and paid tiers. The free plan lets you claim your profile, respond to reviews, and display a basic Trustpilot widget. For e-commerce brands, the paid tiers add automated review invitations, product-level reviews, and advanced analytics.

Trustpilot's TrustBox widgets embed on product pages, checkout pages, and landing pages. Displaying reviews near purchase decisions [boosts conversion rates by up to 270%](https://spiegel.medill.northwestern.edu/online-reviews/).

> **Our Take:** Trustpilot is the default for e-commerce brands. The Google integration and consumer recognition make it uniquely valuable. But it is a review display platform, not a full reputation management suite.

### Where It Falls Short

Trustpilot does not aggregate reviews from other platforms. You only manage Trustpilot reviews within Trustpilot. If you need to monitor Google, Yelp, and Facebook reviews from one dashboard, you still need a separate tool.

The review gating controversy also matters. Trustpilot prohibits filtering reviews by sentiment before publishing. Some businesses prefer platforms where they can route unhappy customers to private feedback first.

### Key Features

- TrustBox widgets for product pages and checkout flows

## 9. GatherUp. Best for Automated Review Follow-Up

GatherUp (formerly GetFiveStars) specializes in the post-service feedback loop. The platform automates review requests, captures first-party feedback, and routes customers to the right review platform based on their satisfaction level.

### What It Does Well

GatherUp's multi-step campaign system sends an initial feedback request. Based on the response, it either directs satisfied customers to leave a public review or captures private feedback from dissatisfied customers. This two-step approach protects your public rating.

The first-party review feature collects testimonials on your own website. Those reviews include [schema markup](/blog/schema-markup-seo-guide) that can trigger rich snippets in search results. For businesses focused on [SEO](/blog/seo-statistics), first-party reviews provide unique content that third-party platforms do not offer.

GatherUp also offers Net Promoter Score tracking. You can measure customer loyalty over time and correlate NPS trends with review performance.

> **Our Take:** GatherUp's two-step review funnel is the most thoughtful on this list. The first-party review approach also gives you SEO benefits that pure third-party platforms miss.

### Where It Falls Short

The interface looks dated compared to Birdeye or Podium. The mobile experience needs improvement. Multi-location management exists but lacks the polish of enterprise-focused competitors.

### Key Features

- Two-step review funnel with satisfaction-based routing
- First-party review collection with schema markup for SEO
- Net Promoter Score tracking and trend analysis
- Automated SMS and email review request campaigns
- Review widget builder for website social proof display

### Pricing

- **Single Location:** $99/month. Full feature set for 1 location
- **Multi-Location:** Custom pricing. Volume discounts available
- **Agency:** Custom pricing. White-label and client management
- **Free trial:** Demo available on request

### Who Should Use GatherUp

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best review management software in 2026?**

Birdeye is the best overall review management software for most businesses. It covers 200+ review sites, offers AI response tools, and handles multi-location management. For small businesses on a budget, NiceJob at $75/month delivers the strongest automation per dollar. For agencies, BrightLocal bundles review monitoring with [local SEO tools](/best/local-seo-tools) starting at $39/month.

**Are there free review management tools?**

Trustpilot offers a permanent free plan for basic profile management and review response. Google Business Profile itself is free and lets you respond to Google reviews directly. BrightLocal offers a 14-day free trial. For free review monitoring without management features, Google Alerts can track brand mentions. The limitation of all free options: no automated review collection.

**How much does review management software cost?**

Review management software ranges from $39/month (BrightLocal) to $1,000+/month (Reputation.com). Most small businesses spend $75 to $299/month. Enterprise platforms with custom pricing typically start at $1,000. The price usually scales with location count. A single-location business should expect $75 to $150/month for a capable tool.

**Do online reviews actually affect local SEO rankings?**

Yes. Google confirms that review quantity, quality, and response rate influence local pack rankings. [Local SEO statistics](/blog/local-seo-statistics) show that businesses in the top 3 local results average 47 reviews. A 1-star rating increase can boost revenue by 5 to 9%. Responding to reviews signals active management, which Google rewards. Pairing review management with [local SEO](/blog/local-seo-guide) and [Google Business Profile optimization](/blog/optimize-google-business-profile) creates the strongest local ranking foundation.

**Can AI respond to reviews automatically?**

Most modern review management tools offer AI-generated response suggestions. Birdeye, Podium, and Reputation.com all include this feature. The AI drafts a response based on review sentiment and content. However, fully automated publishing without human review carries risk. A poorly generated response to a negative review can make the situation worse. Best practice: use AI to draft, then approve before publishing.

**What is the difference between review management and reputation management?**

Review management focuses specifically on collecting, monitoring, and responding to customer reviews. Reputation management is broader. It includes review management plus social media monitoring, brand mention tracking, crisis response, and public relations. Tools like Birdeye and Reputation.com span both categories. Tools like NiceJob and Grade.us focus on reviews specifically.

## 2. Google Business Profile. Best Free Foundation for Real Estate SEO

Before spending a dollar on any tool, every real estate agent needs a fully optimized Google Business Profile. It's free. And it controls whether you appear when a seller searches "real estate agent near me" or a buyer searches "best realtor in [city]."

Google Business Profile controls your appearance in the local 3-pack (the map results above organic listings). For real estate agents, this is where motivated sellers and relocating buyers start their search. A complete GBP with accurate info, photos, and regular posts outranks an incomplete profile every time.

The key actions: claim and verify your listing, select the right category ("Real estate agent" as primary, with secondary categories like "Real estate agency" or "Real estate consultant"), add your service areas (every neighborhood and zip code you serve), upload real photos of you, your team, and properties you've sold (not stock photos), respond to every client review, and post updates at least weekly.

Here's a move most agents miss. Add every neighborhood you serve as a service area. Google uses this data to determine relevance. If you serve 12 neighborhoods, list all 12. An agent with "Buckhead, Midtown, Virginia-Highland, Decatur" in their service areas ranks for more location-specific searches than one who lists only "Atlanta."

> **Our Take:** GBP is not optional. It's the foundation. Every tool on this list either optimizes your GBP, posts to your GBP, or tracks your GBP rankings. But none of them matter if your profile is unclaimed, has wrong hours, or lists a brokerage phone number nobody answers. Spend 2 hours setting this up properly before investing in any paid tool.

### Where It Falls Short

Manual everything. Writing GBP posts, responding to reviews, uploading listing photos, updating your service areas, all done by hand. No automation. No scheduling. No analytics beyond basic view counts and search query data. Google's built-in insights are surface-level. You can see how many people viewed your profile, but you can't see where you rank in Maps across your service area, and for agents covering 15+ neighborhoods, that's a critical blind spot.

### Key Features

- Service area configuration (add every neighborhood you serve)

- Review management (respond to client reviews)

## The Real Estate SEO Playbook: What Actually Moves Rankings

Here's what we've learned publishing content for real estate agents across 50+ markets.

### The Neighborhood Content Strategy

Zillow can't write about what it's like to live in your neighborhoods. You can.

Create a page for every neighborhood you serve. Not a listing page, a guide. "Living in Lakewood, Dallas: Everything You Need to Know." Cover schools, restaurants, commute times, home price ranges, walkability, parks, and what makes it unique. This is content Zillow will never create. At 30 articles per month, you can cover every neighborhood in your market within 2-3 months. Then layer in seasonal content: "[City] Housing Market Report Q1 2026," "Best Time to Sell in [City]," "Is [City] a Buyer's Market in 2026?"

### The Review Velocity Game

Google's local algorithm weights recent reviews heavily. An agent who received 10 reviews in the last 30 days outranks an agent with 50 reviews. all from 2 years ago.

That's why review automation matters. Manual requests get forgotten. Automated text messages after closings don't. The consistency is what compounds.

### The GBP Activity Signal

Google rewards active profiles. An agent posting to GBP 3-4 times per week signals to Google that the business is active, relevant, and engaged. An agent who posted once in March and nothing since? Google treats that profile as less relevant.

30 GBP posts per month means you're posting almost daily. That's a signal no inactive competitor can match.

Writing 30 blog posts a month takes a full-time writer. Or one of these tools.

That's the promise, anyway. Every AI writing tool claims to produce "high-quality content in minutes." We wanted to know which ones actually deliver. and which ones leave you rewriting every paragraph.

We tested 12 AI blog writing tools over 4 weeks. For each tool, we generated articles on the same 5 topics across different niches: local services, B2B SaaS, health and wellness, finance, and e-commerce. Here's what we found.

Most tools produce decent first drafts. A handful produce publish-ready content. One publishes it for you automatically. And a few still require so much editing that you're better off writing from scratch.

We publish 30+ articles per month for businesses across 70+ industries. We built theStacc ourselves, so we know what separates a real content engine from a glorified autocomplete tool. Below, honest breakdowns of all 12 tools, including real pricing, cost per article, and exactly who each one is built for.

Here's how it works. You enter a keyword. Surfer analyzes the top-ranking pages, builds a content brief with optimal word count, headings, and NLP terms, then generates a full article that hits those targets. You get a real-time content score as the article is written. The result is content that's structurally optimized before you touch it.

The writing quality is above average for this category. Articles read naturally, follow logical structures, and incorporate target keywords without stuffing. Surfer AI also handles internal linking suggestions and image insertion. though both require manual execution.

The catch? Surfer AI charges per article on top of the monthly subscription. The Essential plan at $89/month includes 30 AI articles. That works out to about $3/article for the AI generation, but you're still editing, formatting, and publishing manually. Add the time cost and the real per-article number climbs fast.

## 7. Frase. Best AI Blog Writing Tool for Research and Briefs

Frase approaches AI writing from the research side. Instead of starting with "generate an article," Frase starts with "here's everything the top-ranking articles cover, now write something better."

The content brief generator is the standout feature. Enter a keyword, and Frase analyzes the top 20 SERP results, extracts common topics, headings, questions, and statistics, then organizes them into a structured brief. That brief becomes the foundation for your article, whether you write it yourself or use Frase's AI writer.

The AI writer itself is functional but not the strongest on this list. It follows the brief structure well, but output quality sits in the "needs moderate editing" category. Sentences can feel generic. Transitions are sometimes clunky. You'll spend 15–30 minutes polishing a 1,500-word article.

At $15/month for 10 AI articles, the per-article cost is $1.50. But the real value is the brief, even if you use a different AI writer for the actual content. A Frase brief + Jasper for writing, for example, produces better results than either tool alone.

The real question: is Frase a writing tool or a research tool?

It's both. But it's a better research tool than writing tool.

> **Our Take:** "Frase's content briefs are the best in this category. Hands down. The AI writer is serviceable but not exceptional. The ideal use case is Frase for research + another tool for writing. If you only use one tool, expect to edit heavily."

No local SEO. No GBP. No social media. Frase is laser-focused on research and writing. and the writing half needs work.

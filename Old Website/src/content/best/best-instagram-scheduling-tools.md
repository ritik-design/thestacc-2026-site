## 1. Later. Best Instagram Scheduling Tool Overall

Later started as "Latergramme" and was built specifically for Instagram before expanding to other platforms. That Instagram DNA shows in every feature. The visual grid planner is the best in the category. You drag and drop posts to see exactly how your feed will look before anything publishes.

### What It Does Well

Later's visual content calendar lets you plan your Instagram grid with precision. Drag photos into position, rearrange them, and preview the final feed layout. For brands where Instagram aesthetics drive sales, the grid planner alone justifies the price.

Later auto-publishes feed posts, carousels, Stories, and Reels. The Linkin.bio feature creates a clickable landing page from your Instagram bio link. The analytics dashboard shows engagement by post type, best posting times, and audience demographics.

> **Our Take:** Later is the default choice for Instagram-focused brands. The grid planner is genuinely useful. If your Instagram feed aesthetic matters to your business, no other tool matches this.

### Where It Falls Short

The free plan limits you to 5 posts per profile per month. That is barely 1 post per week. The inbox feature only covers Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok. And analytics are locked behind the Growth plan at $33/month.

### Key Features

- Visual Instagram grid planner (drag-and-drop)
- Auto-publish Reels, Stories, and carousels
- Linkin.bio clickable landing page
- Best time to post suggestions
- Hashtag analytics and suggestions

### Pricing

- **Free:** 1 social set, 5 posts/profile/month
- **Starter:** $25/month ,  30 posts/profile, 1 social set
- **Growth:** $33/month ,  150 posts, 3 social sets, full analytics
- **Advanced:** $66/month. Unlimited posts, 6 social sets

### Who Should Use Later

**Strong fit:**
- Instagram-focused brands and creators who care about grid aesthetics
- E-commerce brands using Linkin.bio to drive traffic from Instagram
- Small businesses that plan 2 to 4 weeks of visual content in advance

**Probably not right for:**
- Businesses that need a multi-platform scheduler with equal depth across all networks
- Budget-conscious users who need more than 5 free posts per month

Scheduling tools help you post at the right time. They do not write captions, design graphics, or plan your [content strategy](/blog/create-content-calendar-seo). If you want someone to handle all of that, consider a done-for-you service instead of a scheduling tool.

Buffer is the best free Instagram scheduling tool for its simplicity (3 channels, auto-publish Reels). Pallyy is the best free option with a visual grid planner (15 posts/month). Both handle basic Instagram scheduling without requiring payment.

**Can you schedule Instagram Reels in advance?**

Yes. All 10 tools on this list support Reels scheduling with auto-publish. In 2026, Reels auto-publish is standard across all major Instagram schedulers. You upload the video, write the caption, set the time, and the tool publishes automatically.

**How often should you post on Instagram?**

3 to 5 feed posts per week is the minimum for consistent growth. The ideal mix in 2026 is [60 to 70% Reels](https://buffer.com/resources/state-of-social-media-engagement-2026/), 20 to 30% carousels, and 10% single images. Daily Stories keep you visible between posts. Consistency matters more than volume.

Most schedulers support Stories scheduling with either auto-publish or push notifications. Sked Social, Later, Buffer, and SocialBee all auto-publish Stories directly. Tailwind does not support Stories auto-publish. Check each tool's current feature list before committing.

Planly at $15/month is the cheapest paid option with Reels and Stories support. Buffer Essentials at $6/month per channel is cheaper if you only need 1 Instagram account. Both Buffer and Pallyy offer free plans for basic scheduling.

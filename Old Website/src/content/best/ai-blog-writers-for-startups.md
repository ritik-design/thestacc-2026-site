## FAQs

**Can AI blog writers actually help startups rank on Google?**

Yes. AI-generated content ranks when it targets real keywords, matches search intent, and follows on-page SEO best practices. Google doesn't penalize AI content. they penalize unhelpful content, regardless of who or what wrote it. Tools that include SERP analysis (Koala, Frase, Surfer, Scalenut, theStacc) produce content more likely to rank because it's informed by what's already working.

**How many blog posts should a startup publish per month?**

Minimum 4 to build any momentum. 15-20 for steady growth. 30+ for aggressive topical authority building. The compounding effect of SEO means more consistent output produces disproportionately better results. A startup publishing 30 articles/month for 6 months will outrank one publishing 5 articles/month for 3 years.

**Is AI-written content good enough for startup blogs?**

For informational blog posts, yes. AI writers in 2026 produce content that's 80-90% as good as a mid-level freelance writer. The remaining 10-20% gap matters for thought leadership, case studies, and opinion pieces. For SEO-focused blog content designed to capture search traffic, AI output is more than sufficient. especially when the alternative is publishing nothing.

**What's the cheapest AI blog writer for startups?**

Koala.sh at $9/month for 15 articles. That's $0.60 per article. ContentBot at $19/month for 10 autopilot articles is the cheapest with auto-publishing. If you're optimizing for total cost including your time, theStacc at $99/month for 30 articles with zero hours required is the lowest effective cost for a founder whose time is worth more than $0/hour.

**Do I need an AI blog writer if I use ChatGPT?**

ChatGPT writes text. It doesn't research keywords, analyze SERPs, optimize for SEO, generate meta tags, structure articles for rankings, or publish to your CMS. Using ChatGPT for blog content is like using a word processor. the writing happens, but the SEO strategy, optimization, and publishing are still on you. Dedicated AI blog writers automate the full workflow, not just the writing step.

**Should I use Jasper or theStacc for my startup blog?**

Different tools for different needs. Jasper is a writing assistant. you write with its help, then edit and publish. Best for founders who enjoy writing and want brand voice control. theStacc is a publishing service. content appears on your site without your involvement. Best for founders who want results without the process. Jasper costs $49-125/month plus 20-30 hours of your time. theStacc costs $99/month plus 0 hours.

Yes, but usually unnecessary. The most effective combination: use theStacc ($99/month) for your core 30 articles/month, then use Surfer SEO ($89/month) to optimize any articles your team writes manually. Total: $188/month for automated output plus manual optimization. Avoid stacking 3-4 writing tools. you'll spend more time managing tools than producing content.

theStacc: 15 minutes to setup, articles start publishing within 24-48 hours. ContentBot: 30 minutes, first autopilot article within a week. Byword: 1 hour to generate and publish a batch.

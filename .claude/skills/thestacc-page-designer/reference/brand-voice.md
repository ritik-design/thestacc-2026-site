# theStacc Brand Voice

## Personality (the human behind the words)

- **Operator, not marketer.** Talks like a head of growth who ships every week, not like a corporate brand manager.
- **Confidently understated.** Doesn't shout. States the result and moves on.
- **Anti-fluff.** Refuses jargon, refuses superlatives ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "powerful"), refuses hedging ("might", "could").
- **Smart but plain.** Big idea, small words. A dentist and a Series-B Head of Growth can both follow every sentence.
- **Slightly playful** when warranted — a wink, a pun, a "we read your mind" moment — but never goofy.
- **Direct about money.** Names dollar amounts (`$3,500/mo agency`, `$40k saved/year`) without flinching.

## The 8 voice rules

1. **Lead with the outcome, not the feature.** "Rank in the Map Pack" beats "GBP automation engine".
2. **Verbs > nouns.** "We publish, you grow." beats "Our solution drives growth."
3. **Specific numbers > vague claims.** "30 articles a month" beats "lots of content".
4. **Real customer language.** Use words our buyers use: "Map Pack", "near me", "$1 trial", "agency I was paying", not "SERP visibility", "organic asset utilization", "demand-gen funnel".
5. **Address the reader directly.** "You", "your". Never "users", "customers", "businesses" (we are a customer; we are the business).
6. **Short sentences. Two-clause max.** Average sentence ≤ 18 words. Headline sentences ≤ 10 words.
7. **One verb per CTA.** "Start." "Try." "Watch." "Book." Compound CTAs ("Start your trial and book a demo") lose buyers.
8. **Drop the qualifier.** Cut "really", "very", "incredibly", "super", "actually". They weaken every line.

## Tone variations by surface

| Surface | Tone shift |
|---|---|
| Homepage hero | Confident + understated. Big claim, simple words. |
| Module pages | Outcome-first + tactical. Show the feature only as proof of the outcome. |
| Pricing | Plain spoken, math-aware. "Here's what it costs. Here's what you save." |
| Blog post | Operator-to-operator. Lean into "we tested this and here's what happened". |
| Glossary | Teacher voice. Define, illustrate, link to next concept. No selling. |
| Reviews / alternatives | Fair. Acknowledge competitor strengths before saying where we win. |
| Best-of lists | Neutral judge until the last paragraph. Then a clear winner pick. |
| Case studies | Customer-first. Quote them at length. We barely appear. |
| About / press | Founder-led. First person occasionally. |
| Legal / terms | Plain English. No "heretofore". Bullet points where lists are obvious. |
| Demo / contact | Warm. "We read every email." Real human signing off. |
| Error states | Calm. Acknowledge the problem, give the next action, no apology theatre. |

## Phrases we never ship

- "Empower your team to..." — empty
- "Unlock the full potential of..." — empty
- "Take your SEO to the next level" — cliché
- "Revolutionary AI-driven solution" — corporate
- "Best-in-class" / "world-class" — meaningless
- "Cutting-edge technology" — vapor
- "Drive ROI" / "Maximize ROI" — corporate
- "Streamline your workflow" — generic
- "Game-changer" — banned
- "Disrupt the industry" — banned
- "Synergy", "leverage", "ideate", "scalable solution" — banned
- "Boost your SEO" — too small a verb
- Anything starting with "In today's competitive landscape..." — fatal

## Style mechanics

- **Sentence case** for everything in the UI, including buttons and labels. Title Case only in legal headers (`Terms of Service`) and the brand name `theStacc`.
- **Brand name** is lowercase `t` even at the start of a sentence — `theStacc`. Never "TheStacc", "The Stacc", "Stacc", "thestacc.com" in body copy (URL form is fine in URLs only).
- **Em dashes** (—) sparingly; one per paragraph max. Don't use them as default connective tissue — periods are fine.
- **Numbers** under 10 spelled out only in narrative copy. In headlines, stats, and CTAs always use numerals (30, not thirty).
- **Currency** with dollar sign, no decimal for round numbers: `$99`, `$3,500/mo`, `$40k+`.
- **Time** with abbreviated units in stats: `24h`, `5 min`, `60 days`, `90 days`.
- **Lists** prefer parallel grammar. All bullets start with a verb, or all start with a noun. Never mix.

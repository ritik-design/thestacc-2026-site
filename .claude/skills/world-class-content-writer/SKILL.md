## ENFORCEMENT RULES — READ BEFORE EVERY EXECUTION

> Non-negotiable. AI content fails without these.

### Universal quality gates
1. **Word count:** 2,500+ words standard, 3,500+ for pillars/guides.
2. **H2 count:** 6+ standard, 10+ for guides.
3. **Per-H2 minimum:** 300 words of real substance. No thin sections.
4. **Subheader density:** one H2 or H3 every 150–200 words.
5. **Rich element ratio:** one table, list, comparison, framework, or example block per 400 words.
6. **FAQ count:** 8–20, sourced from real PAA / Reddit / forum data.
7. **Zero fluff.** Zero filler. Zero generic intros.
8. **Every claim cited:** stat, study, named source, or first-person experience.

### Architecture rules
1. **One article per execution.** Never batch.
2. **Research is mandatory** before any body content.
3. **Outline is approved (or self-verified) before prose.**
4. **Every major section answers What, Why, How, When, Who.**
5. **Output is a finished `.astro` file** ready to drop into `src/pages/{collection}/{slug}/index.astro`.

## thestacc VOICE — 8 rules + word swap table

### The 8 voice rules
1. **Outcome > feature.** "Rank in the Map Pack" beats "GBP automation engine".
2. **Verbs > nouns.** "We publish, you grow" beats "Our solution drives growth".
3. **Specific numbers > vague claims.** "30 articles a month" beats "lots of content".
4. **Real customer language.** "Map Pack", "near me", "$1 trial", "agency I was paying" — never "SERP visibility", "demand-gen funnel".
5. **Address the reader directly.** "You", "your". Never "users", "customers", "businesses" (they ARE the business).
6. **Short sentences. Two-clause max.** Avg ≤ 18 words. Headlines ≤ 10 words.
7. **One verb per CTA.** "Start." "Try." "Watch." "Book."
8. **Drop the qualifier.** Cut "really", "very", "incredibly", "super", "actually".

### Word swap table

| ✅ Use | ❌ Never |
|---|---|
| publishes | outputs, delivers, leverages |
| writes | generates, creates content |
| ranks | improves visibility, boosts SERP performance |
| gets you found | drives discoverability |
| article | piece of content, asset |
| free trial / $1 trial | freemium, no-risk evaluation |
| customers | clients (we're SaaS, not an agency) |
| people | users, individuals |
| your business | your organization, your brand |
| every month | monthly, recurring, ongoing |
| in 60 days | in the near term, over time |
| 92% | the majority of |
| dentists, plumbers | service-based SMBs |
| ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini | LLMs, generative AI |

## PHASE 4 — WRITE THE FULL ARTICLE

### 4.1 Sticky Intro (mandatory)
- Exactly 50–100 words
- Confirms reader is in the right place
- Names a specific pain
- Previews what they will learn
- Creates curiosity

❌ "In today's fast-paced digital landscape, content marketing is more important than ever."
✅ "Most SEO guides recycle 2019 tactics. This article covers the 12 ranking factors that actually move the needle in 2026 — including the one that lifted our organic traffic 214% in 90 days."

### 4.2 Body section writing
For every H2:
1. **Open with a direct answer** (1–2 sentences).
2. **Expand with substance** — 300–600 words.
3. **Include at least one rich element**: list, table, framework, example, stat, quote.
4. **Bucket brigades** to keep flow: "Here's the thing:", "Now:", "But here's the catch:", "The best part?", "So:".
5. **End with a transition.**

### 4.3 Subheader rules

| ❌ Weak | ✅ Strong |
|---|---|
| Benefits | 7 Benefits That Actually Move the Needle |
| Examples | Real Examples From $1M+ Campaigns |
| Tips | 12 Tips From 500+ Client Audits |
| Mistakes | The 8 Mistakes That Cost Teams $50K+ |
| Conclusion | Your 30-Day Action Plan |

### 4.4 Copywriting frameworks (use, don't name)
| Framework | Use case |
|---|---|
| AIDA | Intros, CTAs |
| PAS | Problem sections |
| BAB | Transformation sections |
| 4P | Product/service sections |
| FAB | Feature sections |

### 4.5 Dwell-time toolkit
Sticky intro · above-fold value · long-form depth · rich examples · visual placeholders · tables · comparisons · named frameworks · checklists · FAQs.

## PHASE 5 — EEAT, SEO, AIO, GEO

### EEAT
- **Experience:** "In our 30-day test...", "We ran this on a 4.2M URL site..."
- **Expertise:** correct industry terminology, nuanced explanation, named techniques
- **Authority:** named experts, cited studies, linked research
- **Trustworthiness:** balanced (pros AND cons), honest limitations, updated date

### SEO
Keyword placement: H1 · intro (first 100w) · 2+ H2s · conclusion · URL · meta title · meta description.
Add LSI keywords, related entities (people, tools, companies), topical entities.
Proper H1→H2→H3 hierarchy. Descriptive alt text. Internal + external links marked.

### AIO (AI Overview)
Every major section contains:
- A definition ("[Term] is...")
- A 40–80w direct-answer block right after the H2
- A bullet summary (3–5 points)
- A key takeaway sentence

### GEO (per AI platform)

|---|---|

| Gemini | Entity-rich, fact-based, structured signals |
| Claude | Balanced perspective, nuance, citations |

Every major section answers **What, Why, How, When, Who**.

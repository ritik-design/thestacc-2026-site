title: Data Sync & Status
---

 <p>When you add a business location to Local SEO, theStacc reaches out to Google to pull in your live data - your star rating, your reviews, and a starter set of local keywords. This page explains exactly what gets synced, how to read the sync status, when to run a manual sync, and what happens if part of a sync fails.</p>
<h2 id="what-syncs-after-you-add-a-location">What syncs after you add a location<a aria-label="Copy link to What syncs after you add a location" class="heading-anchor" href="#what-syncs-after-you-add-a-location">#</a></h2>
<p>The moment you confirm a business (or add a new location), theStacc kicks off a background sync. You don't have to wait on the page - it runs on its own and the dashboard updates as it goes.</p>
<p>A sync pulls three things, in order:</p>
<ol><li><strong>Business info</strong> - your current <strong>star rating</strong> and <strong>total review count</strong>, pulled straight from your Google Business Profile listing.</li><li><strong>Reviews</strong> - up to <strong>100 of your most recent reviews</strong>, imported so you can read and respond to them inside theStacc.</li><li><strong>Keywords</strong> - <strong>10 starter local keywords</strong> generated for your business, so you have something to work with right away. You can add, edit, or remove keywords later from your keyword settings.</li></ol>
<h3 id="a-note-on-rating-and-review-count">A note on rating and review count<a aria-label="Copy link to A note on rating and review count" class="heading-anchor" href="#a-note-on-rating-and-review-count">#</a></h3>
<p>Your <strong>rating</strong> and <strong>review count</strong> always come from Google's actual listing - they are never calculated from the handful of reviews theStacc imports. A sync only imports a sample of your reviews (up to 100), so averaging that sample would give a wrong number. This is why your displayed rating matches Google exactly, even when theStacc has only imported some of your reviews.</p>
<p>If Google has no rating data for your listing yet (common for brand-new businesses or those with a very thin profile), theStacc records that the sync ran but leaves your rating blank, showing a dash (-) instead of a misleading 0.</p>
<h3 id="fetching-reviews-can-take-a-moment">Fetching reviews can take a moment<a aria-label="Copy link to Fetching reviews can take a moment" class="heading-anchor" href="#fetching-reviews-can-take-a-moment">#</a></h3>
<p>Pulling reviews from Google is the slowest step. <strong>Taking 30 or more seconds is completely normal</strong> - theStacc has to ask Google for your reviews and wait for the results to come back. The dashboard shows "Fetching reviews (this may take a moment)..." while this happens. It is not stuck - just let it run.</p>
<h3 id="what-each-review-brings-in">What each review brings in<a aria-label="Copy link to What each review brings in" class="heading-anchor" href="#what-each-review-brings-in">#</a></h3>
<p>For every review theStacc imports, it captures:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Author name</strong> - the reviewer's name</li><li><strong>Author photo</strong> - the reviewer's profile image</li><li><strong>Star rating</strong> - 1 to 5 stars</li><li><strong>Review text</strong> - what the customer wrote</li><li><strong>Date posted</strong> - the timestamp of the review</li></ul>
<p>These are the fields you'll see on the <strong>Local SEO &gt; Reviews</strong> page. New reviews come in marked as "new" so you can spot which ones still need a response. See <a href="/docs/local-seo/reviews/">Reviews</a> for how to read, filter, and respond to them.</p>
<h2 id="reading-the-sync-status">Reading the sync status<a aria-label="Copy link to Reading the sync status" class="heading-anchor" href="#reading-the-sync-status">#</a></h2>
<p>While a sync is running, the dashboard shows where it is in the process. There are six possible states:</p>
<div class="table-wrap"><table><thead><tr><th>Status</th><th>What it means</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td><strong>Idle</strong></td><td>No sync is running. This is the resting state.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Syncing business data</strong></td><td>Pulling your rating and review count from Google.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Fetching reviews</strong></td><td>Importing your reviews. This is the step that can take 30+ seconds.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Generating keywords</strong></td><td>Creating your starter local keywords.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Completed</strong></td><td>The sync finished and your data is refreshed.</td></tr><tr><td><strong>Failed</strong></td><td>The sync hit a problem. Your dashboard will show why and let you retry.</td></tr></tbody></table></div>
<p>A sync always moves through these stages in order: <strong>idle, then syncing business, then syncing reviews, then syncing keywords, then either completed or failed.</strong> The dashboard updates the message automatically as each stage finishes, so you can watch the progress in real time.</p>
<h2 id="running-a-manual-sync">Running a manual sync<a aria-label="Copy link to Running a manual sync" class="heading-anchor" href="#running-a-manual-sync">#</a></h2>
<p>Your data refreshes automatically the first time you set up a location, and again on a return visit if your data has gone stale. But you can also refresh it yourself at any time.</p>
<p>To run a manual sync:</p>
<ol><li>Open your Local SEO dashboard for the location.</li><li>Click the <strong>Sync Data</strong> button (it has a refresh icon).</li><li>Watch the status message - it will move through "Syncing business data," "Fetching reviews," and "Generating keywords" before showing "Sync complete! Data refreshed."</li></ol>
<p>When to use <strong>Sync Data</strong>:</p>
<ul><li><strong>You just got new reviews on Google</strong> and want them pulled in now rather than waiting for the next automatic refresh.</li><li><strong>Your star rating or review count changed</strong> and you want the dashboard to match Google.</li><li><strong>A previous sync failed</strong> and you want to try again.</li><li><strong>Your data looks stale</strong> - for example, the "Last synced" timestamp is old.</li></ul>
<p>Running <strong>Sync Data</strong> requires editor access to the project. If the button is greyed out, you have view-only access; ask a project owner or admin to run the sync or grant you editor rights.</p>
<p>If you click <strong>Sync Data</strong> while a sync is already running, theStacc won't start a second one - it simply lets the in-progress sync finish.</p>
<h2 id="partial-syncs-when-one-step-fails-but-others-succeed">Partial syncs: when one step fails but others succeed<a aria-label="Copy link to Partial syncs: when one step fails but others succeed" class="heading-anchor" href="#partial-syncs-when-one-step-fails-but-others-succeed">#</a></h2>
<p>The three steps - business info, reviews, and keywords - run independently. If one step fails, theStacc keeps the data from the steps that worked rather than throwing everything away.</p>
<p>For example: if your <strong>business info syncs successfully</strong> but the <strong>reviews step fails</strong> (this can happen for some international listings, or if Google is temporarily slow to respond), your rating, review count, and keywords are still saved. The sync is marked <strong>completed</strong> because useful data came through. You simply won't have the reviews from that run.</p>
<p>A sync is only marked <strong>failed</strong> when *all three* steps fail - meaning theStacc couldn't pull your business info, your reviews, *or* generate keywords. In that case nothing useful came back, and the dashboard shows the error.</p>
<h3 id="how-to-retry-a-partial-or-failed-sync">How to retry a partial or failed sync<a aria-label="Copy link to How to retry a partial or failed sync" class="heading-anchor" href="#how-to-retry-a-partial-or-failed-sync">#</a></h3>
<p>Either way, the fix is the same: click <strong>Sync Data</strong> to run the whole sync again. A retry re-attempts every step, so a step that failed last time gets another chance. Reviews and keywords you already have are preserved - theStacc skips anything it has already imported and only adds what's new, so retrying never creates duplicates.</p>
<p>If retrying doesn't resolve a failure, see <a href="/docs/local-seo/error-handling/">Error Handling &amp; Sync Failures</a> for how to read the error details and what each type of failure means.</p>
<h2 id="stuck-syncs-and-the-5-minute-rule">Stuck syncs and the 5-minute rule<a aria-label="Copy link to Stuck syncs and the 5-minute rule" class="heading-anchor" href="#stuck-syncs-and-the-5-minute-rule">#</a></h2>
<p>Very occasionally a sync can get "stuck" - for example, if the server restarts in the middle of one. Without a safeguard, the location could stay frozen in a "syncing" state and the <strong>Sync Data</strong> button would appear to do nothing.</p>
<p>theStacc guards against this automatically. <strong>If a sync has been in progress for more than 5 minutes, it's treated as stale.</strong> The next time you click <strong>Sync Data</strong> (or the next automatic sync runs), theStacc clears the stuck sync and starts a fresh one in its place. You don't have to do anything special - just click <strong>Sync Data</strong> again and it will run.</p>
<p>This means a wedged sync can never block you permanently. The worst case is a single click that doesn't appear to respond, after which the next click takes over cleanly.</p>
<h2 id="how-sync-errors-are-recorded">How sync errors are recorded<a aria-label="Copy link to How sync errors are recorded" class="heading-anchor" href="#how-sync-errors-are-recorded">#</a></h2>
<p>When a sync fails, theStacc stores the details so the dashboard can show you what went wrong instead of leaving you guessing. A stored error includes:</p>
<ul><li><strong>An error code</strong> - a short label for the type of failure (for example, a temporary AI overload versus a generic sync failure).</li><li><strong>A message</strong> - a plain-language explanation of what happened.</li><li><strong>A retry-after hint</strong> - for failures that are temporary (like a busy AI service), how long to wait before trying again. For most errors this is zero, meaning you can retry right away.</li><li><strong>A timestamp</strong> - when the failure occurred.</li></ul>
<p>When the failure is a temporary AI overload, the dashboard shows a retry badge with a button so you can try again in one click. For other failures, the dashboard shows the error message along with the <strong>Sync Data</strong> button to retry. The error is cleared automatically the next time a sync completes successfully.</p>
<p>For a fuller breakdown of error types, what they mean, and how to recover from each one, see <a href="/docs/local-seo/error-handling/">Error Handling &amp; Sync Failures</a>.</p>

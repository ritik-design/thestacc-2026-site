title: Reviews
---

 <p>Your Google reviews are one of the biggest factors in whether a nearby customer chooses you. theStacc pulls every review for your Google Business Profile into one place so you can read them, reply quickly with an AI-drafted response, and keep an eye on your overall rating - all without leaving the dashboard.</p>
<h2 id="keep-dashboard">Keep dashboard<a aria-label="Copy link to Keep dashboard" class="heading-anchor" href="#keep-dashboard">#</a></h2>
<p>Open <strong>Local SEO &gt; Reviews</strong> for the business you want to manage. theStacc shows the reviews it has synced from your Google Business Profile, newest first.</p>
<p>If you don't see any reviews yet, click <strong>Sync Data</strong> to fetch them. theStacc reads your reviews from Google (it does not delete or alter them) and refreshes automatically when your data is stale - see <a href="/docs/local-seo/data-sync/">Data Sync</a> for how and when syncing happens. Each pull captures both your newest reviews and your lowest-rated ones, so negative reviews that need a reply never get buried.</p>
<p>When reviews refresh, any work you've already done is preserved. A review you've replied to or marked as handled keeps its status; only genuinely new reviews come in flagged as needing attention.</p>
<h3 id="sub-tabs">Sub-tabs<a aria-label="Copy link to Sub-tabs" class="heading-anchor" href="#sub-tabs">#</a></h3>
<p>At the top of the list you can switch between two views:</p>
<ul><li><strong>All Reviews</strong> - every review for this business.</li><li><strong>Replied</strong> - only reviews that already have an AI response drafted, copied, or published.</li></ul>
<h2 id="filtering-and-sorting">Filtering and sorting<a aria-label="Copy link to Filtering and sorting" class="heading-anchor" href="#filtering-and-sorting">#</a></h2>
<h3 id="filter-by-status">Filter by status<a aria-label="Copy link to Filter by status" class="heading-anchor" href="#filter-by-status">#</a></h3>
<p>Every review carries a status that tells you where it is in your workflow:</p>
<ul><li><strong>New</strong> - synced from Google and not yet handled. These are the ones that need your attention.</li><li><strong>Response generated</strong> - theStacc has drafted an AI reply, but you haven't acted on it yet. The dashboard labels this <strong>Responded</strong>.</li><li><strong>Response copied</strong> - you copied the reply to paste into Google yourself. The dashboard labels this <strong>Copied</strong>.</li><li><strong>Response published</strong> - the reply was posted directly to your Google Business Profile from theStacc.</li><li><strong>Ignored</strong> - a review you've chosen to set aside so it stops showing up as needing attention.</li></ul>
<h3 id="filter-by-rating">Filter by rating<a aria-label="Copy link to Filter by rating" class="heading-anchor" href="#filter-by-rating">#</a></h3>
<p>Use the rating dropdown to narrow the list:</p>
<ul><li><strong>All Reviews</strong> - the full history.</li><li><strong>Needs Attention</strong> - every review rated <strong>3 stars or lower</strong>. These are the reviews where a thoughtful reply matters most.</li><li><strong>5 Star</strong>, <strong>4 Star</strong>, <strong>3 Star</strong>, <strong>2 Star</strong>, <strong>1 Star</strong> - show only reviews with that exact rating.</li></ul>
<p>Each option shows a live count next to it, so you can see at a glance how many 1-star reviews you have or how many still need attention.</p>
<h3 id="sorting">Sorting<a aria-label="Copy link to Sorting" class="heading-anchor" href="#sorting">#</a></h3>
<p>Reviews can be ordered by:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Newest</strong> (the default) - most recent reviews first.</li><li><strong>Oldest</strong> - oldest reviews first.</li><li><strong>Lowest</strong> - lowest star ratings first, so unhappy customers rise to the top.</li><li><strong>Highest</strong> - highest star ratings first.</li></ul>
<h2 id="review-details">Review details<a aria-label="Copy link to Review details" class="heading-anchor" href="#review-details">#</a></h2>
<p>Each review in the list shows:</p>
<ul><li>The reviewer's <strong>name</strong> (or "Anonymous" if Google didn't share one) and profile photo when available.</li><li>A <strong>star rating</strong> badge, color-coded green for 4-5 stars, amber for 3 stars, and red for 1-2 stars.</li><li>The <strong>date</strong> the review was posted.</li><li>The <strong>review text</strong>. If the customer left a rating with no written review, theStacc shows "Rating only - no written review."</li><li>A <strong>status badge</strong> showing where the review is in your workflow.</li><li>Your <strong>AI response</strong> (if one has been drafted) and, where relevant, the date it was generated, copied, or published.</li></ul>
<h2 id="ai-responses">AI responses<a aria-label="Copy link to AI responses" class="heading-anchor" href="#ai-responses">#</a></h2>
<p>For any review, theStacc can draft a professional, personalized reply for you.</p>
<ol><li>Find the review you want to reply to.</li><li>Click <strong>Generate Response</strong>.</li><li>theStacc writes a reply and the review moves to the <strong>Responded</strong> status.</li></ol>
<p>The reply is tailored to the review's tone:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Positive reviews (4-5 stars)</strong> get a warm thank-you that references what the customer praised and invites them back.</li><li><strong>Neutral reviews (3 stars)</strong> thank the customer, acknowledge their concerns, and offer to improve their next visit.</li><li><strong>Negative reviews (1-2 stars)</strong> open with a sincere apology, take responsibility without being defensive, address the specific issue, and offer a way to make it right.</li></ul>
<p>Responses are short and natural - usually two to four sentences - and use your business name. They avoid generic filler, excuses, and blaming the customer.</p>
<h3 id="add-your-own-touch">Add your own touch<a aria-label="Copy link to Add your own touch" class="heading-anchor" href="#add-your-own-touch">#</a></h3>
<p>When generating a reply you can optionally provide:</p>
<ul><li>An <strong>owner or signer name</strong> - the reply is signed off from that person instead of "the business team."</li><li><strong>Additional context</strong> - anything specific you want the reply to mention or account for (for example, "we've since fixed the wait-time issue" or "offer them a free coffee on their next visit").</li></ul>
<h3 id="regenerate">Regenerate<a aria-label="Copy link to Regenerate" class="heading-anchor" href="#regenerate">#</a></h3>
<p>Not happy with the draft? Click <strong>Regenerate</strong> to get a fresh take. theStacc writes a new reply from scratch, and you can keep regenerating until it sounds right.</p>
<h3 id="copy-or-publish">Copy or publish<a aria-label="Copy link to Copy or publish" class="heading-anchor" href="#copy-or-publish">#</a></h3>
<p>Once you have a reply you like, you have two ways to use it:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Copy</strong> - copies the reply text to your clipboard so you can paste it into Google yourself. theStacc marks the review as <strong>Copied</strong> so you can track which ones you've handled manually.</li><li><strong>Post Reply</strong> - publishes the reply directly to your Google Business Profile, no copy-paste needed. This button only appears when your Google Business Profile is connected. (See <a href="/docs/local-seo/google-business-profile/">Google Business Profile</a> to connect.)</li></ul>
<p>You can only post a reply after a response has been generated for that review.</p>
<h2 id="publishing-a-reply-directly">Publishing a reply directly<a aria-label="Copy link to Publishing a reply directly" class="heading-anchor" href="#publishing-a-reply-directly">#</a></h2>
<p>When your Google Business Profile is connected, <strong>Post Reply</strong> sends your drafted response straight to Google.</p>
<p>To post the reply to the correct review, theStacc matches your review against the review inbox on your connected profile. It matches on the <strong>reviewer's name</strong>, the <strong>full review text</strong>, and the <strong>timestamp</strong> (within a 24-hour window). Requiring an exact text match plus a tight time window makes it effectively impossible to reply to the wrong review.</p>
<p>A few things to know:</p>
<ul><li>If theStacc can't confidently single out the matching review - for example, two different reviews look identical - it will refuse to post and ask you to copy and paste the reply manually instead. This is a safety feature, not a bug.</li><li>If the review hasn't finished syncing to your connected profile yet, posting may not be available immediately. Try again in a little while, or use <strong>Copy</strong> to publish it manually.</li><li>If a publish attempt fails, theStacc records the reason and shows it on the review so you know exactly what went wrong before trying again.</li></ul>
<p>After a successful post, the review moves to the <strong>Published</strong> status and is labeled <strong>Published Reply</strong>.</p>
<h2 id="compliance-check-for-regulated-businesses">Compliance check for regulated businesses<a aria-label="Copy link to Compliance check for regulated businesses" class="heading-anchor" href="#compliance-check-for-regulated-businesses">#</a></h2>
<p>If your business is in a regulated field (such as legal, healthcare, or finance) and you've turned on the compliance layer, every reply is checked <strong>before</strong> it can be posted to Google.</p>
<p>The check runs automatically when you click <strong>Post Reply</strong>. If the reply contains language that needs a human's review, theStacc holds it back, does not post it, and shows you the reason on the review. There's no way to override the hold - this is by design, so a non-compliant reply can never reach the public. Fix the reply (regenerate it or adjust your context) and try again, or copy it out for manual handling after your own review.</p>
<p>For most businesses this check does nothing at all and replies post normally. To learn what the compliance layer does and how to enable it, see <a href="/docs/local-seo/advertising-compliance/">Advertising Compliance</a>.</p>
<h2 id="metrics">Metrics<a aria-label="Copy link to Metrics" class="heading-anchor" href="#metrics">#</a></h2>
<p>The Reviews dashboard tracks the health of your reputation over time:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Total reviews</strong> - the total number of reviews on your Google Business Profile, taken directly from Google's own count.</li><li><strong>Average rating</strong> - your overall star rating, taken directly from Google.</li><li><strong>Rating distribution</strong> - how many reviews you have at each star level, from 1 to 5.</li><li><strong>Reviews this week</strong> - how many new reviews came in over the last seven days.</li><li><strong>Needing a response</strong> - how many reviews are still in the <strong>New</strong> status and haven't been handled.</li><li><strong>Needs attention</strong> - how many reviews are rated 3 stars or lower.</li></ul>
<p>Your total review count and average rating always come straight from Google rather than being calculated from the sample theStacc syncs, so they match what customers see on your listing.</p>
<h2 id="best-practices">Best practices<a aria-label="Copy link to Best practices" class="heading-anchor" href="#best-practices">#</a></h2>
<ul><li><strong>Respond to every review</strong>, positive or negative. An active review profile is a local ranking signal and shows prospective customers you're engaged.</li><li><strong>Reply quickly</strong> - within 24 to 48 hours has the most impact. Use the <strong>Needs Attention</strong> filter to triage the reviews that matter most first.</li><li><strong>Personalize the AI draft.</strong> The generated reply is a strong starting point - add the owner name or extra context, or regenerate, so it sounds like a real person from your team.</li><li><strong>Lead with empathy on negative reviews.</strong> Apologize, take responsibility, and move the conversation offline by offering a way to make it right.</li><li><strong>Connect your Google Business Profile</strong> so you can post replies in one click instead of copying and pasting. See <a href="/docs/local-seo/google-business-profile/">Google Business Profile</a>.</li><li><strong>Keep your reviews fresh</strong> by letting theStacc sync regularly, so new reviews surface for a reply while they're still recent. See <a href="/docs/local-seo/data-sync/">Data Sync</a>.</li></ul>

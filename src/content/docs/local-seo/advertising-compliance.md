---
title: Advertising Compliance & Validation
---

 <p>If your business advertises in a regulated profession (for example healthcare, law, mortgage, real estate, or contracting), theStacc can check your Local SEO content against advertising-compliance rules <strong>before</strong> it goes live. This page explains exactly what gets checked, what blocks a publish, and where the result is recorded.</p>
<blockquote><p>This is an optional, opt-in feature. If you have not turned on a compliance profile for your project, nothing on this page applies and your posts and review replies publish exactly as they always have. See <strong>Compliance Profiles</strong> under Content SEO at <a href="/docs/content-seo/compliance-profiles/">/docs/content-seo/compliance-profiles</a> to learn how to enable and configure it.</p></blockquote>
<h2 id="what-gets-checked">What gets checked<a aria-label="Copy link to What gets checked" class="heading-anchor" href="#what-gets-checked">#</a></h2>
<p>When a project has a compliance profile enabled, two Local SEO surfaces are validated right before they publish:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Google Business Profile posts</strong> (both when you publish manually and when auto-publish runs on schedule)</li><li><strong>Review replies</strong> posted to Google Business Profile</li></ul>
<p>Both surfaces are short and length-constrained (a GBP post or a 2 to 4 sentence review reply cannot carry a full legal disclaimer), so theStacc runs a <strong>short-form</strong> version of the same compliance engine used for your blog content. This keeps the focus on the substance of what you can and cannot say, rather than forcing a license number or full disclaimer footer into a place it does not fit.</p>
<p>The check looks at four things:</p>
<h3 id="1-violations">1. Violations<a aria-label="Copy link to 1. Violations" class="heading-anchor" href="#1-violations">#</a></h3>
<p>These are things your rule pack says you must <strong>never</strong> say in advertising for your profession. theStacc detects them three ways:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Exact phrase matches</strong> against a blocklist of prohibited terms.</li><li><strong>Pattern matches</strong> for prohibited phrasing (for example a stated rate that must always be accompanied by an APR disclosure).</li><li><strong>A judgment check</strong> for the cases a simple keyword search cannot catch, such as fabricated testimonials, prohibited guarantees, unsubstantiated claims, or protected health information. This runs as a single, fast classification pass. It only decides "does this rule fire, yes or no" - it never rewrites your content. When it is genuinely unsure, it is built to err on the side of flagging for a human to look at.</li></ul>
<h3 id="2-missing-required-insertions">2. Missing required insertions<a aria-label="Copy link to 2. Missing required insertions" class="heading-anchor" href="#2-missing-required-insertions">#</a></h3>
<p>Some rule packs require specific disclosures (for example a firm name or a license number). On these short surfaces, required disclosures live at the <strong>profile or bio level</strong>, not stuffed into every post, so a missing disclosure is <strong>recorded</strong> but does <strong>not</strong> by itself stop a short post from publishing. The substantive prohibitions above are what gate a short post.</p>
<h3 id="3-severity">3. Severity<a aria-label="Copy link to 3. Severity" class="heading-anchor" href="#3-severity">#</a></h3>
<p>Every check rolls up to one overall severity:</p>
<ul><li><strong>none</strong> - nothing fired. Safe to publish.</li><li><strong>hold_for_review</strong> - something needs a human to look at it before it goes out.</li><li><strong>block</strong> - a hard violation that must not publish.</li></ul>
<p>When more than one issue is found, the strictest one wins (block outranks hold-for-review outranks none).</p>
<h3 id="4-never-autopilot">4. Never-autopilot<a aria-label="Copy link to 4. Never-autopilot" class="heading-anchor" href="#4-never-autopilot">#</a></h3>
<p>Some rule packs (for example certain attorney-advertising rules in specific states) forbid auto-publishing advertising at all. When a project's rules include one of these, the content is flagged <strong>never-autopilot</strong>. This flag holds the publish regardless of whether any other violation fired.</p>
<h2 id="what-blocks-a-publish">What blocks a publish<a aria-label="Copy link to What blocks a publish" class="heading-anchor" href="#what-blocks-a-publish">#</a></h2>
<p>The gate policy is simple and the same for GBP posts and review replies:</p>
<p><strong>A publish is stopped whenever the result is <code>block</code>, <code>hold_for_review</code>, or <code>never-autopilot</code>.</strong></p>
<p>When that happens:</p>
<ul><li>The content is <strong>not</strong> posted to Google Business Profile.</li><li>The <strong>reason is recorded</strong> on the item so you can see why. A GBP post is marked as failed to publish, with the reason saved in its publish error. A review reply has the reason saved in its response publish error.</li><li>If you triggered the publish from the app, you get a clear message back explaining the hold instead of a silent success.</li></ul>
<h3 id="there-is-no-override-here">There is no override here<a aria-label="Copy link to There is no override here" class="heading-anchor" href="#there-is-no-override-here">#</a></h3>
<p>Unlike long-form blog content (where a held draft can be reviewed and approved by a person), <strong>Local SEO has no override path</strong>. A held or blocked GBP post or review reply cannot be force-published. To get it out, you <strong>edit the content to resolve the issue and publish again</strong>. The corrected version is re-checked from scratch.</p>
<h3 id="auto-publish-scheduled-posts">Auto-publish (scheduled posts)<a aria-label="Copy link to Auto-publish (scheduled posts)" class="heading-anchor" href="#auto-publish-scheduled-posts">#</a></h3>
<p>When the daily auto-publish job runs, there is no person in the loop to clear a hold. So for scheduled GBP posts, <strong>block, hold-for-review, and never-autopilot all stop the post the same way</strong> - it is marked failed-to-publish with the recorded reason and skipped. You can then open it, fix it, and publish manually.</p>
<h2 id="what-happens-if-the-check-itself-errors">What happens if the check itself errors<a aria-label="Copy link to What happens if the check itself errors" class="heading-anchor" href="#what-happens-if-the-check-itself-errors">#</a></h2>
<p>theStacc is deliberately careful here, because the worst outcome would be shipping unreviewed regulated advertising.</p>
<ul><li><strong>The check is fail-closed.</strong> If theStacc cannot load your compliance profile, or the validation errors part-way through, the content is <strong>held for review</strong> rather than published. A check that could not run is never treated as a pass.</li><li><strong>One narrow fail-open case:</strong> if the shared compliance engine itself is unavailable in the environment (an infrastructure-level problem that would affect every project equally, not a signal about your specific content), the publish proceeds as it normally would. This prevents a platform-wide outage from silently blocking everyone, while still failing closed on anything that is actually about your content or your profile.</li></ul>
<p>Projects <strong>without</strong> a compliance profile are always a no-op - they are never held by this gate.</p>
<h2 id="the-audit-trail">The audit trail<a aria-label="Copy link to The audit trail" class="heading-anchor" href="#the-audit-trail">#</a></h2>
<p>Every time the gate evaluates a Google Business Profile post - whether it passes, holds, or blocks - theStacc records a compliance check on the post for your records. That record includes:</p>
<ul><li>The <strong>rule-pack versions</strong> that were applied (so you can show exactly which version of which rules ran).</li><li>The <strong>violations</strong> that fired.</li><li>Which required disclosures were supplied automatically and which were still missing.</li><li>The <strong>result</strong>: passed or not, the severity, the review level, and the never-autopilot flag.</li><li>A <strong>timestamp</strong> of when the check ran.</li></ul>
<p>This is your "we assisted, the rules were applied, here is what happened" evidence trail. Because review replies are stored more simply, they do not carry this full record - for a held reply, the reason is saved directly on the review's response publish error field instead.</p>

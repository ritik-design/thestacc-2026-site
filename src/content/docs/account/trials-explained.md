---
title: Trials Explained
---

 <p>Every theStacc module comes with a low-cost trial so you can see real output before you commit to a paid plan. This article explains exactly how trials work - what you pay, what you get, how long they last, and what happens when one ends.</p>
<h2 id="the-1-trial">The $1 trial<a aria-label="Copy link to The $1 trial" class="heading-anchor" href="#the-1-trial">#</a></h2>
<p>Each module - <strong>Content SEO</strong>, <strong>Local SEO</strong>, and <strong>Social Media</strong> - has its own trial that starts at <strong>$1</strong>. You pay a single dollar to unlock the module and start generating content right away.</p>
<p>During a trial you get:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Full feature access</strong> - the trial is not a stripped-down version. You can use the module exactly as a paying customer would, so the work you create during the trial is real, usable output.</li><li><strong>Days remaining on your dashboard</strong> - your dashboard and the <strong>Settings &gt; Billing</strong> page show how many days are left in your trial, and you will see a reminder as the end date approaches.</li></ul>
<p>Because each module is billed separately, you can trial one module, two, or all three - you only ever pay for what you start.</p>
<h2 id="how-long-a-trial-lasts">How long a trial lasts<a aria-label="Copy link to How long a trial lasts" class="heading-anchor" href="#how-long-a-trial-lasts">#</a></h2>
<p>The standard trial runs for <strong>3 days</strong>. The countdown starts the moment your trial begins, and the exact end date is shown on your billing page.</p>
<p>You get <strong>one trial per module</strong>. Once a module's trial has been used, that module will not offer another $1 trial - if you want to keep using it after the trial, you move to a paid plan. (Trials are per module, so trialing Content SEO does not use up your Local SEO or Social trial.)</p>
<h2 id="choosing-your-paid-plan-at-checkout">Choosing your paid plan at checkout<a aria-label="Copy link to Choosing your paid plan at checkout" class="heading-anchor" href="#choosing-your-paid-plan-at-checkout">#</a></h2>
<p>When you start a trial, you pick the paid plan you want to continue on at checkout. That choice is what the trial converts into:</p>
<ol><li>Open the module and choose to start or upgrade.</li><li>Select the plan tier and billing cycle (monthly or annual) you want.</li><li>Complete checkout for $1.</li></ol>
<p>While the trial is running, your subscription status shows as <strong>Trialing</strong> on the billing page. When the trial converts, that same subscription becomes <strong>Active</strong> on the plan you chose - there is no second checkout to complete and no gap in access.</p>
<h2 id="subscription-status-during-and-after-a-trial">Subscription status during and after a trial<a aria-label="Copy link to Subscription status during and after a trial" class="heading-anchor" href="#subscription-status-during-and-after-a-trial">#</a></h2>
<p>Your module subscription can be in one of these states:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Trialing</strong> - your trial is active and you have full access.</li><li><strong>Active</strong> - your paid plan is running.</li><li><strong>Past Due</strong> - a payment failed and needs attention.</li><li><strong>Cancelled</strong> - the subscription has been cancelled (access continues until the end of the current period).</li></ul>
<p>You can see the status of every module under <strong>Settings &gt; Billing</strong>.</p>
<h2 id="what-happens-when-a-trial-ends">What happens when a trial ends<a aria-label="Copy link to What happens when a trial ends" class="heading-anchor" href="#what-happens-when-a-trial-ends">#</a></h2>
<p>A trial is the first cycle of a subscription, so what happens next depends on whether you let it convert or you cancel.</p>
<h3 id="if-you-do-nothing">If you do nothing<a aria-label="Copy link to If you do nothing" class="heading-anchor" href="#if-you-do-nothing">#</a></h3>
<p>The trial converts to the paid plan you selected at checkout, and your subscription moves from <strong>Trialing</strong> to <strong>Active</strong>. Your dashboard continues working without interruption and your content keeps generating on schedule.</p>
<h3 id="if-billing-does-not-go-through">If billing does not go through<a aria-label="Copy link to If billing does not go through" class="heading-anchor" href="#if-billing-does-not-go-through">#</a></h3>
<p>If the subscription cannot continue - for example a payment cannot be collected - the module subscription stops and access to that module is switched off. Anything you have already published stays exactly where it is; theStacc never removes published content.</p>
<h3 id="cancelling-before-or-after-you-are-charged">Cancelling before (or after) you are charged<a aria-label="Copy link to Cancelling before (or after) you are charged" class="heading-anchor" href="#cancelling-before-or-after-you-are-charged">#</a></h3>
<p>You can cancel any module at any time. Cancellation is handled in the secure billing portal:</p>
<ol><li>Go to <strong>Settings &gt; Billing</strong>.</li><li>Choose the module you want to cancel and select the option to cancel the plan.</li><li>You are taken to the billing portal (provided by our payment processor, Dodo Payments) in a new tab.</li><li>Confirm the cancellation there.</li></ol>
<p>When you cancel, your access continues until the end of the current period - you are not cut off the instant you click cancel. There is no contract and no cancellation fee.</p>
<p>You can also use the billing portal to update your payment method, view your payment history, and download invoices.</p>
<h2 id="local-seo-trial-specifics">Local SEO trial specifics<a aria-label="Copy link to Local SEO trial specifics" class="heading-anchor" href="#local-seo-trial-specifics">#</a></h2>
<p>The Local SEO trial follows the same $1, <strong>3-day</strong> structure, with these module limits while you are on the trial:</p>
<ul><li><strong>1 location</strong> - you can connect and optimize one Google Business Profile location.</li><li><strong>3 local posts</strong> - you can generate up to 3 posts for that location during the trial.</li></ul>
<p>To keep going past the trial - or to add more locations and posts - move to a paid Local SEO plan. For the full list of paid limits, see the <a href="/docs/local-seo/overview/">Local SEO Overview</a>.</p>
<h2 id="social-media-trial-specifics">Social Media trial specifics<a aria-label="Copy link to Social Media trial specifics" class="heading-anchor" href="#social-media-trial-specifics">#</a></h2>
<p>The Social Media trial is also a $1, <strong>3-day</strong> trial, with these limits:</p>
<ul><li><strong>3 posts</strong> - you can generate up to 3 social posts during the trial.</li><li><strong>3 connected platforms</strong> - you can connect up to 3 social platforms.</li></ul>
<p>Paid Social plans raise both the monthly post quota and the number of platforms you can connect. See the <a href="/docs/social-media/overview/">Social Media Overview</a> for details.</p>
<h2 id="admin-granted-trials">Admin-granted trials<a aria-label="Copy link to Admin-granted trials" class="heading-anchor" href="#admin-granted-trials">#</a></h2>
<p>In some cases - for example a guided demo or a special arrangement with our team - theStacc may grant access directly instead of through the standard $1 checkout. An admin-granted plan can be set up <strong>without an expiry date</strong>, so it does not count down or end like a normal 3-day trial. If your access was set up this way, your billing page reflects the access you were granted rather than a self-serve trial countdown. If you have questions about an access grant, contact our team.</p>

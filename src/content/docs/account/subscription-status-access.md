---
title: Subscription Status & Access
---

 <p>Every module you run (Content SEO, Local SEO, Social Media) has its own subscription, and that subscription's <strong>status</strong> decides whether you can keep generating content. This page explains each status, what it means for your access, and where to see it.</p>
<p>To see your current plan and status at any time, open <strong>Settings &gt; Billing</strong>. Each module is tracked separately there, so you might have one module active and another in a different state.</p>
<h2 id="the-four-statuses">The four statuses<a aria-label="Copy link to The four statuses" class="heading-anchor" href="#the-four-statuses">#</a></h2>
<h3 id="active">Active<a aria-label="Copy link to Active" class="heading-anchor" href="#active">#</a></h3>
<p>A paid subscription that is running normally. You have full access to the module and can generate content up to your plan's limit. Nothing is required from you - your card is charged automatically each billing cycle.</p>
<h3 id="trialing">Trialing<a aria-label="Copy link to Trialing" class="heading-anchor" href="#trialing">#</a></h3>
<p>Your trial period is running. You get <strong>full access to all of the module's features</strong> during the trial, exactly like a paid plan. Your dashboard and billing page show the days remaining, so you always know how long is left.</p>
<p>When the trial ends, you either convert to a paid plan (access continues seamlessly) or access turns off. You can upgrade to a paid plan at any point during the trial - see <a href="/docs/account/billing/">Billing &amp; Plans</a>.</p>
<h3 id="past-due">Past due<a aria-label="Copy link to Past due" class="heading-anchor" href="#past-due">#</a></h3>
<p>A scheduled payment failed. This usually means the card on file expired, was declined, or hit a limit. <strong>Action is needed</strong>: update your payment method so billing can retry and your access is restored.</p>
<p>To fix it, open your billing portal from <strong>Settings &gt; Billing</strong>, update your card, and the next charge will go through. If you are unsure, contact support and we will help you get it sorted.</p>
<h3 id="cancelled">Cancelled<a aria-label="Copy link to Cancelled" class="heading-anchor" href="#cancelled">#</a></h3>
<p>The subscription has been cancelled, but <strong>you keep access until the end of the current billing period</strong>. You do not lose anything the moment you cancel - you have already paid for the rest of the cycle, so you can keep using the module right up to the period end date.</p>
<p>When that date passes, access turns off. Anything you have already published stays exactly where it is - theStacc never removes content you have published to your site or social accounts. To come back, just start a new subscription for the module.</p>

<h2 id="admin-granted-plans">Admin-granted plans<a aria-label="Copy link to Admin-granted plans" class="heading-anchor" href="#admin-granted-plans">#</a></h2>
<p>If our team has granted you a plan directly - for example a demo or an extended evaluation - your billing page may show a trial-style label even though there is <strong>no expiry date</strong>. These grants are real, active access: they are tied to your module activation, not to a trial clock, so they will not flip off when a leftover trial date passes. If you have an admin-granted plan, treat it as active. If anything looks off, reach out to support.</p>
<h2 id="how-access-works-across-your-team">How access works across your team<a aria-label="Copy link to How access works across your team" class="heading-anchor" href="#how-access-works-across-your-team">#</a></h2>
<p>A module subscription belongs to the <strong>project owner's account</strong>. Everyone who collaborates on that project rides on the owner's plan - so an invited teammate (or a collaborator from a different account) can create and edit content on a paid project even if their own account has never subscribed. The owner is paying; the rest of the team is along for the ride.</p>
<p>This is intentional and is how access is checked everywhere in the product. The one thing it does <strong>not</strong> do is leak plan details to people who should not see them: if someone is not actually a member of a project, the system falls back to checking their own account instead of the owner's, so plan and billing information is never disclosed to an outsider who simply knows a project exists.</p>
<p>For more on roles and inviting people, see <a href="/docs/account/workspace-team/">Workspace &amp; Team</a>.</p>
<h2 id="your-plan-limit-and-how-it-resets">Your plan limit and how it resets<a aria-label="Copy link to Your plan limit and how it resets" class="heading-anchor" href="#your-plan-limit-and-how-it-resets">#</a></h2>
<p>Your plan includes a content allowance that refreshes every cycle. The number shown on your billing page is your <strong>per-cycle limit</strong> - what you can produce in the current cycle - not a pooled total for the whole subscription.</p>
<p>This matters most on annual plans. For example, a plan written as <strong>30 per cycle</strong> on a yearly subscription gives you 30 each month (resetting monthly), not 360 to spend however you like across the year. There are two separate timelines:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Your quota cycle</strong> - the window your usage is counted against. It resets on its schedule (most plans reset monthly), giving you a fresh allowance each time.</li><li><strong>Your billing window</strong> - when your card is charged next. On a yearly plan this is once a year, even though your content allowance resets every cycle within it.</li></ul>
<p>Each module has its own independent allowance and its own cycle. If you hit the cap for a cycle, generation pauses for that module until the cycle resets - the in-app message tells you when that is. To learn how allowances are counted per module (and what does and does not use up a slot), see <a href="/docs/account/billing/">Billing &amp; Plans</a>.</p>
<h2 id="where-to-check-your-plan-and-status">Where to check your plan and status<a aria-label="Copy link to Where to check your plan and status" class="heading-anchor" href="#where-to-check-your-plan-and-status">#</a></h2>
<ol><li>Open <strong>Settings &gt; Billing</strong>.</li><li>Find the module you want to check (Content SEO, Local SEO, or Social Media).</li><li>You will see its current <strong>status</strong> (Active, Trialing, Past Due, or Cancelled), your plan name, your per-cycle allowance, and your current period dates.</li></ol>
<p>During a trial, the days remaining also appear on your dashboard so you can keep an eye on it without digging into settings.</p>

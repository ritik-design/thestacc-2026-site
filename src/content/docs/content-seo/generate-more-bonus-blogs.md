---
title: Generate More & Bonus Blogs
---

 <p>Most of the time, theStacc plans your blogs for you and lays them out across your subscription period. But sometimes you want a burst of extra posts - a seasonal push, a new product launch, or simply because you have room left on your plan and want to use it. That is what <strong>Generate More</strong> is for.</p>
<p>Generate More adds a batch of extra "bonus" blogs to a project. Each one arrives with a real AI-written title, focus keyword, and category - not a blank placeholder - so you can review them and decide when each should publish.</p>
<h2 id="what-generate-more-does">What Generate More does<a aria-label="Copy link to What Generate More does" class="heading-anchor" href="#what-generate-more-does">#</a></h2>
<p>When you ask for more blogs, theStacc does two things in one request:</p>
<ol><li><strong>Creates the bonus blogs.</strong> It adds the number you asked for to the project, each in <strong>Awaiting date</strong> status. They do not land on your calendar with a date yet - they wait for you to choose when each one publishes.</li><li><strong>Fills in titles, keywords, and categories.</strong> In a single batched AI call, it plans all of the new blogs at once using your business analysis, so each bonus blog gets a real title, a focus keyword, and a category instead of generic "Bonus blog" text.</li></ol>
<p>Because everything is planned in one call, adding ten bonus blogs is about as fast as adding one - it usually takes 10 to 20 seconds. While that is running, keep the dialog open so the request can finish.</p>
<p>Nothing is generated yet at this stage. The bonus blogs are planned, but no article body or image is written until you give each one a publish date (see <a href="#picking-a-publish-date">Picking dates</a> below). Until then, they cost you nothing.</p>
<h2 id="where-to-find-it">Where to find it<a aria-label="Copy link to Where to find it" class="heading-anchor" href="#where-to-find-it">#</a></h2>
<p>Generate More lives on your <strong>Content SEO</strong> dashboard. When the button is available for your project, you will see a <strong>Generate more (up to N)</strong> button near your blog list, where N is how many more blogs you can add right now.</p>
<p>The button is only shown when it can actually do something. It stays hidden when:</p>
<ul><li>The project is still loading.</li><li>Your plan gives unlimited blogs - in that case you generate freely through the normal flow, so a "more" button would have nothing to add.</li><li>You are on a fixed-allotment plan that already filled its whole period up front (for example, a 30-blog plan where all 30 are already scheduled in your first cycle). Those customers add more blogs by generating the next period's plan instead.</li><li>You have no headroom left this period, or the current period has already ended.</li></ul>
<h2 id="what-you-need-before-it-will-work">What you need before it will work<a aria-label="Copy link to What you need before it will work" class="heading-anchor" href="#what-you-need-before-it-will-work">#</a></h2>
<p>Generate More only works when all of the following are true.</p>
<h3 id="an-active-plan-that-covers-today">An active plan that covers today<a aria-label="Copy link to An active plan that covers today" class="heading-anchor" href="#an-active-plan-that-covers-today">#</a></h3>
<p>The project must have an <strong>active subscription</strong> for the Content SEO module - not cancelled, not an expired trial. If the plan is no longer active, the request is refused with a message asking you to update your plan or contact support.</p>
<p>There must also be a <strong>content plan whose date range covers today</strong>. Generate More attaches the new bonus blogs to your current content plan. If no plan covers the current date, you will see a message asking you to generate a content plan first. See <a href="/docs/content-seo/content-plans/">Content Plans &amp; Keywords</a> for how plans are created.</p>
<p>If you collaborate on someone else's project through a share link or invite, the <strong>project owner's</strong> plan and quota are what get checked - not your own account's.</p>
<h3 id="available-headroom">Available headroom<a aria-label="Copy link to Available headroom" class="heading-anchor" href="#available-headroom">#</a></h3>
<p>Every project has a blog quota for its current billing period. <strong>Headroom</strong> is simply how many more blogs you can still add this period:</p>
<blockquote><p>headroom = your period quota − blogs already scheduled this period</p></blockquote>
<p>The dialog shows you this directly - for example, "You have 12 of 30 blogs scheduled. Add up to 18 more." You can request any number from 1 up to your remaining headroom in a single click. If you ask for more than your headroom, the request is rejected and you are pointed toward upgrading your plan for more capacity.</p>
<h2 id="bonus-blogs-count-against-your-quota">Bonus blogs count against your quota<a aria-label="Copy link to Bonus blogs count against your quota" class="heading-anchor" href="#bonus-blogs-count-against-your-quota">#</a></h2>
<p>This is the key thing to understand: <strong>bonus blogs are not free extras on top of your plan.</strong> Each bonus blog you add consumes one slot of your period quota, exactly like a normally planned blog. That is why the dialog caps your request at your remaining headroom.</p>
<p>The period here is your <strong>subscription's billing window</strong>, not a fixed calendar month. A yearly plan, for example, can pace its blogs across the whole year, so headroom is measured against your actual renewal cycle.</p>
<p>One flexible exception: when you pick a publish date for a bonus blog, you may choose a date in a <strong>future</strong> period. theStacc allows this on purpose - if you have paid through that future period, you can plan ahead into it. Scheduling a bonus blog into a later period frees that slot back up in your current period's headroom.</p>
<h2 id="limits-on-how-many-you-can-add">Limits on how many you can add<a aria-label="Copy link to Limits on how many you can add" class="heading-anchor" href="#limits-on-how-many-you-can-add">#</a></h2>
<p>There are a few different limits at play, so here is exactly how they fit together:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Your headroom</strong> is the everyday limit. On a capped plan, you can never add more than your plan's quota minus what is already scheduled. This is the limit you will hit in normal use.</li><li><strong>A hard ceiling of 200 blogs per request</strong> applies to every request, regardless of plan. This exists mainly to protect unlimited-style plans from accidentally asking for a huge batch in one click. If you request more than 200, you are asked to add 200 or fewer and simply click again for the rest.</li><li><strong>Fixed-allotment plans</strong> (for example, plans that grant 30 blogs per period) are naturally bounded by that allotment through your headroom - there is no separate per-click number for them.</li></ul>
<p>If you drive theStacc through the <strong>agent (MCP) integration</strong>, note that the <code>generate_more</code> tool there accepts <strong>1 to 10</strong> blogs per call by design, even though the underlying limit is higher. Agents add blogs in smaller, reviewable batches.</p>
<h2 id="picking-a-publish-date">Picking a publish date<a aria-label="Copy link to Picking a publish date" class="heading-anchor" href="#picking-a-publish-date">#</a></h2>
<p>A bonus blog cannot generate until you tell theStacc when it should publish. Right after you add bonus blogs, a <strong>Pick a date for your bonus blogs</strong> panel appears on your dashboard listing each new blog with its AI-filled title and keyword.</p>
<p>To schedule one:</p>
<ol><li>Find the bonus blog in the <strong>Awaiting date</strong> panel.</li><li>Choose a date in the date picker. You can pick <strong>today or any future date</strong> - past dates are not allowed. (If you are in a timezone where it is already "tomorrow" relative to our servers, the system gently nudges a just-past date forward to today so you are not blocked near midnight.)</li><li>Click <strong>Set date</strong>.</li></ol>
<p>That moves the blog from <strong>Awaiting date</strong> to <strong>To be generated</strong>: it drops off the awaiting list, joins your regular content calendar, and becomes eligible to generate on the date you chose - automatically by the scheduler, or whenever you trigger it manually.</p>
<p>You do not have to schedule them all at once. Bonus blogs sit safely in <strong>Awaiting date</strong> for as long as you like, and theStacc never generates one until it has a date. To understand every status a blog can be in, see <a href="/docs/content-seo/blog-status-lifecycle/">Blog Status Lifecycle</a>. For how the calendar and scheduled dates drive automatic generation, see <a href="/docs/content-seo/time-based-scheduling/">Time-Based Scheduling</a>.</p>
<h2 id="if-titles-did-not-finish-generating">If titles did not finish generating<a aria-label="Copy link to If titles did not finish generating" class="heading-anchor" href="#if-titles-did-not-finish-generating">#</a></h2>
<p>Adding the bonus blogs and writing their titles are two separate steps. Occasionally the blogs are created successfully but the batched title-fill step does not complete - for example, the AI call returns nothing, only some of the titles come back, or the project is missing the business analysis snapshot needed to plan titles.</p>
<p>When this happens:</p>
<ul><li><strong>Your bonus blogs still exist.</strong> They were created and counted against your quota - you have not lost them.</li><li>You will see a clear warning that the bonus blogs were added but <strong>title generation didn't complete</strong>, sometimes with a short reason (for example, "Filled 7 of 10 bonus blogs").</li><li>Any blog whose title did not fill shows as <strong>(Awaiting title...)</strong> in the panel.</li></ul>
<h3 id="how-to-recover">How to recover<a aria-label="Copy link to How to recover" class="heading-anchor" href="#how-to-recover">#</a></h3>
<p>You have two paths, and you can use both:</p>
<ol><li><strong>Pick dates now anyway.</strong> A missing title does not block scheduling. You can still set a publish date for each bonus blog. When a blog generates, theStacc refreshes its title and brief from your current business info as part of the generation step, so a blank title fills in then.</li><li><strong>Re-run the title planning.</strong> Use the regenerate-plan flow to fill titles and keywords for the awaiting blogs without waiting for generation. If the failure was caused by a missing business analysis snapshot, re-running your onboarding analysis first (or contacting support) restores what the title planner needs.</li></ol>
<p>If titles repeatedly fail to fill across retries, reach out to support so we can check the project's analysis data.</p>
<h2 id="quick-reference">Quick reference<a aria-label="Copy link to Quick reference" class="heading-anchor" href="#quick-reference">#</a></h2>
<ul><li><strong>What it adds:</strong> N bonus blogs in <strong>Awaiting date</strong> status, each with an AI-written title, keyword, and category planned in one batched call.</li><li><strong>Requires:</strong> an active Content SEO plan, a content plan covering today, and remaining headroom.</li><li><strong>Counts against quota:</strong> yes - each bonus blog uses one slot of your period quota.</li><li><strong>Per-request ceiling:</strong> up to your headroom, with a hard cap of 200 per request (1 to 10 per call via the agent integration).</li><li><strong>To generate one:</strong> pick a publish date (today or later) to move it from <strong>Awaiting date</strong> to <strong>To be generated</strong>.</li></ul>

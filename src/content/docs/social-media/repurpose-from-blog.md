title: Repurpose from Blog
---

 <p>Already wrote a great blog post? Repurpose it into social media drafts in one click. theStacc reads the article and writes a social post that leads with its single most valuable idea - so you don't have to start from a blank page every time you want to share your work.</p>
<p>This is an emerging feature, so the steps and options below describe how it works today. Expect it to grow over time.</p>
<h2 id="what-it-does">What it does<a aria-label="Copy link to What it does" class="heading-anchor" href="#what-it-does">#</a></h2>
<p>From a blog post, theStacc creates <strong>social drafts</strong> written for social media - a caption plus a generated image, not just a copy of your article.</p>
<ul><li>The draft is <strong>generated for the platform</strong>, so the wording fits a social audience rather than reading like the blog headline.</li><li>If the article has a published link, theStacc includes it in the caption when it reads naturally.</li><li>Every draft created this way keeps a <strong>reference back to the blog it came from</strong> (its source blog). That reference is what lets theStacc group your repurposed drafts together and tell them apart from posts you create directly in Social.</li></ul>
<p>Repurposed drafts land alongside your other social posts and publish the same way any social post does.</p>
<h2 id="repurposing-a-blog">Repurposing a blog<a aria-label="Copy link to Repurposing a blog" class="heading-anchor" href="#repurposing-a-blog">#</a></h2>
<p>You start from the blog itself:</p>
<ol><li>Open the blog post from your content list.</li><li>In the blog's side panel, find the <strong>Repurpose to Social</strong> button.</li><li>Click it. theStacc creates the social drafts and a preview opens so you can see the caption and image.</li></ol>
<p>The <strong>Repurpose to Social</strong> button only appears once a blog has real, finished content worth sharing - that means a post that is published, approved, scheduled, or waiting for your review. A blog that hasn't been generated yet has nothing to repurpose, so the button stays hidden until it's ready.</p>
<p>Generation runs in the background, so you can close the preview without losing anything - the drafts keep building and the button turns into a live progress bar you can tap to reopen.</p>
<h2 id="finding-your-repurposed-drafts">Finding your repurposed drafts<a aria-label="Copy link to Finding your repurposed drafts" class="heading-anchor" href="#finding-your-repurposed-drafts">#</a></h2>
<p>Repurposed drafts are saved with your project, so you can come back to them later.</p>
<p>If you have the Social Media module, the simplest way to see only these drafts is to <strong>filter your posts list to repurposed-only</strong>. That narrows the list to posts that were created from a blog (the ones carrying a source-blog reference) and hides everything you created directly in Social.</p>
<p>If you don't have the Social Media module, you'll find your repurposed drafts in the <strong>Generated social drafts</strong> section on your content dashboard. Each one shows its image and caption; click a draft to open the preview, where you can publish it or pick up where you left off.</p>
<h2 id="works-even-without-a-social-subscription">Works even without a Social subscription<a aria-label="Copy link to Works even without a Social subscription" class="heading-anchor" href="#works-even-without-a-social-subscription">#</a></h2>
<p>You do <strong>not</strong> need a Social Media subscription to repurpose a blog or to find the drafts you created.</p>
<ul><li>A project on the Content SEO module gets to try this - the first repurpose on a project is free.</li><li>After generating, you can <strong>connect any one social account and publish that first draft for free</strong>. No plan needed for your first one.</li><li>Listing your repurposed drafts is never blocked by a subscription. Even a blog-only project can always pull up its own repurposed drafts and review them.</li></ul>
<p>When you're ready to share every blog across all your platforms - with full scheduling and editing - that's what the Social Media module adds.</p>
<h2 id="good-to-know">Good to know<a aria-label="Copy link to Good to know" class="heading-anchor" href="#good-to-know">#</a></h2>
<ul><li>This is a newer, evolving feature. What it generates and how it behaves may expand, so treat the details here as today's behavior.</li><li>Repurposed drafts have no content pillar (they came from a blog, not from your social content plan), so they show up labeled generally rather than under a pillar.</li></ul>
